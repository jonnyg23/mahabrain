 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra IX.: Tauba (Repentance) or Barāat (Immunity).</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 14</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/009/009_111.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>111.  Inna All<u>a</u>ha ishtar<u>a</u> mina almu/mineena anfusahum waamw<u>a</u>lahum bi-anna lahumu aljannata yuq<u>a</u>tiloona fee sabeeli All<u>a</u>hi fayaqtuloona wayuqtaloona waAAdan AAalayhi <u>h</u>aqqan fee a<b>l</b>ttawr<u>a</u>ti wa<b>a</b>l-injeeli wa<b>a</b>lqur-<u>a</u>ni waman awf<u>a</u> biAAahdihi mina All<u>a</u>hi fa<b>i</b>stabshiroo bibayAAikumu alla<u>th</u>ee b<u>a</u>yaAAtum bihi wa<u>tha</u>lika huwa alfawzu alAAa<i><u>th</u></i>eem<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>111.  God hath purchased of the Believers<br> Their persons and their goods;<br> For theirs (in return)<br> Is the Garden (of Paradise):<br> They fight in His Cause,<br> And slay and are slain:<br> A promise binding on Him<br> In Truth, through the Law,<br> The Gospel, and the Qur-ān:<br> And who is more faithful<br> To his Covenant than God?<br> Then rejoice in the bargain<br> Which ye have concluded:<br> That is the achievement supreme.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/009/009_112.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>112.  A<b>l</b>tt<u>a</u>-iboona alAA<u>a</u>bidoona al<u>ha</u>midoona a<b>l</b>ss<u>a</u>-i<u>h</u>oona a<b>l</b>rr<u>a</u>kiAAoona a<b>l</b>ss<u>a</u>jidoona al-<u>a</u>miroona bi<b>a</b>lmaAAroofi wa<b>al</b>nn<u>a</u>hoona AAani almunkari wa<b>a</b>l<u>ha</u>fi<i><u>th</u></i>oona li<u>h</u>udoodi All<u>a</u>hi wabashshiri almu/mineen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>112.  Those that turn (to God)<br> In repentance; that serve Him,<br> And praise Him; that wander<br> In devotion to the Cause of God;<br> That bow down and prostrate themselves<br> In prayer; that enjoin good<br> And forbid evil; and observe<br> The limits set by God;—<br> (These do rejoice). So proclaim<br> The glad tidings to the Believers.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/009/009_113.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>113.  M<u>a</u> k<u>a</u>na li<b>l</b>nnabiyyi wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>eena <u>a</u>manoo an yastaghfiroo lilmushrikeena walaw k<u>a</u>noo olee qurb<u>a</u> min baAAdi m<u>a</u> tabayyana lahum annahum a<u>s</u>-<u>ha</u>bu alja<u>h</u>eem<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>113.  It is not fitting,<br> For the Prophet and those<br> Who believe, that they should<br> Pray for forgiveness<br> For Pagans, even though<br> They be of kin, after it is<br> Clear to them that they<br> Are companions of the Fire.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/009/009_114.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>114.  Wam<u>a</u> k<u>a</u>na istighf<u>a</u>ru ibr<u>a</u>heema li-abeehi ill<u>a</u> AAan mawAAidatin waAAadah<u>a</u> iyy<u>a</u>hu falamm<u>a</u> tabayyana lahu annahu AAaduwwun lill<u>a</u>hi tabarraa minhu inna ibr<u>a</u>heema laaww<u>a</u>hun <u>h</u>aleem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>114.  And Abraham prayed<br> For his father's forgiveness<br> Only because of a promise<br> He had made to him.<br> But when it became clear<br> To him that he was<br> An enemy to God, he<br> Dissociated himself from him:<br> For Abraham was most<br> Tender-hearted, forbearing.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/009/009_115.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>115.  Wam<u>a</u> k<u>a</u>na All<u>a</u>hu liyu<u>d</u>illa qawman baAAda i<u>th</u> had<u>a</u>hum <u>h</u>att<u>a</u> yubayyina lahum m<u>a</u> yattaqoona inna All<u>a</u>ha bikulli shay-in AAaleem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>115.  And God will not mislead<br> A people after He hath<br>Guided them, in order that<br> He may make clear to them<br> What to fear (and avoid)—<br> For God hath knowledge<br> Of all things.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/009/009_116.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>116.  Inna All<u>a</u>ha lahu mulku a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>w<u>a</u>ti wa<b>a</b>l-ar<u>d</u>i yu<u>h</u>yee wayumeetu wam<u>a</u> lakum min dooni All<u>a</u>hi min waliyyin wal<u>a</u> na<u>s</u>eer<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>116.  Unto God belongeth<br> The dominion of the heavens<br> And the earth. He giveth life<br> And He taketh it. Except for Him<br> Ye have no protector<br> Nor helper.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/009/009_117.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>117.  Laqad t<u>a</u>ba All<u>a</u>hu AAal<u>a</u> a<b>l</b>nnabiyyi wa<b>a</b>lmuh<u>a</u>jireena wa<b>a</b>l-an<u>sa</u>ri alla<u>th</u>eena ittabaAAoohu fee s<u>a</u>AAati alAAusrati min baAAdi m<u>a</u> k<u>a</u>da yazeeghu quloobu fareeqin minhum thumma t<u>a</u>ba AAalayhim innahu bihim raoofun ra<u>h</u>eem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>117.  God turned with favour<br> To the Prophet, the Muhājirs,<br> And the Anṣār,—who followed<br> Him in a time of distress,<br> After that the hearts of a part<br> Of them had nearly swerved<br> (From duty); but He turned<br> To them (also): for He is<br> Unto them Most Kind,<br> Most Merciful.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/009/009_118.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>118.  WaAAal<u>a</u> a<b>l</b>ththal<u>a</u>thati alla<u>th</u>eena khullifoo <u>h</u>att<u>a</u> i<u>tha</u> <u>da</u>qat AAalayhimu al-ar<u>d</u>u bim<u>a</u> ra<u>h</u>ubat wa<u>da</u>qat AAalayhim anfusuhum wa<i><u>th</u></i>annoo an l<u>a</u> maljaa mina All<u>a</u>hi ill<u>a</u> ilayhi thumma t<u>a</u>ba AAalayhim liyatooboo inna All<u>a</u>ha huwa a<b>l</b>ttaww<u>a</u>bu a<b>l</b>rra<u>h</u>eem<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>118.  (He turned in mercy also)<br> To the three who were left<br> Behind; (they felt guilty)<br> To such a degree that the earth<br>Seemed constrained to them,<br> For all its speciousness,<br> And their (very) Souls seemed<br> Straitened to them,<br> And they perceived that<br> There is no fleeing from God<br> (And no refuge) but to Himself.<br> Then He turned to them,<br> That they might repent:<br> For God is Oft-Returning,<br> Most Merciful.</p> <div class="filenav">
