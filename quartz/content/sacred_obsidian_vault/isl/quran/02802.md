 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XXVIII.: Qaṣaṣ, or the Narration.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 2</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_014.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>14.  Walamm<u>a</u> balagha ashuddahu wa<b>i</b>staw<u>a</u> <u>a</u>tayn<u>a</u>hu <u>h</u>ukman waAAilman waka<u>tha</u>lika najzee almu<u>h</u>sineen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>14.  When he reached full age,<br> And was firmly established<br> (In life), We bestowed on him<br> Wisdom and knowledge: for thus<br> Do We reward those<br> Who do good.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_015.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>15.  Wadakhala almadeenata AAal<u>a</u> <u>h</u>eeni ghaflatin min ahlih<u>a</u> fawajada feeh<u>a</u> rajulayni yaqtatil<u>a</u>ni h<u>atha</u> min sheeAAatihi wah<u>atha</u> min AAaduwwihi fa<b>i</b>stagh<u>a</u>thahu alla<u>th</u>ee min sheeAAatihi AAal<u>a</u> alla<u>th</u>ee min AAaduwwihi fawakazahu moos<u>a</u> faqa<u>da</u> AAalayhi q<u>a</u>la h<u>atha</u> min AAamali a<b>l</b>shshay<u>ta</u>ni innahu AAaduwwun mu<u>d</u>illun mubeen<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>15.  And he entered the City<br> At a time when its people<br> Were not watching: and he<br> Found there two men fighting,—<br> One of his own religion,<br> And the other, of his foes.<br> Now the man of his own<br> Religion appealed to him<br> Against his foe, and Moses<br> Struck him with his fist<br> And made an end of him.<br> He said: "This is a work<br> Of Evil (Satan): for he is<br> An enemy that manifestly<br> Misleads!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_016.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>16.  Q<u>a</u>la rabbi innee <i><u>th</u></i>alamtu nafsee fa<b>i</b>ghfir lee faghafara lahu innahu huwa alghafooru a<b>l</b>rra<u>h</u>eem<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>16.  He prayed: "O my Lord!<br> I have indeed wronged my soul!<br> Do Thou then forgive me!"<br> So (God) forgave him: for He<br> Is the Oft-Forgiving, Most Merciful.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_017.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>17.  Q<u>a</u>la rabbi bim<u>a</u> anAAamta AAalayya falan akoona <i><u>th</u></i>aheeran lilmujrimeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>17.  He said: "O my Lord!<br> For that Thou hast bestowed<br> Thy Grace on me, never<br> Shall I be a help<br> To those who sin!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_018.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>18.  Faa<u>s</u>ba<u>h</u>a fee almadeenati kh<u>a</u>-ifan yataraqqabu fa-i<u>tha</u> alla<u>th</u>ee istan<u>s</u>arahu bi<b>a</b>l-amsi yasta<u>s</u>rikhuhu q<u>a</u>la lahu moos<u>a</u> innaka laghawiyyun mubeen<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>18.  So he saw the morning<br> In the City, looking about,<br> In a state of fear, when<br> Behold, the man who had,<br> The day before, sought his help<br> Called aloud for his help<br> (Again). Moses said to him:<br> "Thou art truly, it is clear,<br> A quarrelsome fellow!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_019.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>19.  Falamm<u>a</u> an ar<u>a</u>da an yab<u>t</u>isha bi<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>ee huwa AAaduwwun lahum<u>a</u> q<u>a</u>la y<u>a</u> moos<u>a</u> atureedu an taqtulanee kam<u>a</u> qatalta nafsan bi<b>a</b>l-amsi in tureedu ill<u>a</u> an takoona jabb<u>a</u>ran fee al-ar<u>d</u>i wam<u>a</u> tureedu an takoona mina almu<u>s</u>li<u>h</u>een<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>19.  Then, when he decided to lay<br> Hold of the man who was<br> An enemy to both of them,<br> That man said: "O Moses!<br> Is it thy intention to slay me<br> As thou slewest a man<br> Yesterday? Thy intention is<br> None other than to become<br> A powerful violent man<br> In the land, and not to be<br> One who sets things right!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_020.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>20.  Waj<u>a</u>a rajulun min aq<u>sa</u> almadeenati yasAA<u>a</u> q<u>a</u>la y<u>a</u> moos<u>a</u> inna almalaa ya/tamiroona bika liyaqtulooka fa<b>o</b>khruj innee laka mina a<b>l</b>nn<u>as</u>i<u>h</u>een<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>20.  And there came a man,<br> Running, from the furthest end<br> Of the City. He said:<br> "O Moses! the Chiefs<br> Are taking counsel together<br> About thee, to slay thee:<br> So get thee away, for I<br> Do give thee sincere advice."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_021.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>21.  Fakharaja minh<u>a</u> kh<u>a</u>-ifan yataraqqabu q<u>a</u>la rabbi najjinee mina alqawmi a<b>l</b><i><u>thth</u></i><u>a</u>limeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>21.  He therefore got away therefrom,<br> Looking about, in a state<br> Of fear. He prayed:<br> "O my Lord! save me<br> From people given to wrong-doing."</p> <div class="filenav">
