 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra II.: Baqara, or the Heifer.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 37</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_267.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>267.  Y<u>a</u> ayyuh<u>a</u> alla<u>th</u>eena <u>a</u>manoo anfiqoo min <u>t</u>ayyib<u>a</u>ti m<u>a</u> kasabtum wamimm<u>a</u> akhrajn<u>a</u> lakum mina al-ar<u>d</u>i wal<u>a</u> tayammamoo alkhabeetha minhu tunfiqoona walastum bi-<u>a</u>khi<u>th</u>eehi ill<u>a</u> an tughmi<u>d</u>oo feehi wa<b>i</b>AAlamoo anna All<u>a</u>ha ghaniyyun <u>h</u>ameed<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>267.  O ye who believe!<br> Give of the good things<br> Which ye have (honourably) earned,<br> And of the fruits of the earth<br> Which We have produced<br>For you, and do not even aim<br> At getting anything<br> Which is bad, in order that<br> Out of it ye may give away<br> Something, when ye yourselves<br> Would not receive it<br> Except with closed eyes.<br> And know that God<br> Is Free of all wants,<br> And Worthy of all praise.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_268.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>268.  A<b>l</b>shshay<u>ta</u>nu yaAAidukumu alfaqra waya/murukum bi<b>a</b>lfa<u>h</u>sh<u>a</u>-i wa<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hu yaAAidukum maghfiratan minhu wafa<u>d</u>lan wa<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hu w<u>a</u>siAAun AAaleem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>268.  The Evil One threatens<br> You with poverty<br> And bids you to conduct<br> Unseemly. God promiseth<br> You His forgiveness<br> And bounties.<br> And God careth for all<br> And He knoweth all things.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_269.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>269.  Yu/tee al<u>h</u>ikmata man yash<u>a</u>o waman yu/ta al<u>h</u>ikmata faqad ootiya khayran katheeran wam<u>a</u> ya<u>thth</u>akkaru ill<u>a</u> oloo al-alb<u>a</u>b<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>269.  He granteth wisdom<br> To whom He pleaseth;<br> And he to whom wisdom<br> Is granted receiveth<br> Indeed a benefit overflowing;<br> But none will grasp the Message<br> But men of understanding.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_270.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>270.  Wam<u>a</u> anfaqtum min nafaqatin aw na<u>th</u>artum min na<u>th</u>rin fa-inna All<u>a</u>ha yaAAlamuhu wam<u>a</u> li<b>l</b><i><u>thth</u></i><u>a</u>limeena min an<u>sa</u>r<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>270.  And whatever ye spend<br> In charity or devotion,<br> Be sure God knows it all.<br> But the wrong-doers<br> Have no helpers.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_271.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>271.  In tubdoo a<b>l</b><u>ss</u>adaq<u>a</u>ti faniAAimm<u>a</u> hiya wa-in tukhfooh<u>a</u> watu/tooh<u>a</u> alfuqar<u>a</u>a fahuwa khayrun lakum wayukaffiru AAankum min sayyi-<u>a</u>tikum wa<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hu bim<u>a</u> taAAmaloona khabeer<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>271.  If ye disclose (acts<br> Of) charity, even so<br> It is well,<br> But if ye conceal them,<br> And make them reach<br> Those (really) in need,<br> That is best for you:<br> It will remove from you<br> Some of your (stains<br> Of) evil. And God<br> Is well acquainted<br> With what ye do.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_272.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>272.  Laysa AAalayka hud<u>a</u>hum wal<u>a</u>kinna All<u>a</u>ha yahdee man yash<u>a</u>o wam<u>a</u> tunfiqoo min khayrin fali-anfusikum wam<u>a</u> tunfiqoona ill<u>a</u> ibtigh<u>a</u>a wajhi All<u>a</u>hi wam<u>a</u> tunfiqoo min khayrin yuwaffa ilaykum waantum l<u>a</u> tu<i><u>th</u></i>lamoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>272.  It is not required<br> Of thee (O Apostle),<br> To set them on the right path,<br> But God sets on the right path<br> Whom He pleaseth.<br> Whatever of good ye give<br> Benefits your own souls,<br> And ye shall only do so<br> Seeking the "Face"<br> Of God. Whatever good<br> Ye give, shall be<br> Rendered back to you,<br> And ye shall not<br> Be dealt with unjustly.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/002/002_273.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>273.  Lilfuqar<u>a</u>-i alla<u>th</u>eena o<u>hs</u>iroo fee sabeeli All<u>a</u>hi l<u>a</u> yasta<u>t</u>eeAAoona <u>d</u>arban fee al-ar<u>d</u>i ya<u>h</u>sabuhumu alj<u>a</u>hilu aghniy<u>a</u>a mina a<b>l</b>ttaAAaffufi taAArifuhum biseem<u>a</u>hum l<u>a</u> yas-aloona a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>sa il<u>ha</u>fan wam<u>a</u> tunfiqoo min khayrin fa-inna All<u>a</u>ha bihi AAaleem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>273.  (Charity is) for those<br> In need, who, in God's cause<br> Are restricted (from travel),<br> And cannot move about<br>In the land, seeking<br> (For trade or work):<br> The ignorant man thinks,<br> Because of their modesty,<br> That they are free from want.<br> Thou shaltḳnow them<br> By their (unfailing) mark:<br> They beg not importunately<br> From all and sundry.<br> And whatever of good<br> Ye give, be assured<br> God knoweth it well.</p> <div class="filenav">
