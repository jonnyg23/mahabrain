 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XXVIII.: Qaṣaṣ, or the Narration.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 4</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_029.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>29.  Falamm<u>a</u> qa<u>da</u> moos<u>a</u> al-ajala was<u>a</u>ra bi-ahlihi <u>a</u>nasa min j<u>a</u>nibi a<b>l</b><u>tt</u>oori n<u>a</u>ran q<u>a</u>la li-ahlihi omkuthoo innee <u>a</u>nastu n<u>a</u>ran laAAallee <u>a</u>teekum minh<u>a</u> bikhabarin aw ja<u>th</u>watin mina a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>ri laAAallakum ta<u>st</u>aloon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>29.  Now when Moses had fulfilled<br> The term, and was travelling<br> With his family, he perceived<br> A fire in the direction<br> Of Mount Ṭūr. He said<br> To his family: "Tarry ye;<br> I perceive a fire; I hope<br> To bring you from there<br> Some information, or a burning<br> Firebrand, that ye may<br> Warm yourselves."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_030.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>30.  Falamm<u>a</u> at<u>a</u>h<u>a</u> noodiya min sh<u>at</u>i-i alw<u>a</u>di al-aymani fee albuqAAati almub<u>a</u>rakati mina a<b>l</b>shshajarati an y<u>a</u> moos<u>a</u> innee an<u>a</u> All<u>a</u>hu rabbu alAA<u>a</u>lameen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>30.  But when he came<br> To the (Fire), a voice<br>Was heard from the right bank<br> Of the valley, from a tree<br> In hallowed ground:<br> "O Moses! Verily<br> I am God, the Lord<br> Of the Worlds…</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_031.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>31.  Waan alqi AAa<u>sa</u>ka falamm<u>a</u> ra<u>a</u>h<u>a</u> tahtazzu kaannah<u>a</u> j<u>a</u>nnun wall<u>a</u> mudbiran walam yuAAaqqib y<u>a</u> moos<u>a</u> aqbil wal<u>a</u> takhaf innaka mina al-<u>a</u>mineen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>31.  "Now do thou throw thy rod!"<br> But when he saw it<br> Moving (of its own accord)<br> As if it had been a snake,<br> He turned back in retreat,<br> And retraced not his steps:<br> "O Moses!" (it was said),<br> "Draw near, and fear not:<br> For thou art of those<br> Who are secure.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_032.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>32.  Osluk yadaka fee jaybika takhruj bay<u>da</u>a min ghayri soo-in wa<b>o</b><u>d</u>mum ilayka jan<u>ah</u>aka mina a<b>l</b>rrahbi fa<u>tha</u>nika burh<u>a</u>n<u>a</u>ni min rabbika il<u>a</u> firAAawna wamala-ihi innahum k<u>a</u>noo qawman f<u>a</u>siqeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>32.  "Move thy hand into<br> Thy bosom, and it will<br> Come forth white without stain<br> (Or harm), and draw thy hand<br> Close to thy side<br> (To guard) against fear.<br> Those are the two credentials<br> From thy Lord to Pharaoh<br> And his Chiefs: for truly<br> They are a people<br> Rebellious and wicked."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_033.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>33.  Q<u>a</u>la rabbi innee qataltu minhum nafsan faakh<u>a</u>fu an yaqtuloon<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>33.  He said: "O my Lord!<br> I have slain a man<br>Among them, and I fear<br> Lest they slay me.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_034.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>34.  Waakhee h<u>a</u>roonu huwa af<u>s</u>a<u>h</u>u minnee lis<u>a</u>nan faarsilhu maAAiya rid-an yu<u>s</u>addiqunee innee akh<u>a</u>fu an yuka<u>thth</u>iboon<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>34.  "And my brother Aaron—<br> He is more eloquent in speech<br> Than I: so send him<br> With me as a helper,<br> To confirm (and strengthen) me:<br> For I fear that they may<br> Accuse me of falsehood."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_035.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>35.  Q<u>a</u>la sanashuddu AAa<u>d</u>udaka bi-akheeka wanajAAalu lakum<u>a</u> sul<u>ta</u>nan fal<u>a</u> ya<u>s</u>iloona ilaykum<u>a</u> bi-<u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tin<u>a</u> antum<u>a</u> wamani ittabaAAakum<u>a</u> algh<u>a</u>liboon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>35.  He said: "We will certainly<br> Strengthen thy arm through<br> Thy brother, and invest you both<br> With authority, so they<br> Shall not be able to<br> Touch you: with Our Signs<br> Shall ye triumph,—you two<br> As well as those<br> Who follow you."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_036.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>36.  Falamm<u>a</u> j<u>a</u>ahum moos<u>a</u> bi-<u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tin<u>a</u> bayyin<u>a</u>tin q<u>a</u>loo m<u>a</u> h<u>atha</u> ill<u>a</u> si<u>h</u>run muftaran wam<u>a</u> samiAAn<u>a</u> bih<u>atha</u> fee <u>a</u>b<u>a</u>-in<u>a</u> al-awwaleen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>36.  When Moses came to them<br> With Our Clear Signs, they said:<br> "This is nothing but sorcery<br> Faked up: never did we<br> Hear the like among our fathers<br> Of old!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_037.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>37.  Waq<u>a</u>la moos<u>a</u> rabbee aAAlamu biman j<u>a</u>a bi<b>a</b>lhud<u>a</u> min AAindihi waman takoonu lahu AA<u>a</u>qibatu a<b>l</b>dd<u>a</u>ri innahu l<u>a</u> yufli<u>h</u>u a<b>l</b><i><u>thth</u></i><u>a</u>limoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>37.  Moses said: "My Lord<br> Knows best who it is<br> That comes with guidance<br> From Him and whose End<br> Will be best in the Hereafter:<br> Certain it is that<br> The wrong-doers will not prosper."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_038.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>38.  Waq<u>a</u>la firAAawnu y<u>a</u> ayyuh<u>a</u> almalao m<u>a</u> AAalimtu lakum min il<u>a</u>hin ghayree faawqid lee y<u>a</u> h<u>a</u>m<u>a</u>nu AAal<u>a</u> a<b>l</b><u>tt</u>eeni fa<b>i</b>jAAal lee <u>s</u>ar<u>h</u>an laAAallee a<u>tt</u>aliAAu il<u>a</u> il<u>a</u>hi moos<u>a</u> wa-innee laa<i><u>th</u></i>unnuhu mina alk<u>ath</u>ibeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>38.  Pharaoh said: "O Chiefs!<br> No god do I know for you<br> But myself: therefore,<br> O Haman! light me a (kiln<br> To bake bricks) out of clay,<br> And build me a lofty<br> Palace, that I may mount up<br> To the god of Moses:<br> But as far as I am concerned,<br> I think (Moses) is a liar!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_039.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>39.  Wa<b>i</b>stakbara huwa wajunooduhu fee al-ar<u>d</u>i bighayri al<u>h</u>aqqi wa<i><u>th</u></i>annoo annahum ilayn<u>a</u> l<u>a</u> yurjaAAoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>39.  And he was arrogant and insolent<br> In the land, beyond reason,—<br> He and his hosts: they thought<br> That they would not have<br> To return to Us!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_040.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>40.  Faakha<u>th</u>n<u>a</u>hu wajunoodahu fanaba<u>th</u>n<u>a</u>hum fee alyammi fa<b>o</b>n<i><u>th</u></i>ur kayfa k<u>a</u>na AA<u>a</u>qibatu a<b>l</b><i><u>thth</u></i><u>a</u>limeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>40.  So We seized him<br> And his hosts, and We<br> Flung them into the sea:<br> Now behold what was the End<br> Of those who did wrong!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_041.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>41.  WajaAAaln<u>a</u>hum a-immatan yadAAoona il<u>a</u> a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>ri wayawma alqiy<u>a</u>mati l<u>a</u> yun<u>s</u>aroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>41.  And We made them (but)<br> Leaders inviting to the Fire;<br> And on the Day of Judgment<br> No help shall they find.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/028/028_042.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>42.  WaatbaAAn<u>a</u>hum fee h<u>ath</u>ihi a<b>l</b>dduny<u>a</u> laAAnatan wayawma alqiy<u>a</u>mati hum mina almaqboo<u>h</u>een<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>42.  In this world We made<br> A Curse to follow them:<br> And on the Day of Judgment<br> They will be among<br> The loathed (and despised).</p> <div class="filenav">
