 <p><a name="page_337"><font size="1" color="green">p. 337</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">TABLE OF THE FOUR FORMS OF THE MANIFESTATION OF CONSCIOUSNESS</h1>  
 <p><font size="-1" color="green">For ease of online reading, I have exchanged the rows and columns in this chart.—JBH</font></p>  
 <p> </p>  <table border="1" width="100%">  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="154">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>1ST   FORM</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>2ND   FORM</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>3RD   FORM</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>4TH   FORM</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="154">   
 <p>THE   SENSE OF SPACE AND TIME</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   sense of one-dimensional space. The world on the line. The line as space, everything   else as time. Everything except things lying on this line is in motion.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   sense of two-dimensional space. The world on the plane. The plane as space,   everything else as time. Angles and curves as motions.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   sense of three-dimensional space. The world in an infinite sphere. The <i>sphere</i> as space. Everything else as   time. Phenomena as motions. A becoming and changing universe.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   sense of four-dimensional space.<br>   Spatial sensation of time.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="154">   
 <p>PSYCHOLOGY</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Appearance   of the first sensation. Sensation a unit. Its division into two. The gradual   evolution of sensations and the accumulation of remembrances concerning them.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Perception.   The expression of sensations by cries, sounds, motions. The absence of words   and speech. Were there speech it would consist of substantives only.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Concept.<br>   Words.<br>   Judgment.<br>   Syllogism.<br>   Reasoning.<br>   Speech.<br>   Written language.<br>   Allegory. <br>   Emotions.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Self-consciousness.<br>   New sensations.<br>   Higher emotions.<br>   Expansion of concepts.<br>   Direct knowledge.<br>   Symbolism.<br>   Cosmic consciousness.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="154">   
 <p>LOGIC</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   absence of thinking, or a <i>confused</i>   thinking of the 2nd form.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>This   is this.<br>   That is that.<br>   This is not that.<br>   The beginnings of logic. The logic of the uniqueness of each separate thing.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>A   is A.<br>   A is not Not-A.<br>   Everything is either A or Not-A.<br>   Dualistic logic.<br>   A logic of antitheses.<br>   Syllogism.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>A   is both A and Not-A.<br>   <i>Tat twam asi</i>. <i>Thou art that</i>.<br>   "Tertium Organum."<br>   Logic of the unity of all.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="154">   
 <p>MATHEMATICS</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   absence of numeration, or a <i>confused</i>   numeration of the 2nd form.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   comparison of <i>separate</i> visible   objects or <i>separate</i> perceptions.<br>   The direct sensation of quantity. <i>Computation</i>   within the limits of this sensation.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Every   magnitude is equal to itself. The part is less than the whole, etc.<br>   Finite and constant numbers. The geometry of Euclid.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>A   magnitude can be not equal to itself. The part can be equal to the whole,   etc.<br>   Metageometry.<br>   Mathematics of variable and infinite magnitudes.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="154">   
 <p>FORMS   OF ACTIONS</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Reflex,   unconscious, responsive action to external irritation.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Instinct.   "Emotional" end expedient action without consciousness of results.   Seeming consciousness.<br>   Inability to manipulate a lever.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   consciousness of the purpose of actions performed. The <i>possibility</i> of a consciousness of results. The cause of actions   in the outer world in impressions received from the outer world. The   impossibility of in dependent actions without impulses coming from the   outside.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   starting of conscious actions. The starting of actions with the understanding   of their cosmical meaning and purposes. The commencement of independent   actions <i>proceeding from oneself</i>.<br>   MAGIC.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="154">   
 <p>MORALS</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Unconscious   actions (like the actions of a man asleep).</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   beginnings of the maternal, family, and tribal instincts.<br>   Laws of the life of the <i>species</i> as   a condition of evolution. The unconscious submission to the "group   soul" of the species manifesting through instincts.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Logical   and conventional division into good and evil. The submission to the group   consciousness of the family, of the clan, of the tribe, of the nation, of   humanity, of the class, of the party, of a custom, of a fashion, etc.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   return to the law inside oneself. <i>A new   conscience</i>. Emancipation from submission to the group-consciousness. The   realization of oneself as an independent unit.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="154">   
 <p>FORMS   OF CONSCIOUSNESS</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Potential   consciousness. Consciousness in a latent state—asleep. Consciousness as   in sleep without dreams.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Simple   consciousness. "It pains me," but the impossibility of saying,   "I am conscious that it pains me." The reflected state of   consciousness. Vision as in dreams. The passive state of consciousness.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   ability to think of one's states of consciousness. The division of I and   Not-I. Active consciousness.<br>   <i>The moment when further evolution can   be conscious only</i>.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   commencement of self-consciousness.<br>   Ecstatic states.<br>   Transitions to cosmic consciousness.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="154">   
 <p>FORMS   OF KNOWLEDGE</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Unconscious   receptivity of the environment, and unconscious reaction to it.<br>   "ADAPTABILITY."</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   beginnings of attention. Observation. The accumulation of instincts. The   recognition of everything <i>sensed</i> as   real. The failure to discriminate between that which is illusory and that   which is real.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Experience.   Experimental knowledge. A complete and deep division and mutual   misunderstanding between four forms of knowledge—religion, philosophy,   science and art.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   beginning of the development of forms of knowledge. Mystic knowledge. A new   sensation of time. The sensation of infinity. The sensation of the unreality   of the phenomenal, visible world. A knowledge of the hidden substance of   things by their outer signs. Unfoldment of the "world of the   wondrous." Co-ordination in a complete whole of religion, philosophy,   science and art.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="154">   
 <p>FORMS   OF SCIENCE</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>An   accumulation of "traces" from the produced reflexes. The appearance   of instinct and the accumulation of simple instincts.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Personal   knowledge. Impotence to communicate experience. The beginnings of the   communication of experience in the training of the young.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Positive   science. Positive philosophy. Materialism. Spiritualistic philosophy.   Dogmatic religions. Spiritism and pseudo-occultism. Sectarianism. Dualism.   Matter and spirit. Separation of different forms of science.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Idealistic   philosophy. Mathematics of the infinite. <i>Tertium   Organum</i>. Mystical religion. God and the Cosmos—one. The sensation   of a living and conscious universe. The union of all sciences into one.   Occultism. Understanding of "<i>Dharma</i>,"   i.e., of laws of relativity.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="154">   
 <p>DIFFERENT   BEINGS</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   lower animal. Cells of the tissues and organs of the body. The   one-dimensional being. <i>Vegetative or   semi-vegetative life</i>.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   higher animal. The body of man. The two-dimensional being. The absence of   duality, divisibility and disharmony. <i>Animal   life</i>.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>Man.   A three-dimensional being <i>outwardly</i>   and dual <i>inwardly</i>. Inner warfare.   The impossibility of attaining inner harmony. The "soul" as the   battlefield of the "spirit" and the "flesh." The kingdom   of the personal. Unconscious automatism. The absence of personal immortality.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="166">   
 <p>The   beginnings of the transition to a new type, and a new sensation of space.   Victory of consciousness. "Men of cosmical consciousness." Triumph   of the super-personal principle. Conscious automatism. The attainment of   inner unity and harmony. The "soul" as the center of independent   actions, The beginnings of personal immortality.</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>
