 <h3 align="center">21. SECOND SERIES IN PRAISE OF NUT, UTTERANCES 443-452.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterances
 443</i>.</p>
 
 <p>823a. To say: Nut, two eyes are come forth from thy head.</p>
 
 <p>823b. Thou hast taken possession of Horus and his Great-in-charms;</p>
 
 <p>823c. thou hast taken possession of Set and his Great-in-charms.</p>
 
 <p>823d. Nut, thou hast numbered thy children, in thy name of "<i>rp.t</i>-sedan-chair of Heliopolis."</p>
 
 <p>823e. Thou shalt reclaim N. also for life; he shall not perish.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterances
 444-445</i>.</p>
 
 <p>824a. To say: Nut, thou hast dawned as king of Lower
  Egypt, because thou hast gained power over the gods,</p>
 
 <p>824b. together with their <i>kas</i>,
 together with their heritage,</p>
 
 <p>824c. together with their food, together with all their possessions.</p>
 
 <p>824d. Nut, him thou causest to endure, he will live.</p>
 
 <p>824e. Nut, if thou livest, N. will live.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 446</i>.</p>
 
 <p>825a. To say: Osiris N., thy mother, Nut, has spread herself over thee,</p>
 
 <p>825b. that she may hide thee from all evil things.</p>
 
 <p>825c. Nut has guarded thee from all evil;</p>
 
 <p>825d. thou art the greatest among her children.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 447</i>.</p>
 
 <p>826a. To say: He is gone who went to his <i>ka</i>;
 Osiris is gone to his <i>ka</i>; Set is gone
 to his <i>ka</i>;</p>
 
 <p>826b. <i>Mḫnti-irti</i> is gone to his <i>ka</i>; thou thyself art gone to thy <i>ka</i>.</p>
 
 <p>827a. O N., he who comes, comes, thou shalt not be in need;</p>
 
 <p>827b. thy mother comes, thou shalt not be in need; Nut, thou shalt not be in
 need;</p>
 
 <p>827c. protectress of the great, thou shalt not be in need; protectress of
 the fearful, thou shalt not be in need.</p>
 
 <p>828a. She protects thee, she prevents thy need, she gives back thy head to
 thee;</p>
 
 <p>828b. she collects thy bones for thee;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_155"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 155</font></a></p>
 
 <p>828c. she brings thy heart into thy body for thee.</p>
 
 <p>829a. Thou art (henceforth?) chief of those who were before thee;</p>
 
 <p>829b. thou commandest those who will be after thee.</p>
 
 <p>829c. Thou causest thy house to prosper after thee; thou protectest thy
 children from sorrow.</p>
 
 <p>829d. Thy purity is the purity of the gods, who have gone to their <i>kas</i>;</p>
 
 <p>829e. thy purity is the purity of the gods who have passed on, and so do not
 suffer hardship.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 448</i>.</p>
 
 <p>830a. To say: Thot, heal N., that he may live,</p>
 
 <p>830b. that what is against him may cease. Thot, give him the eye of Horus.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 449</i>.</p>
 
 <p>831. To say: Horus, who art in Osiris N., take the eye of Horus to thyself.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 450</i>.</p>
 
 <p>832a. To say: He is gone, who went to his <i>ka</i>; Osiris is gone to his <i>ka</i>;
 Set is gone to his <i>ka</i>;</p>
 
 <p>832b. <i>Mḫnti-irti</i> is gone to his <i>ka</i>; N. is gone to his <i>ka</i>.</p>
 
 <p>833a. O N., thou art gone, that thou mayest live; thou art gone, that thou
 mayest not die;</p>
 
 <p>833b. thou art gone, that thy spirit may be at the head of the spirits, that
 thou mayest be powerful at the head of the living;</p>
 
 <p>833c. that thou mayest be mighty (a soul), and thou art mighty (a soul);
 that thou mayest be honoured, and thou art honoured.</p>
 
 <p>834a. He who comes, comes; thou shalt not be in need.</p>
 
 <p>834b. Thy mother comes to thee, thou shalt not be in need; Nut comes to
 thee, thou shalt not be in need;</p>
 
 <p>834c. the protectress of the great comes to thee, thou shalt not be in need.</p>
 
 <p>835a. She protects thee, she prevents thy need, she gives back thy head to
 thee;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_156"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 156</font></a></p>
 
 <p>835b. she assembles thy bones for thee, she unites thy limbs for thee;</p>
 
 <p>835c. she brings thy heart into thy body for thee.</p>
 
 <p>836a. Thou art (henceforth?) chief of those who were before thee;</p>
 
 <p>836b. thou commandest those who were before thee;</p>
 
 <p>836c. thou protectest thy children from sorrow.</p>
 
 <p>836d. Thy purity is the purity of the gods,</p>
 
 <p>836e. the lords of want, who have gone to their <i>kas</i>.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 451</i>.</p>
 
 <p>837a. To say: O N., awake, raise thyself up,</p>
 
 <p>837b. stand up, that thou mayest be pure, that thy <i>ka</i> may be pure,</p>
 
 <p>837c. that thy soul may be pure, that thy might may be pure.</p>
 
 <p>838a. Thy mother comes to thee, Nut comes to thee, the great protectress
 comes to thee;</p>
 
 <p>838b. she purifies thee, N., she protects thee, N.,</p>
 
 <p>838c. she prevents thy need.</p>
 
 <p>839a. O N., thou art pure, thy <i>ka</i>
 is pure,</p>
 
 <p>839b. thy might which is among the spirits is pure, thy soul which is among
 the gods is pure.</p>
 
 <p>840a. O N.,</p>
 
 <p>840b. "Thy bones are united for thee; take to thee thy head," says
 Geb.</p>
 
 <p>840c. Let him efface the evil which is in thee, N., says Atum.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 452</i>.</p>
 
 <p>841a. To say: O N., stand up, that thou mayest be pure, that thy <i>ka</i> may be pure.</p>
 
 <p>841b. Horus purifies thee in <i>ḳbḥ.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p>842a. Thy purification is the purification of Shu, thy purification is the
 purification of Tefnut,</p>
 
 <p>842b. thy purification is the purification of the four spirits of the
 houses,</p>
 
 <p>842 C. when they rejoice in Buto because thou art pure.</p>
 
 <p>842d. Thy mother Nut purifies thee, the great protectress, she protects
 thee.</p>
 
 <p>843a. "Take to thee thy head; thy bones are united for thee," says
 Geb.</p>
 
 <p>843b. "Effaced be the evil which is with N., destroyed shall be the
 evil which is with him," says Atum.</p>
