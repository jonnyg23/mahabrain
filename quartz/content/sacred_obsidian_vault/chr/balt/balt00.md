 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Baltimore Catchecism</i>, [1891], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h1><b> <a></a>Baltimore Catechism No. 1 </b></h1> <h3 align="center">LESSON FIRST: ON THE END OF MAN</h3> 
 <p> </p> <h4>1. Q. Who made the world?<br>   A. God made the world. </h4> 
 <p> </p> <h4><a>2. Q. Who is God?</a><br>   A. God is the Creator of heaven and earth, and of all things. </h4> 
 <p><b> </b></p> 
 <p><b>3. Q. What is man?<br>   </b>A. Man is a creature composed of body and soul, and made to the image and    likeness of God.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>6. Q. Why did God make you?<br>   </b>A. God made me to know Him, to love Him, and to serve Him in this world,    and to be happy with Him for ever in heaven. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>9. Q. What must we do to save our souls?<br>   </b>A. To save our souls, we must worship God by faith, hope, and charity; that    is, we must believe in Him, hope in<br>   Him, and love Him with all our heart. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>10. Q. How shall we know the things which we are to believe?<br>   </b>A. We shall know the things which we are to believe from the Catholic Church,    through which God speaks to us.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>11. Q. Where shall we find the chief truths which the Church teaches?<br>   </b>A. We shall find the chief truths which the Church teaches in the Apostles'    Creed. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>12. Q. Say the Apostles' Creed.</b></p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>LESSON SECOND: ON GOD AND HIS PERFECTIONS</b></p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>13. Q. What is God?<br>   A</b>. God is a spirit infinitely perfect.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>14. Q. Had God a beginning<br>   </b>A. God had no beginning; He always was and He always will be.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>15. Q. Where is God?<br>   </b>A. God is everywhere.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>16. Q. If God is everywhere, why do we not see Him?<br>   </b>A. We do not see God, because He is a pure spirit and cannot be seen with    bodily eyes.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>17. Q. Does God see us?<br>   </b>A. God sees us and watches over us. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>18. Q. Does God know all things?<br>   </b>A. God knows all things, even our most secret thoughts, words, and actions.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>19. Q. Can God do all things?<br>   </b>A. God can do all things, and nothing is hard or impossible to Him.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>20. Q. Is God just, holy, and merciful?<br>   </b>A. God is all just, all holy, all merciful, as He is infinitely perfect.  </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>LESSON THIRD: ON THE UNITY AND TRINITY OF GOD</b></p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>21. Q. Is there but one God?<br>   </b>A. Yes; there is but one God. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>22. Q. Why can there be but one God?<br>   </b>A. There can be but one God, because God, being supreme and infinite, cannot    have an equal.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>23. Q. How many Persons are there in God?<br>   </b>A. In God there are three Divine Persons, really distinct, and equal in    all things-the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost.</p> 
 <p><b> </b></p> 
 <p><b>24. Q. Is the Father God?<br>   </b>A. The Father is God and the first Person of the Blessed Trinity. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>25. Q. Is the Son God?<br>   </b>A. The Son is God and the second Person of the Blessed Trinity. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>26. Q. Is the Holy Ghost God?<br>   </b>A. The Holy Ghost is God and the third Person of the Blessed Trinity.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>27. Q. What is the Blessed Trinity?<br>   </b>A. The Blessed Trinity is one God in three Divine Persons. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>29. Q. Are the three Divine Persons one and the same God?<br>   </b>A. The three Divine Persons are one and the same God, having one and the    same Divine nature. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>LESSON FOURTH: ON THE ANGELS AND OUR FIRST PARENTS</b></p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>34. Q. Which are the chief creatures of God?<br>   </b>A. The chief creatures of God are men and angels.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>35. Q. What are angels?<br>   </b>A. Angels are bodiless spirits created to adore and enjoy God in heaven.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>39. Q. Who were the first man and woman?<br>   </b>A. The first man and woman were Adam and Eve.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>40. Q. Were Adam and Eve innocent and holy when they came from the hand    of God?<br>   </b>A. Adam and Eve were innocent and holy when they came from the hand of God.  </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>43. Q. Did Adam and Eve remain faithful to God?<br>   </b>A. Adam and Eve did not remain faithful to God; but broke His command by    eating the forbidden fruit.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>44. Q. What befell Adam and Eve on account of their sin?<br>   </b>A. Adam and Eve on account of their sin lost innocence and holiness, and    were doomed to misery and death.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>45. Q. What evil befell us through the disobedience of our first parents?<br>   </b>A. Through the disobedience of our first parents we all inherit their sin    and punishment, as we should have shared in their happiness if they had remained    faithful.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>47. Q. What is the sin called which we inherit from our first parents?<br>   </b>A. The sin which we inherit from our first parents is called original sin.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>50. Q. Was any one ever preserved from original sin?<br>   </b>A. The Blessed Virgin Mary, through the merit of her Divine Son, was preserved    free from the guilt of original sin, and this privilege is called her Immaculate    Conception.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>LESSON FIFTH: ON SIN AND ITS KINDS</b></p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>51. Q. Is original sin the only kind of sin?<br>   </b>A. Original sin is not the only kind of sin; there is another kind of sin,    which we commit ourselves, called actual sin. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>52. Q. What is actual sin?<br>   </b>A. Actual sin is any willful thought, word, deed or omission contrary to    the law of God.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>53. Q. How many kinds of actual sin are there?<br>   </b>A. There are two kinds of actual sin-mortal and venial.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>54. Q. What is mortal sin?<br>   </b>A. Mortal sin is a grievous offense against the law of God.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>57. Q. What is venial sin?</b></p> 
 <p>A. Venial sin is a slight offense against the law of God in matters of less    importance; or in matters of great importance it is an offence committed with    out sufficient reflection or full consent of the will. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>59. Q. Which are the chief sources of sin?<br>   </b>A. The chief sources of sin are seven: Pride, Covetousness, Lust, Anger,    Gluttony, Envy, and Sloth; and they are commonly called capital sins.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>LESSON SIXTH: ON THE INCARNATION AND REDEMPTION</b></p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>60. Q. Did God abandon man after he fell into sin?<br>   </b>A. God did not abandon man after he fell into sin, but promised him a Redeemer,    who was to satisfy for man's sin and reopen to him the gates of heaven.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>61. Q. Who is the Redeemer?<br>   </b>A. Our Blessed Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ is the Redeemer of mankind.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>62. Q. What do you believe of Jesus Christ?<br>   </b>A. I believe that Jesus Christ is the Son of God, the second Person of the    Blessed Trinity, true God and true man. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>69. Q. What do you mean by the Incarnation?<br>   </b>A. By the Incarnation I mean that the Son of God was made man. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>70. Q. How was the Son of God made man?<br>   </b>A. The Son of God was conceived and made man by the power of the Holy Ghost,    in the womb of the Blessed Virgin Mary.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>74. Q. On what day was the Son of God conceived and made man?<br>   </b>A. The Son of God was conceived and made man on Annunciation day-the day    on which the angel Gabriel announced to the Blessed Virgin Mary that she was    to be the Mother of God. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>75. Q. On what day was Christ born?<br>   </b>A. Christ was born on Christmas day in a stable at Bethlehem, over nineteen    hundred years ago.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>LESSON SEVENTH: ON OUR LORD'S PASSION, DEATH, RESURRECTION,    AND ASCENSION</b></p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>78. Q. What did Jesus Christ Suffer?<br>   </b>A. Jesus Christ suffered a bloody sweat, a cruel scourging, was crowned    with thorns, and was crucified.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>79. Q. On what clay did Christ die?<br>   </b>A. Christ died on Good Friday. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>83. Q. Why did Christ suffer and die?<br>   </b>A. Christ suffered and died for our sins</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>89. Q. On what day did Christ rise from the dead?<br>   </b>A. Christ rose from the dead, glorious and immortal, on Easter Sunday, the    third day after His death.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>91. Q. After Christ had remained forty days on earth, whither did He go?<br>   </b>A. After forty days Christ ascended into heaven, and the day on which He    ascended into heaven is called Ascension day.</p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>LESSON EIGHTH: ON THE HOLY GHOST AND HIS DESCENT UPON    THE APOSTLES</b></p> 
 <p> </p> <ul>   <li>De-scent', the act of coming down.    </li><li>En-a'ble, to make able.    </li><li>En-light'en, to make them understand better.    </li><li>Pen'te-cost, the fiftieth day after Easter.    </li><li>Preach, declare publicly, spread by word of mouth.    </li><li>Sanc'ti-fy, to make holy.    </li><li>Strength'en, make strong.    </li><li>Whit'sun-day, white Sunday.  </li></ul> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>94. Q. Who is the Holy Ghost?<br>   </b>A. The Holy Ghost is the third Person of the Blessed Trinity. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>97. Q. On what day did the Holy Ghost come down upon the Apostles?<br>   </b>A. The Holy Ghost came down upon the Apostles ten days after the Ascension    of our Lord; and the day on which He came down upon the Apostles is called Whitsunday,    or Pentecost. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p> <b>99. Q. Who sent the Holy Ghost upon the Apostles?<br>   </b>A. Our Lord Jesus Christ sent the Holy Ghost upon the Apostles. </p> 
 <p> </p> 
 <p><b>100. Q. Why did Christ send the Holy Ghost?<br>   </b>A. Christ sent the Holy Ghost to sanctify His Church, to enlighten and strengthen    the Apostles, and to enable them to preach the Gospel.</p>
