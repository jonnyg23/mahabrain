 <p><a name="P3379_886872"></a>
 <sup>128 </sup>The reference is to the ancient custom of using wax and certain earths and clays for the purpose of affixing, by means of the ring, a seal with an impression on any object which it was desired to secure. Thus Herodotus, ii. 38, tell us how the Egyptians marked the pure victim by wrapping it round the horns with papyrus, and then smearing some sealing earth (<font face="SPIonic">gh=n shmantri/da</font>) on it, and stamping it with a ring. See also Cicero, <i>Pro Flacco</i>, where he speaks of the <i>laudatio obsignata cretâ illa Asiatica</i>; and Plautus, <i>Pseudolus</i>, Scene i., where he mentions the <i>expressam in cera ex annulo suam imaginem</i>, etc. [Compare vol. v. p. 466, note 3, this series.]</p>
