 <p><a name="page_237"><font size="1" color="green">p. 237</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">Scientific Names of the Animals</h1>  
 <p>FOR purposes of exact identification the scientific names of the mammals, birds, reptiles and a few other animals mentioned in the text are here given. Most of these were originally First People; they turned into animals at or about the time real people were created.</p>  
 <p>The Indian names of the First People who turned into these animals are given at the beginning of each story.</p>  <h3 align="center">MAMMALS</h3>  
 <p>Antelope, <i>Antilocapra americana</i></p>  
 <p>Badger, <i>Taxidea taxus neglecta</i></p>  
 <p>Bear--See Black, Grizzly, and Cinnamon Bear</p>  
 <p>Black Bear, <i>Ursus americanus</i> (black phase)</p>  
 <p>Bobcat or Wildcat, <i>Lynx fasciatus pallescens</i></p>  
 <p>Cinnamon Bear, <i>Ursus americanus</i> (brown or red phase)</p>  
 <p>Cougar, <i>Felis hippolestes</i></p>  
 <p>Coyote, <i>Canis ochropus</i></p>  
 <p>Deer, Columbia Blacktail, <i>Odocoileus columbianus</i></p>  
 <p>Elk, <i>Cervus nannodes</i> (California Valley Elk)</p>  
 <p>Fox, See Gray Fox</p>  
 <p>Gopher, <i>Thomomys</i>--several species</p>  
 <p>Gray Tree Squirrel, <i>Sciurus fossor</i></p>

 <p>Mountain Lion--See Cougar</p>  
 <p>Raccoon, <i>Procyon psora</i></p>  
 <p>Skunk (large kind), <i>Mephitis occidentalis</i></p>

 <p>White-footed Mouse, <i>Peromyscus gambeli</i></p>  
 <p>Woodrat, <i>Neotoma fuscipes streatori</i> (of the Sierra foothills)</p>  <h3 align="center">BIRDS</h3>
