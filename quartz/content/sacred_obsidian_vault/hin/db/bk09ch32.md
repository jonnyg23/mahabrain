 <p align="center"><b>THE NINTH BOOK</b></p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="center"><b>Chapter XXXII</b></p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="center"><b>On the enumeration of various hells for sinners</b></p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"><a name="page_926"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 926</font></a></p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">1-28. Nârâya<u>n</u>a said :-- Then, initiating her with the Great Seed, the Âdi Radical Mantra of the Mahâ Sakti, Srî Bhuvanesvarî in accordance with due rules, the son of Sûrya began to recite the various effects of various Karmas, auspicious and inauspicious. Never do the persons go to hell when they perform good Karmas; it is only the bad works that lead men to hells. The different Purâ<u>n</u>as narrate various heavens. The Jîvas go to those places as the effects of their various good Karmas. The good Karmas do not lead men to hells; but the bad Karmas do lead them veritably to various hideous hells. In different Sâstras, different hell-pits are ascertained. Different works lead men to different hells. O Child! Those hell-pits are very wide, deep, painful and tormenting, very horrible and ugly. Of these, eighty six pits or Ku<u>nd</u>as are prominent. Many other Ku<u>nd</u>as exist. Now listen to the names of the Ku<u>nd</u>as mentioned in the Vedas. Their names are :-- Vah<u>n</u>i Ku<u>nd</u>a, Tapta Ku<u>nd</u>a, K<u>s</u>âra Ku<u>nd</u>a, Bhayânaka Ku<u>nd</u>a, Vi<u>t</u> Ku<u>nd</u>a, Mûtra Ku<u>nd</u>a, Sle<u>s</u>ma Ku<u>nd</u>a, Gara Ku<u>nd</u>a, Dû<u>s</u>ikâ Ku<u>nd</u>a, Vasâ Ku<u>nd</u>a, Sukra Ku<u>nd</u>a, So<u>n</u>ita Ku<u>nd</u>a, Asrû Ku<u>nd</u>a, Gâtramala Ku<u>nd</u>a, Kar<u>n</u>amala Ku<u>nd</u>a, Majjâ Ku<u>nd</u>a, Mâmsa Ku<u>nd</u>a, impassable Nakra Ku<u>nd</u>a, Loma Ku<u>nd</u>a, Kesa Ku<u>nd</u>a, impassable Asthi Ku<u>nd</u>a, Tâmra Ku<u>nd</u>a, the exceedingly hot and painful Lauha Ku<u>nd</u>a (the pit of molten iron), Charma Ku<u>nd</u>a, the hot Surâ Ku<u>nd</u>a, sharp Thorny Ku<u>nd</u>a, Vi<u>s</u>a Ku<u>nd</u>a, the hot Taila Ku<u>nd</u>a, very heavy Astra Ku<u>nd</u>a, Krimi Ku<u>nd</u>a, Pûya Ku<u>nd</u>a, terrible Sarpa</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"><a name="page_927"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 927</font></a></p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'">Ku<u>nd</u>a, Masaka Ku<u>nd</u>a, Damsa Ku<u>nd</u>a, dreadful Garala Ku<u>nd</u>a, Vajra Dam<u>st</u>ra Vrischika Ku<u>nd</u>a, Sara Ku<u>nd</u>a, Sûla Ku<u>nd</u>a, awful Kha<u>d</u>ga Ku<u>nd</u>a, Gola Ku<u>nd</u>a, Nakra Ku<u>nd</u>a, sorrowful Kâka Ku<u>nd</u>a, Manthâna Ku<u>nd</u>a, Vîja Ku<u>nd</u>a, painful Vajra Ku<u>nd</u>a, hot Pâtsâ<u>n</u>a Ku<u>nd</u>a, sharp Pâsâ<u>n</u>a Ku<u>nd</u>a, Lâlâ Ku<u>nd</u>a, Masî Ku<u>nd</u>a, Chakra Ku<u>nd</u>a, Vakra Ku<u>nd</u>a, very terrible Kurma Ku<u>nd</u>a, Jvâlâ Ku<u>nd</u>a, Bhasma Ku<u>nd</u>a, Dagdha Ku<u>nd</u>a, and others. Besides these, there are the Taptasûchî, Asipatra, K<u>s</u>uradhâra, Sûchîmukha, Gokhâmûkha, Kûmbhîpâka, Kâlasûtra, Matsyoda, Krimi, Kantuka, Pâmsubhojya, Pâsave<u>st</u>a, Sûlaprota, Prakampana, Ulkâmakha, Andhakûpa, Vedhana, Tâ<u>d</u>ana, Jâlarandhra, Dehachûrna, Dalana, So<u>s</u>a<u>n</u>a, Ka<u>s</u>a, Sûrpa, Jvâlâmûkha, Dhûmândha, Nâgave<u>st</u>ana and various others. O Savitri! The Ku<u>nd</u>as give much pain and torment greatly the sinners; they are under the constant watch of innumerable servants. They hold rods in their hands; some of them have nooses; others hold clubs, Saktis, awful scimitars; they are fierce fanatics, maddened with vanity. All are filled with Tamogu<u>n</u>as, merciless, irresistible, energetic, fearless and tawny-eyed (like copper). Some of them are Yogis; some are Siddhas, they assume various forms. When the sinners are about to die, they see these servants of Yama. But those who do their own duties, who are Sâktas, Sauras, or Gâ<u>n</u>apatyas or those who are virtuous Siddha Yogis, they never see the servants of Yama. Those who are engaged in their own Dharmas, who are possessed of wisdom, who are endowed with knowledge, who are mentally strong, who are untouched by fear, who are endowed with the feelings of the Devas, and those who are real Vai<u>sn</u>avas, they never see these servants of Yama. O Chaste One! Thus I have enumerated to you the Ku<u>nd</u>as. Now hear who live in the Ku<u>nd</u>as.</p>   
 <p align="none;text-autospace:none'"> </p>   
 <p>Here ends the Thirty-second Chapter of the Ninth Book on the enumeration of various hells for sinners in the Mahâpurâ<u>n</u>am Srî Mad Devî Bhâgavatam of 18,000 verses by Mahar<u>s</u>i Veda Vyâsa.</p>
