 <p><a name="page_60"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 60</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE EIGHTH.</h3>  
 <p>TAKEN upon a base art thou. Thee for the Âdityas.<br> Here, O Far-striding Vishnu, is thy Soma. Guard it from<br> injury. Let them not harm thee.<br> 2 Neer art thou fruitless, Indra; neer dost thou desert thy<br> worshipper.<br> But now, O Liberal Lord, thy bounty as a God is poured<br> forth ever more and more. Thee for the Âdityas.<br> 3 Never art thou neglectful: thou guardest both races with<br> thy care.<br> The Soma feast, O Fourth Âditya, is thy strength. Amrit<br> is stablished in the heavens. Thee for the Âdityas.<br> 4 The sacrifice obtains the Gods acceptance. Be graciously<br> inclined to us, Âdityas.<br> Hitherward let your favour be directed and be our best deliverer<br> from trouble. Thee for the Âdityas.<br> 5 This is thy Soma draught, O bright Âditya: take delight<br> therein.<br> To this mine utterance, O ye men, give credence, what good<br> the man and wife obtain by praying:<br> A manly son is burn and gathers riches, and thrives for ever<br> sinless in the dwelling.<br> 6 Fair wealth, O Savitar, to-day, to-morrow, fair wealth produce<br> for us each day that passes.<br> May we, through this our song, be happy gainers, God! of<br> a fair and spacious habitation.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_61"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 61</font></a></p>  
 <p>7 Taken upon a base art thou.<br> Savitar's giver of delight art thou. Giver of joy art thou:<br> vouchsafe me joy.<br> Speed thou the sacrifice, speed thou the sacrifice's lord to<br> win his share. Thee for the God, for Savitar.<br> 8 Taken upon a base art thou.<br> Thou art a good protector, firmly stablished. To the Great<br> Bull be reverential homage. Thee for the Visvedevas.<br> This is thy home: Thee for the Visvedevas.<br> 9 Taken upon a base art thou.<br> May it be mine to prosper the libations of thee Brihaspati's<br> son, O radiant Soma, of thee, strong Indu, mated with<br> thy Consorts.<br> I am in heaven above, on earth beneath it. The intermediate<br> region was my father.<br> I saw the Sun both from above and under. I am what Gods<br> in secret hold the highest.<br> 10 Agni, associate with the Dames, accordant with the God<br> Tvashtar, drink. All-hail!<br> Thou art Prajâpati, strong male, impregner: may I obtain<br> from thee, strong male, impregner, a son who shall himself<br> become a father.<br> 11 Taken upon a base art thou.<br> Thou art bay-coloured, Yoker of Bay Coursers. Thee for<br> the pair of tawny-coloured horses.<br> United with the Soma, ye, for Indra, are corn for his two<br> tawny steeds to feed on.<br> 12 That draught of thine which winneth cows or horses, offered<br> with sacrificial text and lauded</p>  
 <p><a name="page_62"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 62</font></a></p>  
 <p>With chanted hymns and songs of adorationof that permitted<br> do I take permitted.<br> 13 Of sin against the Gods thou art atonement. Of sin against<br> mankind thou art atonement.<br> For sin against the Fathers thou atonest. Of sin against<br> oneself thou art atonement.<br> Of every sort of sin thou art atonement. The sin that I<br> have knowingly committed, the sin that unawares I have<br> committed, of all that wickedness thou art the atonement.<br> 14 We with our bodies have again united, with lustre, vital sap,<br> and happy spirit.<br> Giver of boons, may Tvashtar grant us riches and smooth<br> whateer was injured in our body.<br> 15 Lead us with thought to wealth in kine, O Indra, to princes,<br> Lord of Bounty! and to welfare.<br> Lead thou us on to God-inspired devotion, to favour of the<br> Gods who merit worship. All-hail!<br> 16 Verse 14 repeated.<br> 17 May this please Savitar and liberal Dhâtar, Prajâpati the<br> Treasure-Guard, bright Agni,<br> Tvashtar, and Vishnu: blessing him with children, grant<br> store of riches to the Sacrificer.<br> 18 Gods, we have made your seats easy of access, who, pleased<br> with us, have come to this libation.<br> Bearing and bringing hitherward your treasures, grant to<br> this man, good Lords, abundant riches. All-hail!<br> 19 The willing Gods whom, God, thou hast brought hither, send<br> them to their own dwelling-place, O Agni.<br> As all of you have eaten and have drunken, approach the<br> air, the heat, the light of heaven.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_63"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 63</font></a></p>  
 <p>20 Here, Agni, as this sacrifice proceedeth, have we elected thee<br> to be our Hotar.<br> Special have been thine offerings and thy labour. Well<br> knowing sacrifice, as sage, come near us.<br> 21 Do ye, O Gods, discoverers of the Pathway, go forward on<br> the path when ye have found it.<br> O God, thou Lord and Master of the Spirit, bestow<br> All-hail!this sacrifice on Vâta.<br> 22 Go, Sacrifice, to the sacrifice: seek thou the sacrifice's lord,<br> seek thine own home. All-hail!<br> Lord of the sacrifice, this is thy sacrifice, followed by many<br> heroes, loud with hymns of praise. Accept it thou.<br> All-hail!<br> 23 Become no serpent thou, become no viper.<br> King Vat-tins hath made a spacious pathway, a pathway for<br> the Sun wherein to travel.<br> Where no way was he made him set his footstep, and warned<br> afar whateer afflicts the spirit.<br> To Varuna be reverential homage! Varuna's noose beneath<br> our feet is trampled.<br> 24 The waters, face of Agni, have I entered, O Waters Child,<br> repelling evil spirits.<br> Offer the fuel in each home, O Agni. Let thy tongue dart<br> All-hail!to meet the butter.<br> 25 Thy heart is in the flood, within the waters. With thee let<br> plants and waters be commingled,<br> That, Lard of Sacrifice, we may adore thee with singing<br> praise and telling forth our homage. All-hail!<br> 26 This, O celestial Waters, is your offspring. Support him<br> dearly loved and gently nurtured.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_64"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 64</font></a></p>  
 <p>This is thy station, O celestial Soma; therein bring happiness<br> and ward off evil.<br> 17 O restless Purifying Bath, thou glidest onward restlessly.<br> May I with aid of Gods remove the stain of sin against the<br> Gods, and wash away with mortals help the wrong that<br> hath been done to men. Preserve me, God, from injury,<br> from the loud-roaring demon foe. Thou art the fuel of<br> the Gods.<br> 28 Let, still unborn, the ten-month calf move with the following<br> after-birth.<br> Even as the-wind is moving, as the gathered flood of ocean<br> moves,<br> So may this ten-month calf come forth together with the<br> after-birth.<br> 29 O thou who hast a womb of gold and offspring meet for<br> sacrifice,<br> Him with all limbs unbroken have I brought together with<br> his dam. All-hail!<br> 30 Multiform, rich in wondrous operation, the strong juice<br> hath enrobed itself with greatness.<br> Let the worlds praise her uniped and biped, three-footed<br> and four-footed and eight-footed. All-hail!<br> 31 Verily, best of guardians hath he in whose dwelling-place<br> ye drink,<br> O Maruts, giants of the sky.<br> 32 May Heaven and Earth, the Mighty Pair, besprinkle this<br> our sacrifice,<br> And feed us full with nourishments.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_65"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 65</font></a></p>  
 <p>33 Slayer of Vritra, mount thy car: thy Bay Steeds have been<br> yoked by prayer.<br> May, with its voice, the pressing-stone draw thine attention<br> hither ward.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. For Indra thee, for Shodasî.<br> This is a dwelling-place for thee. For Indra thee, for<br> Shodasî.<br> 34 Harness thy pair of strong Bay Steeds, long-maned, whose<br> bodies fill the girths,<br> And, Indra, Soma-drinker, come to listen to our songs of<br> praise.<br> Taken upon a base, etc., as in 33.<br> 35 His pair of tawny Coursers bring Indra of unresisted might<br> Hither to Rishis songs of praise and sacrifice performed by<br> men.<br> Taken upon a base, etc., as in 33.<br> 36 Than whom there is none other born more mighty, who<br> hath pervaded all existing creatures<br> Prajâpati, rejoicing in his offspring, he, Shodasî, maintains<br> the three great lustres.<br> 37 Indra chief Lord and Varuna the Sovran have made this<br> draught of thine the first and foremost.<br> I, after, drink their draught. May she, the Goddess of<br> Speech, rejoicing, sate herself with SomaAll-hail!with<br> Prâna as her feast-companion.<br> 38 Skilled in thy task, O Agni, pour lustre and hero strength<br> on us,<br> Granting me wealth and affluence.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. For Agni thee, for splendour<br> This is thy home. For Agni thee, for splendour.<br> Thou, lustrous Agni, mid the Gods art splendid. May I<br> among mankind be bright with lustre.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_66"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 66</font></a></p>  
 <p>39 Arising in thy might thy jaws thou shookest, Indra, having<br> drunk<br> The Soma which the mortar pressed.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. For Indra thee, for mighty<br> strength.<br> This is thy home. For Indra thee, for might.<br> Among the Gods thou art the mightiest, Indra. Among<br> mankind I fain would be most mighty.<br> 40 His herald rays are seen afar refulgent oer the world of<br> men,<br> Like flames of fire that burn and blaze.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. For Sûrya, for the Bright One,<br> thee.<br> This is thy home. For Sûrya, for the Bright One, thee.<br> Thou among Gods art brightest, brightest Sûrya. Among<br> mankind I fain would be the brightest.<br> 41 His herald rays bear him aloft, the God who knoweth all<br> that lives, Sûrya, that all may look at him.<br> Taken upon a base, etc., as in 40.<br> 42 Smell thou the vat. Let Soma drops pass into thee, O<br> Mighty One.<br> Return again with store of sap. Pour for us wealth in thou-<br> sands thou with full broad streams and floods of milk. A<br> Let riches come again to me.<br> 43 Idâ, delightful, worshipful, loveable, splendid, shining One,<br> Inviolable, full of sap, the Mighty One, most glorious,<br> These are thy names, O Cow: tell thou the Gods that I act<br> righteously.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_67"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 67</font></a></p>  
 <p>44 O Indra, beat our foes away, humble the men who challenge us:<br> Send down to nether darkness him who seeks to do us injury.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. For Indra, foe-dispeller, thee.<br> This is thy home. For Indra, foe-dispeller, thee.<br> 45 Let us invoke to-day, to aid our labour, the Lord of Speech,<br> the thought-swift Visvakarman.<br> May he hear kindly all our invocations, who gives all bliss<br> for aid, whose works are righteous.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. For Indra Visvakarman thee.<br> This is thy home. For Indra Visvakarman thee.<br> 46 With strengthening libation, Visvakarman, thou madest<br> Indra an undying guardian.<br> The people of old time bowed down before him because the<br> Mighty One was meet for worship.<br> Taken upon a base, etc., as in 45.<br> 47 Taken upon a base art thou.<br> I take thee lord of Gâyatrî for Agni. For Indra take I thee<br> the lord of Trishtup.<br> I lake thee lord of Jagatî for All-Gods. Anushtup is the<br> song that sings thy praises.<br> 48 I stir thee for the fall of cloud-borne waters. I stir thee for<br> the fall of streams that gurgle. I stir thee for the fall of<br> those that gladden. I stir thee for their fall who are most<br> lovely. I stir thee for their fall that are the sweetest. I<br> stir thee for the waters fall, I stir thee, pure one, in the<br> pure, in the day's form, in Sûrya's beams.<br> 49 The Bull's majestic form is shining brightly, the pure the<br> pure's preceder, Soma Soma's.<br> Whatever name invincible, stimulating, is thine, O Soma,<br> for that name I take thee.<br> All-hail to Soma, unto thee, O Soma.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_68"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 68</font></a></p>  
 <p>50 O radiant Soma, eagerly draw nigh to Agni's well-loved food.<br> O radiant Soma, willingly go to the food that Indra loves.<br> Go, radiant Soma, as our friend, to the All-Gods beloved food.<br> 51 Here is delight: enjoy yourselves; here surety, surety of<br> your own. All-hail!<br> Loosing the suckling to his dam, the suckling as he milks<br> his dam<br> May he maintain the growth of wealth among us. All-hail!<br> 52 Thou art the Session's happy termination.<br> We have attained the light and grown immortal.<br> We have gone up from earth to sky, have found the Gods<br> and heaven and light.<br> 53 Indra and Parvata, our champions in the fight, drive ye away<br> the man who fain would war with us, drive him far from<br> us with the bolt.<br> Welcome to him concealed afar shall be the lair that he<br> hath found.<br> So may the Render rend our foes on every side, rend them.<br> O Hero, everywhere.<br> Earth! Ether! Sky! May we be rich in offspring, rich in<br> brave sons and rich in food to feed us.<br> 54 Parameshthin when contemplated. Prajâpati in uttered<br> speech.<br> Food when approached. Savitar in the partition. Visvakarman<br> in Consecration. Pûshan in the Soma-purchasing<br> cow.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_69"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 69</font></a></p>  
 <p>55 As Indra and the Maruts he is stationed ready for the sale:<br> Asura, being bought and sold. Mitra when purchased;<br> Vishnu Sipivishta when on the Sacrificer's thigh he resteth;<br> Vishnu Naraudhisha brought on the barrow;<br> 56 Soma when come: when seated on the platform, Varuna;<br> Agni in the sacred fire-place;<br> Indra upon the sacrificial barrow; Atharvan when deposited<br> for pounding;<br> 57 All-Gods when offered in the scattered fragments; Vishnu,<br> the guard of those who soothe his anger, when he is filled<br> and swelling in the waters; Yama in pressing; Vishnu<br> in collection;<br> Vâyu what time they cleanse and purify him; the Bright<br> when cleansed; the Bright with milk about him; Manthin<br> commingled with the meal of barley;<br> 58 All-Gods when he is drawn away in beakers; Life when<br> uplifted for the fire-oblation; Rudra when offered; Vâta<br> when reverted; Man-viewer when beheld; drink when<br> they drink him; deposited, the Nârâsamsa Fathers;<br> 59 Sindhu when ready for the bath that cleanses the sea<br> when he is carried to the waters; Water is he when he is<br> plunged beneath it.<br> To those most mighty hath it gone, most manly in vigour,<br> by whose strength the worlds were stablished,<br> Who rule as Lords resistless in their grandeur, Vishnu and<br> Varuna, at the prayer of morning.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_70"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 70</font></a></p>  
 <p>60 To Gods, to sky the sacrifice hath gone: come riches thence<br> to me!<br> To men, to air the sacrifice hath gone: come riches thence<br> to me!<br> To Fathers, earth, the sacrifice hath gone: come riches<br> thence to me!<br> Whatever sphere the sacrifice hath reached, may wealth<br> come thence to me<br> 61 The threads that have been spun, the four-and-thirty, which<br> stablish this our sacrifice with Svadhâ,<br> Of these I join together what is broken. All-hail! to Gods<br> go the warm milk oblation!<br> 62 Spread far and wide is sacrifice's milking: eightfold along<br> the heaven hath it extended.<br> Pour, Sacrifice! in plenty on mine offspring: may I obtain<br> prosperity for ever. All-hail!<br> 63 Soma, send wealth in gold and steeds and heroes. All-hail!<br> bring hitherward booty in cattle.</p>
