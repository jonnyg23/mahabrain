 <p><a name="page_51"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 51</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE SEVENTH.</h3>  
 <p>FLOW for Vâchaspati, cleansed by hands from the two off-shoots<br> of the Bull.<br> Flow pure, a Deity thyself, for Deities whose share thou art.<br> 2 Sweeten the freshening draughts we drink.<br> Soma, whatever name thou hast, unconquerable, giving life,<br> To that thy Soma, Soma! Hail!<br> 3 Self-made art thou from all the Powers that are in heaven<br> and on the earth.<br> May the Mind win thee, thee, All-hail! for Sûrya, O thou<br> nobly-born.<br> Thee for the Deities who sip light-atoms.<br> Truly fulfilled, O Plant divine, be that for which I pray to<br> thee.<br> With ruin falling from above may So-and-So be smitten,<br> crash!<br> Thee for out-breathing, thee for breath diffused!<br> 4 Taken upon a base art thou. Hold in, Rich Lord! be Soma's<br> guard.<br> Be thou protector of our wealth: win strengthening food by<br> sacrifice.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_52"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 52</font></a></p>  
 <p>5 The heaven and spacious earth I lay within thee, I lay within<br> thee middle air's wide region.<br> Accordant with the Gods lower and higher, Rich Lord, rejoice<br> thee in the Antaryâma.<br> O Self-made art thou . . . . . light-atoms (verse 3 repeated).<br> Thee for the upward breath.<br> 7 O Vâyu, drinker of the pure, be near us: a thousand teams<br> are thine, All-bounteous Giver.<br> To thee the rapture-giving juice is offered, whose first<br> draught, God, thou takest as thy portion.<br> 8 These, Indra-Vâyu! have been shed; come for our offered<br> dainties sake:<br> The drops are yearning for you both.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. For Vâyu, Indra-Vâyu, thee.<br> This is thy home. Thee for the close-knit friends.<br> 9 This Soma hath been shed for you, Law-strengtheners,<br> Mitra-Varuna!<br> Here listen ye to this my call.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. For Mitra thee, for Varuna.<br> 10 May we, possessing much, delight in riches, Gods in oblation,<br> and the kine in pasture;<br> And that Milch-cow who shrinks not from the milking,<br> O Indra-Varuna, give to us daily.<br> This is thy home. Thee for the righteous Twain.<br> 11 Distilling honey is your whip, Asvins, and full of pleasantness:<br> Sprinkle therewith the sacrifice.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. Thee for the Asvins. This is<br> thy home. Thee for the Honey-lovers.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_53"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 53</font></a></p>  
 <p>12 Thou in the first old time, as all were wont, so now drawest<br> from him, light-finder, throned on sacred grass,<br> Preeminence and strength, from him turned hither, swift,<br> roaring, who winneth those whereby thou waxest strong.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. Thee for Sanda.<br> This is thy home. Protect thou manly power.<br> Sanda hath been removed, may Deities who drink the pure<br> libation lead thee forward. Invincible art thou.<br> 13 Well stored with heroes and begetting heroes, with growth<br> of wealth surround the Sacrificer.<br> The Bright, conjoined with Heaven and with Earth, with<br> the brightly-shining one.<br> Expelled is Sanda. Thou art Sukra's dwelling.<br> 14 May we, O radiant Soma, be the keepers of thine uninjured<br> strength and growth of riches.<br> This is the first all-bounteous Consecration: he the first,<br> Varuna, Mitra, and Agni.<br> 15 He is the first Brihaspati, the Prudent. Offer ye juice with<br> Svâhâ! to that Indra.<br> Content be priestly offices, those with good sacrifice of meath,<br> those that are pleased when they have gained fair offerings<br> with the solemn Hail!<br> The Kindler of the Fire hath sacrificed.<br> 16 See, Vena, born in light hath driven hither on chariot of<br> the air the calves of Prisni.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_54"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 54</font></a></p>  
 <p>Singers with hymns caress him as an infant there where the<br> waters and the sunlight mingle.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. Thee for Marka.<br> 17 To his oblation, swift as thought ye hurried and welcomed<br> eagerly the prayers he offered.<br> With arrows in his hand the Very Mighty forced from him<br> all obedience of a servant.<br> This is thy dwelling-place. Protect the people. Marka hath<br> been removed.<br> Gods, drinkers of the Man thin, lead thee forward! Invincible<br> art thou.<br> 18 Well stored with people and begetting people, with growth<br> of wealth surround the Sacrificer.<br> The Manthin joined with Heaven and Earth and with the<br> Manthin-shining one.<br> Expelled is Marka. Thou art Manthin's dwelling.<br> 19 O ye eleven Gods whose home is heaven, O ye eleven who<br> make earth your dwelling.<br> Ye who with might, eleven, live in waters, accept this sacrifice,<br> Ye Gods, with pleasure.<br> 20 Taken upon a base art thou. Thou art Âgrayana, good first<br> libation.<br> Be thou the guard of sacrifice: protect the sacrifice's lord.<br> Vishnu with might protect thee. Guard thou Vishnu.<br> Guard on all sides the Soma sacrifices.<br> 21 Soma flows pure, Soma flows pure for this Priesthood, for<br> the Nobility, pure for the worshipper who presses out<br> the juice, flows pure for food and energy, for waters and<br> for plants; flows pure for general prosperity. Thee for the<br> Universal Gods. This is thy home. Thee for the Universal<br> Gods.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_55"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 55</font></a></p>  
 <p>22 Taken upon a base art thou. For Indra Lord of the Brihat,<br> strong with vital vigour, I take thee lover of the invocation.<br> Indra, what mighty vigour thou possessest, for that do<br> I take thee, take thee for Vishnu<br> This is thy home. Thee for the recitations.<br> For the Gods take I thee, the Gods protector; yea, for the<br> sacrifice's life I take thee.<br> 23 For Mitra-Varuna thee, the Gods protector, yea, for the<br> sacrifice's life I take thee.<br> For Indra, thee, etc. For Indra-Agni, thee, etc. For Indra-<br> Varuna, thee, etc. For Indra-Brihaspati thee, etc. For<br> Indra-Vishnu thee, etc.<br> 24 Him, messenger of earth and head of heaven, Agni <a href="errata.htm#2">Vaisvânara</a>,<br> born in holy Order,<br> The Sage, the King, the Guest of men, a vessel fit for their<br> mouths, the Gods have generated.<br> 25 Taken upon a base art thou. Firm, firmly resting, the<br> firmest of the firm, the most securely grounded of those<br> who never have been shaken.<br> This is thy home. Thee for Vaisvânara.<br> I pour forth with firm mind, with voice, firm Soma. So<br> now may Indra verily make our people all of. One heart<br> and mind and free from foemen.<br> 26 Whatever drop of thine leaps forth, whatever stalk from<br> the bowls lap, shaken by the press-stone,<br> From the Adhvaryu's hand or from the filter, that, consecrated<br> in my mind with Vashat I offer unto thee with<br> cry of Svâhâ!<br> Thou art the way by which the Gods ascended.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 56</font></a></p>  
 <p>27 Giver of splendour, grow thou pure for splendour for my<br> outward breath.<br> Giver of splendour, grow thou pure for splendour for my<br> spreading breath.<br> Giver, etc. ... ... ... for my upward breath.<br> Giver, etc. ... ... ... for my power of speech.<br> Giver, etc. ... ... ... for my sense and will.<br> Giver, etc. ... ... ... for my hearing power.<br> Givers of splendor, grow ye pure for splendour for my<br> orbs of sight.<br> 28 Giver of splendour, grow thou pure for splendour for my<br> living self.<br> Giver, etc. ... ... ... for my energy.<br> Giver, etc. ... ... ... for my vital power.<br> Givers of splendour, grow ye pure for splendour for all<br> sprung from me.<br> 29 Who art thou? Which of all art thou? Whose art thou?<br> Who art thou by name?<br> Even thou on whose name we have meditated, then whom<br> we have delighted with our Soma.<br> 30 Taken upon a base art thou. For Madhu thee: Taken<br> upon a base art thou. For Mâdhava thee.<br> Taken, etc. For Sukra thee. Taken, etc. For Suchi thee.<br> Taken, etc. For Nabhas thee. Taken, etc. For Nabhasya<br> thee. Taken, etc. For Food thee. Taken, etc. For<br> Energy thee. Taken, etc. For Sahas thee. Taken, etc.<br> For Sahasya thee. Taken, etc. For Tapas thee. Taken, etc.<br> For Tapasya thee. Taken, etc. For Amhasaspati thee.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a></p>  
 <p>31 Moved, Indra-Agni, by our hymns, come to the juice, the<br> precious dew.<br> Drink ye thereof, impelled by song.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. For Indra-Agni thee. This<br> is thy dwelling. Thee for Indra-Agni.<br> 32 Hitherward! they who light the flame and straightway<br> strew the sacred grass,<br> Whose Friend is Indra ever young.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. For Indra-Agni thee. This<br> is thy dwelling. Thee for Indra-Agni.<br> 33 Ye Visvedevas who protect, reward, and cherish men, approach<br> Your worshipper's drink-offering.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. Thee for the Universal Gods.<br> This is thy home. Thee for the Visvedevas.<br> 34 O ye All-Gods, come hitherward: hear this my invocation<br> seat<br> Yourselves upon this sacred grass.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. Thee for the Universal Gods.<br> This is thy home. Thee for the Visvedevas.<br> 35 Here drink the Soma, Indra girt by Maruts! as thou didst<br> drink the juice beside Sâryâta.<br> Under thy guidance, in thy keeping, Hero! the singers serve,<br> skilled in fair sacrifices.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. For Indra girt by Maruts thee.<br> This is thy home. For Indra girt by Maruts thee.<br> 36 The Bull whose strength hath waxed, whom Maruts follow,<br> free-giving Indra, the Celestial Ruler,<br> Mighty, all-conquering, the victory-giver, him we invoke to<br> give us new protection.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. For Indra girt by Maruts thee.<br> This is thy home. For Indra girt by Maruts thee.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. Thee for the Maruts energy.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_58"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 58</font></a></p>  
 <p>37 Indra, accordant with the banded Maruts, drink Soma,<br> Hero! as wise Vritra-slayer.<br> Slay thou our foemen, drive away assailants, and make us<br> safe on every side from danger.<br> Taken, etc. For Indra girt by Maruts thee. This is thy<br> home. For Indra girt by Maruts thee.<br> 38 Drink, Indra Marut-girt, as Bull, the Soma: for joy, for<br> rapture even as thou pleasest.<br> Pour down the wave of meath within thy belly thou art<br> the King of juices shed fortnightly.<br> Taken, etc.......Maruts thee (as above).<br> 39 Great, hero-like, controlling men is Indra, unwasting in<br> his powers, doubled in vastness.<br> He, turned to us, hath grown to hero vigour: broad, wide,<br> he hath been decked by those who serve him.<br> Taken upon a base art thou. Thee for Mahendra.<br> This is thy dwelling-place. Thee for Mahendra.<br> 40 Indra, great in his power and might, and like Parjanya rich<br> In rain,<br> Is magnified by Vatsa's lauds.<br> Taken, etc. (as in 39).<br> 41 His bright rays bear him up aloft, the God who knoweth<br> all that lives,<br> Sûrya, that all may look on him. All-hail!<br> 42 The brilliant presence of the Gods hath risen, the eye of<br> Mitra, Varuna, and Agni.<br> Soul of all moving, soul of all that moves not, the Sun<br> hath filled the air and earth and heaven.<br> 43 By goodly paths lead us to riches, Agni, thou God who<br> knowest every sacred duty.<br> Remove the sin that makes us stray and wander: most<br> ample adoration will we bring thee.<br> 44 Wide room and comfort may this Agni give us, and go<br> before us cleaving down our foemen.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_59"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 59</font></a></p>  
 <p>May he win booty in the fight for booty: may he quell<br> foes in his triumphant onset.<br> 45 I through your beauty have attained to beauty. The Tutha,<br> the omniscient, allot you!<br> Go forth, bright-gifted! on the path of Order. Look thou<br> upon the heaven and air's mid-region. Unite thee with<br> the priests who keep the Sadas.<br> 46 This day may it he mine to find a Brâhman sprung from a<br> lauded father and grandfather,<br> Offspring of Rishis and himself a Rishi, the fit recipient of<br> priestly guerdon.<br> Go to the Gods, bestowed by me, and enter into him who<br> gives.<br> 47 To Agni, yea, to me let Varuna give thee. May I gain life<br> that shall endure for ever.<br> Be thou strong vital power to him who gives thee, and com-<br> fort unto me the gift's receiver.<br> To Rudra, yea, to me let Varuna........................for ever.<br> Be thou the breath of life to him who gives thee, and vigour<br> unto me the gift's receiver.<br> To me Brihaspati let Varuna........................for ever.<br> Be thou a covering skin to him who gives thee, and comfort<br> unto me the gift's receiver.<br> To Varna, yea, to me let Varuna........................for ever.<br> Be thou a steed to him who gives the guerdon, and vital<br> power to me the gift's receiver.<br> 48 Who hath bestowed it? Upon whom bestowed it?<br> Desire bestowed it, for Desire he gave it. Desire is giver<br> and Desire receiver. This, O Desire, to thee is dedicated.</p>
