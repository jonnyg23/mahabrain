 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XVI. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. PRIEST of all sacrifices hast thou been appointed by the Gods,<br> Agni, amid the race of man.<br> 2 So with thy joyous tongues for us sacrifice nobly in this rite.<br> Bring thou the Gods and worship them.<br> 3 For well, O God, Disposer, thou knowest, straight on, the paths and ways,<br> Agni, most wise in sacrifice.<br> 4 Thee, too, hath Bhārata of old, with mighty men, implored for bliss.<br> And worshipped thee the worshipful.<br> 5 Thou givest these abundant boons to Divodāsa pouring forth,<br> To Bharadvāja offering gifts.<br> 6 Do thou, Immortal Messenger, bring hither the Celestial Folk;<br> Hearing the singer's eulogy.<br> 7 Mortals with pious thought implore thee, Agni, God, at holy rites,<br> To come unto the feast of Gods.<br> 8 I glorify thine aspect and the might of thee the Bountilul.<br> All those who love shall joy in thee,<br> 9 Invoker placed by Manus, thou, Agni, art near, the wisest Priest:<br> Pay worship to the Tribes of Heaven.<br> 10 Come, Agni, lauded, to the feast; come to the offering of the gifts.<br> As Priest be seated on the grass.<br> 11 So, Aṅgiras, we make thee strong with fuel and with holy oil.<br> Blaze high, thou youngest of the Gods.<br> 12 For us thou winnest, Agni, God, heroic strength exceeding great,<br> Far-spreading and of high renown.<br> 13 Agni, Atharvan brought thee forth, by rubbing, from the lotus-flower,<br> The head of Visva, of the Priest.<br> 14 Thee. Vṛtra's slayer, breaker down of castles, hath Atharvan's son,<br> Dadhyac the Ṛṣi, lighted up.<br> 15 The hero Pathya kindled thee the Dasyus'. most destructive foe,<br> Winner of spoil in every fight.<br> 16 Come, here, O Agni, will I sing verily other songs to thee,<br> And with these drops shalt thou grow strong.<br> 17 Whereer thy mind applies itself, vigour preeminent hast thou:<br> There wilt thou gain a dwelling-place.<br> 18 Not for a moment only lasts thy bounty, good to many a one!<br> Our service therefore shalt thou gain.<br> 19 Agni, the Bhārata, hath been sought, the Vṛtra-slayer, marked of all,<br> Yea, Divodāsa's Hero Lord.<br> 20 For he gave riches that surpass in greatness all the things of earth,<br> Fighting untroubled, unsubdued.<br> 21 Thou, Agni, as in days of old, with recent glory, gathered light,<br> Hast overspread the lofty heaven.<br> 22 Bring to your Agni, O my friends, boldly your laud and sacrifice:<br> Give the Disposer praise and song.<br> 23 For as sagacious Herald he hath sat through every age of man,<br> Oblation-bearing messenger.<br> 24 Bring those Two Kings whose ways are pure, Ādityas, and the Marut host,<br> Excellent God! and Heaven and Earth.<br> 25 For strong and active mortal man, excellent, Agni, is the look Of thee Immortal, Son of Strength<br> 26 Rich through his wisdom, noblest be the giver serving thee to-day:<br> The man hath brought his hymn of praise.<br> 27 These, Agni, these are helped by thee, who strong and active all their lives,<br> O'ercome the malice of the foe, fight down the malice ofthe foe.<br> 28 May Agni with his pointed blaze cast down each fierce devouring fiend<br> May Agni win us wealth by war.<br> 29 O active Jātavedas, bring riches with store of hero sons:<br> Slay thou the demons, O Most Wise.<br> 30 Keep us, O Jātavedas, from the troubling of the man of sin:<br> Guard us thou Sage who knowest prayer.<br> 31 Whatever sinner, Agni, brings oblations to procure our death,<br> Save us from woe that he would work.<br> 32 Drive from us with thy tongue, O God, the man who doeth evil deeds,<br> The mortal who would strike us dead.<br> 33 Give shelter reaching far and wide to Bharadvāja, conquering Lord!<br> Agni, send wealth most excellent.<br> 34 May Agni slay the Vṛtras,fain for riches, through the lord of song,<br> Served with oblation, kindled, bright.<br> 35 His Father's Father, shining in his Mother's everlasting side,<br> Set on the seat of holy Law.<br> 36 O active Jātavedas, bring devotion that wins progeny, Agni, that it may shine to heaven.<br> 37 O Child of Strength, to thee whose look is lovely we with dainty food,<br> O Agni, have poured forth our songs.<br> 38 To thee for shelter are we come, as to the shade from fervent heat<br> Agni, who glitterest like gold.<br> 39 Mighty as one who slays with shafts, or like a bull with sharpened horn,<br> Agni, thou breakest down the forts.<br> 40 Whom, like an infant newly born, devourer, in their arms they bear,<br> Men's Agni, skilled in holy rites.<br> 41 Bear to the banquet of the Gods the God best finder-out of wealth,<br> Let him he seated in his place.<br> 42 In Jātavedas kindle ye the dear guest who hath now appeared<br> In a soft place, the homestead's Lord.<br> 43 Harness, O Agni, O thou God, thy steeds which are most excellent:<br> They bear thee as thy spirit wills.<br> 44 Come hither, bring the Gods to us to taste the sacrificial feast,<br> To drink the draught of Soma juice.<br> 45 O Agni of the Bharatas, blaze high with everlasting might,<br> Shine forth and gleam, Eternal One.<br> 46 The mortal man who serves the God with banquet, and, bringing gifts at sacrifice, lauds Agni,<br> May well attract, with prayer and hands uplifted, the Priest of Heaven and Earth, true Sacrificer.<br> 47 Agni, we bring thee, with our hymn, oblation fashioned in the heart.<br> Let these be oxen unto thee, let these be bulls and kine to thee.<br> 48 The Gods enkindle Agni, best slayer of Vṛtra, first in rank,<br> The Mighty, One who brings us wealth and crushes down the Rākṣasas.</p>
