 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XIII. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. INDRA, when Soma juices flow, makes his mind pure and meet for lauds.<br> He gains the power that brings success, for great is he.<br> 2 In heaven's first region, in the seat of Gods, is he who brings success,<br> Most glorious, prompt to save, who wins the water-floods.<br> 3 Him, to win strength, have I invoked, even Indra mighty for the fray.<br> Be thou most near to us for bliss, a Friend to aid.<br> 4 Indra, Song -lover, here for thee the worshipper's libation flows.<br> Rejoicing in this sacred grass thou shinest forth.<br> 5 Even now, O Indra, give us that which, pressing juice, we crave of thee.<br> Bring us wealth manifold which finds the light of heaven.<br> 6 What time the zealous worshipper hath boldly sung his songs to thee,<br> Like branches of a tree up-grows what they desire.<br> 7 Generate songs even as of old, give car unto the singer's call.<br> Thou for the pious hast grown great at each carouse.<br> 8 Sweet strains that glorify him play like waters speeding down a slope,<br> Yea, him who in this song is called the Lord of Heaven;<br> 9 Yea, who alone is called the Lord, the single Ruler of the folk,<br> By worshippers seeking aid: may he joy in the draught.<br> 10 Praise him, the Glorious, skilled in song, Lord of the two victorious Bays:<br> They seek the worshipper's abode who bows in prayer.<br> 11 Put forth thy strength: with dappled Steeds come, thou of mighty intellect,<br> With swift Steeds to the sacrifice, for tis thy joy.<br> 12 Grant wealth to those who praise thee, Lord of Heroes, Mightiest Indra: give<br> Our princes everlasting fame and opulence.<br> 13 I call thee when the Sun is risen, I call thee at the noon of day:<br> With thy car-horses, Indra, come wellpleased to us.<br> 14 Speed forward hither, come to us, rejoice thee in the milky draught:<br> Spin out the thread of ancient time, as well is known.<br> 15 If, Śakra, Vṛtra-slayer, thou be far away or near to us.<br> Or in the sea, thou art the guard of Soma juice.<br> 16 Let songs we sing and Soma-drops expressed by us make Indra strong:<br> The tribes who bring oblations find delight in him.<br> 17 Him sages longing for his aid, with offerings brought in eager haste,<br> Him. even as branches, all mankind have made to grow.<br> 18 At the Trkadrukas the Gods span sacrifice that stirred the mind:<br> May our songs strengthen him who still hath strengthened us.<br> 19 When, true to duty, at due times the worshipper offers lauds to thee,<br> They call him Purifier, Pure, and Wonderful.<br> 20 That mind of Rudra, fresh and strong, moves conscious in the ancient ways,<br> With reference whereto the wise have ordered this.<br> 21 If thou elect to be my Friend drink of this sacrificial juice,<br> By help whereof we may subdue all enemies.<br> 22 O Indra, Lover of the song, when shall thy praiser be most blest?<br> When wilt thou grant us wealth in herds of kine and steeds?<br> 23 And thy two highIy-lauded Bays, strong stallions, draw thy car who art<br> Untouched by age, most gladdening car for which we pray.<br> 24 With ancient offerings we implore the Young and Strong whom many praise.<br> He from of old hath sat upon dear sacred grass.<br> 25 Wax miglity, thou whom many laud for aids which Ṛṣis have extolled.<br> Pour down for us abundant food and guard us well.<br> 26 O Indra, Caster of the Stone, thou helpest him who praises thee:<br> From sacrifice I send to thee a mindyoked hymn.<br> 27 Here, yoking for the Soma-draught these Horses, sharers of thy feast,<br> Thy Bay Steeds, Indra, fraught with weal tb, consent to come.<br> 28 Attendants on thy glory, let the Rudras roar assent to thee,<br> And all the Marut companies come tothe feast.<br> 29 These his victorious followers bold in the heavens the place they love,<br> Leagued in the heart of sacrifice, as well we know.<br> 30 That we may long behold the light, what time the ordered rite proceeds,<br> He duly measures, as he views, the sacrifice.<br> 31 O Indra, strong is this thy car, and strong are these Bay Steeds of thine:<br> O Śatakratu, thou art strong, strong is our call.<br> 32 Strong is the press-stone, strong thy joy, strong is the flowing Soma juice:<br> Strong is the rite thou furtherest, strong is our call.<br> 33 As strong I call on thee the Strong, O Thunderer with thy thousand aids:<br> For thou hast won the hymn of praise. Strong is our call.</p>
