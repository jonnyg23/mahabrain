 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN LXIV. Soma Pavamana.</h3>  
 <p>1. Soma, thou art a splendid Steer, a Steer, O God, with steerlike sway:<br> Thou as a Steer ordainest laws.<br> 2 Steer-strong thy might is as a steer's, steerstrong thywood, steer-like thy drink<br> A Steer indeed, O Steer, art thou.<br> 3 Thou, Indu, as a vigorous horse, hast neighed together steeds and kine:<br> Unbar for us the doors to wealth.<br> 4 Out of desire of cows and steeds and horses. potent Soma-drops,<br> Brilliant and swift, have been effused.<br> 5 They purified in both the hands, made beautiful by holy men,<br> Flow onward to the fleecy cloth.<br> 6 These Soma juices shall pour forth all treasures for the worshipper<br> From heaven and earth and firmament.<br> 7 The streams of Pavamana, thine, Finder of all, have been effused,<br> Even as Sūrya's rays of light.<br> 8 Making the light that shines from heaven thou flowest on to every form<br> Soma, thou swellest like a sea.<br> 9 Urged on thou sendest out thy voice, O Pavamana; thou hast moved,<br> Like the God Sūrya, to the sieve.<br> 10 Indu, Enlightener, Friend, hath been purified by the sages' hymns:<br> So starts the charioteer his steed-<br> 11 Thy God-delighting wave which hath flowed to purifying seive,<br> Alighting in the home of Law.<br> 12 Flow to our sieve, a gladdening draught that hath most intercourse with Gods,<br> Indu, to Indra for his drink.<br> 13 Flow onward with a stream for food, made beautiful by sapient men:<br> Indu with sheen approach the milk.<br> 14 While thou art cleansed, Song-Lover, bring comfort and vigour to the folk,<br> Poured, Tawny One! on milk and curds.<br> 15 Purified for the feast of Gods, go thou to Indra's special place,<br> Resplendent, guided by the strong.<br> 16 Accelerated by the hymn, the rapid drops of Soma juice<br> Have flowed, urged onward, to the lake.<br> 17 Easily have the living drops, made beautiful, approached the lake,<br> Yea, to the place of sacrifice.<br> 18 Compass about, our faithful Friend, all our possessions with thy might:<br> Guard, hero like, our sheltering home.<br> 19 Loud neighs the Courser Etaśa, with singers, harnessed for the place,<br> Guided for travel to the lake.<br> 20 What time the Swift One resteth in the golden place of sacrifice,<br> He leaves the foolish far away.<br> 21 The friends have sung in unison, the prudent wish to sacrifice:<br> Down sink the unintelligent.<br> 22 For Indra girt by Maruts, flow, thou Indu, very rich in sweets,<br> To sit in place of sacrifice.<br> 23 Controlling priests and sages skilled in holy song adorn thee well:<br> The living make thee beautiful.<br> 24 Aryaman, Mitra, Varuṇa drink Pavamana's juice, yea, thine:<br> O Sage, the Maruts drink thereof.<br> 25 O Soma, Indu, thou while thou art purified urgest onward speech.<br> Thousandfold, with the lore of hymns.<br> 26 Yea, Soma, Indu, while thou art purified do thou bring to us<br> Speech thousandfold that longs for war.<br> 27 O Indu, Much-invoked, while thou art purifying, as the Friend.<br> Of these men enter thou the lake.<br> 28 Bright are these Somas blent with milk, with light that flashes brilliantly. And form that utters loud acclaim.<br> 29 Led by his drivers, and sent forth, the Strong Steed hath come nigh for spoil,<br> Like warriors when they stand arrayed.<br> 30 Specially, Soma, coming as a Sage from heaven to prosper us,<br> Flow like the Sun for us to see.</p>
