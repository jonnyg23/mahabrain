 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN XLIV. Indra.</h3>  
 <p>1. THAT which is wealthiest, Wealthy God in splendoursmost illustrious,<br> Soma is pressed: thy gladdening draught, Indra! libation's Lord! is this.<br> 2 Effectual, Most Effectual One! thine, as bestowing wealth of hymns,<br> Soma is pressed: thy gladdening draught, Indra! libation's Lord! is this.<br> 3 Wherewith thou art increased in strength, and conquerest with thy proper aids,<br> Soma is pressed: thy gladdening draught, Indra! libation's Lord! is this.<br> 4 Him for your sake I glorify as Lord of Strength who wrongeth none,<br> The Hero Indra, conquering all, Most Bounteous, God of all the tribes.<br> 5 Those Goddesses, both Heaven and Earth, revere the power and might of him,<br> Him whom our songs increase in strength, the Lord of bounty swift to come.<br> 6 To seat your Indra, I will spread abroad with power this song of praise.<br> The saving succours that abide in him, like songs, extend and grow.<br> 7 A recent Friend, he found the skilful priest: he drank, and showed forth treasure from the Gods.<br> He conquered, borne by strong all-shaking mares, and was with far-spread power his friends' Protector.<br> 8 In course of Law the sapient juice was quaffed: the Deities to glory turned their mind.<br> Winning through hymns a lofty title, he, the Lovely, made his beauteous form apparent.<br> 9 Bestow on us the most illustrious strength ward off men's manifold malignities.<br> Give with thy might abundant vital force, and aid us graciously in gaining riches.<br> 10 We turn to thee as Giver, liberal Indra. Lord of the Bay Steeds, be not thou ungracious.<br> No friend among mankind have we to lookto: why have men called thee him who spurs the niggard?<br> 11 Give us not up, Strong Hero! to the hungry: unharmed be we whom thou, so rich, befriendest.<br> Full many a boon hast thou for men demolish those who present no gifts nor pour oblations.<br> 12 As Indra thundering impels the rain-clouds, so doth he send us store of kine and horses.<br> Thou art of old the Cherisher of singers let not the rich who bring no gifts deceive thee.<br> 13 Adbyaryu, hero, bring to mighty Indrafor he is King thereof-the pressed-out juices;<br> To him exalted by the hymns and praises, ancient and modern, of the singing Ṛṣis.<br> 14 In the wild joy of this hath Indra, knowing full many a form, struck down resistless Vṛtras.<br> Proclaim aloud to him the savoury Soma so that the Hero, strong of jaw, may drink it.<br> 15 May Indra drink this Soma poured to please him, and cheered therewith slay Vṛtra with his thunder.<br> Come to our sacrifice even from a distance, good lover of our songs, the bard's Supporter.<br> 16 The cup whence Indra drinks the draught is present: the Amṛta dear to Indra hath been drunken,<br> That it may cheer the God to gracious favour, and keep far from us hatred and affliction.<br> 17 Therewith enraptured, Hero, slay our foemen, the unfriendly, Maghavan be they kin or strangers,<br> Those who still aim their hostile darts to smite us, turn them to flight, O Indra, crush and kill them.<br> 18 O Indra Maghavan, in these our battles win easy paths for us and ample freedom.<br> That we may gain waters and seed and offspring, set thou our princes on thy side, O Indra.<br> 19 Let thy Bay Stallions, harnessed, bring thee hither, Steeds with strong chariot and strong reins to hold them,<br> Strong Horses, speeding hither, bearing thunder, well-harnessed, for the strong exciting potion.<br> 20 Beside the vat, Strong God! stand thy strong Horses, shining with holy oil, like waves exulting.<br> Indra, they bring to thee, the Strong and Mighty, Soma of juices shed by mighty press-stones.<br> 21 Thou art the Bull of earth, the Bull of heaven, Bull of the rivers, Bull of standing waters.<br> For thee, the Strong, O Bull, hath Indu swollen. juice pleasant, sweet to drink, for thine election.<br> 22 This God, with might, when first he had his being, with Indra for ally, held fast the Paṇi.<br> This Indu stole away the warlike weapons, and foiled the arts of his malignant father.<br> 23 The Dawns he wedded to a glorious Consort, and set within the Sun the light that lights him.<br> He found in heaven, in the third lucid regions, the threefold Amṛta in its close concealment.<br> 24 He stayed and held the heaven and earth asunder: the chariot with the sevenfold reins he harnessed.<br> This Soma Set with power within the milch-kine a spring whose ripe contents ten fingers empty.</p>
