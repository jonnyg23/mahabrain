 <p>THE Gnostic heresies play so important a part in the Christianity of the first three centuries that they naturally come up for treatment in all the Church histories for that period, such as those of Gieseler, Neander, Hase, and Schaff, as well as in the histories of Christian doctrine (Hagenbach, F. K. Meier, F. C. Baur, A. Neander, L. Noack, &amp;c.), and even in the histories of philosophy of Ueberweg, Zeller, &amp;c. But the monographic treatment of the subject from the theological standpoint--the Gnostic gems attracted early attention--may be said to have begun with</p>  
 <p>A. Neander. <i>Die genetische Entwickelung der vornehmsten gnostischen Systeme</i>. Berl. 1818. [Sober and clear.]</p>  
 <p>C. A. Lewald.--<i>De Doctrina Gnostica</i>. 1818.</p>  
 <p>J. Matter.--<i>Histoire critique du gnosticisme et de son influence sur les autres sectes religieuses et philosophiques pendant les six premiers siècles</i>. Paris, 2 tomes, 1828. [Second and best edition, Strasbourg, 3 tomes, 1843. Somewhat superficial and viewy, but still useful; giving outlines of whole subject, including iconography.]</p>  
 <p>E. Burton.--<i>Inquiry into the Heresies of the Apostolic Age</i>. Oxford, 1830. [Bampton lectures for 1829, uncritical; superseded for English readers by Mansel.]</p>  
 <p>J. A. Moehler.--<i>Versuch über d. Ursprung des Gnosticismus</i>. Tab. 1831.</p>  
 <p>F. C. Baur. <i>Die Christliche Gnosis</i>. Tübingen, 1835. [The place of publication indicates the tendency of this publication. Baur's views are read to best advantage in his <i>Das Christenthum der 3 ersten Jahrhunderte</i>.]</p>  
 <p>Norton.--<i>History of the Gnostics</i>. 1845.</p>  
 <p>H. Rossel.--<i>Geschichte der Untersuchungen über d. Gnosticismus in his Theologische Nachlass</i>. Berl. 1847.</p>  
 <p>R. A. Lipsius.--<i>Gnosticismus</i> in Ersch and Grüber. Bnd. 71. 1860. [The starting-point of new lines of inquiry rendered necessary by the publication by Miller of the <i>Philosophumena</i> attributed to Hippolytus (Clar. Press, 1851).]</p>  
 <p>W. Möller.--<i>Geschichte der Cosmologie der griechischen Kirche bis an Origenes</i>. Leipzig, 1862.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_450"><font size="1" color="green">p. 450</font></a></p>  
 <p>H. L. Mansel.--<i>The Gnostic Heresies of the First and Second Centuries</i>. London, 1875. [Posthumous. Edited by J. B. Lightfoot. Best English work, clear, fair, mainly founded on Lipsius, classification of sects geographical. His arrangement is: <i>Notices of Gnosticism in New Test</i>. (iv. v.)--<i>Precursors, Simon Magus and Menander</i> (vi.)--<i>The Ophite Sects</i> (vii.)--<i>Cerinthus, Carpocrates, Nazarenes and Ebionites</i> (viii.)--<i>Syrian Sects, Saturninus, Tatian, Bardesanes</i> (ix.)--<i>Egyptian, Basilides</i> (x.)--<i>Valentinus and Valentinians</i> (xi. xii.)--<i>Asiatic Gnosticism, Marcion</i> (xiii.)--<i>Judaising Reaction, Clementines, Elkesaites</i> (xiv.).]</p>  
 <p>A. Hilgenfeld.--<i>Die Ketzergeschichte des Urchristenthums</i>. Leipzig, 1884. [Ill-arranged, but at present the work round which discussion on Gnosticism centres.]</p>  
 <p>These are the chief monographs on the whole subject. Besides these, several articles in theological reviews may be mentioned, many of them reaching the length of monographs. They were mostly occasioned by the various publications mentioned above, as can be seen by their dates. F. R. Lücke in <i>Berliner theol. Zeitsch</i>. 1819; J. C. L. Gieseler in <i>Hall. lit. Zeit</i>. 1823; and in <i>Studien u. Kritiken</i>, 1830; F. C. Baur. <i>ibid</i>., 1837; H. T. Cheever in <i>Amer. Bibl. Repos</i>. 1840; R. Baxmann, <i>Deutsche Ztst</i>. 1861 [translated <i>Amer. Theol. Rev</i>. 1862]; Hilgenfeld in <i>Ztst. f. wiss. theol</i>. Bd. xiii. Articles in encyclopedias often show original research, or present useful summaries: besides the epoch-making one of Lipsius in Ersch and Gruber, reference may be made to the same writer's article "Gnosis" in Schenkel's <i>Bibel-Lexikon</i> (1868); C. P. Wing in McClintock and Strong, vol. iii. 1873 [useful bibliography]; W. L. Alexander in last edition of Kitto and J. L. Jacobi in Herzog-Plitt.</p>  
 <p> </p>

 <p>Norberg.--<i>Codex Nazareus vel Liber Adami</i>. Leiden, 1815.</p>
