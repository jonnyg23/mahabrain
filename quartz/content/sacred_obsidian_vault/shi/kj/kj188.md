 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_2408"></a><a href="kj188.htm#fr_2413">A1:1</a> There are few various readings of the text of these poems. Where any occur, the translator has been guided by the decisions of Motowori and Moribe. Occasionally these two authorities differ as to the division of the words into lines, and Moribe in particular does not hesitate to propose such emendations as seem to him necessary. The translator has in almost all cases adhered to the traditional text, but gives in foot-notes such emendations as appear worthy of notice. Moribe's division of the lines being in almost every case preferable to Motowori's, it has however here been generally adopted.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2409"></a><a href="kj188.htm#fr_2414">A3:2</a> Motowori reads <i>Magishi    Atane tsuki</i> as two lines.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2410"></a><a href="kj188.htm#fr_2415">A3:3</a> Motowori reads <i>Sagiri    ni Tatamu zo</i> as two lines.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2411"></a><a href="kj188.htm#fr_2416">A4:4</a> Motowori reads <i>Mitani    Futa watarasu</i> as two lines.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2412"></a><a href="kj188.htm#fr_2417">A5:5</a> Motowori reads <i>Uda no Taka-ki ni</i> as two lines.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2413"></a><a href="kj188.htm#fr_2418">A5:6</a> Moribe emends <i>haru</i> to <i>hari</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2414"></a><a href="kj188.htm#fr_2419">A5:7</a> Motowori divides these lines thus: <i>Tachi-soba no mi no     Nakeku we</i>.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2415"></a><a href="kj188.htm#fr_2420">A9:8</a> Motowori reads <i>gomoreru</i> as a line by itself, and similarly <i>uruhashi</i> as a line by itself.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_2416"></a><a href="kj188.htm#fr_2421">A9:9</a> Moribe restores the reading of the first line of this poem to <i>Naduzki-ta no</i>, and both he and Motowori suggest conjectural concluding lines to supplement the evidently incomplete text.
