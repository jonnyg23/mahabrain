 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Explanatory Notes on the Whole Bible</i>, by John Wesley, [1754-65], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#001">Ezekiel Chapter 43</a></h3> 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#002">Ezekiel 43:2</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_002">eze 43:2</a></font></span></p>Came - When the glory departed, it went eastward, and now it returns, it comes from the east. And his voice - Though by the voice of God, thunder is sometimes meant, yet here it was an articulate voice. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#003">Ezekiel 43:3</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_003">eze 43:3</a></font></span></p>And it - This glory of the God of Israel. To destroy - To declare, that their sins would ruin their city, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze009.htm#003">Eze 9:3-4</a>. I fell - Overwhelmed, and as it were swallowed up. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#004">Ezekiel 43:4</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_004">eze 43:4</a></font></span></p>Came - The sins of Israel caused the glory of the Lord to go out of his house, now the repentance of Israel is blest with the return of this glory. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#006">Ezekiel 43:6</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_006">eze 43:6</a></font></span></p>The man - Christ. Stood - To encourage, and strengthen him. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#007">Ezekiel 43:7</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_007">eze 43:7</a></font></span></p>He - The glorious God of Israel. My throne - The throne of his grace is in his temple; in the dispensations of grace, God manifests himself a king. My feet - Speaking after the manner of men, and expressing his abode and rest, in his temple, as the type, in his church, as the antitype. In their high places - Perhaps some kings were buried in the temples of their idols, near the idols they worshipped. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#008">Ezekiel 43:8</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_008">eze 43:8</a></font></span></p>Their threshold - The kings of Judah and Israel, built temples and altars for their idols, and these are called their thresholds. They erected these in the courts, or near the courts of the temple. Abominations - Idolatries, and wickednesses not to be named. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#009">Ezekiel 43:9</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_009">eze 43:9</a></font></span></p>Far from me - From my temple. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#010">Ezekiel 43:10</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_010">eze 43:10</a></font></span></p>Son of man - Ezekiel, who is called thus above eighty times in this book. Shew - Describe it to them in all the parts. To the house - To the rulers, prophets, and priests especially, not excluding others. Their iniquities - When they shall blush to see what glory their iniquities had ruined. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#012">Ezekiel 43:12</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_012">eze 43:12</a></font></span></p>The law - This is the first comprehensive rule: holiness becomes God's house; and this relative holiness referred to personal and real holiness. The top - The whole circuit of this mountain shall be holy, but the top of it on which the temple stands, shall be most holy. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#013">Ezekiel 43:13</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_013">eze 43:13</a></font></span></p>The altar - Of burnt - offerings. And an hand - breath - The sacred cubit, three inches longer than the common cubit. The bottom - The ledge or settle, fastened to the altar on all sides at the bottom, shall be a cubit in height. The breadth - From the edge of this bench on the outside to the edge where it joined the body of the altar, a cubit, and this the breadth, twenty one inches, broad enough for the priests to walk on. Border - A ledge going round on all the squares. The edge - On the outer edge of this settle a span high. The back - As the back bears burdens, so this was to bear the weight of the whole altar. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#014">Ezekiel 43:14</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_014">eze 43:14</a></font></span></p>From the bottom - From the first ledge, which was a cubit broad, and a cubit high from the ground. To the lower - To the top of that square settle, which is called lower, because another settle is raised upon it. Two cubits - In height. The lesser - From the highest edge of the uppermost settle, down to the cubit broad ledge about the lower settle. The greater - So called, because it exceeded the upper settle a cubit in breadth. Four cubits - ln height. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#015">Ezekiel 43:15</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_015">eze 43:15</a></font></span></p>Four cubits - In height. From the altar - From the top of the altar. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#017">Ezekiel 43:17</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_017">eze 43:17</a></font></span></p>Stairs - Or steps, for such they needed, (probably each stair about one fourth of a cubit,) to carry them, up to the first and second settles. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#019">Ezekiel 43:19</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_019">eze 43:19</a></font></span></p>Give - Direct, or command that it be given. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#020">Ezekiel 43:20</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_020">eze 43:20</a></font></span></p>Shalt take - Appoint it to be taken. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#021">Ezekiel 43:21</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_021">eze 43:21</a></font></span></p>He - The priest. In the appointed place - That is, in the court of the house, and on the altar appointed; this is the first day's sacrifice. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#022">Ezekiel 43:22</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_022">eze 43:22</a></font></span></p>They - The priests in attendance. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#023">Ezekiel 43:23</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_023">eze 43:23</a></font></span></p>Shalt offer - On the third day, and so on, through seven days. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#024">Ezekiel 43:24</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_024">eze 43:24</a></font></span></p>Shalt offer - Direct them to offer. Salt - It may allude to the perpetuity of the covenant thus made by sacrifice. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#026">Ezekiel 43:26</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_026">eze 43:26</a></font></span></p>They - The priests in course. 
 <p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/eze043.htm#027">Ezekiel 43:27</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_027">eze 43:27</a></font></span></p>I will accept you - Those that give themselves to God, shall be accepted of God, their persons first, and then their performances, through the mediator.
