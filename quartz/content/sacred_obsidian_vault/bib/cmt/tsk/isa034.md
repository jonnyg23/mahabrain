 </p><p align="center"><a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa034.htm#001">Isaiah 34:1</a></p> 
 <p><span class="margnote"><font color="GREEN" size="-1"><a name="an_001">isa 34:1</a></font></span></p>
 <p>Come:  This and the following chapter, as Bp. Lowth observes, form one distinct prophecy; an entire, regular, and beautiful poem, consisting of two parts; the first containing a denunciation of Divine vengeance against the enemies of the people or church of God; the second describing the flourishing state of that church consequent upon those judgments.  The event foretold is represented as of the highest importance, and of universal concern; all nations are called upon to attend to the declaration of it; and the wrath of God is denounced against all the nations who had provoked to anger the Defender of the cause of Zion.  By a figure frequently occurring in the prophetical writings, the cities and people mentioned here, who were remarkably distinguished as the enemies of the people of God, are put for those enemies in general. <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa018.htm#003">Isa 18:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa033.htm#013">Isa 33:13</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa041.htm#001">Isa 41:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa043.htm#009">Isa 43:9</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/isa049.htm#001">Isa 49:1</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#003">Jdg 5:3</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/jdg005.htm#031">Jdg 5:31</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa049.htm#001">Psa 49:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa049.htm#002">Psa 49:2</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa050.htm#001">Psa 50:1</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/psa096.htm#010">Psa 96:10</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar016.htm#015">Mar 16:15</a>, <a href="../../../bib/kjv/mar016.htm#016">Mar 16:16</a>; <a href="../../../bib/kjv/rev002.htm#007">Rev 2:7</a>
