 <td valign="top">
 <p align="center"><a href="../kjv/pro026.htm">KJV</a></p><p>1  As snow<sup><a href="h7950.htm">7950</a></sup> in summer,<sup><a href="h7019.htm">7019</a></sup> and as rain<sup><a href="h4306.htm">4306</a></sup> in harvest,<sup><a href="h7105.htm">7105</a></sup> so<sup><a href="h3651.htm">3651</a></sup> honor<sup><a href="h3519.htm">3519</a></sup> is not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> seemly<sup><a href="h5000.htm">5000</a></sup> for a fool.<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> </p>
 <p>2  As the bird<sup><a href="h6833.htm">6833</a></sup> by wandering,<sup><a href="h5110.htm">5110</a></sup> as the swallow<sup><a href="h1866.htm">1866</a></sup> by flying,<sup><a href="h5774.htm">5774</a></sup> so<sup><a href="h3651.htm">3651</a></sup> the curse<sup><a href="h7045.htm">7045</a></sup> causeless<sup><a href="h2600.htm">2600</a></sup> shall not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> come.<sup><a href="h0935.htm">935</a></sup> </p>
 <p>3  A whip<sup><a href="h7752.htm">7752</a></sup> for the horse,<sup><a href="h5483.htm">5483</a></sup> a bridle<sup><a href="h4964.htm">4964</a></sup> for the ass,<sup><a href="h2543.htm">2543</a></sup> and a rod<sup><a href="h7626.htm">7626</a></sup> for the fool's<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> back.<sup><a href="h1460.htm">1460</a></sup> </p>
 <p>4  Answer<sup><a href="h6030.htm">6030</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> a fool<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> according to his folly,<sup><a href="h0200.htm">200</a></sup> lest<sup><a href="h6435.htm">6435</a></sup> thou<sup><a href="h0859.htm">859</a></sup> also<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> be like<sup><a href="h7737.htm">7737</a></sup> unto him. </p>
 <p>5  Answer<sup><a href="h6030.htm">6030</a></sup> a fool<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> according to his folly,<sup><a href="h0200.htm">200</a></sup> lest<sup><a href="h6435.htm">6435</a></sup> he be<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> wise<sup><a href="h2450.htm">2450</a></sup> in his own conceit.<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> </p>
 <p>6  He that sendeth<sup><a href="h7971.htm">7971</a></sup> a message<sup><a href="h1697.htm">1697</a></sup> by the hand<sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> of a fool<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> cutteth off<sup><a href="h7096.htm">7096</a></sup> the feet,<sup><a href="h7272.htm">7272</a></sup> and drinketh<sup><a href="h8354.htm">8354</a></sup> damage.<sup><a href="h2555.htm">2555</a></sup> </p>
 <p>7  The legs<sup><a href="h7785.htm">7785</a></sup> of the lame<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h6455.htm">6455</a></sup> are not equal:<sup><a href="h1809.htm">1809</a></sup> so is a parable<sup><a href="h4912.htm">4912</a></sup> in the mouth<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> of fools.<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> </p>
 <p>8  As he that bindeth<sup><a href="h6887.htm">6887</a></sup> a stone<sup><a href="h0068.htm">68</a></sup> in a sling,<sup><a href="h4773.htm">4773</a></sup> so<sup><a href="h3651.htm">3651</a></sup> is he that giveth<sup><a href="h5414.htm">5414</a></sup> honor<sup><a href="h3519.htm">3519</a></sup> to a fool.<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> </p>
 <p>9  As a thorn<sup><a href="h2336.htm">2336</a></sup> goeth up<sup><a href="h5927.htm">5927</a></sup> into the hand<sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> of a drunkard,<sup><a href="h7910.htm">7910</a></sup> so is a parable<sup><a href="h4912.htm">4912</a></sup> in the mouth<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> of fools.<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> </p>
 <p>10  The great<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> God that formed<sup><a href="h2342.htm">2342</a></sup> all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> things both rewardeth<sup><a href="h7936.htm">7936</a></sup> the fool,<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> and rewardeth<sup><a href="h7936.htm">7936</a></sup> transgressors.<sup><a href="h5674.htm">5674</a></sup> </p>
 <p>11  As a dog<sup><a href="h3611.htm">3611</a></sup> returneth<sup><a href="h7725.htm">7725</a></sup> to<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> his vomit,<sup><a href="h6892.htm">6892</a></sup> so a fool<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> returneth<sup><a href="h8138.htm">8138</a></sup> to his folly.<sup><a href="h0200.htm">200</a></sup> </p>
 <p>12  Seest<sup><a href="h7200.htm">7200</a></sup> thou a man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> wise<sup><a href="h2450.htm">2450</a></sup> in his own conceit?<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> there is more hope<sup><a href="h8615.htm">8615</a></sup> of a fool<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> than of<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> him. </p>
 <p>13  The slothful<sup><a href="h6102.htm">6102</a></sup> man saith,<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> There is a lion<sup><a href="h7826.htm">7826</a></sup> in the way;<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> a lion<sup><a href="h0738.htm">738</a></sup> is in<sup><a href="h0996.htm">996</a></sup> the streets.<sup><a href="h7339.htm">7339</a></sup> </p>
 <p>14  As the door<sup><a href="h1817.htm">1817</a></sup> turneth<sup><a href="h5437.htm">5437</a></sup> upon<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> his hinges,<sup><a href="h6735.htm">6735</a></sup> so doth the slothful<sup><a href="h6102.htm">6102</a></sup> upon<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> his bed.<sup><a href="h4296.htm">4296</a></sup> </p>
 <p>15  The slothful<sup><a href="h6102.htm">6102</a></sup> hideth<sup><a href="h2934.htm">2934</a></sup> his hand<sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> in his bosom;<sup><a href="h6747.htm">6747</a></sup> it grieveth<sup><a href="h3811.htm">3811</a></sup> him to bring it again<sup><a href="h7725.htm">7725</a></sup> to<sup><a href="h0413.htm">413</a></sup> his mouth.<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> </p>
 <p>16  The sluggard<sup><a href="h6102.htm">6102</a></sup> is wiser<sup><a href="h2450.htm">2450</a></sup> in his own conceit<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> than seven<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h7651.htm">7651</a></sup> men that can render<sup><a href="h7725.htm">7725</a></sup> a reason.<sup><a href="h2940.htm">2940</a></sup> </p>
 <p>17  He that passeth by,<sup><a href="h5674.htm">5674</a></sup> and meddleth<sup><a href="h5674.htm">5674</a></sup> with<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> strife<sup><a href="h7379.htm">7379</a></sup> belonging not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> to him, is like one that taketh<sup><a href="h2388.htm">2388</a></sup> a dog<sup><a href="h3611.htm">3611</a></sup> by the ears.<sup><a href="h0241.htm">241</a></sup> </p>
 <p>18  As a mad<sup><a href="h3856.htm">3856</a></sup> man who casteth<sup><a href="h3384.htm">3384</a></sup> firebrands,<sup><a href="h2131.htm">2131</a></sup> arrows,<sup><a href="h2671.htm">2671</a></sup> and death,<sup><a href="h4194.htm">4194</a></sup> </p>
 <p>19  So<sup><a href="h3651.htm">3651</a></sup> is the man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> that deceiveth<sup><a href="h7411.htm">7411</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> his neighbor,<sup><a href="h7453.htm">7453</a></sup> and saith,<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> Am not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> I<sup><a href="h0589.htm">589</a></sup> in sport?<sup><a href="h7832.htm">7832</a></sup> </p>
 <p>20  Where no<sup><a href="h0657.htm">657</a></sup> wood<sup><a href="h6086.htm">6086</a></sup> is, there the fire<sup><a href="h0784.htm">784</a></sup> goeth out:<sup><a href="h3518.htm">3518</a></sup> so where there is no<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> talebearer,<sup><a href="h5372.htm">5372</a></sup> the strife<sup><a href="h4066.htm">4066</a></sup> ceaseth.<sup><a href="h8367.htm">8367</a></sup> </p>
 <p>21  As coals<sup><a href="h6352.htm">6352</a></sup> are to burning coals,<sup><a href="h1513.htm">1513</a></sup> and wood<sup><a href="h6086.htm">6086</a></sup> to fire;<sup><a href="h0784.htm">784</a></sup> so is a contentious<sup><a href="h4079.htm">4079</a></sup> man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> to kindle<sup><a href="h2787.htm">2787</a></sup> strife.<sup><a href="h7379.htm">7379</a></sup> </p>
 <p>22  The words<sup><a href="h1697.htm">1697</a></sup> of a talebearer<sup><a href="h5372.htm">5372</a></sup> are as wounds,<sup><a href="h3859.htm">3859</a></sup> and they<sup><a href="h1992.htm">1992</a></sup> go down<sup><a href="h3381.htm">3381</a></sup> into the innermost parts<sup><a href="h2315.htm">2315</a></sup> of the belly.<sup><a href="h0990.htm">990</a></sup> </p>
 <p>23  Burning<sup><a href="h1814.htm">1814</a></sup> lips<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> and a wicked<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> are like a potsherd<sup><a href="h2789.htm">2789</a></sup> covered<sup><a href="h6823.htm">6823</a></sup> with silver<sup><a href="h3701.htm">3701</a></sup> dross.<sup><a href="h5509.htm">5509</a></sup> </p>
 <p>24  He that hateth<sup><a href="h8130.htm">8130</a></sup> dissembleth<sup><a href="h5234.htm">5234</a></sup> with his lips,<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> and layeth up<sup><a href="h7896.htm">7896</a></sup> deceit<sup><a href="h4820.htm">4820</a></sup> within<sup><a href="h7130.htm">7130</a></sup> him; </p>
 <p>25  When<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> he speaketh<sup><a href="h6963.htm">6963</a></sup> fair,<sup><a href="h2603.htm">2603</a></sup> believe<sup><a href="h0539.htm">539</a></sup> him not:<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> for<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> there are seven<sup><a href="h7651.htm">7651</a></sup> abominations<sup><a href="h8441.htm">8441</a></sup> in his heart.<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> </p>
 <p>26  Whose hatred<sup><a href="h8135.htm">8135</a></sup> is covered<sup><a href="h3680.htm">3680</a></sup> by deceit,<sup><a href="h4860.htm">4860</a></sup> his wickedness<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> shall be showed<sup><a href="h1540.htm">1540</a></sup> before the whole congregation.<sup><a href="h6951.htm">6951</a></sup> </p>
 <p>27  Whoso diggeth<sup><a href="h3738.htm">3738</a></sup> a pit<sup><a href="h7845.htm">7845</a></sup> shall fall<sup><a href="h5307.htm">5307</a></sup> therein: and he that rolleth<sup><a href="h1556.htm">1556</a></sup> a stone,<sup><a href="h0068.htm">68</a></sup> it will return<sup><a href="h7725.htm">7725</a></sup> upon<sup><a href="h0413.htm">413</a></sup> him. </p>
 <p>28  A lying<sup><a href="h8267.htm">8267</a></sup> tongue<sup><a href="h3956.htm">3956</a></sup> hateth<sup><a href="h8130.htm">8130</a></sup> those that are afflicted<sup><a href="h1790.htm">1790</a></sup> by it; and a flattering<sup><a href="h2509.htm">2509</a></sup> mouth<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> worketh<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> ruin.<sup><a href="h4072.htm">4072</a></sup> </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/pro026.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Quomodo nix in æstate, et pluviæ in messe,<br> sic indecens est stulto gloria.<br></p>
 <p>2 Sicut avis ad alia transvolans, et passer quolibet vadens,<br> sic maledictum frustra prolatum in quempiam superveniet.<br></p>
 <p>3 Flagellum equo, et camus asino,<br> et virga in dorso imprudentium.<br></p>
 <p>4 Ne respondeas stulto juxta stultitiam suam,<br> ne efficiaris ei similis.<br></p>
 <p>5 Responde stulto juxta stultitiam suam,<br> ne sibi sapiens esse videatur.<br></p>
 <p>6 Claudus pedibus, et iniquitatem bibens,<br> qui mittit verba per nuntium stultum.<br></p>
 <p>7 Quomodo pulchras frustra habet claudus tibias,<br> sic indecens est in ore stultorum parabola.<br></p>
 <p>8 Sicut qui mittit lapidem in acervum Mercurii,<br> ita qui tribuit insipienti honorem.<br></p>
 <p>9 Quomodo si spina nascatur in manu temulenti,<br> sic parabola in ore stultorum.<br></p>
 <p>10 Judicium determinat causas,<br> et qui imponit stulto silentium iras mitigat.<br></p>
 <p>11 Sicut canis qui revertitur ad vomitum suum,<br> sic imprudens qui iterat stultitiam suam.<br></p>
 <p>12 Vidisti hominem sapientem sibi videri?<br> magis illo spem habebit insipiens.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>13 [Dicit piger: Leo est in via,<br> et leæna in itineribus.<br></p>
 <p>14 Sicut ostium vertitur in cardine suo,<br> ita piger in lectulo suo.<br></p>
 <p>15 Abscondit piger manum sub ascella sua,<br> et laborat si ad os suum eam converterit.<br></p>
 <p>16 Sapientior sibi piger videtur<br> septem viris loquentibus sententias.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>17 [Sicut qui apprehendit auribus canem,<br> sic qui transit impatiens et commiscetur rixæ alterius.<br></p>
 <p>18 Sicut noxius est qui mittit sagittas et lanceas in mortem,<br></p>
 <p>19 ita vir fraudulenter nocet amico suo,<br> et cum fuerit deprehensus dicit: Ludens feci.<br></p>
 <p>20 Cum defecerint ligna extinguetur ignis,<br> et susurrone subtracto, jurgia conquiescent.<br></p>
 <p>21 Sicut carbones ad prunas, et ligna ad ignem,<br> sic homo iracundus suscitat rixas.<br></p>
 <p>22 Verba susurronis quasi simplicia,<br> et ipsa perveniunt ad intima ventris.<br></p>
 <p>23 Quomodo si argento sordido ornare velis vas fictile,<br> sic labia tumentia cum pessimo corde sociata.<br></p>
 <p>24 Labiis suis intelligitur inimicus,<br> cum in corde tractaverit dolos.<br></p>
 <p>25 Quando submiserit vocem suam, ne credideris ei,<br> quoniam septem nequitiæ sunt in corde illius.<br></p>
 <p>26 Qui operit odium fraudulenter,<br> revelabitur malitia ejus in consilio.<br></p>
 <p>27 Qui fodit foveam incidet in eam,<br> et qui volvit lapidem revertetur ad eum.<br></p>
 <p>28 Lingua fallax non amat veritatem,<br> et os lubricum operatur ruinas.]</p>
