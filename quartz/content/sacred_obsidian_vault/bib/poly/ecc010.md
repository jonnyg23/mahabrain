 <p align="center"><a href="../kjv/ecc010.htm">KJV</a></p><p>1  Dead<sup><a href="h4194.htm">4194</a></sup> flies<sup><a href="h2070.htm">2070</a></sup> cause the ointment<sup><a href="h8081.htm">8081</a></sup> of the apothecary<sup><a href="h7543.htm">7543</a></sup> to send forth<sup><a href="h5042.htm">5042</a></sup> a stinking savor:<sup><a href="h0887.htm">887</a></sup> so doth a little<sup><a href="h4592.htm">4592</a></sup> folly<sup><a href="h5531.htm">5531</a></sup> him that is in reputation<sup><a href="h3368.htm">3368</a></sup> for wisdom<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h2451.htm">2451</a></sup> and honor.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3519.htm">3519</a></sup> </p>
 <p>2  A wise man's<sup><a href="h2450.htm">2450</a></sup> heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> is at his right hand;<sup><a href="h3225.htm">3225</a></sup> but a fool's<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> at his left.<sup><a href="h8040.htm">8040</a></sup> </p>
 <p>3  Yea also,<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> when he that is a fool<sup><a href="h7945.htm">7945</a></sup> <sup><a href="h5530.htm">5530</a></sup> walketh<sup><a href="h1980.htm">1980</a></sup> by the way,<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> his wisdom<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> faileth<sup><a href="h2638.htm">2638</a></sup> him, and he saith<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> to every one<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> that he<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> is a fool.<sup><a href="h5530.htm">5530</a></sup> </p>
 <p>4  If<sup><a href="h0518.htm">518</a></sup> the spirit<sup><a href="h7307.htm">7307</a></sup> of the ruler<sup><a href="h4910.htm">4910</a></sup> rise up<sup><a href="h5927.htm">5927</a></sup> against<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> thee, leave<sup><a href="h5117.htm">5117</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> thy place;<sup><a href="h4725.htm">4725</a></sup> for<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> yielding<sup><a href="h4832.htm">4832</a></sup> pacifieth<sup><a href="h5117.htm">5117</a></sup> great<sup><a href="h1419.htm">1419</a></sup> offenses.<sup><a href="h2399.htm">2399</a></sup> </p>
 <p>5  There is<sup><a href="h3426.htm">3426</a></sup> an evil<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> which I have seen<sup><a href="h7200.htm">7200</a></sup> under<sup><a href="h8478.htm">8478</a></sup> the sun,<sup><a href="h8121.htm">8121</a></sup> as an error<sup><a href="h7684.htm">7684</a></sup> which proceedeth<sup><a href="h7945.htm">7945</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3318.htm">3318</a></sup> from<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> the ruler:<sup><a href="h7989.htm">7989</a></sup> </p>
 <p>6  Folly<sup><a href="h5529.htm">5529</a></sup> is set<sup><a href="h5414.htm">5414</a></sup> in great<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> dignity,<sup><a href="h4791.htm">4791</a></sup> and the rich<sup><a href="h6223.htm">6223</a></sup> sit<sup><a href="h3427.htm">3427</a></sup> in low place.<sup><a href="h8216.htm">8216</a></sup> </p>
 <p>7  I have seen<sup><a href="h7200.htm">7200</a></sup> servants<sup><a href="h5650.htm">5650</a></sup> upon<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> horses,<sup><a href="h5483.htm">5483</a></sup> and princes<sup><a href="h8269.htm">8269</a></sup> walking<sup><a href="h1980.htm">1980</a></sup> as servants<sup><a href="h5650.htm">5650</a></sup> upon<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> the earth.<sup><a href="h0776.htm">776</a></sup> </p>
 <p>8  He that diggeth<sup><a href="h2658.htm">2658</a></sup> a pit<sup><a href="h1475.htm">1475</a></sup> shall fall<sup><a href="h5307.htm">5307</a></sup> into it; and whoso breaketh<sup><a href="h6555.htm">6555</a></sup> a hedge,<sup><a href="h1447.htm">1447</a></sup> a serpent<sup><a href="h5175.htm">5175</a></sup> shall bite<sup><a href="h5391.htm">5391</a></sup> him. </p>
 <p>9  Whoso removeth<sup><a href="h5265.htm">5265</a></sup> stones<sup><a href="h0068.htm">68</a></sup> shall be hurt<sup><a href="h6087.htm">6087</a></sup> therewith; and he that cleaveth<sup><a href="h1234.htm">1234</a></sup> wood<sup><a href="h6086.htm">6086</a></sup> shall be endangered<sup><a href="h5533.htm">5533</a></sup> thereby. </p>
 <p>10  If<sup><a href="h0518.htm">518</a></sup> the iron<sup><a href="h1270.htm">1270</a></sup> be blunt,<sup><a href="h6949.htm">6949</a></sup> and he<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> do not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> whet<sup><a href="h7043.htm">7043</a></sup> the edge,<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> then must he put to<sup><a href="h1396.htm">1396</a></sup> more strength:<sup><a href="h2428.htm">2428</a></sup> but wisdom<sup><a href="h2451.htm">2451</a></sup> is profitable<sup><a href="h3504.htm">3504</a></sup> to direct.<sup><a href="h3787.htm">3787</a></sup> </p>
 <p>11  Surely<sup><a href="h0518.htm">518</a></sup> the serpent<sup><a href="h5175.htm">5175</a></sup> will bite<sup><a href="h5391.htm">5391</a></sup> without<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> enchantment;<sup><a href="h3908.htm">3908</a></sup> and a babbler<sup><a href="h1167.htm">1167</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3956.htm">3956</a></sup> is no<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> better.<sup><a href="h3504.htm">3504</a></sup> </p>
 <p>12  The words<sup><a href="h1697.htm">1697</a></sup> of a wise man's<sup><a href="h2450.htm">2450</a></sup> mouth<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> are gracious;<sup><a href="h2580.htm">2580</a></sup> but the lips<sup><a href="h8193.htm">8193</a></sup> of a fool<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> will swallow up<sup><a href="h1104.htm">1104</a></sup> himself. </p>
 <p>13  The beginning<sup><a href="h8462.htm">8462</a></sup> of the words<sup><a href="h1697.htm">1697</a></sup> of his mouth<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> is foolishness:<sup><a href="h5531.htm">5531</a></sup> and the end<sup><a href="h0319.htm">319</a></sup> of his talk<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> is mischievous<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> madness.<sup><a href="h1948.htm">1948</a></sup> </p>
 <p>14  A fool<sup><a href="h5530.htm">5530</a></sup> also is full<sup><a href="h7235.htm">7235</a></sup> of words:<sup><a href="h1697.htm">1697</a></sup> a man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> cannot<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> tell<sup><a href="h3045.htm">3045</a></sup> what shall be;<sup><a href="h4100.htm">4100</a></sup> <sup><a href="h7945.htm">7945</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> and what<sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> shall be<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> after<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0310.htm">310</a></sup> him, who<sup><a href="h4310.htm">4310</a></sup> can tell<sup><a href="h5046.htm">5046</a></sup> him? </p>
 <p>15  The labor<sup><a href="h5999.htm">5999</a></sup> of the foolish<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> wearieth<sup><a href="h3021.htm">3021</a></sup> every one of them, because<sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> he knoweth<sup><a href="h3045.htm">3045</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> how to go<sup><a href="h1980.htm">1980</a></sup> to<sup><a href="h0413.htm">413</a></sup> the city.<sup><a href="h5892.htm">5892</a></sup> </p>
 <p>16  Woe<sup><a href="h0337.htm">337</a></sup> to thee, O land,<sup><a href="h0776.htm">776</a></sup> when thy king<sup><a href="h7945.htm">7945</a></sup> <sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> is a child,<sup><a href="h5288.htm">5288</a></sup> and thy princes<sup><a href="h8269.htm">8269</a></sup> eat<sup><a href="h0398.htm">398</a></sup> in the morning!<sup><a href="h1242.htm">1242</a></sup> </p>
 <p>17  Blessed<sup><a href="h0835.htm">835</a></sup> art thou, O land,<sup><a href="h0776.htm">776</a></sup> when thy king<sup><a href="h7945.htm">7945</a></sup> <sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> is the son<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> of nobles,<sup><a href="h2715.htm">2715</a></sup> and thy princes<sup><a href="h8269.htm">8269</a></sup> eat<sup><a href="h0398.htm">398</a></sup> in due season,<sup><a href="h6256.htm">6256</a></sup> for strength,<sup><a href="h1369.htm">1369</a></sup> and not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> for drunkenness!<sup><a href="h8358.htm">8358</a></sup> </p>
 <p>18  By much slothfulness<sup><a href="h6103.htm">6103</a></sup> the building<sup><a href="h4746.htm">4746</a></sup> decayeth;<sup><a href="h4355.htm">4355</a></sup> and through idleness<sup><a href="h8220.htm">8220</a></sup> of the hands<sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> the house<sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> droppeth through.<sup><a href="h1811.htm">1811</a></sup> </p>
 <p>19  A feast<sup><a href="h3899.htm">3899</a></sup> is made<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> for laughter,<sup><a href="h7814.htm">7814</a></sup> and wine<sup><a href="h3196.htm">3196</a></sup> maketh merry:<sup><a href="h8055.htm">8055</a></sup> <sup><a href="h2416.htm">2416</a></sup> but money<sup><a href="h3701.htm">3701</a></sup> answereth<sup><a href="h6030.htm">6030</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> things. </p>
 <p>20  Curse<sup><a href="h7043.htm">7043</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> the king,<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> no<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> not in thy thought;<sup><a href="h4093.htm">4093</a></sup> and curse<sup><a href="h7043.htm">7043</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> the rich<sup><a href="h6223.htm">6223</a></sup> in thy bedchamber:<sup><a href="h2315.htm">2315</a></sup> <sup><a href="h4904.htm">4904</a></sup> for<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> a bird<sup><a href="h5775.htm">5775</a></sup> of the air<sup><a href="h8064.htm">8064</a></sup> shall carry<sup><a href="h1980.htm">1980</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0853.htm">853</a></sup> the voice,<sup><a href="h6963.htm">6963</a></sup> and that which hath<sup><a href="h1167.htm">1167</a></sup> wings<sup><a href="h3671.htm">3671</a></sup> shall tell<sup><a href="h5046.htm">5046</a></sup> the matter.<sup><a href="h1697.htm">1697</a></sup> </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../sep/ecc010.htm">Sep</a></p><p>1 μυῖαι θανατοῦσαι σαπριοῦσιν σκευασίαν ἐλαίου ἡδύσματος τίμιον ὀλίγον σοφίας ὑπὲρ δόξαν ἀφροσύνης μεγάλης </p>
 <p>2 καρδία σοφοῦ εἰς δεξιὸν αὐτοῦ καὶ καρδία ἄφρονος εἰς ἀριστερὸν αὐτοῦ </p>
 <p>3 καί γε ἐν ὁδῷ ὅταν ἄφρων πορεύηται καρδία αὐτοῦ ὑστερήσει καὶ ἃ λογιεῖται πάντα ἀφροσύνη ἐστίν </p>
 <p>4 ἐὰν πνεῦμα τοῦ ἐξουσιάζοντος ἀναβῇ ἐπὶ σέ τόπον σου μὴ ἀφῇς ὅτι ἴαμα καταπαύσει ἁμαρτίας μεγάλας </p>
 <p>5 ἔστιν πονηρία ἣν εἶδον ὑπὸ τὸν ἥλιον ὡς ἀκούσιον ὃ ἐξῆλθεν ἀπὸ προσώπου τοῦ ἐξουσιάζοντος </p>
 <p>6 ἐδόθη ὁ ἄφρων ἐν ὕψεσι μεγάλοις καὶ πλούσιοι ἐν ταπεινῷ καθήσονται </p>
 <p>7 εἶδον δούλους ἐφ᾽ ἵππους καὶ ἄρχοντας πορευομένους ὡς δούλους ἐπὶ τῆς γῆς </p>
 <p>8 ὁ ὀρύσσων βόθρον ἐν αὐτῷ ἐμπεσεῖται καὶ καθαιροῦντα φραγμόν δήξεται αὐτὸν ὄφις </p>
 <p>9 ἐξαίρων λίθους διαπονηθήσεται ἐν αὐτοῖς σχίζων ξύλα κινδυνεύσει ἐν αὐτοῖς </p>
 <p>10 ἐὰν ἐκπέσῃ τὸ σιδήριον καὶ αὐτὸς πρόσωπον ἐτάραξεν καὶ δυνάμεις δυναμώσει καὶ περισσεία τοῦ ἀνδρείου σοφία </p>
 <p>11 ἐὰν δάκῃ ὁ ὄφις ἐν οὐ ψιθυρισμῷ καὶ οὐκ ἔστιν περισσεία τῷ ἐπᾴδοντι </p>
 <p>12 λόγοι στόματος σοφοῦ χάρις καὶ χείλη ἄφρονος καταποντιοῦσιν αὐτόν </p>
 <p>13 ἀρχὴ λόγων στόματος αὐτοῦ ἀφροσόνη καὶ ἐσχάτη στόματος αὐτοῦ περιφέρεια πονηρά </p>
 <p>14 καὶ ὁ ἄφρων πληθύνει λόγους οὐκ ἔγνω ὁ ἄνθρωπος τί τὸ γενόμενον καὶ τί τὸ ἐσόμενον ὀπίσω αὐτοῦ τίς ἀναγγελεῖ αὐτῷ </p>
 <p>15 μόχθος τῶν ἀφρόνων κοπώσει αὐτούς ὃς οὐκ ἔγνω τοῦ πορευθῆναι εἰς πόλιν </p>
 <p>16 οὐαί σοι πόλις ἧς ὁ βασιλεύς σου νεώτερος καὶ οἱ ἄρχοντές σου ἐν πρωίᾳ ἐσθίουσιν </p>
 <p>17 μακαρία σύ γῆ ἧς ὁ βασιλεύς σου υἱὸς ἐλευθέρων καὶ οἱ ἄρχοντές σου πρὸς καιρὸν φάγονται ἐν δυνάμει καὶ οὐκ αἰσχυνθήσονται </p>
 <p>18 ἐν ὀκνηρίαις ταπεινωθήσεται ἡ δόκωσις καὶ ἐν ἀργίᾳ χειρῶν στάξει ἡ οἰκία </p>
 <p>19 εἰς γέλωτα ποιοῦσιν ἄρτον καὶ οἶνος εὐφραίνει ζῶντας καὶ τοῦ ἀργυρίου ἐπακούσεται σὺν τὰ πάντα </p>
 <p>20 καί γε ἐν συνειδήσει σου βασιλέα μὴ καταράσῃ καὶ ἐν ταμιείοις κοιτώνων σου μὴ καταράσῃ πλούσιον ὅτι πετεινὸν τοῦ οὐρανοῦ ἀποίσει σὺν τὴν φωνήν καὶ ὁ ἔχων τὰς πτέρυγας ἀπαγγελεῖ λόγον </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/ecc010.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Muscæ morientes perdunt suavitatem unguenti.<br> Pretiosior est sapientia et gloria,<br> parva et ad tempus stultitia.<br></p>
 <p>2 Cor sapientis in dextera ejus,<br> et cor stulti in sinistra illius.<br></p>
 <p>3 Sed et in via stultus ambulans,<br> cum ipse insipiens sit,<br> omnes stultos æstimat.<br></p>
 <p>4 Si spiritus potestatem habentis ascenderit super te,<br> locum tuum ne demiseris,<br> quia curatio faciet cessare peccata maxima.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>5 [Est malum quod vidi sub sole,<br> quasi per errorem egrediens a facie principis:<br></p>
 <p>6 positum stultum in dignitate sublimi,<br> et divites sedere deorsum.<br></p>
 <p>7 Vidi servos in equis,<br> et principes ambulantes super terram quasi servos.<br></p>
 <p>8 Qui fodit foveam incidet in eam,<br> et qui dissipat sepem mordebit eum coluber.<br></p>
 <p>9 Qui transfert lapides affligetur in eis,<br> et qui scindit ligna vulnerabitur ab eis.<br></p>
 <p>10 Si retusum fuerit ferrum,<br> et hoc non ut prius, sed hebetatum fuerit,<br> multo labore exacuetur,<br> et post industriam sequetur sapientia.<br></p>
 <p>11 Si mordeat serpens in silentio,<br> nihil eo minus habet qui occulte detrahit.<br></p>
 <p>12 Verba oris sapientis gratia,<br> et labia insipientis præcipitabunt eum;<br></p>
 <p>13 initium verborum ejus stultitia,<br> et novissimum oris illius error pessimus.<br></p>
 <p>14 Stultus verba multiplicat.<br> Ignorat homo quid ante se fuerit;<br> et quid post se futurum sit, quis ei poterit indicare?<br></p>
 <p>15 Labor stultorum affliget eos,<br> qui nesciunt in urbem pergere.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>16 [Væ tibi, terra, cujus rex puer est,<br> et cujus principes mane comedunt.<br></p>
 <p>17 Beata terra cujus rex nobilis est,<br> et cujus principes vescuntur in tempore suo,<br> ad reficiendum, et non ad luxuriam.<br></p>
 <p>18 In pigritiis humiliabitur contignatio,<br> et in infirmitate manuum perstillabit domus.<br></p>
 <p>19 In risum faciunt panem et vinum<br> ut epulentur viventes;<br> et pecuniæ obediunt omnia.<br></p>
 <p>20 In cogitatione tua regi ne detrahas,<br> et in secreto cubiculi tui ne maledixeris diviti:<br> quia et aves cæli portabunt vocem tuam,<br> et qui habet pennas annuntiabit sententiam.]</p>
