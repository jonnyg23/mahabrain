 <p align="center"><a href="../kjv/pro028.htm">KJV</a></p><p>1  The wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> flee<sup><a href="h5127.htm">5127</a></sup> when no<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> man pursueth:<sup><a href="h7291.htm">7291</a></sup> but the righteous<sup><a href="h6662.htm">6662</a></sup> are bold<sup><a href="h0982.htm">982</a></sup> as a lion.<sup><a href="h3715.htm">3715</a></sup> </p>
 <p>2  For the transgression<sup><a href="h6588.htm">6588</a></sup> of a land<sup><a href="h0776.htm">776</a></sup> many<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> are the princes<sup><a href="h8269.htm">8269</a></sup> thereof: but by a man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> of understanding<sup><a href="h0995.htm">995</a></sup> and knowledge<sup><a href="h3045.htm">3045</a></sup> the state<sup><a href="h3651.htm">3651</a></sup> thereof shall be prolonged.<sup><a href="h0748.htm">748</a></sup> </p>
 <p>3  A poor<sup><a href="h7326.htm">7326</a></sup> man<sup><a href="h1397.htm">1397</a></sup> that oppresseth<sup><a href="h6231.htm">6231</a></sup> the poor<sup><a href="h1800.htm">1800</a></sup> is like a sweeping<sup><a href="h5502.htm">5502</a></sup> rain<sup><a href="h4306.htm">4306</a></sup> which leaveth no<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> food.<sup><a href="h3899.htm">3899</a></sup> </p>
 <p>4  They that forsake<sup><a href="h5800.htm">5800</a></sup> the law<sup><a href="h8451.htm">8451</a></sup> praise<sup><a href="h1984.htm">1984</a></sup> the wicked:<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> but such as keep<sup><a href="h8104.htm">8104</a></sup> the law<sup><a href="h8451.htm">8451</a></sup> contend<sup><a href="h1624.htm">1624</a></sup> with them. </p>
 <p>5  Evil<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> men<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> understand<sup><a href="h0995.htm">995</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> judgment:<sup><a href="h4941.htm">4941</a></sup> but they that seek<sup><a href="h1245.htm">1245</a></sup> the LORD<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> understand<sup><a href="h0995.htm">995</a></sup> all<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> things. </p>
 <p>6  Better<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> is the poor<sup><a href="h7326.htm">7326</a></sup> that walketh<sup><a href="h1980.htm">1980</a></sup> in his uprightness,<sup><a href="h8537.htm">8537</a></sup> than he that is perverse<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h6141.htm">6141</a></sup> in his ways,<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> though he<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> be rich.<sup><a href="h6223.htm">6223</a></sup> </p>
 <p>7  Whoso keepeth<sup><a href="h5341.htm">5341</a></sup> the law<sup><a href="h8451.htm">8451</a></sup> is a wise<sup><a href="h0995.htm">995</a></sup> son:<sup><a href="h1121.htm">1121</a></sup> but he that is a companion<sup><a href="h7462.htm">7462</a></sup> of riotous<sup><a href="h2151.htm">2151</a></sup> men shameth<sup><a href="h3637.htm">3637</a></sup> his father.<sup><a href="h0001.htm">1</a></sup> </p>
 <p>8  He that by usury<sup><a href="h5392.htm">5392</a></sup> and unjust gain<sup><a href="h8636.htm">8636</a></sup> increaseth<sup><a href="h7235.htm">7235</a></sup> his substance,<sup><a href="h1952.htm">1952</a></sup> he shall gather<sup><a href="h6908.htm">6908</a></sup> it for him that will pity<sup><a href="h2603.htm">2603</a></sup> the poor.<sup><a href="h1800.htm">1800</a></sup> </p>
 <p>9  He that turneth away<sup><a href="h5493.htm">5493</a></sup> his ear<sup><a href="h0241.htm">241</a></sup> from hearing<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h8085.htm">8085</a></sup> the law,<sup><a href="h8451.htm">8451</a></sup> even<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> his prayer<sup><a href="h8605.htm">8605</a></sup> shall be abomination.<sup><a href="h8441.htm">8441</a></sup> </p>
 <p>10  Whoso causeth the righteous<sup><a href="h3477.htm">3477</a></sup> to go astray<sup><a href="h7686.htm">7686</a></sup> in an evil<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> way,<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> he shall fall<sup><a href="h5307.htm">5307</a></sup> himself<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> into his own pit:<sup><a href="h7816.htm">7816</a></sup> but the upright<sup><a href="h8549.htm">8549</a></sup> shall have good<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> things in possession.<sup><a href="h5157.htm">5157</a></sup> </p>
 <p>11  The rich<sup><a href="h6223.htm">6223</a></sup> man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> is wise<sup><a href="h2450.htm">2450</a></sup> in his own conceit;<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> but the poor<sup><a href="h1800.htm">1800</a></sup> that hath understanding<sup><a href="h0995.htm">995</a></sup> searcheth him out.<sup><a href="h2713.htm">2713</a></sup> </p>
 <p>12  When righteous<sup><a href="h6662.htm">6662</a></sup> men do rejoice,<sup><a href="h5970.htm">5970</a></sup> there is great<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> glory:<sup><a href="h8597.htm">8597</a></sup> but when the wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> rise,<sup><a href="h6965.htm">6965</a></sup> a man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> is hidden.<sup><a href="h2664.htm">2664</a></sup> </p>
 <p>13  He that covereth<sup><a href="h3680.htm">3680</a></sup> his sins<sup><a href="h6588.htm">6588</a></sup> shall not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> prosper:<sup><a href="h6743.htm">6743</a></sup> but whoso confesseth<sup><a href="h3034.htm">3034</a></sup> and forsaketh<sup><a href="h5800.htm">5800</a></sup> them shall have mercy.<sup><a href="h7355.htm">7355</a></sup> </p>
 <p>14  Happy<sup><a href="h0835.htm">835</a></sup> is the man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> that feareth<sup><a href="h6342.htm">6342</a></sup> always:<sup><a href="h8548.htm">8548</a></sup> but he that hardeneth<sup><a href="h7185.htm">7185</a></sup> his heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> shall fall<sup><a href="h5307.htm">5307</a></sup> into mischief.<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> </p>
 <p>15  As a roaring<sup><a href="h5098.htm">5098</a></sup> lion,<sup><a href="h0738.htm">738</a></sup> and a ranging<sup><a href="h8264.htm">8264</a></sup> bear;<sup><a href="h1677.htm">1677</a></sup> so is a wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> ruler<sup><a href="h4910.htm">4910</a></sup> over<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> the poor<sup><a href="h1800.htm">1800</a></sup> people.<sup><a href="h5971.htm">5971</a></sup> </p>
 <p>16  The prince<sup><a href="h5057.htm">5057</a></sup> that wanteth<sup><a href="h2638.htm">2638</a></sup> understanding<sup><a href="h8394.htm">8394</a></sup> is also a great<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> oppressor:<sup><a href="h4642.htm">4642</a></sup> but he that hateth<sup><a href="h8130.htm">8130</a></sup> covetousness<sup><a href="h1215.htm">1215</a></sup> shall prolong<sup><a href="h0748.htm">748</a></sup> his days.<sup><a href="h3117.htm">3117</a></sup> </p>
 <p>17  A man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> that doeth violence<sup><a href="h6231.htm">6231</a></sup> to the blood<sup><a href="h1818.htm">1818</a></sup> of any person<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> shall flee<sup><a href="h5127.htm">5127</a></sup> to<sup><a href="h5704.htm">5704</a></sup> the pit;<sup><a href="h0953.htm">953</a></sup> let no<sup><a href="h0408.htm">408</a></sup> man stay<sup><a href="h8551.htm">8551</a></sup> him. </p>
 <p>18  Whoso walketh<sup><a href="h1980.htm">1980</a></sup> uprightly<sup><a href="h8549.htm">8549</a></sup> shall be saved:<sup><a href="h3467.htm">3467</a></sup> but he that is perverse<sup><a href="h6140.htm">6140</a></sup> in his ways<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> shall fall<sup><a href="h5307.htm">5307</a></sup> at once.<sup><a href="h0259.htm">259</a></sup> </p>
 <p>19  He that tilleth<sup><a href="h5647.htm">5647</a></sup> his land<sup><a href="h0127.htm">127</a></sup> shall have plenty<sup><a href="h7646.htm">7646</a></sup> of bread:<sup><a href="h3899.htm">3899</a></sup> but he that followeth<sup><a href="h7291.htm">7291</a></sup> after vain<sup><a href="h7386.htm">7386</a></sup> persons shall have poverty<sup><a href="h7389.htm">7389</a></sup> enough.<sup><a href="h7646.htm">7646</a></sup> </p>
 <p>20  A faithful<sup><a href="h0530.htm">530</a></sup> man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> shall abound<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> with blessings:<sup><a href="h1293.htm">1293</a></sup> but he that maketh haste<sup><a href="h0213.htm">213</a></sup> to be rich<sup><a href="h6238.htm">6238</a></sup> shall not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> be innocent.<sup><a href="h5352.htm">5352</a></sup> </p>
 <p>21  To have respect<sup><a href="h5234.htm">5234</a></sup> of persons<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> is not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> good:<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> for for<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> a piece<sup><a href="h6595.htm">6595</a></sup> of bread<sup><a href="h3899.htm">3899</a></sup> that man<sup><a href="h1397.htm">1397</a></sup> will transgress.<sup><a href="h6586.htm">6586</a></sup> </p>
 <p>22  He<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> that hasteth<sup><a href="h0926.htm">926</a></sup> to be rich<sup><a href="h1952.htm">1952</a></sup> hath an evil<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> eye,<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> and considereth<sup><a href="h3045.htm">3045</a></sup> not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> that<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> poverty<sup><a href="h2639.htm">2639</a></sup> shall come upon<sup><a href="h0935.htm">935</a></sup> him. </p>
 <p>23  He that rebuketh<sup><a href="h3198.htm">3198</a></sup> a man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> afterwards<sup><a href="h0310.htm">310</a></sup> shall find<sup><a href="h4672.htm">4672</a></sup> more favor<sup><a href="h2580.htm">2580</a></sup> than he that flattereth<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h2505.htm">2505</a></sup> with the tongue.<sup><a href="h3956.htm">3956</a></sup> </p>
 <p>24  Whoso robbeth<sup><a href="h1497.htm">1497</a></sup> his father<sup><a href="h0001.htm">1</a></sup> or his mother,<sup><a href="h0517.htm">517</a></sup> and saith,<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> It is no<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> transgression;<sup><a href="h6588.htm">6588</a></sup> the same<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> is the companion<sup><a href="h2270.htm">2270</a></sup> of a destroyer.<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> <sup><a href="h7843.htm">7843</a></sup> </p>
 <p>25  He that is of a proud<sup><a href="h7342.htm">7342</a></sup> heart<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> stirreth up<sup><a href="h1624.htm">1624</a></sup> strife:<sup><a href="h4066.htm">4066</a></sup> but he that putteth his trust<sup><a href="h0982.htm">982</a></sup> in<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> the LORD<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> shall be made fat.<sup><a href="h1878.htm">1878</a></sup> </p>
 <p>26  He<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> that trusteth<sup><a href="h0982.htm">982</a></sup> in his own heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> is a fool:<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> but whoso walketh<sup><a href="h1980.htm">1980</a></sup> wisely,<sup><a href="h2451.htm">2451</a></sup> he<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> shall be delivered.<sup><a href="h4422.htm">4422</a></sup> </p>
 <p>27  He that giveth<sup><a href="h5414.htm">5414</a></sup> unto the poor<sup><a href="h7326.htm">7326</a></sup> shall not<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> lack:<sup><a href="h4270.htm">4270</a></sup> but he that hideth<sup><a href="h5956.htm">5956</a></sup> his eyes<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> shall have many<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> a curse.<sup><a href="h3994.htm">3994</a></sup> </p>
 <p>28  When the wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> rise,<sup><a href="h6965.htm">6965</a></sup> men<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> hide themselves:<sup><a href="h5641.htm">5641</a></sup> but when they perish,<sup><a href="h0006.htm">6</a></sup> the righteous<sup><a href="h6662.htm">6662</a></sup> increase.<sup><a href="h7235.htm">7235</a></sup> </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/pro028.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Fugit impius nemine persequente;<br> justus autem, quasi leo confidens, absque terrore erit.<br></p>
 <p>2 Propter peccata terræ multi principes ejus;<br> et propter hominis sapientiam, et horum scientiam quæ dicuntur,<br> vita ducis longior erit.<br></p>
 <p>3 Vir pauper calumnians pauperes<br> similis est imbri vehementi in quo paratur fames.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>4 [Qui derelinquunt legem laudant impium;<br> qui custodiunt, succenduntur contra eum.<br></p>
 <p>5 Viri mali non cogitant judicium;<br> qui autem inquirunt Dominum animadvertunt omnia.<br></p>
 <p>6 Melior est pauper ambulans in simplicitate sua<br> quam dives in pravis itineribus.<br></p>
 <p>7 Qui custodit legem filius sapiens est;<br> qui autem comessatores pascit confundit patrem suum.<br></p>
 <p>8 Qui coacervat divitias usuris et fnore,<br> liberali in pauperes congregat eas.<br></p>
 <p>9 Qui declinat aures suas ne audiat legem,<br> oratio ejus erit execrabilis.<br></p>
 <p>10 Qui decipit justos in via mala, in interitu suo corruet,<br> et simplices possidebunt bona ejus.<br></p>
 <p>11 Sapiens sibi videtur vir dives;<br> pauper autem prudens scrutabitur eum.<br></p>
 <p>12 In exsultatione justorum multa gloria est;<br> regnantibus impiis, ruinæ hominum.<br></p>
 <p>13 Qui abscondit scelera sua non dirigetur;<br> qui autem confessus fuerit et reliquerit ea, misericordiam consequetur.<br></p>
 <p>14 Beatus homo qui semper est pavidus;<br> qui vero mentis est duræ corruet in malum.<br></p>
 <p>15 Leo rugiens et ursus esuriens,<br> princeps impius super populum pauperem.<br></p>
 <p>16 Dux indigens prudentia multos opprimet per calumniam;<br> qui autem odit avaritiam, longi fient dies ejus.<br></p>
 <p>17 Hominem qui calumniatur animæ sanguinem,<br> si usque ad lacum fugerit, nemo sustinet.<br></p>
 <p>18 Qui ambulat simpliciter salvus erit;<br> qui perversis graditur viis concidet semel.<br></p>
 <p>19 Qui operatur terram suam satiabitur panibus;<br> qui autem sectatur otium replebitur egestate.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>20 [Vir fidelis multum laudabitur;<br> qui autem festinat ditari non erit innocens.<br></p>
 <p>21 Qui cognoscit in judicio faciem non bene facit;<br> iste et pro buccella panis deserit veritatem.<br></p>
 <p>22 Vir qui festinat ditari, et aliis invidet,<br> ignorat quod egestas superveniet ei.<br></p>
 <p>23 Qui corripit hominem gratiam postea inveniet apud eum,<br> magis quam ille qui per linguæ blandimenta decipit.<br></p>
 <p>24 Qui subtrahit aliquid a patre suo et a matre,<br> et dicit hoc non esse peccatum,<br> particeps homicidæ est.<br></p>
 <p>25 Qui se jactat et dilatat, jurgia concitat;<br> qui vero sperat in Domino sanabitur.<br></p>
 <p>26 Qui confidit in corde suo stultus est;<br> qui autem graditur sapienter, ipse salvabitur.<br></p>
 <p>27 Qui dat pauperi non indigebit;<br> qui despicit deprecantem sustinebit penuriam.<br></p>
 <p>28 Cum surrexerint impii, abscondentur homines;<br> cum illi perierint, multiplicabuntur justi.]</p>
