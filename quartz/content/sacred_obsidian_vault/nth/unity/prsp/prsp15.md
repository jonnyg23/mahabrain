 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/uni_t.jpg"></a><br><i>Prosperity</i>, by Charles Fillmore, [1936], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"> <br> Lesson One<br> Spiritual Substance, The Fundamental Basis Of The Universe<br> </h3>  
 <p>1. What is Divine Mind? </p> 
 <p>2. What is man, and how is he connected with divine ideas? </p> 
 <p>3. What great change in methods of production and distribution seems about to be made? How will it affect our prosperity? </p> 
 <p>4. What is the ether of science and metaphysics? To what extent has man drawn on it, and what are its possibilities? </p> 
 <p>5. What did Jesus demonstrate regarding the kingdom of the ether? </p> 
 <p>6. What is the source of all material, according to science? According to Jesus? </p> 
 <p>7. What is the simplest and surest way to lay hold of substance? </p> 
 <p>8. Explain from this viewpoint how substance can never be depleted. </p> 
 <p>9. Why does God give to just and unjust alike, to all equally? </p> 
 <p>10. How does this truth of the ether help us better to understand the nature of God as pure being or Spirit? </p> 
 <p>11. What is symbolized by gold and silver? Why are they precious? </p> 
 <p>12. What is the threefold activity through which substance must go on its way to becoming manifest as material? </p> 
 <p>13. If substance is omnipresent and man can control its manifestation, why does man suffer from lack and limitation? </p> 
 <p>14. Explain the teaching of Jesus that it is hard for a </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_191"><font size="1" color="green">p. 191</font></a></p> 
 <p>rich man to enter the kingdom of the heavens. </p> 
 <p>15. What is meant by "property rights" and the right to wealth? What error is implied in this doctrine? To whom do ideas belong? </p> 
 <p>16. What are some of the "great possessions" that must be unloaded before we can enter the kingdom of consciousness? </p> 
 <p>17. After recognizing the existence, potentiality, and availability of universal substance, what is the next step in demonstration? </p> 
 <p>18. Can the kingdom be found by one with selfish motives? Why should we desire healing and prosperity? </p> 
 <p>19. What is the prosperity consciousness? Give examples. How can it be cultivated wisely? </p> 
 <p>20. What will be the social and economic results of a widespread prosperity consciousness in the whole race? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Two </p> 
 <p>Spiritual Mind, The Omnipresent Directive Principle Of Prosperity </p> 
 <p>1. Why are ideas the most important things in life? </p> 
 <p>2. What is desire in origin, purpose, and result? </p> 
 <p>3. What is the difference between "is-ness" and "existence"? </p> 
 <p>4. What is the difference between "being" and "appearance"? </p> 
 <p>5. What is the relation of figures to the problem they help solve? How does this illustrate spiritual reality and material phenomena? </p> 
 <p>6. What is implied in the fact that man can conceive of an ideal world? </p> 
 <p>7. Should we deny the existence of material things? Can we do so successfully? What should we deny about the things of the outer? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_192"><font size="1" color="green">p. 192</font></a></p> 
 <p>8. What is the "I AM identity"? How does it differ from Divine Mind? </p> 
 <p>9. Why is spiritual understanding important? How is it gained? </p> 
 <p>10. What connection exists between ideas in Divine Mind? </p> 
 <p>11. What divine idea is back of riches? What ideas are the "parents" of this idea? How can this knowledge help us in demonstrating? </p> 
 <p>12. Are all men equally entitled to wealth? What ideas should accompany the acquiring, using, and spending of wealth? </p> 
 <p>13. Do we expect God to give us actual loaves of bread when we pray the Lord's Prayer? What does He give instead of material things? </p> 
 <p>14. Why do people have dreams? Do dreams help men with their problems? </p> 
 <p>15. What is the value of relaxation and the silence when we seek God's gifts? </p> 
 <p>16. What relation has prosperity demonstration to the kingdom-of-the-heavens consciousness? </p> 
 <p>17. What is the physical, psychological, and spiritual reason for preparing the way for the prosperity demonstration? </p> 
 <p>18. What does the parable of the lilies teach us about substance? </p> 
 <p>19. What effect does the attitude of thanksgiving and praise have upon our prosperity? </p> 
 <p>20. Why is asking in the name of Jesus Christ more effective than any other prayer? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Three </p> 
 <p>Faith In The Invisible Substance, The Key To Demonstration </p> 
 <p>1. What is the starting point in building a prosperity consciousness? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_193"><font size="1" color="green">p. 193</font></a></p> 
 <p>2. What is the relation between faith and substance? </p> 
 <p>3. What does it mean to "have" faith? </p> 
 <p>4. What is a "seeking" faith? For what does it seek? </p> 
 <p>5. Explain how doubt retards manifestation. </p> 
 <p>6. What is the difference between the conception of John the Baptist and that of Jesus? </p> 
 <p>7. How do love and understanding assist faith in its accomplishments? </p> 
 <p>8. Are difficult experiences necessary in life? Why do we have them? </p> 
 <p>9. Show that it is sinful to think and talk hard times, lack, and other limitations. </p> 
 <p>10. Explain the symbology of the five loaves and two fishes. </p> 
 <p>11. How does fear produce a stagnation in financial circulation? How does confidence or faith restore normal conditions? </p> 
 <p>12. How can we go into "the upper room" to wait for the power from on high? </p> 
 <p>13. How does your mind create? Are its creations always real? </p> 
 <p>14. Why should our faith be in Spirit rather than in material things? </p> 
 <p>15. Show how faith is essential to success in the professions, in manufacturing, in sales, and in other lines of activity. </p> 
 <p>16. What do the Bible characters represent to us today? What Bible personage represents faith? </p> 
 <p>17. Is science antagonistic to religion or helpful to its cause? </p> 
 <p>18. What is the relation between material and substance? </p> 
 <p>19. Is there any lack of anything anywhere? What are we to overcome? </p> 
 <p>20. What affirmations help most to banish fear and abide in the consciousness of plenty? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_194"><font size="1" color="green">p. 194</font></a></p> 
 <p>Lesson Four </p> 
 <p>Man, The Inlet And Outlet Of Divine Mind </p> 
 <p>1. What is meant by Principle as applied to prosperity? </p> 
 <p>2. How do we establish a consciousness of Principle as related to us? </p> 
 <p>3. How can the study of Truth make one happier, healthier, more beautiful, more prosperous? </p> 
 <p>4. What is a miracle? Is prosperity miraculous? </p> 
 <p>5. How are the keepers of divine law rewarded, and its breakers punished? </p> 
 <p>6. What are the legislative, judicial, and executive phases of the divine law? </p> 
 <p>7. What is the first rule of the divine law? </p> 
 <p>8. What is the effect of thinking and speaking of everything as good? </p> 
 <p>9. Is there any virtue in poverty? </p> 
 <p>10. What is meant by the "far country," and what is the homeland of the prodigal son? </p> 
 <p>11. What is the psychological and spiritual effect of old clothes? </p> 
 <p>12. What is symbolized by the putting on of new shoes. </p> 
 <p>13. How is true substance wasted, and what is the connection between waste and want? </p> 
 <p>14. What is our best insurance of financial security? </p> 
 <p>15. How does the law "Seek and ye shall find" apply to prosperity? </p> 
 <p>16. Should one who works harder or has more ability receive a greater reward than another? </p> 
 <p>17. What power has love in helping one to demonstrate prosperity? </p> 
 <p>18. How does the subconscious mind help or hinder in demonstration? </p> 
 <p>19. What form does God's answer to prayer take? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_195"><font size="1" color="green">p. 195</font></a></p> 
 <p>How do we know when a prayer is answered? </p> 
 <p>20. Must one be morally worthy to become prosperous? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Five </p> 
 <p>The Law That Governs The Manifestation Of Supply </p> 
 <p>1. What in our consciousness is represented by Moses? By Joshua? By Jesus? </p> 
 <p>2. What is the metaphysical significance of eating? How do we break bread in the four-dimensional world? </p> 
 <p>3. What retards manifestation when we work to attain the consciousness of abundance? </p> 
 <p>4. What do we mean by the "one law"? How may we know it? How keep it? </p> 
 <p>5. Explain how our ability to use wealth wisely to a large degree determines our prosperity. </p> 
 <p>6. Is it necessary to beseech God for prosperity? To ask? To thank? </p> 
 <p>7. How do we look or go "within"? </p> 
 <p>8. Compare the sense mind with the spiritual mind and show how true prosperity depends on the latter. </p> 
 <p>9. What is the light theory of matter formation, and how does it agree with the teaching of the New Testament? </p> 
 <p>10. Where and what is heaven? How is the soul formed? </p> 
 <p>11. How did King Solomon demonstrate great prosperity? </p> 
 <p>12. What is meant by "laying hold of" the substance? </p> 
 <p>13. How did Jesus develop His consciousness of omnipresent substance and what did that consciousness ultimately do for Him? </p> 
 <p>14. What is the true interpretation of "rich man" in the famous parable of the camel and the needle's eye? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_196"><font size="1" color="green">p. 196</font></a></p> 
 <p>15. How do we constantly "turn stones into bread," and what results? </p> 
 <p>16. What does the parable of the talents teach us regarding prosperity? </p> 
 <p>17. What six steps necessary to manifestation may be discerned in the story of creation? </p> 
 <p>18. What do we contribute to the world by raising our own consciousness to the prosperity level? </p> 
 <p>19. Analyze and explain the statement "I trust Thy universal law of prosperity in all my affairs." </p> 
 <p>Lesson Six </p> 
 <p>Wealth Of Mind Expresses Itself In Riches </p> 
 <p>1. What is prosperity? </p> 
 <p>2. Explain the prosperity law that Jesus gave. </p> 
 <p>3. What relation has a prosperity consciousness to wealth in the outer? </p> 
 <p>4. What is the "sin of riches"? </p> 
 <p>5. What causes crop failure and famine in some countries? </p> 
 <p>6. Why did Jesus carry no money and own no property? </p> 
 <p>7. What is the only thing that can satisfy human longing, and where is it found? </p> 
 <p>8. Is the law of prosperity limited to thought? What else is needful? </p> 
 <p>9. Over what is man given dominion by his Creator? </p> 
 <p>10. How can man master his fear of financial lack? </p> 
 <p>11. Why are prosperity prayers sometimes unanswered? </p> 
 <p>12. What is the true idea of God, and how does man give it form? </p> 
 <p>13. What causes "depressions" in the affairs of men and nations? </p> 
 <p>14. What part does self-control in the matter of sensation </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_197"><font size="1" color="green">p. 197</font></a></p> 
 <p>play in prosperity demonstration? </p> 
 <p>15. What is the relative importance of denial and affirmation in the demonstration of prosperity? </p> 
 <p>16. Who are the real producers of wealth in the nation? </p> 
 <p>17. What is the law of increase as applied to Mind substance? </p> 
 <p>18. How should we prepare for an increased prosperity? </p> 
 <p>19. Should we be specific and definite in our prayers for increase? </p> 
 <p>20. Write a prosperity affirmation of your own embodying the four essential steps of recognition, love, faith, and praise. </p> 
 <p>Lesson Seven </p> 
 <p>God Has Provided Prosperity For Every Home </p> 
 <p>1. Of what great spiritual power is the home the symbol? </p> 
 <p>2. What has the "atmosphere" of a home to do with its prosperity? How may an atmosphere of worry and fear be changed? </p> 
 <p>3. Explain the importance of speaking true words in the home. </p> 
 <p>4. Aside from the feeling of religious duty, why should we be thankful for what we have and express our thanks often? </p> 
 <p>5. Is it good policy to condemn the furnishings in the home or to be apologetic about them? </p> 
 <p>6. Should our homes be ostentatious and rich looking to attract prosperity? </p> 
 <p>7. Why should we be individual in furnishing the home rather than following the "accepted" or "in-the-mode" style only? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_198"><font size="1" color="green">p. 198</font></a></p> 
 <p>8. How will a deep and sincere love for God attract prosperity? </p> 
 <p>9. Why must there be love and understanding between members of the family to insure a prosperous home? </p> 
 <p>10. Explain the law of "Love thy neighbor" as applied to home prosperity. </p> 
 <p>11. What is God's will for the home, and how does the home express it? </p> 
 <p>12. Explain how trying to live and do as others live and do may hold back our prosperity demonstration. </p> 
 <p>13. How can we use our will to help the demonstration of home prosperity? </p> 
 <p>14. Why should the individual express his own ideas in order to demonstrate? </p> 
 <p>15. Where and how is the prosperity demonstration started? </p> 
 <p>16. Do we have any personal claim on God's substance? </p> 
 <p>17. How do we "pour" substance into the "empty" places of the home? </p> 
 <p>18. Why is it necessary to have determination in order to demonstrate? </p> 
 <p>19. Does the possession of material things give satisfaction? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Eight </p> 
 <p>God Will Pay Your Debts </p> 
 <p>1. What law of mind is observed in true forgiveness? </p> 
 <p>2. Why should we trust rather than distrust people? </p> 
 <p>3. Is there any such thing as debt in Truth? Why? </p> 
 <p>4. Where must we start in forgiving our debtors and creditors? </p> 
 <p>5. How can we forgive ourselves for holding others in our debt? </p> 
 <p>6. What is the only sure way of getting out and staying out of debt? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_199"><font size="1" color="green">p. 199</font></a></p> 
 <p>7. Explain forgiveness as a good method of bill collecting. </p> 
 <p>8. How does God forgive our debts? How does His love pay our debts? </p> 
 <p>9. How do debt and worry about debt affect health? What is the remedy? </p> 
 <p>10. Does God have a place in modern business? </p> 
 <p>11. What are the merits and the dangers of installment buying? </p> 
 <p>12. What is the importance of paying all obligations promptly? </p> 
 <p>13. What kind of thoughts should one hold toward creditors? Debtors? </p> 
 <p>14. What dominant belief has caused world depression, and how must it be overcome? What is our part in its overcoming? </p> 
 <p>15. Is the credit system responsible for widespread debt? </p> 
 <p>16. Does our faith in supply justify us in assuming obligations and trusting that we shall be able to pay when the time comes? </p> 
 <p>17. What is the value of prayer in gaining freedom from debt? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Nine </p> 
 <p>Tithing, The Road To Prosperity </p> 
 <p>1. What is a "tithe," and how was tithing started? </p> 
 <p>2. What benefits accrue to the tither, according to the promises of the Bible? </p> 
 <p>3. Should one regard one's tithe as an investment that pays rewards? </p> 
 <p>4. In what way is giving a divine grace? </p> 
 <p>5. What was the practical plan that Paul suggested to the Corinthians? </p> 
 <p>6. What effect does a willing and cheerful spirit have </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_200"><font size="1" color="green">p. 200</font></a></p> 
 <p>on the giver, the gift, and the receiver? </p> 
 <p>7. How can faith be exercised in giving? </p> 
 <p>8. How should wisdom be employed in giving? </p> 
 <p>9. How can one who is puzzled about giving--as regards how much, when, and where--be helped by the decision to tithe? </p> 
 <p>10. What should tithing mean to the farmer? Businessman? Professional man? Mechanic? Laborer? </p> 
 <p>11. How does tithing help fulfill "the first and greatest commandment" about loving God and the neighbor? </p> 
 <p>12. Aside from Bible promises, do we have direct evidence that tithing increases prosperity? Cite instances. </p> 
 <p>13. Should the tithe have a definite place in the personal or family budget? Should we keep a record of our giving, as we do of other disbursements? </p> 
 <p>14. Why is the regular tithe, though it may be small, better than the occasional giving of a larger gift in a lump sum? </p> 
 <p>15. What is the psychological basis and effect of tithing? </p> 
 <p>16. What attitude should one assume toward a seemingly delayed demonstration? </p> 
 <p>17. Should we look for our good to come through the channel of those to whom we give or serve? </p> 
 <p>18. Why is it better to give without thought or expectation of return? </p> 
 <p>19. What must we do about receiving what God has and desires for us? </p> 
 <p>20. Discuss giving as a form of affirmation. </p> 
 <p>Lesson Ten </p> 
 <p>Right Giving, The Key To Abundant Receiving </p> 
 <p>1. In what ways is the religion of Jesus applicable to the problems of daily living? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_201"><font size="1" color="green">p. 201</font></a></p> 
 <p>2. State briefly the law of giving and receiving that Jesus taught. </p> 
 <p>3. Why has the teaching of Jesus not been more effective in changing conditions in the world and in individual life? </p> 
 <p>4. Why is economic reform so much needed at the present time? </p> 
 <p>5. Can any effective reform be based on the material phase of the economic problem? Why? </p> 
 <p>6. Why do men who direct finance and business fail to seek any advice or assistance from the church? </p> 
 <p>7. Why is individual reform necessary before national or world changes can be made? </p> 
 <p>8. What do metaphysical teaching and study contribute toward world betterment? </p> 
 <p>9. How does the desire for the accumulation of money and goods affect the finer nature and sensibilities of people? </p> 
 <p>10. Does avarice or greed have any effect on the health of men? </p> 
 <p>11. What is the chief cause of stagnation in money circulation and its attendant evils? </p> 
 <p>12. What rule did Jesus give us for freeing ourselves from financial lack? </p> 
 <p>13. Is the method practiced by the early Christians practicable in the world as it is today? </p> 
 <p>14. What substitute is now being advocated for the commercial standard of payments for goods and service? </p> 
 <p>15. What is the divine law of equilibrium? Why does it not seem to operate in financial matters? </p> 
 <p>16. Is there any direct connection between poverty and ill-health? How may this problem be approached? Is there a problem at the other extreme--great wealth? How may it be solved? </p> 
 <p>17. What is meant by the "race consciousness"? Can we escape its effects? How can we help to change it for the better? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_202"><font size="1" color="green">p. 202</font></a></p> 
 <p>18. What should we do about saving money for the future? </p> 
 <p>19. What attitude should we take toward charity? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Eleven </p> 
 <p>Laying Up Treasures </p> 
 <p>1. What is the law of conservation as applied to spiritual substance? </p> 
 <p>2. What is the difference between hoarding and conserving? </p> 
 <p>3. Is accumulation of substance necessary to demonstration? </p> 
 <p>4. How is spiritual substance accumulated? How dissipated? </p> 
 <p>5. What is the true objective of man's life? </p> 
 <p>6. Explain why character development must be a part of our study of the demonstration of prosperity. </p> 
 <p>7. Is the ambition for wealth commendable or reprehensible? </p> 
 <p>8. Of what is gold the symbol, metaphysically understood? </p> 
 <p>9. What is the deceitfulness of riches? What is money? </p> 
 <p>10. Does great wealth bring happiness? Does extreme poverty make one any better than the rich? What is the truth about riches? </p> 
 <p>11. What is the only true deed or title to possessions? </p> 
 <p>12. What do we want when we ask for "our daily bread"? </p> 
 <p>13. How does the hoarding of money injure society? </p> 
 <p>14. Should we prepare for that "rainy day" by saving part of our money? </p> 
 <p>15. Was Jesus poor? Was He ever in want? What does it mean to turn stones into bread? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_203"><font size="1" color="green">p. 203</font></a></p> 
 <p>16. Explain the meaning of the rich man and the eye of the needle. </p> 
 <p>17. What are some of the financial and bodily results of the pinching attitude toward money? </p> 
 <p>18. What attitude toward hard times and lack is most helpful to us? </p> 
 <p>19. How shall we learn to get the most and give the most with the means we have at our disposal? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Twelve </p> 
 <p>Overcoming The Thought Of Lack </p> 
 <p>1. Why must we constantly examine our thoughts and separate them? </p> 
 <p>2. By what standard do we judge our thoughts? </p> 
 <p>3. What is the work of the superconscious mind in the body? </p> 
 <p>4. How may we use this superconscious mind in our outer affairs? </p> 
 <p>5. What is the importance of thought elimination or mental clean-up? </p> 
 <p>6. How do we go about this work of eliminating error thoughts? </p> 
 <p>7. What further benefits accrue from the use of denial words? </p> 
 <p>8. Where or what is the "kingdom of the heavens," and what is it like? </p> 
 <p>9. What is meant by "loosing in heaven"? What should we loose and what should we guard against loosing? </p> 
 <p>10. Where and what is "substance"? How do we contact it? </p> 
 <p>11. What is the result of clinging to past ideas and methods? </p> 
 <p>12. What is mortality? Do we escape it by dying? How otherwise? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_204"><font size="1" color="green">p. 204</font></a></p> 
 <p>13. Explain the new birth and the relation of denial to it. </p> 
 <p>14. What do Bible characters mean to us? What can they do for us? </p> 
 <p>15. What do David and Goliath stand for in consciousness? </p> 
 <p>16. What is the modern "golden calf" that most men worship? </p> 
 <p>17. Name some of the evil results of the error of money worship. </p> 
 <p>18. Would the doing away with money entirely solve the problem? What is the solution? </p> 
 <p>19. What "little but mighty" idea in consciousness is symbolized by David? </p> 
 <p>20. What are the weapons of this David that slay the giant fear? </p>
