 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/uni_t.jpg"></a><br><i>Mysteries of John</i>, by Charles Fillmore, [1946], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"> <br> Question Helps for students of Mysteries of John<br> </h3>  
 <p>

 <p>Chapter 1 </p> 
 <p>1. What is the original creative word or logos? </p> 
 <p>2. Explain why the Word and the divine creative process are identical. </p> 
 <p>3. What is the metaphysical significance of the Book of John? </p> 
 <p>4. Explain the parallel between Genesis and John in relation to the ideal man. </p> 
 <p>5. What does John the Baptist represent? </p> 
 <p>6. What is the nature of the rock upon which Jesus built? </p> 
 <p>7. What is the true light? </p> 
 <p>8. How do we become children of God? </p> 
 <p>9. When was the "Christ or Word" incorporated into man's being? </p> 
 <p>10. Why are receptivity and belief essential? </p> 
 <p>11. How does the Word become flesh? </p> 
 <p>12. What is meant by "The law was given through Moses"? </p> 
 <p>13. Explain the statement "Grace and truth came through Jesus Christ." </p> 
 <p>14. Compare discernment with intellectual perception. </p> 
 <p>15. Explain the office of "baptism through denial, plus forgiveness" in the regenerative process. </p> 
 <p>16. Why is the recognition of oneself as the "Son of God" of primary importance? </p> 
 <p>17. What does baptism by John the Baptist symbolize? </p> 
 <p>18. What is the nature of the Father-Mind? </p> 
 <p>19. What is the function of the Holy Spirit? </p> 
 <p>20. How is the spiritual mind cultivated? </p> 
 <p>21. What mental attitudes are necessary to "put on Christ"? </p> 
 <p>22. What is the result of conscious recognition of the Christ Mind? </p> 
 <p>23. What quality does Andrew represent? Why is it important? </p> 
 <p>24. What is the metaphysical significance of Philip, Andrew, and Peter's being of the city of Bethsaida? </p> 
 <p>25. Why is it wise to develop the faculty of imagination </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_186"><font size="1" color="green">p. 186</font></a></p> 
 <p>under the direction of Spirit? </p> 
 <p>26. What does Bartholomew represent? </p> 
 <p>27. What is "consciousness"? "Form"? </p> 
 <p>28. What spiritual faculty is necessary for penetrating into the "fourth dimension." </p> 
 <p>Chapter 2 </p> 
 <p>1. What is the spiritual interpretation of marriage? Of Mary? Of Eve? Of Jesus? Of the disciples? </p> 
 <p>2. What does the name Cana mean? Galilee? Philip? </p> 
 <p>3. What mind activities are involved in the process of regeneration? </p> 
 <p>4. Give the inner meaning of "waterpots." </p> 
 <p>5. Explain "I, if I be lifted up . . . will draw all men unto myself." </p> 
 <p>6. What does the marriage in Cana of Galilee symbolize? Give the symbolism of this miracle in detail. </p> 
 <p>7. What does Capernaum designate? </p> 
 <p>8. What does the Passover symbolize? </p> 
 <p>9. Name the steps taken in body cleansing. Why must we deal with the mind in both the absolute and the relative? </p> 
 <p>10. Symbolically what is "the Christ light"? </p> 
 <p>11. How is a thought atmosphere produced? </p> 
 <p>12. Explain Paul's admonition "Think on these things." </p> 
 <p>13. What is the result of man's mastery of mind? </p> 
 <p>14. When is the word of God fulfilled in man? </p> 
 <p>15. Is Truth self-evident? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 3 </p> 
 <p>I. What does Nicodemus stand for in individual consciousness? </p> 
 <p>2. What is the "new birth"? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_187"><font size="1" color="green">p. 187</font></a></p> 
 <p>3. What are the steps in evolution? </p> 
 <p>4. How is permanent peace to be established? </p> 
 <p>5. How is man restored to the heavenly state? </p> 
 <p>6. What does believing in Jesus do for man? </p> 
 <p>7. How do we become like Jesus Christ? </p> 
 <p>8. How do we experience salvation? </p> 
 <p>9. What is represented by Jesus? By Judea? Metaphysically interpreted, what is the relationship of Jesus and John the Baptist? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 4 </p> 
 <p>1. What does Samaria represent? Sychar? </p> 
 <p>2. Where is "Jacob's well" situated? </p> 
 <p>3. Interpret metaphysically Jesus' resting by Jacob's well. </p> 
 <p>4. How does one worship God truly? </p> 
 <p>5. What is the spiritual significance of Jesus' journey from Judea to Galilee? </p> 
 <p>6. What is the "well of water springing up unto eternal life"? </p> 
 <p>7. Is the subconscious the true source of wisdom? Why do you answer as you do? </p> 
 <p>8. What is the symbology of the Samaritans' being regarded as pretenders by the Israelites? </p> 
 <p>9. From what sources does the soul draw its life? What is the ultimate source? </p> 
 <p>10. What is the explanation of spiritual things on a material basis? </p> 
 <p>11. In what way is Christ the discerner of thoughts? </p> 
 <p>12. Explain metaphysically the relation between Jews and Gentiles. </p> 
 <p>13. Jesus met the Samaritan woman by Jacob's well. Interpret. </p> 
 <p>14. Distinguish between intellectual reasoning and Spiritual perception. </p> 
 <p>15. Differentiate between the outer symbol of worship </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_188"><font size="1" color="green">p. 188</font></a></p> 
 <p>and worship in Spirit. </p> 
 <p>16. Is spiritual substance omnipresent? Explain. </p> 
 <p>17. Why is God no respecter of persons? </p> 
 <p>18. Why is it important for man to apprehend the laws of Spirit? </p> 
 <p>19. Why is absent healing possible? Give an instance of such healing. </p> 
 <p>20. What is the result when we attain oneness with the spiritual forces that move the mind? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 5 </p> 
 <p>1. What does Jerusalem represent? </p> 
 <p>2. Metaphysically interpreted, what is a feast in Jerusalem? </p> 
 <p>3. What do sheep symbolize? </p> 
 <p>4. Interpret spiritually the Pool of Bethesda and its five porches. </p> 
 <p>5. Why is the work of Spirit not confined to physical activities? </p> 
 <p>6. Why does divine life take no cognizance of intellectual laws? </p> 
 <p>7. What is the place in demonstration of the "sabbath of the Lord"? </p> 
 <p>8. What is the living power back of all nature? </p> 
 <p>9. Is the divine creative power continually alive? </p> 
 <p>10. Explain the relationship between the Father and the Son. </p> 
 <p>11. How does one honor the Christ? </p> 
 <p>12. Should one exercise wisdom in handling the life one has? Why? </p> 
 <p>13. What is the process involved in "raising the dead"? </p> 
 <p>14. Explain the relationship between judgment and love. </p> 
 <p>15. Why can one not successfully substitute the </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_189"><font size="1" color="green">p. 189</font></a></p> 
 <p>Scriptures for the living Word? </p> 
 <p>16. How did Jesus create? </p> 
 <p>17. Why is the Bible not the living Word of God? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 6 </p> 
 <p>1. Why is it important to have an understanding of soul anatomy and the mind centers in the body? </p> 
 <p>2. Explain the nature of the I AM. </p> 
 <p>3. What part do the spiritual faculties have in the activity of the I AM? </p> 
 <p>4. How does one learn to live by the "living bread"? </p> 
 <p>5. What is the spiritual significance of Jesus and His disciples' going to the mountain? </p> 
 <p>6. Why did Jesus withdraw "into the mountain himself alone"? What is the significance of this for us today? </p> 
 <p>7. Explain the relationship between faith and power in one's walking "upon the waves of life." </p> 
 <p>8. What is the nature of the true source of substance? </p> 
 <p>9. Explain the interpretation of the Almighty as El Shaddai. </p> 
 <p>10. What process is involved in bringing spiritual substance into manifestation? </p> 
 <p>11. What is meant by the "last day"? </p> 
 <p>12. What is the nature of the body of Christ? </p> 
 <p>13. What is the true inner self of every individual? </p> 
 <p>14. How does one form one's environment, one's heaven or hell? </p> 
 <p>15. How do words become active in the body? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 7 </p> 
 <p>1. Why did Jesus' disciples want Him to go up to Jerusalem? </p> 
 <p>2. What human limitation of His ability to demonstrate did Jesus experience? </p> 
 <p>3. Explain the difference between the Pharisaical </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_190"><font size="1" color="green">p. 190</font></a></p> 
 <p>mind and the spiritual mind. </p> 
 <p>4. What effect does the outpouring of the Holy Spirit have within the soul? </p> 
 <p>5. What is the procedure for handling a mixed state of consciousness? </p> 
 <p>6. What is symbolized by the chief priests and officers? </p> 
 <p>7. What does Nicodemus' spiritual conversion prove? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 8 </p> 
 <p>1. What is represented by Jesus' going up to the Mount of Olives? </p> 
 <p>2. What bearing does the story of the woman taken in adultery have on our own unfoldment? </p> 
 <p>3. Explain the Scripture "I am the light of the world: he that followeth me shall not walk in the darkness, but shall have the light of life." </p> 
 <p>4. How does man hinder his soul unfoldment? How can he accelerate it? </p> 
 <p>5. Why must man abide in the Truth? </p> 
 <p>6. How does man gain freedom? What part does concentration play in the quest of freedom? </p> 
 <p>7. What is the result of ancestor worship? </p> 
 <p>8. How does man bind himself to race beliefs regarding ancestry? How can he free himself? </p> 
 <p>9. What is a liar and the "father" of all lies? </p> 
 <p>10. Explain Jesus' words "Before Abraham was born, I am." </p> 
 <p>11. Interpret the three parts in the Trinity? What is the "word"? </p> 
 <p>12. Why were Jesus' words so powerful? </p> 
 <p>13. Can man overcome death? Does this include death of the body? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 9 </p> 
 <p>1. What is the general theme of John 9? </p> 
 <p>2. Distinguish between the tents and tabernacles in </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_191"><font size="1" color="green">p. 191</font></a></p> 
 <p>which the Israelites lived and Solomon's Temple. </p> 
 <p>3. Explain why the sin of omission is greater than the sin of commission? </p> 
 <p>4. What is man's real work? </p> 
 <p>5. What is the significance of the clay placed upon the blind man's eyes? Of its being washed away? </p> 
 <p>6. Explain the reaction that sometimes follows the activity of the "Jews" and "Pharisees" in consciousness? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 10 </p> 
 <p>1. What are the sheep mentioned in John 10? The "thief and a robber"? The porter? The "good shepherd"? </p> 
 <p>2. What part does man's own volition play in his reformation? </p> 
 <p>3. What is the result when the higher and lower forces are "married"? </p> 
 <p>4. What is the legitimate "door" through which spiritual energy enters man's consciousness? </p> 
 <p>5. What does Solomon's Porch symbolize? </p> 
 <p>6. How can a person be sure he is listening to the "still small voice"? </p> 
 <p>7. Metaphysically interpreted, what is the significance of Jesus' activity near the Jordan? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 11 </p> 
 <p>1. What does the name Lazarus mean? What does Lazarus represent? </p> 
 <p>2. What happens when man fails to recognize God as the origin and support of his life? What is the remedy for such a condition? </p> 
 <p>3. Describe one of the first steps in body restoration. </p> 
 <p>4. In what way does affirming life for another help one to overcome death? </p> 
 <p>5. Why do people grow old? </p> 
 <p>6. How is the "stone" rolled away? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_192"><font size="1" color="green">p. 192</font></a></p> 
 <p>7. Why is it important to give thanks for the manifestation even before it appears? </p> 
 <p>8. Explain why Jesus "cried with a loud voice." </p> 
 <p>9. Does the mind always accept Truth without question? </p> 
 <p>10. Explain the symbology of Jesus' withdrawal into Ephraim. </p> 
 <p>11. What does the Feast of the Passover represent? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 12 </p> 
 <p>1. What do we mean by the "fruit" of our thought? </p> 
 <p>2. What is the metaphysical significance of Mary's and Martha's serving Jesus? </p> 
 <p>3. What is symbolized by the anointing of Jesus' feet by Mary? </p> 
 <p>4. How does sense consciousness betray man? </p> 
 <p>5. How does love heal? </p> 
 <p>6. Are "signs" something to be desired for their own sake? </p> 
 <p>7. How is equilibrium restored? </p> 
 <p>8. "We lose our life in the service of the good." Explain. </p> 
 <p>9. What was Jesus' mission on earth? </p> 
 <p>10. Explain why Jesus had no real cause for worry. </p> 
 <p>11. What is represented by the multitude referred to in verse 34? </p> 
 <p>12. What state of consciousness results when the "Pharisaical intellect" rules? </p> 
 <p>13. Where does the preponderance of power in man lie? What is the true glory of man? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 13 </p> 
 <p>1. Can man live unto himself alone? Justify your answer. </p> 
 <p>2. How did sin come into the world? How is it cast out? How did Jesus become the universal Saviour? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_193"><font size="1" color="green">p. 193</font></a></p> 
 <p>3. How can man appropriate His life essence and repay Jesus for His great sacrifice? </p> 
 <p>4. What is soul unfoldment? How did Jesus teach it? </p> 
 <p>5. How does one become truly great? </p> 
 <p>6. Discuss the symbology of Jesus' washing His apostles feet. </p> 
 <p>7. How can one help mankind cleanse their understanding? </p> 
 <p>8. Why did Judas betray Jesus? Why did Jesus permit the betrayal? </p> 
 <p>9. What is true glorification? </p> 
 <p>10. Upon what spiritual quality does the preservation of Christianity depend? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 14 </p> 
 <p>1. Explain the relationship between the Father and the Son. </p> 
 <p>2. Explain "I go to prepare a place for you." Why did Jesus use the word "mansions"? </p> 
 <p>3. How does man find the Father? How does the Father principle operate? </p> 
 <p>4. What faculty does Philip represent? How does this faculty attain its highest expression? </p> 
 <p>5. What is the cause of and remedy for man's failure to demonstrate? </p> 
 <p>6. Why is attention so vital in sustaining our spiritual relation to the Father? </p> 
 <p>7. Where is the Spirit of truth? How is it brought into active touch with our consciousness? </p> 
 <p>8. Explain the Scripture "Ye behold me: because I live, ye shall live also" and "In that day we shall know that I am in my Father, and ye in me, and I in you." </p> 
 <p>9. In what sense is the individual man all that Jesus claimed He was? How does he make it manifest? </p> 
 <p>10. How does Jesus manifest Himself to us today? </p> 
 <p>11. What is the nature and character of the "personality" of the Holy Spirit? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_194"><font size="1" color="green">p. 194</font></a></p> 
 <p>Chapter 15 </p> 
 <p>1. Explain the relation of Jesus to the Father. </p> 
 <p>2. Why must man make intelligent use of his talents and faculties? </p> 
 <p>3. Explain how the power of the word functions through the I AM. </p> 
 <p>4. When does man cease to strive in external ways? </p> 
 <p>5. Give the method used to establish command over the real forces of Being. </p> 
 <p>6. Why did Jesus call the apostles "friends"? </p> 
 <p>7. Name the functions of the Holy Spirit. </p> 
 <p>Chapter 16 </p> 
 <p>1. What is the cause of persecution? </p> 
 <p>2. Why was it expedient for Jesus to go away? </p> 
 <p>3. What is your conception of the Holy Spirit? </p> 
 <p>4. Jesus said, "A little while, and ye behold me no more; and again a little while, and ye shall see me." Elucidate. </p> 
 <p>5. In reality what is man? </p> 
 <p>6. What in consciousness is "the crucifixion"? </p> 
 <p>7. Jesus said, "In that day ye shall ask me no question." Clarify. </p> 
 <p>8. In terms of mind what are "dark sayings"? Interpret "The night is far spent, and the day is at hand"? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 17 </p> 
 <p>1. For what was Jesus asking when He said, "Glorify thy Son, that the Son may glorify thee"? For whom did He pray? </p> 
 <p>2. When had Jesus made union with the Father? </p> 
 <p>3. To what extent does one experience wholeness of mind and body? </p> 
 <p>4. What soul attitudes are necessary to the production of the new race? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_195"><font size="1" color="green">p. 195</font></a></p> 
 <p>5. Explain why man must change the basis of his thinking from a grosset plane to that of pure mind. </p> 
 <p>6. Does God always answer prayer? What is the highest form of prayer? </p> 
 <p>7. What attitude of mind is involved in selfless prayer? </p> 
 <p>8. What is sanctification? What is its result? </p> 
 <p>9. What is man's highest attainment? </p> 
 <p>10. Discuss obliteration of individual consciousness versus expansion of the soul? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 18 </p> 
 <p>1. What does Kidron symbolize? Garden? </p> 
 <p>2. Interpret the action of Judas in the garden of Kidron. </p> 
 <p>3. What action is indicated by Jesus' passing over the brook Kidron? </p> 
 <p>4. Are spiritual forces invulnerable? </p> 
 <p>5. In what way are faith and love helpful in overcoming? </p> 
 <p>6. Distinguish between Truth and the letter of the law. </p> 
 <p>7. Explain the statement "Every idle word that men shall speak, they shall give account thereof." </p> 
 <p>8. What is the remedy for the state of consciousness represented by Barabbas? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 19 </p> 
 <p>1. What is the general theme of John 19? </p> 
 <p>2. Why does the sense man jeer at religion? What is the spiritual significance of arraying Jesus in royal robes? </p> 
 <p>3. What is indicated by Pilate's vacillating attitude? </p> 
 <p>4. Metaphysically explained, why was it necessary for Pilate and the Jews to co-operate in crucifying Jesus? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_196"><font size="1" color="green">p. 196</font></a></p> 
 <p>5. What is symbolized by Jesus' seamless robe? In what way is this symbol helpful today? </p> 
 <p>6. Discuss Jesus' great purpose in becoming one of the human family? </p> 
 <p>7. Was spiritual man crucified on the cross? Explain. </p> 
 <p>8. What in consciousness is "the Preparation"? </p> 
 <p>9. What is the lesson taught in John 19:31-37? </p> 
 <p>10. What is symbolized by Jesus' resting in the tomb of Joseph of Arimathaea? </p> 
 <p>Chapter 20 </p> 
 <p>1. What is the inner meaning of Mary's looking into the tomb? </p> 
 <p>2. Explain the Scripture "I ascend unto my Father and your Father, and my God and your God." </p> 
 <p>3. What is the significance of Jesus' words "Touch me not"? What do the two angels represent? </p> 
 <p>4. What is the most effective way to comfort the bereaved? Does spiritual mind ever grieve? </p> 
 <p>5. What influence does the individual Christian have upon the race? </p> 
 <p>6. What does Thomas symbolize? </p> 
 <p>7. How is the "peace of God, which passeth all understanding" acquired? </p> 
 <p>8. Why is spiritual discernment necessary in understanding the Resurrection? Is the raising of Jesus' flesh body a "mystery"? </p> 
 <p>9. Explain something of the law by which the Resurrection was accomplished. </p> 
 <p>10. On what three planes of consciousness did Jesus develop power? </p> 
 <p>11. How does Jesus quicken the bodies of men today? How does man co-operate with Jesus in the regeneration? </p> 
 <p>12. How does man immortalize his body? In what way is science helpful in the regeneration? </p> 
 <p>13. Is the resurrection of man's flesh body a part of spiritual evolution? </p>

 <p>1. Of what is the net symbolic? The sea? </p> 
 <p>2. What is the spiritual significance of casting the net on the "right side"? </p> 
 <p>3. Why does man sometimes fail to demonstrate substance? </p> 
 <p>4. Discuss the symbology of the bread and fishes on two planes of consciousness. </p> 
 <p>5. What lesson was Jesus teaching when He asked, "Lovest thou me"? Why must love go hand in hand with faith and the other faculties? </p> 
 <p>6. Explain the inner meaning of verse 18. </p> 
 <p>7. In what way may the "manner of death" be said to glorify God? </p> 
 <p>8. Metaphysically what is the "disciple whom Jesus loved"? </p> 
 <p>9. What two attributes of the "mind of the Father" are necessary in claiming one's good? </p> 
 <p>10. How may we "destroy the seeds of death" and implant health and eternal life in the body? </p> 
 <p>11. Why is the resurrected body of Jesus not visible today? How can man follow Jesus' example? </p> 
 <p>12. Paul said, "Be ye transformed by the renewing of your mind." Explain. </p>
