 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/uni_t.jpg"></a><br><i>Jesus Christ heals</i>, by Charles Fillmore, [1939], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"> <br> </h3>  
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_198"><font size="1" color="green">p. 198</font></a></p> 
 <p>Be Thou Made Whole </p> 
 <p>Chapter I </p> 
 <p>1. In what respect do the words Christ and Jesus differ in meaning? </p> 
 <p>2. What is the supreme attainment of every man? </p> 
 <p>3. Is it possible for the Christ to experience death, burial, and resurrection? </p> 
 <p>4. When and where does resurrection take place? How did Jesus resurrect His body? </p> 
 <p>5. Do the words we use have any bearing on the results we get? </p> 
 <p>6. Why is it that man has not brought forth a greater degree of the Christ perfection? </p> 
 <p>7. Are infirmities permanent? How overcome? </p> 
 <p>8. Why did Jesus never give His personality any credit for the wonderful works He did? </p> 
 <p>9. Do all men have access to the Christ within? </p> 
 <p>10. Should man give more attention to the healing power of nature? </p> 
 <p>11. Should the student give his undivided attention to one system of development or should he study many? Explain fully. </p> 
 <p>12. Are there any short cuts into the kingdom of heaven? </p> 
 <p>13. Explain what is meant by the "end of the world," as brought out in the lesson? </p> 
 <p>14. Describe the means by which Jesus undertook to establish the kingdom of heaven on earth. </p> 
 <p>God Presence </p> 
 <p>Chapter II </p> 
 <p>1. How may we know more of God and of ourselves? </p> 
 <p>2. Explain the meaning of "God is the health of His people." </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_199"><font size="1" color="green">p. 199</font></a></p> 
 <p>3. Does God ever will man to be sick? Does He ever use His power to punish His creations? </p> 
 <p>4. How may we say that the attributes of God are expressed? </p> 
 <p>5. Should man ever think of God as being separate from him or outside him? </p> 
 <p>6. Explain how mind is the connecting link between God and man. </p> 
 <p>7. What is the proper procedure in realizing God as health? Is it looked on as practical by the world at large? </p> 
 <p>8. Explain the "still small voice." </p> 
 <p>9. Explain God as principle. Should the truth that God is principle lead us to infer that He is cold and unfeeling? </p> 
 <p>10. What part does man play in the forming of things? </p> 
 <p>11. Is God ever absent from His creations? </p> 
 <p>12. Since intelligence is the light of the world, how may we increase our intelligence? </p> 
 <p>Realization Precedes Manifestation </p> 
 <p>Chapter III </p> 
 <p>1. How are we able to know that mind is the source of all things? </p> 
 <p>2. How should we regard miracles? </p> 
 <p>3. Is it possible for us to do the works that Jesus did? </p> 
 <p>4. Is it possible to explain the laws governing religion from a scientific viewpoint? </p> 
 <p>5. What part does concentration play in our demonstrations? </p> 
 <p>6. Give the metaphysical meaning of the word realization. </p> 
 <p>7. What does faith have to do with the realization of one's ideals? </p> 
 <p>8. Are all prayers answered? </p> 
 <p>9. The text mentions a realm where no effort is required to gain the answer to questions. Explain. </p> 
 <p>10. What is the supreme realization of man? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_200"><font size="1" color="green">p. 200</font></a></p> 
 <p>Producing Results </p> 
 <p>Chapter IV </p> 
 <p>1. What states of mind are necessary if we are to realize perfect health? </p> 
 <p>2. To what phase of being should a person give his </p> 
 <p>attention in order to understand the Scriptures? </p> 
 <p>3. How can healing be instantaneous? </p> 
 <p>4. Explain the meaning of the word sin. </p> 
 <p>5. What is it to forgive sin? </p> 
 <p>6. Should one ever think of oneself as being born in sin? What should be one's viewpoint? </p> 
 <p>7. What is the foundation of the Jesus Christ kingdom? </p> 
 <p>8. What part does love play in our unfoldment? </p> 
 <p>9. Explain fully the meaning of the Trinity. </p> 
 <p>10. What mental attitude should man have toward his body? </p> 
 <p>The Omnipotence Of Prayer </p> 
 <p>Chapter V </p> 
 <p>1. Why is it necessary to affirm a thing one already knows to be true? </p> 
 <p>2. Should everyone pray? Why? </p> 
 <p>3. Explain why Jesus advised us to ask for what we want. </p> 
 <p>4. Is asking always a part of prayer? What other forms of prayer are there? </p> 
 <p>5. Is it ever good for one to be self-righteous? 6. What is the key to all mysteries? </p> 
 <p>7. What part do faith and understanding play in a perfect demonstration? </p> 
 <p>8. What is the real foundation of effective prayer? </p> 
 <p>9. Is it essential to have great faith in order to demonstrate? </p> 
 <p>10. Is silent prayer more effective than audible prayer? Why? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_201"><font size="1" color="green">p. 201</font></a></p> 
 <p>11. What state of mind is necessary if we are to receive clear revelations? </p> 
 <p>12. Why are some healings slow even though prayers are said? </p> 
 <p>God Said, And It Was So </p> 
 <p>Chapter VI </p> 
 <p>1. Explain fully in your own words the power of healing words. </p> 
 <p>2. Did Jesus ever write? Through what channel have we received His words? </p> 
 <p>3. From what standpoint did Jesus say that His words are spirit and are life? </p> 
 <p>4. Does the word of itself have power? What must we do to give full force to our words? </p> 
 <p>5. Is Truth ever the formulated doctrine of any church, creed, or sect? Where may we find Truth? </p> 
 <p>6. What must we do to keep successfully in mind the words of Jesus? </p> 
 <p>7. Is moral goodness always an indication of spirituality? </p> 
 <p>8. What determines the character and the results of our words? </p> 
 <p>9. Is it necessary for us to feel our oneness with God in order to believe in His omnipotence? </p> 
 <p>10. What does the healing of the man at the Pool of Bethesda represent? </p> 
 <p>11. Is it ever possible for anyone to do our spiritual work for us? </p> 
 <p>12. Explain how Jesus made His teachings available to all. </p> 
 <p>Indispensable Assurance </p> 
 <p>Chapter VII </p> 
 <p>1. Is man's desire for a fuller and more excellent life a natural and orderly one? Explain fully. </p> 
 <p>2. Give in your own words a definition of faith. Is it </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_202"><font size="1" color="green">p. 202</font></a></p> 
 <p>natural for man to have faith? </p> 
 <p>3. Is it a falsehood to deny sickness in the face of its appearance? </p> 
 <p>4. How are we to get more life? </p> 
 <p>5. Is it a fact that faith is blind? </p> 
 <p>6. How are we to emulate Jesus? Is it necessary to do so in order to do the works of God? </p> 
 <p>7. Give three steps that are necessary in demonstration. </p> 
 <p>8. Would you call Jesus a master scientist? What did He mean when He said, "In my Father's house are many mansions"? </p> 
 <p>9. Is it better to seek understanding through intellectual reasoning or through divine inspiration? </p> 
 <p>10. Just what do our thoughts and words have to do with our demonstrations? </p> 
 <p>11. Is it always necessary for us to be receptive in order to be healed? Does God ever do things for us against our will? </p> 
 <p>12. Give the difference between a genius and an ordinary man from the metaphysical viewpoint. </p> 
 <p>13. Is it sufficient to be inspired by an ideal, or is something else needed? </p> 
 <p>14. Should one ever take the attitude that things as they are cannot be changed? </p> 
 <p>15. Explain what is meant by giving up the personal and taking up the universal. </p> 
 <p>The Fullness Of Time </p> 
 <p>Chapter VIII </p> 
 <p>1. What part does order play in life? </p> 
 <p>2. Explain fully the power of words. </p> 
 <p>3. Is there any limit to the power of thought? </p> 
 <p>4. What should be our attitude toward hurry? </p> 
 <p>5. How may we become conscious of our I AM? </p> 
 <p>6. How is soul growth related to bodily health? </p> 
 <p>7. Explain the relation between law and order. What part does law and order play in the divine scheme of things? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_203"><font size="1" color="green">p. 203</font></a></p> 
 <p>8. What is the difference between natural law and divine law? </p> 
 <p>9. Were the works that Jesus performed really miracles? Why? </p> 
 <p>10. Is it good to deny the existence of the things of the world? </p> 
 <p>11. Is it necessary to enter fully into the kingdom in order to realize results? </p> 
 <p>12. Give some of the signs that follow the giving of attention to Spirit. </p> 
 <p>Healing Through Praise And Thanksgiving </p> 
 <p>Chapter IX </p> 
 <p>1. Has praise any commercial value? Any spiritual value? Explain. </p> 
 <p>2. How is the body restored to perfection? </p> 
 <p>3. How should the various writers of the Bible be judged? </p> 
 <p>4. Is it possible for us to be of service in establishing peace on earth? </p> 
 <p>5. In your own words tell how the new Christianity and physical science are working together. </p> 
 <p>6. Where is heaven, and how is it formed? </p> 
 <p>7. Explain what Jesus meant when He said that the bread and wine was His body and His blood. </p> 
 <p>8. Does man have the power to form and reform the universal energy? </p> 
 <p>9. Is there more than one God? </p> 
 <p>10. Explain in your own words the meaning of the four horses mentioned in the 6th chapter of Revelation. </p> 
 <p>"I Am The Way, And The Truth, And The Life" </p> 
 <p>Chapter X </p> 
 <p>1. Explain why the word of Spirit is next to Spirit in power. </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_204"><font size="1" color="green">p. 204</font></a></p> 
 <p>2. Compare Jehovah and Christ. How should one go about choosing healing words? </p> 
 <p>3. Why do we find it advantageous to use the name Jesus Christ? </p> 
 <p>4. Did Jesus discover the principles He taught and demonstrated? </p> 
 <p>5. Give the difference between the real spiritual I AM and the personality. How did Jesus handle this proposition? Is it possible for us to do likewise? </p> 
 <p>6. What is the difference between Jehovah God and Elohim God? </p> 
 <p>7. Does the individual I AM contain infinite creative capacity? </p> 
 <p>8. Can the unseen forces be mechanically demonstrated? How? </p> 
 <p>9. What did Jesus mean when He said that we should be judged by our words? What is meant by the "day of judgment"? </p> 
 <p>10. Explain fully the change from the natural life to the spiritual life. What did Paul mean when he said, "I die daily?" </p> 
 <p>11. What must be our viewpoint if we are to be saved? </p> 
 <p>12. Should we expect healing today the same as in the time of Jesus' ministry on earth? </p> 
 <p>Healing Power Of Joy </p> 
 <p>Chapter XI </p> 
 <p>1. Explain how joy acts on fear. </p> 
 <p>2. Explain the relation between happiness and health. </p> 
 <p>3. Do you think that singing can be of benefit toward the realization of perfect health? </p> 
 <p>4. Explain how the whole universe is in vibration. </p> 
 <p>5. What part does zeal play in our unfoldment? Explain fully. </p> 
 <p>6. What is the cause of man's seeming lack of vital energy? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_205"><font size="1" color="green">p. 205</font></a></p> 
 <p>7. Explain the importance of faith. How is faith quickened? </p> 
 <p>8. Does man's thinking have much to do with his spiritual progress? </p> 
 <p>9. Why is it necessary first to study mind in order to understand the real character of God? </p> 
 <p>10. What should we do to bring the Spirit into our consciousness? </p> 
 <p>Holy Spirit Fulfills The Law </p> 
 <p>Chapter XII </p> 
 <p>1. Explain the Holy Spirit from the viewpoint of the Christian metaphysician and the physical scientist. </p> 
 <p>2. How should a good Christian healer regard the Holy Spirit? </p> 
 <p>3. Should we ever hesitate to call on the Holy Spirit? </p> 
 <p>4. Did God create man with a perfect body or perfect-body idea? </p> 
 <p>5. Did Jesus die on the cross? Explain how He was able to resurrect His body. </p> 
 <p>6. Is it to our benefit to know that mind rules matter? Explain. </p> 
 <p>7. Is it possible to escape life's experiences by a change of environment or by death? </p> 
 <p>8. Can "ascension" be gained through physical death? </p> 
 <p>9. What did Jesus mean when He said, "He that hath seen me hath seen the Father"? </p> 
 <p>10. Is it necessary for man to become as a little child? Explain fully. </p> 
 <p>11. Why is it that man must have a mediator in order to contact God? Who is this mediator? </p> 
 <p>12. Since man has seemingly lost contact with Spirit, what is his only salvation? </p>
