 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">THE VISION OF THE HOLY GRAIL</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">EUGENE FIELD</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br> <i> Deere Chryste, let not the cheere of earth,<br> To fill our hearts with heedless mirth<br>    This holy Christmasse time;<br> But give us of thy heavenly cheere<br> That we may hold thy love most deere<br>    And know thy peace sublime.</i> <br> <br> 	   -------<br> <br> Full merry waxed King Pelles court<br> With Yuletide cheere and Yuletide sport,<br>    And, when the board was spread,<br> Now wit ye well 'twas good to see<br> So fair and brave a companie<br>    With Pelles at the head.<br> <br> "Come hence, Elaine," King Pelles cried,<br> "Come hence and sit ye by my side,<br>    For never yet, I trow,<br> Have gentle virtues like to thine<br> Been proved by sword nor pledged in wine,<br>    Nor shall be nevermo!"<br> <br> "Sweete sir, my father," quoth Elaine,<br> "Me it repents to give thee pain--<br>    Yet, tarry I may not;<br> For I shall soond and I shall die<br> If I behold this companie<br>    And see not Launcelot!<br> <br> "My heart shall have no love but this--<br> My lips shall know no other kiss,<br>    Save only, father, thine;<br> So graunt me leave to seek my bower,<br> The lonely chamber in the toure,<br>    Where sleeps his child and mine."<br> <br> Then frowned the King in sore despite;<br> "A murrain seize that traitrous knight,<br>    For that he lies!" he cried--<br> "A base, unchristian paynim he,<br> Else, by my beard, he would not be<br>    A recreant to his bride!<br> <br> "Oh, I had liefer yield my life<br> Than see thee the deserted wife<br>    Of dastard Launcelot!<br> Yet an' thou has no mind to stay,<br> Go with thy damosels away--<br>    Lo, I'll detain ye not."<br> <br> Her damosels in goodly train<br> Back to her chamber led Elaine,<br>    And when her eyes were cast<br> Upon her babe, her tears did flow<br> And she did wail and weep as though<br>    Her heart had like to brast.<br> <br> The while she grieved the Yuletide sport<br> Waxed lustier in King Pelles' court,<br>    And louder, houre by houre,<br> The echoes of the rout were borne<br> To where the lady, all forlorn,<br>    Made moning in the toure,<br> <br> "Swete Chryste," she cried, "ne let me hear<br> Their ribald sounds of Yuletide cheere<br>    That mock at mine and me;<br> Graunt that my sore affliction cease<br> And give me of the heavenly peace<br>    That comes with thoughts of thee!"<br> <br> Lo, as she spake, a wondrous light<br> Made all that lonely chamber bright,<br>    And o'er the infant's bed<br> A spirit hand, as samite pail,<br> Held sodaine foorth the Holy Grail<br>    Above the infant's head.<br> <br> And from the sacred golden cup<br> A subtle incense floated up<br>    And filled the conscious air,<br> Which, when she breathed, the fair Elaine<br> Forgot her grief, forgot her pain.<br>    Forgot her sore despair.<br> <br> And as the Grail's mysterious balm<br> Wrought in her heart a wondrous calm,<br>    Great mervail 'twas to see<br> The sleeping child stretch one hand up<br> As if in dreams he held the cup<br>    Which none mought win but he.<br> <br> Through all the night King Pelles' court<br> Made mighty cheer and goodly sport.<br>    Nor never recked the joy<br> That was vouchsafed that Christmass tide<br> To Launcelot's deserted bride<br>    And to her sleeping boy.<br> <br> <i> Swete Chryste, let not the cheere of earth<br> To fill our hearts with heedless mirth<br>    This present Christmasse night;<br> But send among us to and fro<br> Thy Holy Grail, that men may know<br>    The joy withe wisdom dight.</i> <br>
