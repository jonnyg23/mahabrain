 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">MERLIN:  A POEM</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">MELISSA<br>  [pseudonym of Jane Brereton]</h2>  </center>  <br>  <center>Humbly Inscribed (<i> Octob.</i>  1735.) to <br>  <i> Her</i>  MAJESTY <i> QUEEN GUARDIAN</i> .</center>  <br>  ILLUSTRIOUS  QUEEN !<br>                                          The loyal Zeal excuse,<br>  The fond Ambition, of a <i> British Muse</i> ,<br>  Who wou'd, in <i> Merlin's</i>  Praise, attempt to soar;<br>  And in his <i> Cave,</i>  Your Patronage implore:<br>  Protection seek, beneath Your <i> Royal Name</i> ;<br>  And borrow Strength to rise, from <i> Merlin's</i>  Fame.<br>  <br>     When <i> Sol</i>  to distant Climes had giv'n the Day,<br>  And shone on ours, with pale reflected Ray;<br>  When <i> Night</i>  with solemn pace, advanc'd her Head,<br>  And o'er the Hemisphere her Mantle spread;<br>  Yet thro' the fable Gloom, those Orbs reveal'd,<br>  Which in a Flood of Light, the Day conceal'd:<br>  I, to a Summit, mus'd along, to see<br>  Unnumber'd <i> Suns</i> , which croud the <i> Galaxy.</i> <br>  But <i> Merlin's Cave</i> , had such Impressions made,<br>  And Royal Honours, to his Mem'ry pay'd;<br>  Pleas'd with Reflection, and involv'd in Thought;<br>  Creative <i> Fancy</i> , soon this <i> Vision</i>  wrought.<br>  <br>     Then, lo! beneath a venerable <i> Oak</i> ,<br>  Which oft repell'd the Tempest's furious stroke;<br>  Whose spreading Arms, a wide Circumf'rence show,<br>  And from whose Trunk, springs sacred <i> Misseltoe</i> :<br>  Methought, I saw an awful <i> Shade</i>  arise;<br>  (Fit object only, for Poetick Eyes.)<br>  The Form Majestick, and the Front serene;<br>  <i> Angles</i> , and <i> Circles</i> , on his Robe were seen.<br>  The <i> Northern Crown</i> <sup> 1</sup> , around his Temples shone,<br>  And the <i> Celestial Signs</i>  adorn'd his Zone.<br>  The <i> British Harp</i> , seem'd to support one Hand;<br>  While t'other, gently wav'd the sacred <i> Wand.</i> <br>  The <i> Manes</i>  of great <i> Merlin</i>  stood confess'd;<br>  And my enraptur'd Fancy, thus address'd:<br>  <br>     "Why will <i> Melissa</i> , <i> Merlin's</i>  praise decline,<br>  "Distinguish'd now, by Royal CAROLINE ?<br>  "Believe not <i> such</i> , as wou'd asperse my Name;<br>  "But trust those <i> Authors</i> , who defend my Fame.<br>  "You, to the Royal <i> Grotto</i> , touch'd the Lyre,<br>  "And durst in God-like <i> Newton's</i>  Praise aspire.<br>  "Why shou'd not <i> British Merlin</i> , grace thy Page,<br>  "In <i> Mathematicks</i> , once esteem'd a Sage?<br>  "A well-try'd <i> Genius</i> , cou'd transmit to Fame<br>  "My honour'd Modern <i> Cave</i> , and Ancient <i> Name</i> :<br>  "Might to my ROYAL  GUEST , re-touch the String,<br>  And as <i> he</i> <sup> 2</sup>  sung the <i> Saint</i> , the <i> Prophet</i>  sing.<br>  "But since the <i> Cambrian Bards</i> , neglect the Muse;<br>  "<i> Melissa's</i>  humbler Strains, I'll not refuse.<br>  <br>     "SIX  Centuries, twice told, are now compleat,<br>  "Since <i> Merlin</i>  liv'd on this terrestrial Seat.<br>  "Knowledge appear'd, but dawning to my Sight;<br>  "She blaz'd on <i> Newton</i>  with Meridian Light.<br>  "Yet the faint Glimm'rings, which my <i> Genius</i>  taught,<br>  "Beyond the ken, of human Art, were thought.<br>  "What I by meer mechanick Pow'rs atchiev'd,<br>  "Th' Effects of <i> Magick</i> , then, by most believ'd.<br>  "To <i> Stone-henge</i> , let the Sons of Art, repair,<br>  "And View the Wonders, I, erected There.<br>  "Try, if their Skill improv'd, Mine, e'er can foil;<br>  "Restore the <i> Giants-Dance</i>  t' <i> Hibernian </i> Soil.<br>  "Nor in <i> Geometry</i> , excell'd alone;<br>  "But other <i> Sciences</i>  to me were known.<br>  "I study'd <i> Nature</i> , through her various Ways;<br>  "And chaunted to this <i> Harp, prophetick </i> Lays.<br>  "To <i> Cader Ydris</i> <sup> 3</sup>  oft, I took my Way;<br>  "Rose with the <i> Sun</i> , toil'd up th' Ascent all Day;<br>  "But scarce could reach, the Mountains tow'ring height,<br>  "E'er Radiant <i> Vesper</i> , usher'd in the Night.<br>  "The Summit gain'd, I sought with naked Eye,<br>  "To penetrate, the Wonders of the Sky.<br>  "No Telescopic Glass known in that Age,<br>  "To assist the Optics of the curious Sage.<br>  "Though lov'd <i> Astronomy</i>  oft charm'd my Mind,<br>  "I now erroneous, all my Notions fined.<br>  "I thought bright <i> Sol,</i>  around our <i> Globe</i>  had run;<br>  "Nor knew Earth's Motion, nor the central <i> Sun</i> .<br>  "And had I known; cou'd I Belief have gain'd,<br>  "When Ignorance, and Superstition reign'd?<br>     'Unseen by me, <i> Attraction's</i>  mighty Force,<br>  "And how fierce <i> Comets</i> , run their stated Course;<br>  "Surprizing Scenes! by Heav'n reserv'd in store,<br>  "For its own Fav'rite <i> Newton</i> , to explore.<br>  "With Faculties enlarg'd, <i> He</i> 's gone to prove<br>  "The Laws, and Motions of yon <i> Worlds</i>  above;<br>  "And the vast Circuits of th'Expanse <i> survey,</i> <br>  "View <i> Solar Systems</i>  in the <i> Milky Way</i> .<br>  "My Spirit too through Ether wings its Flight,<br>  "Discov'ring <i> Truths</i> , deny'd my mortal Sight.<br>  "Transported hovers, o'er my native Isle,<br>  "Where Arts improve, and Peace and Plenty smile.<br>  <br>     "But lo!  Boötes drives his radiant Car,<br>  "High on its Course, around the <i> Polar Star</i> ,<br>  "And fiery <i> Draco</i> , drops his starry Crest;<br>  "'Tis time, though shouldst indulge thy needful Rest.<br>  "Yet stay, <i> Melissa!</i> ---try this fav'rite Lyre;<br>  "And <i> Merlin</i>  will the grateful Song inspire.<br>  "To <i> Learning'</i> s <i> Patroness</i> , my Thanks convey;<br>  "And humbly at her Feet, present thy Lay.<br>  "Conscious, how mean, and how unskill'd thy Hand,<br>  "I see thee tremble, at my kind Command.<br>  "Let my Persuasion, once, thy Fears beguile;<br>  "The gracious QUEEN , will condescend to smile.<br>  "For <i> Merlin</i> 's sake, will give <i> Melissa</i>  leave,<br>  "To touch the Strings, in my much-honour'd <i> Cave</i> .<br>     "And <i> Wallia</i> 's gen'rous Prince, will not disdain<br>  "What I foretell; ---tho' low, thy Lyrick Strain.<br> <br>  <hr>  <br>   <a> <b> 1</b>   <i> A</i>  Constellation <i> so call'd.</i> <br>  <br>  </a><a> <b> 2</b>   Author of the L EEK , presented to her Majesty.<br>  <br>  </a><a> <b> 3</b>   A Mountain in <i> Merionethshire</i> , accounted by some the Highest in <i> Britain</i> ; as an Argument for which they alledge, it bears Variety of <i> Alpine</i>  Plants.    <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br>  <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br> <br>
