 <p align="center"><a href="../kjv/pro021.htm">KJV</a></p><p>1  The king's<sup><a href="h4428.htm">4428</a></sup> heart<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> is in the hand<sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> of the LORD,<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> as the rivers<sup><a href="h6388.htm">6388</a></sup> of water:<sup><a href="h4325.htm">4325</a></sup> he turneth<sup><a href="h5186.htm">5186</a></sup> it whithersoever<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> he will.<sup><a href="h2654.htm">2654</a></sup> </p>
 <p>2  Every<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> way<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> of a man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> is right<sup><a href="h3477.htm">3477</a></sup> in his own eyes:<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> but the LORD<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> pondereth<sup><a href="h8505.htm">8505</a></sup> the hearts.<sup><a href="h3826.htm">3826</a></sup> </p>
 <p>3  To do<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> justice<sup><a href="h6666.htm">6666</a></sup> and judgment<sup><a href="h4941.htm">4941</a></sup> is more acceptable<sup><a href="h0977.htm">977</a></sup> to the LORD<sup><a href="h3068.htm">3068</a></sup> than sacrifice.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h2077.htm">2077</a></sup> </p>
 <p>4  A high<sup><a href="h7312.htm">7312</a></sup> look,<sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> and a proud<sup><a href="h7342.htm">7342</a></sup> heart,<sup><a href="h3820.htm">3820</a></sup> and the plowing<sup><a href="h5215.htm">5215</a></sup> of the wicked,<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> is sin.<sup><a href="h2403.htm">2403</a></sup> </p>
 <p>5  The thoughts<sup><a href="h4284.htm">4284</a></sup> of the diligent<sup><a href="h2742.htm">2742</a></sup> tend only<sup><a href="h0389.htm">389</a></sup> to plenteousness;<sup><a href="h4195.htm">4195</a></sup> but of every one<sup><a href="h3605.htm">3605</a></sup> that is hasty<sup><a href="h0213.htm">213</a></sup> only<sup><a href="h0389.htm">389</a></sup> to want.<sup><a href="h4270.htm">4270</a></sup> </p>

 <p>7  The robbery<sup><a href="h7701.htm">7701</a></sup> of the wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> shall destroy<sup><a href="h1641.htm">1641</a></sup> them; because<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> they refuse<sup><a href="h3985.htm">3985</a></sup> to do<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> judgment.<sup><a href="h4941.htm">4941</a></sup> </p>
 <p>8  The way<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> of man<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> is froward<sup><a href="h2019.htm">2019</a></sup> and strange:<sup><a href="h2054.htm">2054</a></sup> but as for the pure,<sup><a href="h2134.htm">2134</a></sup> his work<sup><a href="h6467.htm">6467</a></sup> is right.<sup><a href="h3477.htm">3477</a></sup> </p>

 <p>12  The righteous<sup><a href="h6662.htm">6662</a></sup> man wisely considereth<sup><a href="h7919.htm">7919</a></sup> the house<sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> of the wicked:<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> but God overthroweth<sup><a href="h5557.htm">5557</a></sup> the wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> for their wickedness.<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> </p>
 <p>13  Whoso stoppeth<sup><a href="h0331.htm">331</a></sup> his ears<sup><a href="h0241.htm">241</a></sup> at the cry<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h2201.htm">2201</a></sup> of the poor,<sup><a href="h1800.htm">1800</a></sup> he also<sup><a href="h1571.htm">1571</a></sup> shall cry<sup><a href="h7121.htm">7121</a></sup> himself,<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> but shall not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> be heard.<sup><a href="h6030.htm">6030</a></sup> </p>
 <p>14  A gift<sup><a href="h4976.htm">4976</a></sup> in secret<sup><a href="h5643.htm">5643</a></sup> pacifieth<sup><a href="h3711.htm">3711</a></sup> anger:<sup><a href="h0639.htm">639</a></sup> and a reward<sup><a href="h7810.htm">7810</a></sup> in the bosom<sup><a href="h2436.htm">2436</a></sup> strong<sup><a href="h5794.htm">5794</a></sup> wrath.<sup><a href="h2534.htm">2534</a></sup> </p>
 <p>15  It is joy<sup><a href="h8057.htm">8057</a></sup> to the just<sup><a href="h6662.htm">6662</a></sup> to do<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> judgment:<sup><a href="h4941.htm">4941</a></sup> but destruction<sup><a href="h4288.htm">4288</a></sup> shall be to the workers<sup><a href="h6466.htm">6466</a></sup> of iniquity.<sup><a href="h0205.htm">205</a></sup> </p>
 <p>16  The man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> that wandereth<sup><a href="h8582.htm">8582</a></sup> out of the way<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> of understanding<sup><a href="h7919.htm">7919</a></sup> shall remain<sup><a href="h5117.htm">5117</a></sup> in the congregation<sup><a href="h6951.htm">6951</a></sup> of the dead.<sup><a href="h7496.htm">7496</a></sup> </p>

 <p>20  There is treasure<sup><a href="h0214.htm">214</a></sup> to be desired<sup><a href="h2530.htm">2530</a></sup> and oil<sup><a href="h8081.htm">8081</a></sup> in the dwelling<sup><a href="h5116.htm">5116</a></sup> of the wise;<sup><a href="h2450.htm">2450</a></sup> but a foolish<sup><a href="h3684.htm">3684</a></sup> man<sup><a href="h0120.htm">120</a></sup> spendeth it up.<sup><a href="h1104.htm">1104</a></sup> </p>

 <p>23  Whoso keepeth<sup><a href="h8104.htm">8104</a></sup> his mouth<sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> and his tongue<sup><a href="h3956.htm">3956</a></sup> keepeth<sup><a href="h8104.htm">8104</a></sup> his soul<sup><a href="h5315.htm">5315</a></sup> from troubles.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h6869.htm">6869</a></sup> </p>

 <p>25  The desire<sup><a href="h8378.htm">8378</a></sup> of the slothful<sup><a href="h6102.htm">6102</a></sup> killeth<sup><a href="h4191.htm">4191</a></sup> him; for<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> his hands<sup><a href="h3027.htm">3027</a></sup> refuse<sup><a href="h3985.htm">3985</a></sup> to labor.<sup><a href="h6213.htm">6213</a></sup> </p>

 <p>27  The sacrifice<sup><a href="h2077.htm">2077</a></sup> of the wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> is abomination:<sup><a href="h8441.htm">8441</a></sup> how much more,<sup><a href="h0637.htm">637</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> when he bringeth<sup><a href="h0935.htm">935</a></sup> it with a wicked mind?<sup><a href="h2154.htm">2154</a></sup> </p>

 <p>26 ἀσεβὴς ἐπιθυμεῖ ὅλην τὴν ἡμέραν ἐπιθυμίας κακάς ὁ δὲ δίκαιος ἐλεᾷ καὶ οἰκτίρει ἀφειδῶς </p>

 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎6 ‏פֹּ֣עַל א֭וֹצָרוֹת בִּלְשׁ֣וֹן שָׁ֑קֶר הֶ֥בֶל נִ֝דָּ֗ף מְבַקְשֵׁי־מָֽוֶת׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎7 ‏שֹׁד־רְשָׁעִ֥ים יְגוֹרֵ֑ם כִּ֥י מֵ֝אֲנ֗וּ לַעֲשׂ֥וֹת מִשְׁפָּֽט׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎8 ‏הֲפַכְפַּ֬ךְ דֶּ֣רֶךְ אִ֣ישׁ וָזָ֑ר וְ֝זַ֗ךְ יָשָׁ֥ר פָּעֳלֽוֹ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎9 ‏ט֗וֹב לָשֶׁ֥בֶת עַל־פִּנַּת־גָּ֑ג מֵאֵ֥שֶׁת מִ֝דְיָנִ֗ים וּבֵ֥ית חָֽבֶר׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎10 ‏נֶ֣פֶשׁ רָ֭שָׁע אִוְּתָה־רָ֑ע לֹא־יֻחַ֖ן בְּעֵינָ֣יו רֵעֵֽהוּ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎11 ‏בַּעְנָשׁ־לֵ֭ץ<font color="red"><sup>1</sup></font> יֶחְכַּם־פֶּ֑תִי וּבְהַשְׂכִּ֥יל לְ֝חָכָ֗ם יִקַּח־דָּֽעַת׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎12 ‏מַשְׂכִּ֣יל צַ֭דִּיק לְבֵ֣ית רָשָׁ֑ע מְסַלֵּ֖ף רְשָׁעִ֣ים לָרָֽע׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎13 ‏אֹטֵ֣ם אָ֭זְנוֹ מִזַּעֲקַת־דָּ֑ל גַּֽם־ה֥וּא יִ֝קְרָ֗א וְלֹ֣א יֵעָנֶֽה׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎14 ‏מַתָּ֣ן בַּ֭סֵּתֶר יִכְפֶּה־אָ֑ף וְשֹׁ֥חַד בַּ֝חֵ֗ק חֵמָ֥ה עַזָּֽה׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎15 ‏שִׂמְחָ֣ה לַ֭צַּדִּיק עֲשׂ֣וֹת מִשְׁפָּ֑ט וּ֝מְחִתָּ֗ה לְפֹ֣עֲלֵי אָֽוֶן׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎16 ‏אָדָ֗ם תּ֭וֹעֶה מִדֶּ֣רֶךְ הַשְׂכֵּ֑ל בִּקְהַ֖ל רְפָאִ֣ים יָנֽוּחַ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎17 ‏אִ֣ישׁ מַ֭חְסוֹר אֹהֵ֣ב שִׂמְחָ֑ה אֹהֵ֥ב יַֽיִן־וָ֝שֶׁ֗מֶן לֹ֣א יַעֲשִֽׁיר׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎18 ‏כֹּ֣פֶר לַצַּדִּ֣יק רָשָׁ֑ע וְתַ֖חַת יְשָׁרִ֣ים בּוֹגֵֽד׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎19 ‏ט֗וֹב שֶׁ֥בֶת בְּאֶֽרֶץ־מִדְבָּ֑ר מֵאֵ֖שֶׁת <font color="brown">מדונים</font> <font color="blue">מִדְיָנִ֣ים</font> וָכָֽעַס׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎20 ‏אוֹצָ֤ר׀ נֶחְמָ֣ד וָ֭שֶׁמֶן בִּנְוֵ֣ה חָכָ֑ם וּכְסִ֖יל אָדָ֣ם יְבַלְּעֶֽנּוּ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎21 ‏רֹ֭דֵף צְדָקָ֣ה וָחָ֑סֶד יִמְצָ֥א חַ֝יִּ֗ים צְדָקָ֥ה וְכָבֽוֹד׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎22 ‏עִ֣יר גִּ֭בֹּרִים עָלָ֣ה חָכָ֑ם וַ֝יֹּ֗רֶד עֹ֣ז מִבְטֶחָֽה׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎23 ‏שֹׁמֵ֣ר פִּ֭יו וּלְשׁוֹנ֑וֹ שֹׁמֵ֖ר מִצָּר֣וֹת נַפְשֽׁוֹ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎24 ‏זֵ֣ד יָ֭הִיר לֵ֣ץ שְׁמ֑וֹ ע֝וֹשֶׂ֗ה בְּעֶבְרַ֥ת זָדֽוֹן׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎25 ‏תַּאֲוַ֣ת עָצֵ֣ל תְּמִיתֶ֑נּוּ כִּֽי־מֵאֲנ֖וּ יָדָ֣יו לַעֲשֽׂוֹת׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎26 ‏כָּל־הַ֭יּוֹם הִתְאַוָּ֣ה תַאֲוָ֑ה וְצַדִּ֥יק יִ֝תֵּ֗ן וְלֹ֣א יַחְשֹֽׂךְ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎27 ‏זֶ֣בַח רְ֭שָׁעִים תּוֹעֵבָ֑ה אַ֝֗ף כִּֽי־בְזִמָּ֥ה יְבִיאֶֽנּוּ׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎28 ‏עֵד־כְּזָבִ֥ים יֹאבֵ֑ד וְאִ֥ישׁ שׁ֝וֹמֵ֗עַ לָנֶ֥צַח יְדַבֵּֽר׃ </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎29 ‏הֵעֵ֬ז אִ֣ישׁ רָשָׁ֣ע בְּפָנָ֑יו וְ֝יָשָׁ֗ר ה֤וּא׀ <font color="brown">יכין</font> <font color="blue">יָבִ֬ין</font> <font color="brown">דרכיו</font> <font color="blue">דַּרְכּֽוֹ׃</font> </span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎30 ‏אֵ֣ין חָ֭כְמָה וְאֵ֣ין תְּבוּנָ֑ה וְאֵ֥ין עֵ֝צָ֗ה לְנֶ֣גֶד יְהוָֽה׃ פ</span></p>
 <p align="RIGHT"><span lang="he">‎31 ‏ס֗וּס מ֭וּכָן לְי֣וֹם מִלְחָמָ֑ה וְ֝לַֽיהוָ֗ה הַתְּשׁוּעָֽה׃ </span></p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/pro021.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 [Sicut divisiones aquarum, ita cor regis in manu Domini:<br> quocumque voluerit, inclinabit illud.<br></p>
 <p>2 Omnis via viri recta sibi videtur:<br> appendit autem corda Dominus.<br></p>
 <p>3 Facere misericordiam et judicium<br> magis placet Domino quam victimæ.<br></p>
 <p>4 Exaltatio oculorum est dilatatio cordis;<br> lucerna impiorum peccatum.<br></p>
 <p>5 Cogitationes robusti semper in abundantia;<br> omnis autem piger semper in egestate est.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>6 [Qui congregat thesauros lingua mendacii vanus et excors est,<br> et impingetur ad laqueos mortis.<br></p>
 <p>7 Rapinæ impiorum detrahent eos,<br> quia noluerunt facere judicium.<br></p>
 <p>8 Perversa via viri aliena est;<br> qui autem mundus est, rectum opus ejus.<br></p>
 <p>9 Melius est sedere in angulo domatis,<br> quam cum muliere litigiosa, et in domo communi.<br></p>
 <p>10 Anima impii desiderat malum:<br> non miserebitur proximo suo.<br></p>
 <p>11 Mulctato pestilente, sapientior erit parvulus,<br> et si sectetur sapientem, sumet scientiam.<br></p>
 <p>12 Excogitat justus de domo impii,<br> ut detrahat impios a malo.]<br>
 </p>
 <p>13 [Qui obturat aurem suam ad clamorem pauperis,<br> et ipse clamabit, et non exaudietur.<br></p>
 <p>14 Munus absconditum extinguit iras,<br> et donum in sinu indignationem maximam.<br></p>
 <p>15 Gaudium justo est facere judicium,<br> et pavor operantibus iniquitatem.<br></p>
 <p>16 Vir qui erraverit a via doctrinæ<br> in ctu gigantum commorabitur.<br></p>
 <p>17 Qui diligit epulas in egestate erit;<br> qui amat vinum et pinguia non ditabitur.<br></p>
 <p>18 Pro justo datur impius,<br> et pro rectis iniquus.<br></p>
 <p>19 Melius est habitare in terra deserta<br> quam cum muliere rixosa et iracunda.<br></p>
 <p>20 Thesaurus desiderabilis, et oleum in habitaculo justi:<br> et imprudens homo dissipabit illud.<br></p>
 <p>21 Qui sequitur justitiam et misericordiam<br> inveniet vitam, justitiam, et gloriam.<br></p>
 <p>22 Civitatem fortium ascendit sapiens,<br> et destruxit robur fiduciæ ejus.<br></p>
 <p>23 Qui custodit os suum et linguam suam<br> custodit ab angustiis animam suam.<br></p>
 <p>24 Superbus et arrogans vocatur indoctus,<br> qui in ira operatur superbiam.<br></p>
 <p>25 Desideria occidunt pigrum:<br> noluerunt enim quidquam manus ejus operari.<br></p>
 <p>26 Tota die concupiscit et desiderat;<br> qui autem justus est, tribuet, et non cessabit.<br></p>
 <p>27 Hostiæ impiorum abominabiles,<br> quia offeruntur ex scelere.<br></p>
 <p>28 Testis mendax peribit;<br> vir obediens loquetur victoriam.<br></p>
 <p>29 Vir impius procaciter obfirmat vultum suum;<br> qui autem rectus est corrigit viam suam.<br></p>
 <p>30 Non est sapientia, non est prudentia,<br> non est consilium contra Dominum.<br></p>
 <p>31 Equus paratur ad diem belli;<br> Dominus autem salutem tribuit.]</p>
