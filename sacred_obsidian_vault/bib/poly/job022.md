 <p align="center"><a href="../kjv/job022.htm">KJV</a></p><p>1  Then Eliphaz<sup><a href="h0464.htm">464</a></sup> the Temanite<sup><a href="h8489.htm">8489</a></sup> answered<sup><a href="h6030.htm">6030</a></sup> and said,<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> </p>
 <p>2  Can a man<sup><a href="h1397.htm">1397</a></sup> be profitable<sup><a href="h5532.htm">5532</a></sup> unto God,<sup><a href="h0410.htm">410</a></sup> as he that<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> is wise<sup><a href="h7919.htm">7919</a></sup> may be profitable<sup><a href="h5532.htm">5532</a></sup> unto<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> himself? </p>
 <p>3  Is it any pleasure<sup><a href="h2656.htm">2656</a></sup> to the Almighty,<sup><a href="h7706.htm">7706</a></sup> that<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> thou art righteous?<sup><a href="h6663.htm">6663</a></sup> or<sup><a href="h0518.htm">518</a></sup> is it gain<sup><a href="h1215.htm">1215</a></sup> to him, that<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> thou makest thy ways perfect?<sup><a href="h8552.htm">8552</a></sup> <sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> </p>
 <p>4  Will he reprove<sup><a href="h3198.htm">3198</a></sup> thee for fear<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3374.htm">3374</a></sup> of thee? will he enter<sup><a href="h0935.htm">935</a></sup> with<sup><a href="h5973.htm">5973</a></sup> thee into judgment?<sup><a href="h4941.htm">4941</a></sup> </p>
 <p>5  Is not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> thy wickedness<sup><a href="h7451.htm">7451</a></sup> great?<sup><a href="h7227.htm">7227</a></sup> and thine iniquities<sup><a href="h5771.htm">5771</a></sup> infinite?<sup><a href="h0369.htm">369</a></sup> <sup><a href="h7093.htm">7093</a></sup> </p>
 <p>6  For<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> thou hast taken a pledge<sup><a href="h2254.htm">2254</a></sup> from thy brother<sup><a href="h0251.htm">251</a></sup> for naught,<sup><a href="h2600.htm">2600</a></sup> and stripped<sup><a href="h6584.htm">6584</a></sup> the naked<sup><a href="h6174.htm">6174</a></sup> of their clothing.<sup><a href="h0899.htm">899</a></sup> </p>
 <p>7  Thou hast not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> given water<sup><a href="h4325.htm">4325</a></sup> to the weary<sup><a href="h5889.htm">5889</a></sup> to drink,<sup><a href="h8248.htm">8248</a></sup> and thou hast withheld<sup><a href="h4513.htm">4513</a></sup> bread<sup><a href="h3899.htm">3899</a></sup> from the hungry.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h7457.htm">7457</a></sup> </p>
 <p>8  But as for the mighty<sup><a href="h2220.htm">2220</a></sup> man,<sup><a href="h0376.htm">376</a></sup> he had the earth;<sup><a href="h0776.htm">776</a></sup> and the honorable man<sup><a href="h5375.htm">5375</a></sup> <sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> dwelt<sup><a href="h3427.htm">3427</a></sup> in it. </p>
 <p>9  Thou hast sent widows away<sup><a href="h7971.htm">7971</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0490.htm">490</a></sup> empty,<sup><a href="h7387.htm">7387</a></sup> and the arms<sup><a href="h2220.htm">2220</a></sup> of the fatherless<sup><a href="h3490.htm">3490</a></sup> have been broken.<sup><a href="h1792.htm">1792</a></sup> </p>
 <p>10  Therefore<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> <sup><a href="h3651.htm">3651</a></sup> snares<sup><a href="h6341.htm">6341</a></sup> are round about<sup><a href="h5439.htm">5439</a></sup> thee, and sudden<sup><a href="h6597.htm">6597</a></sup> fear<sup><a href="h6343.htm">6343</a></sup> troubleth<sup><a href="h0926.htm">926</a></sup> thee; </p>
 <p>11  Or<sup><a href="h0176.htm">176</a></sup> darkness,<sup><a href="h2822.htm">2822</a></sup> that thou canst not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> see;<sup><a href="h7200.htm">7200</a></sup> and abundance<sup><a href="h8229.htm">8229</a></sup> of waters<sup><a href="h4325.htm">4325</a></sup> cover<sup><a href="h3680.htm">3680</a></sup> thee. </p>
 <p>12  Is not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> God<sup><a href="h0433.htm">433</a></sup> in the height<sup><a href="h1363.htm">1363</a></sup> of heaven?<sup><a href="h8064.htm">8064</a></sup> and behold<sup><a href="h7200.htm">7200</a></sup> the height<sup><a href="h7218.htm">7218</a></sup> of the stars,<sup><a href="h3556.htm">3556</a></sup> how<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> high they are!<sup><a href="h7311.htm">7311</a></sup> </p>
 <p>13  And thou sayest,<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> How<sup><a href="h4100.htm">4100</a></sup> doth God<sup><a href="h0410.htm">410</a></sup> know?<sup><a href="h3045.htm">3045</a></sup> can he judge<sup><a href="h8199.htm">8199</a></sup> through<sup><a href="h1157.htm">1157</a></sup> the dark cloud?<sup><a href="h6205.htm">6205</a></sup> </p>
 <p>14  Thick clouds<sup><a href="h5645.htm">5645</a></sup> are a covering<sup><a href="h5643.htm">5643</a></sup> to him, that he seeth<sup><a href="h7200.htm">7200</a></sup> not;<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> and he walketh<sup><a href="h1980.htm">1980</a></sup> in the circuit<sup><a href="h2329.htm">2329</a></sup> of heaven.<sup><a href="h8064.htm">8064</a></sup> </p>
 <p>15  Hast thou marked<sup><a href="h8104.htm">8104</a></sup> the old<sup><a href="h5769.htm">5769</a></sup> way<sup><a href="h0734.htm">734</a></sup> which<sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> wicked<sup><a href="h0205.htm">205</a></sup> men<sup><a href="h4962.htm">4962</a></sup> have trodden?<sup><a href="h1869.htm">1869</a></sup> </p>
 <p>16  Which<sup><a href="h0834.htm">834</a></sup> were cut down<sup><a href="h7059.htm">7059</a></sup> out of<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> time,<sup><a href="h6256.htm">6256</a></sup> whose foundation<sup><a href="h3247.htm">3247</a></sup> was overflown<sup><a href="h3332.htm">3332</a></sup> with a flood:<sup><a href="h5104.htm">5104</a></sup> </p>
 <p>17  Which said<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> unto God,<sup><a href="h0410.htm">410</a></sup> Depart<sup><a href="h5493.htm">5493</a></sup> from<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> us: and what<sup><a href="h4100.htm">4100</a></sup> can the Almighty<sup><a href="h7706.htm">7706</a></sup> do<sup><a href="h6466.htm">6466</a></sup> for them? </p>
 <p>18  Yet he<sup><a href="h1931.htm">1931</a></sup> filled<sup><a href="h4390.htm">4390</a></sup> their houses<sup><a href="h1004.htm">1004</a></sup> with good<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> things: but the counsel<sup><a href="h6098.htm">6098</a></sup> of the wicked<sup><a href="h7563.htm">7563</a></sup> is far<sup><a href="h7368.htm">7368</a></sup> from<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> me. </p>
 <p>19  The righteous<sup><a href="h6662.htm">6662</a></sup> see<sup><a href="h7200.htm">7200</a></sup> it, and are glad:<sup><a href="h8055.htm">8055</a></sup> and the innocent<sup><a href="h5355.htm">5355</a></sup> laugh them to scorn.<sup><a href="h3932.htm">3932</a></sup> </p>
 <p>20  Whereas<sup><a href="h0518.htm">518</a></sup> our substance<sup><a href="h7009.htm">7009</a></sup> is not<sup><a href="h3808.htm">3808</a></sup> cut down,<sup><a href="h3582.htm">3582</a></sup> but the remnant<sup><a href="h3499.htm">3499</a></sup> of them the fire<sup><a href="h0784.htm">784</a></sup> consumeth.<sup><a href="h0398.htm">398</a></sup> </p>
 <p>21  Acquaint<sup><a href="h5532.htm">5532</a></sup> now<sup><a href="h4994.htm">4994</a></sup> thyself with<sup><a href="h5973.htm">5973</a></sup> him, and be at peace:<sup><a href="h7999.htm">7999</a></sup> thereby good<sup><a href="h2896.htm">2896</a></sup> shall come<sup><a href="h0935.htm">935</a></sup> unto thee. </p>
 <p>22  Receive,<sup><a href="h3947.htm">3947</a></sup> I pray thee,<sup><a href="h4994.htm">4994</a></sup> the law<sup><a href="h8451.htm">8451</a></sup> from his mouth,<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h6310.htm">6310</a></sup> and lay up<sup><a href="h7760.htm">7760</a></sup> his words<sup><a href="h0561.htm">561</a></sup> in thine heart.<sup><a href="h3824.htm">3824</a></sup> </p>
 <p>23  If<sup><a href="h0518.htm">518</a></sup> thou return<sup><a href="h7725.htm">7725</a></sup> to<sup><a href="h5704.htm">5704</a></sup> the Almighty,<sup><a href="h7706.htm">7706</a></sup> thou shalt be built up,<sup><a href="h1129.htm">1129</a></sup> thou shalt put away iniquity<sup><a href="h5766.htm">5766</a></sup> far<sup><a href="h7368.htm">7368</a></sup> from thy tabernacles.<sup><a href="h4480.htm">4480</a></sup> <sup><a href="h0168.htm">168</a></sup> </p>
 <p>24  Then shalt thou lay up<sup><a href="h7896.htm">7896</a></sup> gold<sup><a href="h1220.htm">1220</a></sup> as<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> dust,<sup><a href="h6083.htm">6083</a></sup> and the gold of Ophir<sup><a href="h0211.htm">211</a></sup> as the stones<sup><a href="h6697.htm">6697</a></sup> of the brooks.<sup><a href="h5158.htm">5158</a></sup> </p>
 <p>25  Yea, the Almighty<sup><a href="h7706.htm">7706</a></sup> shall be<sup><a href="h1961.htm">1961</a></sup> thy defense,<sup><a href="h1220.htm">1220</a></sup> and thou shalt have plenty<sup><a href="h8443.htm">8443</a></sup> of silver.<sup><a href="h3701.htm">3701</a></sup> </p>
 <p>26  For<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> then<sup><a href="h0227.htm">227</a></sup> shalt thou have thy delight<sup><a href="h6026.htm">6026</a></sup> in<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> the Almighty,<sup><a href="h7706.htm">7706</a></sup> and shalt lift up<sup><a href="h5375.htm">5375</a></sup> thy face<sup><a href="h6440.htm">6440</a></sup> unto<sup><a href="h0413.htm">413</a></sup> God.<sup><a href="h0433.htm">433</a></sup> </p>
 <p>27  Thou shalt make thy prayer<sup><a href="h6279.htm">6279</a></sup> unto<sup><a href="h0413.htm">413</a></sup> him, and he shall hear<sup><a href="h8085.htm">8085</a></sup> thee, and thou shalt pay<sup><a href="h7999.htm">7999</a></sup> thy vows.<sup><a href="h5088.htm">5088</a></sup> </p>
 <p>28  Thou shalt also decree<sup><a href="h1504.htm">1504</a></sup> a thing,<sup><a href="h0562.htm">562</a></sup> and it shall be established<sup><a href="h6965.htm">6965</a></sup> unto thee: and the light<sup><a href="h0216.htm">216</a></sup> shall shine<sup><a href="h5050.htm">5050</a></sup> upon<sup><a href="h5921.htm">5921</a></sup> thy ways.<sup><a href="h1870.htm">1870</a></sup> </p>
 <p>29  When<sup><a href="h3588.htm">3588</a></sup> men are cast down,<sup><a href="h8213.htm">8213</a></sup> then thou shalt say,<sup><a href="h0559.htm">559</a></sup> There is lifting up;<sup><a href="h1466.htm">1466</a></sup> and he shall save<sup><a href="h3467.htm">3467</a></sup> the humble person.<sup><a href="h7807.htm">7807</a></sup> <sup><a href="h5869.htm">5869</a></sup> </p>
 <p>30  He shall deliver<sup><a href="h4422.htm">4422</a></sup> the island<sup><a href="h0336.htm">336</a></sup> of the innocent:<sup><a href="h5355.htm">5355</a></sup> and it is delivered<sup><a href="h4422.htm">4422</a></sup> by the pureness<sup><a href="h1252.htm">1252</a></sup> of thine hands.<sup><a href="h3709.htm">3709</a></sup> </p>

 <p>22 ἔκλαβε δὲ ἐκ στόματος αὐτοῦ ἐξηγορίαν καὶ ἀνάλαβε τὰ ῥήματα αὐτοῦ ἐν καρδίᾳ σου </p>

 <p align="center"><a href="../vul/job022.htm">Vul</a></p><p>1 Respondens autem Eliphaz Themanites, dixit:</p>
 <p>2 [Numquid Deo potest comparari homo,<br> etiam cum perfectæ fuerit scientiæ?<br></p>
 <p>3 Quid prodest Deo, si justus fueris?<br> aut quid ei confers, si immaculata fuerit via tua?<br></p>
 <p>4 Numquid timens arguet te,<br> et veniet tecum in judicium,<br></p>
 <p>5 et non propter malitiam tuam plurimam,<br> et infinitas iniquitates tuas?<br></p>
 <p>6 Abstulisti enim pignus fratrum tuorum sine causa,<br> et nudos spoliasti vestibus.<br></p>
 <p>7 Aquam lasso non dedisti,<br> et esurienti subtraxisti panem.<br></p>
 <p>8 In fortitudine brachii tui possidebas terram,<br> et potentissimus obtinebas eam.<br></p>
 <p>9 Viduas dimisisti vacuas,<br> et lacertos pupillorum comminuisti.<br></p>
 <p>10 Propterea circumdatus es laqueis,<br> et conturbat te formido subita.<br></p>
 <p>11 Et putabas te tenebras non visurum,<br> et impetu aquarum inundantium non oppressum iri?<br></p>
 <p>12 an non cogitas quod Deus excelsior cælo sit,<br> et super stellarum verticem sublimetur?<br></p>
 <p>13 Et dicis: Quid enim novit Deus?<br> et quasi per caliginem judicat.<br></p>
 <p>14 Nubes latibulum ejus, nec nostra considerat,<br> et circa cardines cæli perambulat.<br></p>
 <p>15 Numquid semitam sæculorum custodire cupis,<br> quam calcaverunt viri iniqui,<br></p>
 <p>16 qui sublati sunt ante tempus suum,<br> et fluvius subvertit fundamentum eorum?<br></p>
 <p>17 Qui dicebant Deo: Recede a nobis:<br> et quasi nihil posset facere Omnipotens, æstimabant eum,<br></p>
 <p>18 cum ille implesset domos eorum bonis:<br> quorum sententia procul sit a me.<br></p>
 <p>19 Videbunt justi, et lætabuntur,<br> et innocens subsannabit eos:<br></p>
 <p>20 nonne succisa est erectio eorum?<br> et reliquias eorum devoravit ignis?<br></p>
 <p>21 Acquiesce igitur ei, et habeto pacem,<br> et per hæc habebis fructus optimos.<br></p>
 <p>22 Suscipe ex ore illius legem,<br> et pone sermones ejus in corde tuo.<br></p>
 <p>23 Si reversus fueris ad Omnipotentem, ædificaberis,<br> et longe facies iniquitatem a tabernaculo tuo.<br></p>
 <p>24 Dabit pro terra silicem,<br> et pro silice torrentes aureos.<br></p>
 <p>25 Eritque Omnipotens contra hostes tuos,<br> et argentum coacervabitur tibi.<br></p>
 <p>26 Tunc super Omnipotentem deliciis afflues,<br> et elevabis ad Deum faciem tuam.<br></p>
 <p>27 Rogabis eum, et exaudiet te,<br> et vota tua reddes.<br></p>
 <p>28 Decernes rem, et veniet tibi,<br> et in viis tuis splendebit lumen.<br></p>
 <p>29 Qui enim humiliatus fuerit, erit in gloria,<br> et qui inclinaverit oculos, ipse salvabitur.<br></p>
 <p>30 Salvabitur innocens:<br> salvabitur autem in munditia manuum suarum.]</p>
