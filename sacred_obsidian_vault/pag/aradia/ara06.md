 <p><a name="page_21"><font size="1" color="green">p. 21</font></a></p> <h1 align="CENTER">CHAPTER IV</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">The Charm of the Stones Consecrated to Diana</h3> 
 <p>To find a stone with a hole in it is a special sign of the favour of Diana. He who does so shall take it in his hand and repeat the following, having observed the ceremony as enjoined:--</p> <i>
 </i><p align="CENTER">Scongiurazione della pietra bucata.</p>  <dir>  
 <p>Una pietra bucata<br> L'ho trovato;<br> Ne ringrazio il destin,<br> E lo spirito che su questa via<br> Mi ha portata,<br> Che passa essere il mio bene,<br> E la mia buona fortuna!</p> 
 <p>Mi alzo la mattina al alba,<br> E a passegio me ne vo<br> Nelle valli, monti e campi,<br> La fortuna cercarvo<br> Della ruta e la verbena,<br> Quello so porta fortuna</p>  </dir>  
 <p><a name="page_22"><font size="1" color="green">p. 22</font></a></p>  <dir>  
 <p>Me lo tengo in senno chiuso<br> E saperlo nessuno no le deve,<br> E cosi cio che commendo,<br> La verbena far <i>ben</i> per me!<br> Benedica quella strege!<br> Quella fàta che mi segna!"</p> <i>
 </i><p>Diana fu quella<br> Che mi venne la notte in sogno<br> E mi disse: "Se tu voir tener,<br> Le cattive persone da te lontano,<br> Devi tenere sempre ruta con te,<br> Sempre ruta con te e verbena!"</p> <i>
 </i><p>Diana, tu che siei la regina<br> Del cielo e della terra e dell'inferno,<br> E siei la prottetrice degli infelici,<br> Dei ladri, degli assassini, e anche<br> Di donne di mali affari se hai conosciuto,<br> Che non sia stato l'indole cattivo<br> Delle persone, tu <i>Diana</i>,<br> Diana il hai fatti tutti felici!</p> 
 <p>Una altra volta ti scongiuro<br> Che tu non abbia ne pace ne bene,<br> Tu possa essere sempre in mezzo alle pene,<br> Fino che la grazia che io ti chiedo<br> Non mi farai!</p>  </dir>  
 <p><a name="page_23"><font size="1" color="green">p. 23</font></a></p> <i>
 </i><p align="CENTER">Invocation to the Holy-Stone. <a name="fr_8"></a><a href="#fn_8"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  <dir>  
 <p>I have found<br> A holy-stone upon the ground.<br> O Fate! I thank thee for the happy find,<br> Also the spirit who upon this road<br> Hath given it to me;<br> And may it prove to be for my true good<br> And my good fortune!</p> 
 <p>I rise in the morning by the earliest dawn,<br> And I go forth to walk through (pleasant) vales,<br> All in the mountains or the meadows fair,<br> Seeking for luck while onward still I roam,<br> Seeking for rue and vervain scented sweet,<br> Because they bring good fortune unto all.<br> I keep them safely guarded in my bosom,<br> That none may know it--'tis a secret thing,<br> And sacred too, and thus I speak the spell:<br> "O vervain! ever be a benefit,<br> And may thy blessing be upon the witch<br> Or on the fairy who did give thee to me!"</p> 
 <p>It was <i>Diana</i> who did come to me,<br> All in the night in a dream, and said to me:<br> "If thou would'st keep all evil folk afar,<br> Then ever keep the vervain and the rue<br> Safely beside thee!"</p>  </dir>  
 <p>
 </p> 
 <p><a name="page_24"><font size="1" color="green">p. 24</font></a></p>  <dir>  
 <p>Great <i>Diana</i>! thou<br> Who art the queen of heaven and of earth,<br> And of the infernal lands--yea, thou who art<br> Protectress of all men unfortunate,<br> Of thieves and murderers, and of women too<br> Who lead an evil life, and yet hast known<br> That their nature was not evil, thou, Diana,<br> Hast still conferred on them some joy in life. <a name="fr_9"></a><a href="#fn_9"><font size="1">1</font></a></p> 
 <p>Or I may truly at another time<br> So conjure thee that thou shalt have no peace<br> Or happiness, for thou shalt ever be<br> In suffering until thou grantest that<br> Which I require in strictest faith from thee!</p>  </dir>  
 <p>[Here we have again the threatening the deity, just as in Eskimo or other Shamanism, which represents the rudest primitive form of conjuring, the spirits are menaced. A trace of this is to be found among rude Roman Catholics. Thus when St. Bruno, some years ago, at a town in the Romagna, did not listen to the prayers of his devotees for rain, they stuck his image in the mud of the river, head downwards. A rain speedily followed, and the saint was restored in honour to his place in the church.]</p> 
 <p>
 </p> 
 <p><a name="page_25"><font size="1" color="green">p. 25</font></a></p> <i>
 </i><p align="CENTER">The Spell or Conjuration of the Round Stone. <a name="fr_10"></a><a href="#fn_10"><font size="1">1</font></a></p> 
 <p>The finding a <i>round</i> stone, be it great or small, is a good sign (<i>e buono augurio</i>), but it should never be given away, because the receiver will then get the good luck, and some disaster befall the giver.</p> 
 <p>On finding a round stone, raise the eyes to heaven, and throw the stone up three times (catching it every time), and say:--</p>  <dir>  
 <p>Spirito del buono augurio!<br> Sei venuto in mio soccorso,<br> Credi ne avevo gran bisogno,<br> Spirito del folletino rosso<br> Giacche sei venuto in mio soccorso,<br> Ti prego di non mi abbandonare!<br> Ti prego dentro questa palla d'intrare,<br> E nella mia tasca tu possa portare,<br> Cosi in qualunque mia bisogna,<br> In mio aiuto ti posso chiamare,<br> E di giorno e di notte,<br> Tu non mi possa abbandonare.</p> 
 <p>Se danari da qualchuno avanzerò<br> E non mi vorra pagare,<br> Tu folletino rosso me il farei dare!<br> Si questo di non darmeli,<br> Si in testera tu vi anderai<br> E col tua <i>Brié--brié!</i></p>  </dir>  
 <p>
 </p> 
 <p><a name="page_26"><font size="1" color="green">p. 26</font></a></p>  <dir>  
 <p>Se dorme to desterai,<br> Panni dal letto laceraì,<br> Le farai tanta paura<br> Che allora di andare a dormire,<br> Andra alle bische a giuocare,<br> E tu nunqua lo seguirai.</p> 
 <p>E tu col tuo <i>Brié-brié</i>, le dirai,<br> Chi non paga deliti<br> Avranno pene e guai.</p> 
 <p>Cosi il debitare il giorno appresso,<br> O mi portera i danari,<br> O mi il mandera;<br> E cosi, folletino rosso!<br> Mi farai felice in mia vita,<br> Perche in qualcunque mia bisogna,<br> Verai in mio soccorso!</p> 
 <p>Se colla mia amante saro' adirato,<br> Tu spirito del buon augurio mio!<br> Andrai la notte da lei<br> Per i capelli la prenderai,<br> E nel letto mio la porterai;<br> E la mattina quando tutti gli spiriti<br> Vanno a riposare,<br> Tu prima di si' entrare<br> Nella tua palla si porterai<br> La mia bella nel suo letto,<br> Cosi te prego folletino,<br> Di entrare in questa mia palla!</p>  </dir>  
 <p><a name="page_27"><font size="1" color="green">p. 27</font></a></p>  <dir>  
 <p>E di ubbidire a tutti miei commandi!<br> Ed io ti porteró<br> Sempre nella tasca mia,<br> Che tu non mi vada via.</p>  </dir>  <i>
 </i><p align="CENTER">The Conjuration.</p>  <dir>  
 <p>Spirit of good omen,<br> Who art come to aid me,<br> Believe I had great need of thee.<br> Spirit of the Red Goblin,<br> Since thou hast come to aid me in my need,<br> I pray of thee do not abandon me:<br> I beg of thee to enter now this stone,<br> That in my pocket I may carry thee,<br> And so when anything is needed by me,<br> I can call unto thee: be what it may,<br> Do not abandon me by night or day.</p> 
 <p>Should I lend money unto any man<br> Who will not pay when due, I pray of thee,<br> Thou the Red Goblin, make him pay his debt!<br> And if he will not and is obstinate,<br> Go at him with thy cry of "<i>Brié--brié!</i>"<br> And if he sleeps, awake him with a twitch,<br> And pull the covering off and frighten him!<br> And follow him about where'er he goes.</p> 
 <p>So teach him with thy ceaseless "<i>Brié--brié!</i>"<br> That he who obligation e'er forgets<br> Shall be in trouble till he pays his debts. <a name="page_28"><font size="1" color="green">p. 28</font></a><br> And so my debtor on the following day<br> Shall either bring the money which he owes,<br> Or send it promptly: so I pray of thee,<br> O my Red Goblin, come unto my aid!<br> Or should I quarrel with her whom I love,<br> Then, spirit of good luck, I pray thee go<br> To her while sleeping--pull her by the hair,<br> And bear her through the night unto my bed!<br> And in the morning, when all spirits go<br> To their repose, do thou, ere thou return'st<br> Into thy stone, carry her home again,<br> And leave her there asleep. Therefore, O Sprite!<br> I beg thee in this pebble make thy home!<br> Obey in every way all I command.<br> So in my pocket thou shalt ever be,<br> And thou and I will ne'er part company!</p>  </dir>
