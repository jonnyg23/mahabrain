 <h1 align="center">ABORTION IN THE BIBLE</h1>
 <p align="center">by J.B. Hare</p>  
 <p align="center"><small><i>August, 2008</i></small></p>  
 <p align="center"><small><a href="#cnote">copyright terms</a></small></p>
 <p>As far as I can tell there is no use of the term 'abort' in the sense of a medical procedure in the Old or New Testament of the Bible. There are 26 Verses total in the OT and NT with one of the three Greek or Hebrew words in Strong's Lexicon potentially meaning 'abortion.' However, as will be demonstrated, in context, none of the uses in the Bible text has any bearing on the medical procedure of abortion.</p>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p>In other words, <i>there is no Bible verse that can be cited which specifically says 'thou shalt not abort.'</i></p>  
 <p> </p>  
 <p>The data is presented below. The following is a list of each Greek and Hebrew words in the Bible, which have, as one possible meaning, 'abort,' collated with each instance in the Bible. The numbers in the KJV text below are the 'Strongs' numbers for Greek or Hebrew words. Greek entries are prefixed by 'G' and Hebrew entries by 'H'. Numbers in specific verses are distinguished by context.</p>  
 <p>The Strong's numbers can be found in the <a href="../../bib/poly/index.htm">Polyglot Bible</a> if you want to check these results.</p>
 <p> </p>
