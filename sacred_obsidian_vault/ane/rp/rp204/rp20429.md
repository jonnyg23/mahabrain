 <p><a name="page_135"><font size="1" color="green">p. 135</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">MONOLITH INSCRIPTION OF ARGISTIS, KING OF VAN</h3>  
 <p>1. On departing [out] of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Bias</span></p>  
 <p>2. [I conquered the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khu]sas</span>. On approaching the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Didis</span></p>  
 <p>3. (and) the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Zuas</span>, the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Zuas</span></p>  
 <p>4. belonging to the son of Diaus, I partitioned.</p>  
 <p>5. I set up a tablet in the country of the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Zuas</span>. <a name="fr_484"></a><a href="#fn_480"><font size="1">1</font></a></p>  
 <p>6. Among the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Asqalaians</span> 105 palace[s]</p>  
 <p>7. I dug up; 453 cities [I] partitioned.</p>  
 <p>8. The people of three countries [I] despoiled.</p>  
 <p>9. The men with fire [I] burnt.</p>  
 <p>10. In a part of the country the district of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Qalis</span> [I destroyed]</p>  
 <p>11. (as well as) the city of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Sasilus</span> <a name="fr_485"></a><a href="#fn_481"><font size="1">2</font></a> in the country of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Asqalaians</span>. [I carried away]</p>  
 <p>12. 15,181 children, 2734 men,</p>  
 <p>13. 10(?)604 women, 4426 [horses],</p>  
 <p>14. 10(?)478 oxen, (and) 73,7(00) [sheep].</p>  
 <p>15. The [two] kings I reduced to vassalage, <a name="fr_486"></a><a href="#fn_482"><font size="1">3</font></a> (namely) Saski … <a name="fr_487"></a><a href="#fn_483"><font size="1">4</font></a></p>  
 <p>16. the son of Ardarakis (and) Qabi … the son of Baltul.</p>  
 <p>17. Governors (and) lawgivers I established. The king, the son of Dia(us),</p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p>
 </p>  
 <p><a name="page_136"><font size="1" color="green">p. 136</font></a></p>  
 <p>18. I appointed governor. I changed his [name],</p>  
 <p>19. and he, the son of Diaus, to Argistis</p>  
 <p>20. brought 41 manehs of gold, 37 manehs [of silver],</p>  
 <p>21. 1(?)0,000 manehs of bronze, 1000 war magazines, 300 oxen,</p>  
 <p>22. (and) 2(?)0,000 sheep; and he submitted to (my) laws.</p>  
 <p>23. In the [land] of the son of Diaus I established as tribute (and) offerings</p>  
 <p>24. [41 (?)] manehs of gold, 10,000 manehs [of bronze],</p>  
 <p>25. … oxen, 100 wild bulls, 300 sheep, (and) 300 war magazines.</p>  
 <p>26. The cavalry, the horses, (and) the officers [I] took (with me).</p>  
 <p>27. To the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khaldises</span> I prayed, to the powers supreme,</p>  
 <p>28. who have given the land of the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Etiuians</span> as a present</p>  
 <p>29. to the family of Argistis; to <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khaldis</span> the giver,</p>  
 <p>30. to the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khaldises</span>, the supreme, the givers, to the children of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khaldis</span>,</p>  
 <p>31. the propitious, I prayed, even to (the gods) of Argistis</p>  
 <p>32. the son of Menuas; I brought offerings to the <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Khaldis[es]</span>.</p>  
 <p>33. Argistis says: The whole [of the country]</p>  
 <p>34. (and) the fortresses of the son of Diaus [I occupied].</p>  
 <p>35. I conquered the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lusas</span>, the country of Katarzas,</p>  
 <p>36. the country of the son of Erias, the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Gulutakhikhas</span></p>  
 <p>37. (and) the country of the son of Uidhaerus.</p>  
 <p>38. I departed out of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Abunis</span>; the king of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Lusas</span></p>  
 <p>39. I reduced to vassalage; as governor of the country of <span style="font-variant:small-caps;">Igas</span></p>  
 <p>40. … I appointed (him). He submitted to the laws of Argistis.</p>

 <h3 align="CENTER">Footnotes</h3>
 <p><a name="fn_480"></a><a href="rp20429.htm#fr_484">135:1</a> This had already been done by his father Menuas, whose inscription has been copied by Schulz and translated by myself (Sayce, xxx).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_481"></a><a href="rp20429.htm#fr_485">135:2</a> Sasilus is described by Menuas as "a royal city" of the son of Diaus or Diaves, in the country called Dayaeni by the Assyrians.</p>
 <p><a name="fn_482"></a><a href="rp20429.htm#fr_486">135:3</a> Literally "I brought to my side" (<i>va-dia-du-bi</i>).</p>
 <p><a name="fn_483"></a><a href="rp20429.htm#fr_487">135:4</a> He is stated by Menuas to be "a native of the city of Khaldiris," a word which signifies "the Khaldirian" in Vannic, and shows that the Vannic language was spoken in the place.</p><p> </p>    </body>
