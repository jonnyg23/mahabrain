 <p><a name="page_38"><font size="1" color="green">p. 38</font></a> <a name="page_39"><font size="1" color="green">p. 39</font></a></p>  <h1 align="center">The Third Tablet</h1>  
 <p>1. An<u>sh</u>ar opened his mouth, and</p>  
 <p>2. [Unto Gaga], his [minister], spake the word:</p>  
 <p>3. "[O Gaga, thou minis]ter that rejoicest my spirit,</p>  
 <p>4. ''[Unto La<u>h</u>mu and La<u>h</u>]amu will I send thee.</p>  
 <p>5. "[...] thou canst attain,</p>  
 <p>6. ''[...] thou shalt cause to be brought before thee.</p>  
 <p>7. [... let] the gods, all of them,</p>  
 <p>8. "[Make ready for a feast], at a banquet let them sit,</p>  
 <p>9. "[Let them eat bread], let them mix wine,</p>  
 <p>10. ''[That for Marduk], their avenger, they may decree the fate.</p>  
 <p>11. "[Go,] Gaga, stand before them,</p>  
 <p>12. ''[And all that] I, tell thee, repeat unto them, (and say):</p>  
 <p><a name="page_40"><font size="1" color="green">p. 40</font></a> <a name="page_41"><font size="1" color="green">p. 41</font></a></p>  
 <p>13. "[An<u>sh</u>ar], your son, hath sent me,</p>  
 <p>14. "[The purpose] of his heart he hath made known unto me.</p>  
 <p>15. "[He saith that Tia]mat our mother hath conceived a hatred for us,</p>  
 <p>16. "[With all] her force she rageth, full of wrath.</p>  
 <p>17. "All the gods have turned to her,</p>  
 <p>18. "With those, whom ye created, they go at her side.</p>  
 <p>19. ''They are banded together, and at the side of Tiamat they advance;</p>  
 <p>20 . "They are furious, they devise mischief without resting night and day.</p>  
 <p>21. ''They prepare for battle, fuming and raging;</p>  
 <p>22. "They have joined their forces and are making war.</p>  
 <p>23. "Ummu-<u>H</u>ubur, who formed all things,</p>  
 <p>24. "Hath made in addition weapons invincible, she hath spawned monster-serpents,</p>  
 <p>25. "Sharp of tooth and merciless of fang.</p>  
 <p>26. "With poison instead of blood she hath filled heir bodies.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_42"><font size="1" color="green">p. 42</font></a> <a name="page_43"><font size="1" color="green">p. 43</font></a></p>  
 <p>27. "Fierce monster-vipers she hath clothed with terror,</p>  
 <p>28. "With splendour she hath decked them, she hath made them of lofty stature.</p>  
 <p>29. "Whoever beholdeth them, terror overcometh him,</p>  
 <p>30. "Their bodies rear up and none can withstand their attack.</p>  
 <p>31. "She hath set up vipers, and dragons, and the (monster) La<u>h</u>amu,</p>  
 <p>32. "And hurricanes, and raging hounds, and scorpion-men,</p>  
 <p>33. "And mighty tempests, and fish-men, and rams;</p>  
 <p>34. They bear merciless weapons, without fear of the fight.</p>  
 <p>35. "Her commands are mighty, none can resist them;</p>  
 <p>36. "After this fashion, huge of stature, hath she made eleven (monsters).</p>  
 <p>37. "Among the gods who are her sons, inasmuch as he hath given her [support],</p>  
 <p>38. "She hath exalted Kingu; in their midst she hath raised [him] to power.</p>  
 <p>39. ''To march before the forces, [to lead the host],</p>  
 <p>40. "[To] give the battle-signal, to advance [to the attack],</p>  
 <p>41. "[To direct] the battle, to control the [fight],</p>  
 <p><a name="page_44"><font size="1" color="green">p. 44</font></a> <a name="page_45"><font size="1" color="green">p. 45</font></a></p>  
 <p>42. "Unto him [hath she entrusted; in costly raiment] she hath made him sit, (saying):</p>  
 <p>43. "'[I have] uttered thy spell, in the assembly of the gods [I have raised thee to power],</p>  
 <p>44. "'[The] dominion over all the gods [have I entrusted unto thee].</p>  
 <p>45. "'[Be] thou exalted, [thou] my chosen spouse,</p>  
 <p>46. "' May they magnify thy name over all of [them ... the Anunnaki].'</p>  
 <p>47. "She hath given him the Tablets of Destiny, on his breast she laid them, (saying):</p>  
 <p>48. "'Thy command shall not be without avail, and the word of [thy] mouth shall be established.'</p>  
 <p>49. "Now Kingu, (thus) exalted, having received [the power of Anu],</p>  
 <p>50. "Decreed the fate for the gods, her sons, (saying):</p>  
 <p>51. "'Let the opening of your mouth quench the Fire-god;</p>  
 <p>52. "'Whoso is exalted in the battle, let him display (his) might! '</p>  
 <p><a name="page_46"><font size="1" color="green">p. 46</font></a> <a name="page_47"><font size="1" color="green">p. 47</font></a></p>  
 <p>53. ''I sent Anu, but he could not withstand her;</p>  
 <p>54. "Nudimmud was afraid and turned back.</p>  
 <p>55. "But Marduk hath set out, the director of the gods, your son;</p>  
 <p>56. ''To set out against Tiamat his heart hath prompted (him).</p>  
 <p>57. "He opened his mouth and spake unto me, (saying):</p>  
 <p>58. "'If I, your avenger,</p>  
 <p>59. "'Conquer Tiamat and give you life,</p>  
 <p>60. "'Appoint an assembly, make my fate preeminent and proclaim it.</p>  
 <p>61. "'In Up<u>sh</u>ukkinaku seat yourselves joyfully together;</p>  
 <p>62. "'With my word in place of you will I decree fate.</p>  
 <p>63. "'May whatsoever I do remain unaltered,</p>  
 <p>64. "'May the word of my lips never be changed nor made of no avail.'</p>  
 <p>65. "Hasten, therefore, and swiftly decree for him the fate which you bestow,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_48"><font size="1" color="green">p. 48</font></a> <a name="page_49"><font size="1" color="green">p. 49</font></a></p>  
 <p>66. "That he may go and fight your strong enemy!"</p>  
 <p>67. Gaga went, he took his way and</p>  
 <p>68. Humbly before La<u>h</u>mu and La<u>h</u>amu, the gods, his fathers,</p>  
 <p>69. He made obeisance, and he kissed the ground at their feet.</p>  
 <p>70. He humbled himself; then he stood up and spake unto them, (saying):</p>  
 <p>71. "An<u>sh</u>ar, your son, hath sent me,</p>  
 <p>72. ''The purpose of his heart he hath made known unto me.</p>  
 <p>73. "He saith that Tiamat our mother hath conceived a hatred for us,</p>  
 <p>74. "With all her force she rageth, full of wrath.</p>  
 <p>75. "All the gods have turned to her,</p>  
 <p>76. "With those, whom ye created, they go at her side.</p>  
 <p>77. "They are banded together and at the side of Tiamat they advance;</p>  
 <p><a name="page_50"><font size="1" color="green">p. 50</font></a> <a name="page_51"><font size="1" color="green">p. 51</font></a></p>  
 <p>78. ''They are furious, they devise mischief without resting night and day.</p>  
 <p>79. "They prepare for battle, fuming and raging;</p>  
 <p>80. ''They have joined their forces and are making war.</p>  
 <p>81. ''Ummu-<u>H</u>ubur, who formed all things,</p>  
 <p>82. "Hath made in addition weapons invincible, she hath spawned monster-serpents,</p>  
 <p>83. "Sharp of tooth and merciless of fang.</p>  
 <p>84. "With poison instead of blood she hath filled their bodies.</p>  
 <p>85. ''Fierce monster-vipers she hath clothed with terror,</p>  
 <p>86. "With splendour she hath decked them, she hath made them of lofty stature.</p>  
 <p>87. ''Whoever beholdeth them, terror overcometh him,</p>  
 <p>88. "Their bodies rear up and none can withstand their attack.</p>  
 <p>89. ''She hath set up vipers, and dragons, and the (monster) Lahamu,</p>  
 <p>90. "And hurricanes, and raging hounds, and scorpion-men,</p>  
 <p><a name="page_52"><font size="1" color="green">p. 52</font></a> <a name="page_53"><font size="1" color="green">p. 53</font></a></p>  
 <p>91. ''And mighty tempests, and fish-men, and [rams];</p>  
 <p>92. "They bear merciless weapons, without fear of the fight.</p>  
 <p>93. "Her commands are mighty, none can resist them;</p>  
 <p>94. ''After this fashion, huge of stature, hath she made eleven (monsters).</p>  
 <p>95. ''Among the gods who are her sons, inasmuch as he hath given her support,</p>  
 <p>96. "She hath exalted Kingu; in their midst she hath raised him to power.</p>  
 <p>97. "To march before the forces, to lead the host,</p>  
 <p>98. ''To give the battle-signal, to advance to the attack,</p>  
 <p>99. "To direct the battle, to control the fight,</p>  
 <p>100. ''Unto him hath she entrusted; in costly raiment she hath made him sit, (saying):</p>  
 <p>101. "'I have uttered thy spell, in the assembly of the gods I have raised thee to power,</p>  
 <p>102. "'The dominion over all the gods have I entrusted unto thee.</p>  
 <p>103. "'Be thou exalted, thou my chosen spouse,</p>  
 <p>104. "'May they magnify thy name over all of them ... the Anunna[ki].'</p>  
 <p>105. "She hath given him the Tablets of Destiny, on [his] breast [she laid them], (saying):</p>  
 <p><a name="page_54"><font size="1" color="green">p. 54</font></a> <a name="page_55"><font size="1" color="green">p. 55</font></a></p>  
 <p>106. "'Thy command shall not be without avail, [and the word of thy mouth shall be established].</p>  
 <p>107. "Now Kingu, (thus) exalted, [having received the power of Anu],</p>  
 <p>108. "[Decreed the fate] for the gods, her sons, (saying):</p>  
 <p>109. "'Let the opening of your mouth [quench] the Fire-god;</p>  
 <p>110. "'Whoso is exalted in the battle, [let him display] (his) might!'</p>  
 <p>111. "I sent Anu, but he could not [withstand her];</p>  
 <p>112. "Nudimmud was afraid and [turned back].</p>  
 <p>113. "But Marduk hath set out, the director of the[gods, your son];</p>  
 <p>114. "T o set out against Tiamat [his heart hath prompted (him)].</p>  
 <p>115. "He opened his mouth [and spake unto me], (saying):</p>  
 <p>116. "'If I, [your avenger],</p>  
 <p>117. "'Conquer Tiamat and [give you life],</p>  
 <p>118. "'Appoint an assembly, [make my fate preeminent and proclaim it].</p>  
 <p>119. "'In Up<u>sh</u>ukkinaku [seat yourselves joyfully together];</p>  
 <p>120. "'With my word in place of [you will I decree fate].</p>  
 <p>121. "'May whatsoever [I] do remain unaltered,</p>  
 <p>122. "'May the word of [my lips] never be changed nor made of no avail.'</p>  
 <p><a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="green">p. 56</font></a> <a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="green">p. 57</font></a></p>  
 <p>123. ''Hasten, therefore, and swiftly [decree for him] the fate which you bestow,</p>  
 <p>124. "That he may go and fight your strong enemy!"</p>  
 <p>125. La<u>h</u>mu and La<u>h</u>amu heard and cried aloud,</p>  
 <p>126. All of the Igigi wailed bitterly, (saying):</p>  
 <p>127. ''What has been altered so that they should ... [...]</p>  
 <p>128. ''We do not understand the d[eed] of Tiamat!"</p>  
 <p>129. Then did they collect and go,</p>  
 <p>130. The great gods, all of them, who decree [fate].</p>  
 <p>131. They entered in before An<u>sh</u>ar, they filled [...];</p>  
 <p>132. They kissed one another, in the assembly [...].</p>  
 <p>133. They made ready for the feast, at the banquet [they sat];</p>  
 <p>134. They ate bread, they mixed [sesame-wine].</p>  
 <p>135. The sweet drink, the mead, confused their [...],</p>  
 <p>136. They were drunk with drinking, their bodies were filled.</p>  
 <p>137. They were wholly at ease, their spirit was exalted;</p>  
 <p>138. Then for Marduk, their avenger, did they decree the fate.</p>
