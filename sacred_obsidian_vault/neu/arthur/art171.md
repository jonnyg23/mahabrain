 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">GUENEVERE</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">SARA TEASDALE</h2>  </center>  <br>  <br> I was a queen, and I have lost my crown;<br> A wife, and I have broken all my vows;<br> A lover, and I ruined him I loved:--<br> There is no other havoc left to do.<br> A little month ago I was a queen,<br> And mothers held their babies up to see<br> When I came riding out of Camelot.<br> The women smiled, and all the world smiled too.<br> And now, what woman's eyes would smile on me?<br> I am still beautiful, and yet what child<br> Would think of me as some high, heaven-sent thing,<br> An angel, clad in gold and miniver?<br> The world would run from me, and yet I am<br> No different from the queen they used to love.<br> If water, flowing silver over stones,<br> Is forded, and beneath the horses' feet<br> Grows turbid suddenly, it clears again,<br> And men will drink it with no thought of harm.<br> Yet I am branded for a single fault.<br> <br> I was the flower amid a toiling world,<br> Where people smiled to see one happy thing,<br> And they were proud and glad to raise me high;<br> They only asked that I should be right fair,<br> A little kind, and gownèd wondrously,<br> And surely it were little praise to me<br> If I had pleased them well throughout my life.<br> <br> I was a queen, the daughter of a king.<br> The crown was never heavy on my head,<br> It was my right, and was a part of me.<br> The women thought me proud, the men were kind,<br> And bowed down gallantly to kiss my hand,<br> And watched me as I passed them calmly by,<br> Along the halls I shall not tread again.<br> What if, to-night, I should revisit them?<br> The warders at the gates, the kitchen-maids,<br> The very beggars would stand off from me,<br> And I, their queen, would climb the stairs alone,<br> Pass through the banquet-hall, a hated thing,<br> And seek my chambers for a hiding-place,<br> And I should find them but a sepulchre,<br> The very rushes rotted on the floors,<br> The fire in ashes on the freezing hearth.<br> <br> I was a queen, and he who loved me best<br> Made me a woman for a night and day,<br> And now I go unqueened forevermore.<br> <br> A queen should never dream on summer nights,<br> When hovering spells are heavy in the dusk:--<br> I think no night was ever quite so still,<br> So smoothly lit with red along the west,<br> So deeply hushed with quiet through and through.<br> And strangely clear, and sharply dyed with light,<br> The trees stood straight against a paling sky,<br> With Venus burning lamp-like in the west.<br> I walked alone among a thousand flowers,<br> That drooped their heads and drowsed beneath the dew,<br> And all my thoughts were quieted to sleep.<br> Behind me, on the walk, I heard a step--<br> I did not know my heart could tell his tread,<br> I did not know I loved him till that hour.<br> The garden reeled a little, I was weak,<br> And in my breast I felt a wild, sick pain.<br> Quickly he came behind me, caught my arms,<br> That ached beneath his touch; and then I swayed,<br> My head fell backward and I saw his face.<br> <br> All this grows bitter that was once so sweet,<br> And many mouths must drain the dregs of it,<br> But none will pity me, nor pity him<br> Whom Love so lashed, and with such cruel thongs.<br>
