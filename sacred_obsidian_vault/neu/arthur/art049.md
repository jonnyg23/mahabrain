 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">THE DREAM OF SIR GALAHAD</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">MADISON CAWEIN</h2>  </center>  <br>   <i> With the knights Peredur and Gawain he sits, in a chapel in Lyonesse, speaking while the dawn slowly reddens on the sea, gray-seen through the open door.</i>  <br> 		I<br> Cast on sleep there came to me<br> Three great angels, o'er the sea<br> Moaning near the priory:<br> Cloudy clad in awful white,<br> Each one's face, a lucid light,<br> Rayed and blossomed out of night.  <br> <br> 		II<br> <br> In my sleep I saw them rest,<br> Each, a long hand on her breast,<br> Like the new-moon in the west:<br> And their hair like sunset rolled<br> Down their shoulders, burning cold,<br> An insufferable gold.<br> <br> 		III<br> <br> Flaming round each high brow bent<br> Fourfold starry gold, that sent<br> Light before them as they went:<br> 'Neath their burning crowns their eyes<br> Shone like awful stars the skies<br> Rock in shattered storm that flies.<br> <br> 		IV<br> <br> Dark their eyes were, lurid dark;<br> And within their eyes a spark<br> Like the opal's burned:  my sark<br> Seemed to shrivel 'neath their gaze;<br> As, with marvel and amaze, <br> All my soul it seemed to raise.<br> <br> 		V<br> <br> And I saw their mouths were fire,<br> Ruby-red as the desire<br> Of the Sanc Graal:  fair and dire<br> Were their lips, whereon the kiss<br> Of all Heaven lay;  the bliss<br> Of all happiness that is.<br> <br> 		VI<br> <br> Calm as Beauty lying dead,<br> Tapers lit at feet and head,<br> Were they, round whom prayers seemed said:<br> Fragrant as that woman who,<br> Born of blossoms and of dew<br> And of magic, wedded Llew.<br> <br> 		VII<br> <br> And the first one said to me:--<br> "Thou has slept thus holily<br> While seven sands ran shadowy;<br> Earth hath served thee like a slave,<br> Serving us who found thee brave,<br> Pure of life and great to save:<br> <br> 		VIII<br> <br> "Know!"--She touched my brow:  a pain <br> As of arrows pierced my brain:<br> Ceased:  and earth, both sea and plain,<br> Vanished:  and I stood where thought<br> Stands, and worship, spirit-fraught,<br> Watching how the heavens are wrought.<br> <br> 		IX<br> <br> Then the second said to me:<br> "Thou hast come all sinlessly<br> Thro' life's sin-enveloped sea:<br> Know the things thou hast not seen:<br> Filling all the soul with sheen;<br> Meaning more than earth may mean:<br> <br> 		X<br> <br> "See!"--Her voice sang like a lyre,<br> Comprehending all desire<br> In its gamut's throbbing fire:--<br> And my inner eyelids,--which<br> Dimmed clairvoyance,--raised:  and rich,<br> As one chord's vibrating pitch,<br> <br> 		XI<br> <br> Grew my soul with light:  that saw<br> The embodiment of awe,<br> Love, divinity, and law,<br> Orbed and eöned:  and the power,<br> Circumstance, like some vast flower;<br> From which time fell, hour on hour.<br> <br> 		XII<br> <br> 'Neath the third one's mighty will<br> All my soul lay very still,<br> Feeling all its being thrill<br> As she, smiling, said to me:<br> "Thou dost know, and thou canst see:<br> What thou art arise and be!"<br> <br> 		XIII<br> <br> To my lips her lips she pressed;<br> And my new-born soul, thrice-blessed,<br> Clasped her radiance and caressed:<br> Mounted and, in glory clad,<br> Soared with them who chorused glad:<br> "Christ awaits thee, Galahad!"<br>
