 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN">An Arthurian Miscellany at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <center> <h2 align="CENTER">SIR PALAMIDES</h2>  <h3 align="CENTER">by</h3>  <h2 align="CENTER">JOHN GROSVENOR WILSON</h2>  </center>  <br>   Sir Palamides, Saracen, <br>  Right worshipful among the men <br>  Of Arthur's days, rode thro' the fen. <br>  <br>  Till, past the skirts of fen and wood, <br>  On lonely Humber's bank he stood, <br>  Grateful for that sad solitude. <br>  <br>  And looking, aimless, east and west, <br>  Bemoaned his love and beat his breast, <br>  And yearned to end his ceaseless quest. <br>  <br>  When up the stream in silence sped<br>  A royal barge, and huge and red<br>  Grinned at the prow a wyvern's head. <br>  <br>  Red silk swept o'er the deck, and made <br>  A stately couch, whereon was laid <br>  The body of a King, arrayed<br>  <br>  In robes of state, and in the hand<br>  It held a scroll, and to the land<br>  The barge drave fast and keeled the sand. <br>  <br>  Sir Palamides leaped on deck, -- <br>  "Alack!" quoth he, "a royal wreck!" <br>   And touched the crescent at his neck, <br>  <br>  Unrolled the scroll, and this it said -- <br>  "Hermancè, of the City Red, <br>  Father and King, here lieth dead. <br>  <br>  "Murdered by him he loved the best. <br>  Good knight, to set his soul at rest, <br>  Pray sail the river on his quest." <br>  <br>  Then swore the knight, upon his knees, <br>  To sail the quest, and with the breeze<br>  He set the prow toward the seas. <br>  <br>  Adown long Humber swift he sailed, <br>  Anon some castle-warder hailed, <br>  Or knight that rode full-armed and mailed. <br>  <br>  He sailed into the open sea, <br>  And still the wind blew fair and free. <br>  "I marvel at this quest," quoth he. <br>  <br>  But seaward yet the vessel bore, <br>  Until an island rose before, <br>  And straight the barge drave to the shore. <br>  <br>  People that watched there to him spake, -- <br>  "Thou seekest Helius, for whose sake<br>  Our King's good heart did bleed and break. <br>  <br>  "For lo, our King was kind and mild, <br>  But very old, nor blessed with child, <br>  Wherefore he sought this monster wild, <br>  <br>  "Made him as if he were his own; <br>  But he so longed to sit the throne<br>  He killed the King, and we made moan; <br>  <br>  "He and his knights then mocked and said, -- <br>  'Bring forth the royal barge of red<br>  And set it sailing with the dead.' <br>  <br>  "The which we did, but slipt within <br>  His hand the scroll that told the sin, <br>  And now, please God, the right shall win." <br>  <br>  "Yea," said the knight, "an' if God please:" <br>  Addresses himself, and cried, -- "Let these <br>  Base knights come now; I take no ease<br>  <br>  "Until I lay them low in dust<br>  And hang their armor up to rust. <br>  God and the Prophet shield the just!" <br>   <br>  Then Helius and his knights were wroth, <br>  And seizing arms they hurtled forth <br>  As when the wind blows from the north. <br>  <br>  Many they were, and woful strong; <br>  They fought the Saracen so long, <br>  It seemed that right would yield to wrong. <br>  <br>  The people watching made great dole, <br>  And wept with pity for his soul, <br>  And shades of night began to roll. <br>  <br>  Till, with a thrust clear thro' the head, <br>  The Saracen smote Helius dead; <br>  And who were left in terror fled. <br>  <br>  Uprose great shouts of joy, and all, <br>  With banners, marching from the wall<br>  Of the Red City, loud did call. <br>  <br>  And hail him King: whereat the knight <br>  Spake thus, -- "That I have won the fight<br>  I praise God's grace, not mine own might. <br>  <br>  "But as for me -- alas, I needs <br>  Must wander on among the weeds, <br>  That haply I may yet do deeds <br>  <br>  "Of honor fair, and ease my heart, <br>  Wear out the pain, and dull the smart, <br>  Wherefore, good friends, I must depart." <br>  <br>  Then, stepping in a barget gray, <br>  Sir Palamides went his way, <br>  And sighed for Isolt, night and day. <br>  <br>  <br>  <br>
