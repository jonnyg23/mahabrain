 <p> </p>  
 <p><small>THE Feast Day of Mary the Great is the 15th day of August. Early in the morning of this day the people go into their fields and pluck ears of corn, generally here, to make the 'Moilean Moire.' These ears are laid on a rock exposed to the sun, to dry. When dry, they are husked in the hand, winnowed in a fan, ground in a quern, kneaded on a sheep-skin, and formed into a bannock, which is called 'Moilean Moire,' the fatling of Mary. The bannock is toasted before a fire of fagots of rowan, or some other sacred wood. Then the husbandman breaks the bannock and gives a bit to his wife and to each of his children, in order according to their ages, and the family raise the 'Iolach Mhoire Mhathar,' the Paean of Mary Mother who promised <a name="page_195"><font size="1" color="green">p. 195</font></a> to shield them, and who did and will shield them from scath till the day of death. While singing thus, the family walk sunwise round the fire, the father leading, the mother following, and the children following according to age.</small></p>  
 <p>After going round the fire, the man puts the embers of the fagot-fire, with bits of old iron, into a pot, which he carries sunwise round the outside of his house, sometimes round his steadings and his fields, and his flocks gathered in for the purpose. He is followed without as within by his household, all singing the praise of Mary Mother the while.</p>  
 <p>The scene is striking and picturesque, the family being arrayed in their brightest and singing their best.</p>

 <p>LA feill Moire   cubhr,<br>   Mathair Buachaille nan treud,<br>   Bhuain mi beum dhe’n toradh ur,<br>   Chruadhaich mi e caon ri grein,<br>   Shuath mi e gu geur dhe ’n rusg<br>        Le mo bhasa fein.</p>   
 <p>Mheil mi e air   brath Di-aoine,<br>   Dh’ fhuin mi e air cra na caoire,<br>   Bhruich mi e ri aine caorain,<br>   S’ phairtich mi e’n dail mo dhaoine.</p>   
 <p>Chaidh mi   deiseil m’ ardrach,<br>   An ainm Mhoire Mhathar,<br>   A gheall mo ghleidheadh,<br>   A rinn mo ghleidheadh,<br>   A ni mo ghleidheadh,<br>   Ann an sith, ann an ni,<br>   Ann am fireantas cridh,</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>ON the feast   day of Mary the fragrant,<br>   Mother of the Shepherd of the flocks,<br>   I cut me a handful of the new corn,<br>   I dried it gently in the sun,<br>   I rubbed it sharply from the husk<br>        With mine own palms.</p>   
 <p>I ground it in   a quern on Friday,<br>   I baked it on a fan of sheep-skin,<br>   I toasted it to a fire of rowan,<br>   And I shared it round my people.</p>   
 <p>I went sunways   round my dwelling,<br>   In name of the Mary Mother,<br>   Who promised to preserve me,<br>   Who did preserve me,<br>   And who will preserve me,<br>   In peace, in flocks,<br>   In righteousness of heart,</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">
