 <p><small>THE two following poems were got in Kintail. They are obscure in themselves, and the dialect of Kintail in which they were recited <a name="page_173"><font size="1" color="green">p. 173</font></a> increases their obscurity. The reciters repeated them as one poem, but were uncertain whether they were one or two poems.</small></p>

 <p>FHIR a   chruchadh air a chribh,<br>        Fhir a chiosadh le minn an   t-sluaigh,     [binn<br>   Nis bho dh’ f has mi aosda, liath,<br>        Gabh ri m’ fhaosaid, a Dhia! truais.</p>   
 <p>Chan ioghnadh   domh is mor mo lochd,<br>        Is mi an clab-goileam bochd bua’all,<br>   Ri m’ oige gun robh mi baoth,<br>        Ri m’ aois gu bheil mi truagh.</p>   
 <p>Seal mu’n   taine Mac De,<br>        Bha ’n ce na lodruich dhuibh,<br>   Gun ri, gun ro, gun re,<br>        Gun chro, gun chre, gun chruth.</p>   
 <p>Shoillsich   fearann, shoillsich fonn,<br>        Shoillsich an trom fhairge ghlas,<br>   Shoillsich an cruinne ce gu leir,<br>        Ri linn Mhic De tigh’nn gu teach.</p>   
 <p>Sin ’d   uair labhair Moire nan gras,<br>        An Oigh bhaigheil a bha ghnath glic,<br>   ’D uair thug Eosai dhi-se ghradh,<br>        Bu mhiann leis bhi ’n a lathair tric.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>THOU who wert   hanged upon the tree,<br>        And wert crucified by the condemnation of the   people,<br>   Now that I am grown old and grey,<br>        Take to my confession-prayer, O God! pity.</p>   
 <p>No wonder to me   great is my wickedness,<br>        I am a poor clattering cymbal,<br>   In my youth I was profane,<br>        In my age I am forlorn.</p>   
 <p>A time ere came   the Son of God,<br>        The earth was a black morass,<br>   Without star, without sun, without moon,<br>        Without body, without heart, without form.</p>   
 <p>Illumined plains,   illumined hills,<br>        Illumined the great green sea,<br>   Illumined the whole globe together,<br>        When the Son of God came to earth.</p>   
 <p>Then it was   that spoke the Mary of grace,<br>        The Virgin always most kindly and wise,<br>   When Joseph gave to her his love,<br>        He desired to be often in her presence.</p>   </td>  </tr>  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top">

 <p>Bha cumhnant   eadar Eos agus Oigh,<br>        Ann an ordugh dligheach ceart,<br>   Gum biodh cuis ga cur air doigh<br>        Le seula Righ Mor nam feart.</p>   
 <p>Chair iad leis   gu Teampull De,<br>        Far an robh a chleir a steach;<br>   Mar a dh’ orduich an t-Ard High Mor,<br>        Phos iad mu’n taine mach.</p>   
 <p>Thainig aingeal   na dheigh:--<br>        'Eosai, ciod e ’n gleus a th’ ort?'<br>   'Fhuair mi boirionnach bho ’n chleir,<br>        Cha dual domh fein a bhi ceart.'</p>   
 <p>'Eosai, fuirich   ri do cheil,<br>        Chan nodaidh dhuit beud a radh,<br>   Gur h-e th’ agad an Oigh ghlan,<br>        Air nach deachaidh le fear lamh.'</p>   
 <p>'Ciamar a   chreideas mi sin uat?<br>        Agam fein, mo nuar! tha fios--<br>   ’D uair a laigh mi sios ri gual’<br>        Bha leanabh beo a briosg fo crios.'</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top">   
 <p>A compact there   was between Joseph and Virgin,<br>        In order well-becoming and just,<br>   That the compact might be confirmed<br>        By the seal of the Great King of virtues.</p>   
 <p>They went with   him to the Temple of God,<br>        Where the clerics sat within;<br>   As ordained of the Great High King,<br>        They married ere they came out.</p>   
 <p>An angel came   afterwards:<br>        'Joseph, why excited thou?'<br>   'I got a woman from the clerics,<br>        It is not natural for me to be calm.'</p>   
 <p>'Joseph, abide   thou by thy reason,<br>        Not enlightened of thee to find fault,<br>   What thou hast gotten is a virgin pure,<br>        On whom man never put hand.'</p>   
 <p>'How can I   believe that from thee?<br>        I myself, my grief! have knowledge--<br>   When I laid me down by her shoulder<br>        A living child beneath her girdle throbbed.'</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>  </div>
