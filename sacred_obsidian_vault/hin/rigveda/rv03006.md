 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><i>Rig Veda</i>, tr. by Ralph T.H. Griffith, [1896], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr>  <h3 align="center">HYMN VI. Agni.</h3>  
 <p>1. URGED on by deep devotion, O ye singers, bring, pious ones, the God-approaching ladle.<br> Borne onward to the right it travels eastward, and, filled with oil, to Agni bears oblation.<br> 2 Thou at thy birth didst fill both earth and heaven, yea, Most Adorable, thou didst exceed them.<br> Even through the heaven's and through the earth's expanses let thy swift seventongued flames roll on, O Agni.<br> 3 Both Heaven and Earth and Gods who should be worshipped establish thee as Priest for every dwelling,<br> Whenever human families, God-devoted, bringing oblations; laud thy splendid lustre.<br> 4 Firm in the Gods home is the Mighty seated, between vast Heaven and Earth the well-beloved-<br> Those Cows who yield, unharmed, their nectar, Spouses of the Far-Strider, everyoung, united.<br> 5 Great are the deeds of thee, the Great, O Agni: thou by thy power hast spread out earth and heaven.<br> As soon as thou wast born thou wast an envoy, thou, Mighty One, was Leader of the people.<br> 6 Bind to the pole with cords of holy Order the long-maned ruddy steeds who sprinkle fatness.<br> Bring hithier, O thou God, all Gods together: provide them noble worship, Jātavedas.<br> 7 Even from the sky thy brilliant lights shone hither: still hast thou beamed through many a radiant morning,<br> That the Gods praised their joyous Herald's labour eagerly burning, Agni, in the forests.<br> 8 The Gods who take delight in air's wide region, or those the dwellers in heaven's realm of brightness,<br> Or those, the Holy, prompt to hear, our helpers, who, carborne, turn their horses hither, Agni-<br> 9 With these, borne on one ear, Agni, approach us, or borne on many, for thy steeds are able.<br> Bring, witb their Dames, the Gods, the Three and-Thirty, after thy Godlike nature, and be joyful.<br> 10 He is the Priest at whose repeated worship even wide Heaven and Earth sing out for increase.<br> They fair and true and holy coming forward stand at his sacrifice who springs from Order.<br> 11 As holy food, Agni, to thine invoker give wealth in cattle, lasting, rich in marvels.<br> To us be born a son and spreading offspring. Agni, be this thy gracious will to usward.</p>
