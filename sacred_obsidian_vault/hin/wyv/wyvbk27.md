 <p><a name="page_235"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 235</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE TWENTY-SEVENTH</h3>  
 <p>HALF-YEARS and Seasons strengthen thee, O Agni, the Years<br> and all the Verities and Rishis!<br> Flash forth with thy celestial effulgence: illumine all four<br> regions of the heaven.<br> 2 Kindle thee, Agni, rake this man to knowledge: rise up<br> erect for great and happy fortune.<br> Agni, be those uninjured who adore thee, thy priests be<br> glorious and none beside them!<br> 3 The Brahmans present here elect thee, Agni: be thou<br> propitious in our sanctuary.<br> Slayer of rivals, Agni, quell our foemen: watch in thy house<br> with care that never ceases.<br> 4 Even here do thou, O Agni, stablish wealth: let not oppressors<br> injure thee by thinking of thee first.<br> Light be thy task of ruling, Agni, with thy power: may he<br> who worships thee wax strong, invincible.<br> 5 Kind to the people, grasp thy power, O Agni: contend thou<br> with the Friend by way of friendship.<br> Placed, Agni, in the centre of our kinsmen, flash forth to be<br> invoked by Kings around thee.<br> 6 Past those who slay, past enemies, past thoughtless men,<br> past those who hate,<br> Yes, Agni, drive away all woe and trouble: vouchsafe us<br> opulence with men about us.<br> 7 Holder of sway, shine here refulgent, Agni! invincible,<br> unconquered Jâtavedas.<br> Light all the regions, chasing human terrors: with happy<br> helps guard us to-day for increase.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_236"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 236</font></a></p>  
 <p>8 Brihaspati, Savitar, give this man knowledge: sharpen him<br> thoroughly though already sharpened.<br> To great and high felicity exalt him: in him let all the Gods<br> rejoice and triumph.<br> 9 As thou, Brihaspati, from curse hast freed us, from dwelling<br> yonder in the realm of Yama,<br> The Asvins, Leeches of the Gods, O Agni, have chased Death<br> far from us with mighty powers.<br> 10 Looking upon the loftier light, etc., as in XX. 21.<br> 11 Uplifted are the brands that are his fuel: lofty and brilliant<br> are the flames of Agni,<br> Splendidly bright of the Son fair of aspect.<br> 13 Tanûnapât the Asura, all-possessing, God among Gods, the<br> God with mead and butter shall bedew the paths.<br> 13 With mead to sacrifice thou comest, Agni, earnest as friendly-<br> minded Narâsamsa, and Savitar righteous God who brings<br> all blessings.<br> 14 He cometh hitherward with power and fatness, the luminous,<br> implored with adoration.<br> While rites proceed the ladles move to Agni.<br> 15 Let him pay worship to this Agni's greatness, daintily fed:<br> he verily gives enjoyments:<br> The wisest Vasu he, and best wealth giver.<br> 16 Widely expansive, ruling by foundation, the Doors divine<br> and, after, all<br> Preserve this Agni's holy works.<br> 17 May Dawn and Night protecthis heavenly Consortsin<br> a his home this our sacrificial worship.<br> 18 Ye two celestial Hotars, greet with praises this lofty rite of<br> ours, the tongue of Agni.<br> Cause that our sacrifice be well conducted.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_237"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 237</font></a></p>  
 <p>19 Upon this grass three Goddesses be seated, Idâ, Sarasvatî,<br> Bhâratî the Mighty glorified with song.<br> 20 This our productive wondrous flow may Tvashtar pour down<br> on this man's kin, and wealth and heroes.<br> 21 Vanaspatî, presenting of thyself, send God-ward! Let Agni,<br> Immolator, season our oblation.<br> 22 Pay sacrifice to Indra, Jâtavedas Agni! with Hail! All Gods<br> accept the gift we offer!<br> 23 Wise, bright, arranger of his teams, he seeketh men with<br> rich food whose treasures are abundant.<br> They have stood firm of one accord with Vâyu: yea, the<br> men wrought all noble operations.<br> 24 The God whom both these worlds brought forth for riches,<br> whom heavenly Dhishanâ for our wealth appointeth<br> His team of harnessed horses waits on Vâyu and, foremost,<br> on the radiant treasure-holder.<br> 26 What time the mighty waters came containing the universal<br> germ, producing Agni,<br> Thence sprang the Gods one spirit into being. What God<br> shall we adore with our oblation?<br> 26 Who in his might surveyed the floods enclosing productive<br> force and generating Worship,<br> He who is God mid Gods, and none beside himWhat God<br> shall we adore with our oblation?<br> 27 The teams wherewith thou seekest him who offers, within<br> his house, O Vâyu, to direct him,<br> Therewith send wealth to us with full enjoyment, a hero son<br> and gifts of kine and horses.<br> 28 With thy yoked teams in hundreds and in thousands come<br> to our sacrifice and solemn worship.<br> O Vâyu, make thee glad at this libation. Preserve us evermore,<br> ye Gods, with blessings.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_238"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 238</font></a></p>  
 <p>29 Drawn by thy team, O Vâyu, come: to thee is offered this,<br> the pure.<br> Thou visitest the presser's house.<br> 30 Vâyu, the bright is offered thee, best of the meath at holy<br> rites.<br> Come thou to drink the Soma juice, God longed-for, on thy<br> team-drawn car.<br> 31 Lover of worship, leader, come Vâyu with thought, to sacrifice,<br> Propitious with propitious teams!<br> 32 With all the thousand chariots that are thine, O Vâyu, come<br> to us,<br> Team-drawn, to drink the Soma juice.<br> 33 Come thou with one, and ten, O Self-Existent! with two<br> unto the sacrifice, and twenty.<br> Three are the teams and thirty which convey thee. O Vâyu,<br> in this place unyoke thy coursers.<br> 34 Wonderful Vâyu, Lord of Truth, thou who art Tvashtar's<br> Son in-law,<br> Thy saving succour we elect.<br> 35 Like kine unmilked we call aloud, Hero, to thee and sing<br> thy praise,<br> Looker on heavenly light, Lord of this moving world, Lord,<br> Indra! of what moveth not.<br> 36 None other like to thee, of earth or of the heavens, hath<br> been or ever will be born.<br> Desiring horses, Indra, Bounteous Lord! and kine, as men<br> of might we call on thee.<br> 37 That we may win us wealth and power, we poets call on<br> only thee.<br> In war men call on thee, Indra! the hero's Lord, in the<br> steed's race-course call on thee.<br> 38 As such, O Wonderful, whose hand holds thunder, praised<br> as mighty, Caster of the Stone!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_239"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 239</font></a></p>  
 <p>Pour on us boldly, Indra, kine and chariot-steeds ever to be<br> the conqueror's strength.<br> 39 What succour will he bring to us, wonderful, ever-prospering<br> Friend?<br> With what most mighty company?<br> 40 What, genuine and most liberal draught will spirit thee with<br> juice to burst<br> Open een strongly-guarded wealth<br> 41 Do thou who art protector of us thy friends who praise thee<br> With hundred aids approach us.<br> 42 Sing to your Agni with each song, at every sacrifice, for<br> strength.<br> Come, let us praise the Wise and Everlasting God even as a<br> well-belovèd Friend.<br> 43 Protect us, Agni, through the first, protect us through the<br> second hymn.<br> Protect us through three hymns, O Lord of Power and Might;<br> through four hymns, Vasu! guard thou us.<br> 44 The Son of Strength; for is he not our Friend? Let us serve<br> him for offering our gifts.<br> In battles may he be our help and strengthener, yea, be the<br> saviour of our lives.<br> 45 Thou art Samvatsara; thou art Parivatsara; thou art<br> Idâvatsara; thou art Idvatsara; thou art Vatsara.<br> Prosper thy Dawns! Prosper thy Day-and-Nights! Prosper<br> thy Half-months, Months, Seasons, and Years!<br> Combine them for their going and their coming, and send<br> then forward on their ordered courses.<br> In eagle's shape thou art piled up and layered. With that<br> divinity, Angiras-like, lie steady.</p>
