 <p><a name="page_9"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 9</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE SECOND.</h3>  
 <p>THOU art a black-buck dwelling in the covert. I sprinkle<br> thee agreeable to Agni.<br> Thou art the altar. Thee welcome to the sacred grass I<br> sprinkle.<br> Thou art the sacred covering-grass. I sprinkle thee grateful<br> to the sacrificial ladles.<br> 2 Thou art what giveth Aditi her moisture. Thou art the<br> hair-tuft on the head of Vishnu. I spread thee, wool-soft,<br> good for Gods to sit on.<br> Hail to the Earth's Lord! To the World's Lord Hail! Hail<br> to the Lord of Beings!<br> 3 For safety of this all let the Gandharva Visvâvasu lay thee<br> round as a protection. Thou art the Sacrificer's guard,<br> thou, Agni, lauded and worthy to receive laudation.<br> Indra's right arm art thou. For safety . . . . . . laudation.<br> For safety of This Ail, with firm law, northward let Mitra.<br> Varuna lay thee round as keeper. Thou art the Sacrificer's<br> guard . . . . . . laudation.<br> 4 Thee, Sage, who offerest to Gods their banquet, we will<br> enkindle till thou shinest brightly, thee mighty in the<br> sacrifice, O Agni.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_10"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 10</font></a></p>  
 <p>5 Thou art a kindler. From the east let Sûrya keep thee<br> secure from every imprecation.<br> You are the arms of Savitar. I spread thee as soft as wool,<br> good for the Gods to sit on. On thee sit Vasus, Rudras,<br> and Âdityas!<br> 6 Thou who art called Juhû art rich in fatness. On this dear<br> seat, with the dear home, be seated.<br> Thou, Upabhrit by name, art rich in fatness. On this . . . . . . . .<br> be seated.<br> Thou who art called Dhruvâ, art rich in fatness. On this, etc.<br> In the Law's lap they have sat down in safety<br> Guard these. Guard thou the Sacrifice, O Vishnu. Keep<br> thou the Sacrifice's Lord in safety.<br> 7 O Agni, Winner of the Spoil, I cleanse thee, thee who wilt<br> hasten to the spoil, Spoil-winner.<br> Obeisance to the Gods! Svadhâ to Fathers! Be both of you<br> easy for me to handle.<br> 8 May I to-day offer Gods unspilt butter. Let me not with<br> my foot offend thee, Vishnu.<br> Agni, may I approach thy shade abounding in store of riches.<br> Thou art Vishnu's mansion,<br> Hence Indra wrought his deed of manly vigour. The sacrifice<br> stood firmly elevated.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_11"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 11</font></a></p>  
 <p>9 O Agni, undertake the Hotar's office, take on thyself the<br> duty of an envoy.<br> Heaven and Earth guard thee! Guard thou Earth and Heaven.<br> May Indra be, by this presented butter, maker to Gods of<br> fair oblation. Svâhâ! Let light combine with light.<br> 10 Indra bestow on me that Indra-power! May wealth in full<br> abundance gather round us. Let blessings wait on us,<br> yea, real blessings.<br> Our Mother, Earth, hath been invited hither. May Earth,<br> our Mother, in return invite us. I, through my Kindlership,<br> am Agni. Svâhâ!<br> 11 The Father Heaven hath been invited hither. May Heaven<br> the Father in return invite us.<br> By impulse of God Savitar I receive thee with arms of<br> Asvins, with the hands of Pûshan. I feed upon thee<br> with the mouth of Agni.<br> 12 God Savitar, this sacrifice of thine have they proclaimed<br> unto Brihaspati the Brahman Priest.<br> Therefore protect the sacrifice, protect the sacrifice's lord,<br> protect thou me.<br> 13 The butter's rapid flow delight his spirit! Brihaspati extend<br> this act of worship. May he restore the sacrifice uninjured.<br> Here let all Gods rejoice. OM! Step thou forward.<br> 14 This is the stick for kindling thee, O Agni. By means of<br> this grow strong and swell to greatness. May we too grow<br> in strength and swell to greatness.<br> O Agni, thou who winnest food, I cleanse thee, thee who<br> hast hastened to the food, Food-winner.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_12"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 12</font></a></p>  
 <p>15 After the victory of Agni Soma may I obtain the victory.<br> By impulse of sacrificial food I speed me onward.<br> May Agni-Soma drive off him who hates us, drive off the<br> man whom we detest. By impulse of sacrificial food away<br> I drive him.<br> After the victory of Indra-Agni may I obtain the victory.<br> By impulse of sacrificial food I speed me forward.<br> May Indra-Agni . . . . . . I drive him.<br> 16 For Vasus thee. For Rudras thee. Thee for Âdityas.<br> Be, Heaven and Earth, accordant with each other. With<br> rain may Mitra-Varuna assist thee.<br> May the birds go, licking what is anointed.<br> Go to the Maruts speckled mares. Go, having become a<br> speckled cow thyself, to heaven, and from that place bring<br> the rain for us hither.<br> Thou art the eye's guard: guard mine eye, O Agni.<br> I7 The stick which thou, God Agni, laidest round thee, what<br> time thou wast kept hidden by the Panis, this do I bring<br> to thee for thine enjoyment. May it remain with thee<br> and neer be fruitless.<br> Approach, ye two, the place which Agni loveth.<br> 18 The residue ye have to be your portion, mighty by food, ye<br> Gods, ye who are stationed on the grass-bunch, and to be<br> laid as fences.<br> All ye, applauding this my speech, be seated on this grass-bunch<br> and there be joyful. Svâhâ! Vât!</p>  
 <p><a name="page_13"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 13</font></a></p>  
 <p>19 Rich in oil are ye. Guard the two yoke-fellows. Ye two<br> are full of grace, to grace conduct me.<br> Glory to thee, O Sacrifice, and increase! Stand firm in my<br> auspicious, righteous worship.<br> 20 O Agni of unweakened strength, far-reaching, protect me<br> from the lightning-flash, protect me from bondage. from<br> defect in sacrificing, from food injurious to health protect<br> me.<br> Make thou the food that feeds us free from poison in the<br> home good to sit in. Svâhâ! Vât!<br> Hail to the Lord of close embracements, Agni! Hail to Sarasvatî<br> enriched with glory!<br> 21 Veda art thou, whereby, O godlike Veda, thou hast become<br> for Deities their Veda: thereby mayst thou become for<br> me a Veda.<br> O Deities, ye knowers of the Pathway, walk on the pathway<br> having known the Pathway.<br> God, Lord of Spirit, hail! bestow upon the Wind this sacrifice.<br> 22 Blest be the Grass with sacred food and butter. Let Indra<br> be united with the Âdityas, the Vasus, Maruts, and the<br> Visvedevas. Let Svâhâ-offerings rise to heavenly ether.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_14"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 14</font></a></p>  
 <p>23 Who liberates thee from the yoke? He frees thee. For whom?<br> For him he looses thee. For plenty.<br> Thou art the Râkshasas allotted portion.<br> 24 We have combined with lustre, vigour, bodies; we have<br> united with the blessèd spirit. May Tvashtar, bounteous<br> giver, grant us riches, and clear each fault and blemish<br> from the body.<br> 25 By Jagatî metre in the sky strode Vishnu. Therefrom excluded <br> is the man who hates us and whom we detest.<br> By Trishtup metre in the air strode Vishnu. Therefrom, etc.<br> By Gâyatrî upon the earth strode Vishnu. Therefrom, etc.<br> From this food From this resting-place excluded.<br> We have reached heaven. We have combined with lustre.<br> 26 Thou, noblest ray of light, art Self-existent. Giver art thou<br> of splendour. Give me splendour.<br> I move along the path that Sûrya travels.<br> 27 Agni, may I become a good householder, through thee,<br> Home-master, O Householder Agni. Mayst thou become<br> an excellent Householder through me the master of the<br> house, O Agni.<br> Through hundred winters may our household mattersnot<br> like a one-ox carbe smoothly managed.<br> I move along the path that Sûrya travels.<br> 28 I have performed the vow, Lord of Vows, Agni! Full power<br> was mine, and it has proved successful.<br> Now am I he I truly am, no other.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_15"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 15</font></a></p>  
 <p>29 To Agni Hail! who bears gifts due to Sages. To Soma Hail!<br> accompanied by Fathers.<br> Expelled are Asuras and fiends who sate upon the covering<br> grass.<br> 30 The Asuras, attracted by oblation, who roam at will assuming<br> varied figures, from this our world may Agni drive<br> them, whether they clothe themselves in large or little<br> bodies.<br> 31 O Fathers, here enjoy yourselves. Come hither, like bulls,<br> come each to his allotted portion.<br> The Fathers have enjoyed themselves, and hither, like bulls,<br> come each to his allotted portion.<br> 32 Obeisance to your genial sap, O Fathers! Fathers, obeisance<br> unto you for Ardour! Fathers, obeisance Onto you for<br> Svadhâ! Obeisance unto you for Life, O Fathers!<br> Fathers, to you obeisance for the Awful! Fathers, obeisance<br> unto you for Passion! O Fathers, unto you be adoration.<br> Bestow upon us houses, O ye Fathers, what is ours, O<br> Fathers, will we give you. With this your raiment clothe<br> yourselves, O Fathers.<br> 33 Fathers, bestow on me a babe, a boy enwreathed with<br> lotuses, so that there may be here a man.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_16"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 16</font></a></p>  
 <p>34 Bearers of vigour and immortal fatness, milk and sweet beverage<br> and foaming liquor, ye are a freshening draught.<br> Delight my Fathers.</p>
