 <p><a name="page_267"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 267</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE THIRTY-THIRD.</h3>  
 <p>His be the fires, eternal, purifying, protectors of our homes,<br> whose smoke is shining,<br> White, waxing in their strength, for ever stirring, and seated<br> in the wood: like winds are Somas.<br> 2 Gold-coloured, bannered with the smoke, urged by the wind,<br> aloft to heaven<br> Rise, lightly borne, the flames of fire.<br> Bring to us Mitra-Varuna, bring the Gods to the great<br> sacrifice;<br> Bring them, O Agni, to thine home.<br> 4 Yoke, Agni, as a charioteer, thy steeds who best invoke the<br> Gods:<br> As ancient Hotar take thy seat.<br> 5 To fair goals travel Two unlike in semblance: each in<br> succession nourishes an infant.<br> One bears a Godlike Babe of golden colour: bright and<br> fair-shining is be with the other.<br> 6 Here by ordainers was this God appointed first Invoker,<br> best at worship, to be praised at rites,<br> Whom Apnavâna and the Bhrigus caused to shine,<br> bright-coloured in the wood, spreading to every house.<br> 7 Three times a hundred Gods, and thrice a thousand, and<br> three times ten, and nine have worshipped Agni,<br> For him spread sacred grass, balmed him with butter, and<br> stablished him as Priest and Sacrificer.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_268"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 268</font></a></p>  
 <p>8 Him, messenger of earth and head of heaven, Agni Vaisvânara,<br> born in holy Order,<br> The Sage, the King, the Guest of men, a vessel fit for their<br> mouths, the Gods have generated.<br> 9 May Agni slay the foemen,fain for riches, through the<br> love of song<br> Kindled, bright, served with sacrifice.<br> 10 With the All-Gods, with Indra and with Vâyu drink the<br> Soma mead,<br> O Agni, after Mitra's laws.<br> 11 When splendour reached the Lord of men to speed him,<br> down from the heaven was shed the brilliant moisture.<br> Agni brought forth to light and filled with spirit the youthful<br> host benevolent and blameless.<br> 12 Show thyself strong for mighty bliss, O Agni; most excellent<br> be thine effulgent splendours.<br> Make easy to maintain our household lordship and trample<br> down the might of those who hate us.<br> 13 We have elected thee as most delightful for thy beams<br> glow: hear our great laud, O Agni.<br> The best men praise thee as the peer of Indra in strength,<br> mid Gods, like Vâyu in thy bounty.<br> 14 O Agni who art worshipped well, dear let our princes be to<br> thee,<br> Our wealthy patrons who are governors of men, who part in<br> gifts their stalls of kine.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_269"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 269</font></a></p>  
 <p>15 Hear, Agni who hast ears to hear, with all thy train of<br> escort Gods.<br> Let Mitra, Aryaman, seeking betimes our rite, seat them<br> upon the sacred grass.<br> 16 The Freedom of all Gods who merit worship, freely received<br> as Guest in all men's houses,<br> Agni who hath secured the Gods high favour, may he be<br> gracious to us, Jâtavedas.<br> 17 In great enkindled Agni's keeping and, for bliss, free from<br> all sin before Mitra and Varuna,<br> May we share Savitar's best animating help. We crave this<br> gracious favour of the Gods to-day.<br> 18 Like barren cows, moreover, swelled the waters: singers<br> <a href="errata.htm#16">approached</a> thy holy cult, O Indra.<br> Come thou to us as to his team comes Vâyu. Thou through<br> our solemn hymns bestowest bounty.<br> 19 Ye Cows, protect the fount. The two mighty Ones bless the<br> sacrifice.<br> The handles twain are wrought of gold.<br> 20 Now when the Sun hath risen to-day may sinless Mitra,<br> Aryaman,<br> Bhaga, and Savitar speed us forth.<br> 21 Pour on the juice the ornament which reaches both the<br> heaven and earth;</p>  
 <p><a name="page_270"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 270</font></a></p>  
 <p>Supply the liquid to the Bull.<br> Thou in the first old time. See, Vena.<br> 22 As he was rising up they all revered him: self-luminous he<br> travels, clothed in splendour.<br> That is the Bull's, the Asura's lofty nature: he, Omniform,<br> hath reached the eternal waters.<br> 23 I laud your Mighty One who joyeth in the juice, him who<br> is good to all men, who pervadeth all;<br> Indra whose conquering strength is powerful in war, whose<br> fame and manly vigour Heaven and Earth revere.<br> 24 Great is their fuel, strung their laud, wide is their sacrificial<br> post<br> Whose Friend is Indra, ever young.<br> 25 Come, Indra, and delight thee with the juice at all the<br> Soma feasts,<br> Conqueror, mighty in thy strength.<br> 26 Leading his band, Indra encompassed Vritra; weak grew<br> the wily leader of enchanters.<br> He who burns fierce in forests slaughtered Vyamsa, and<br> made the milch-kine of the nights apparent.<br> 27 Whence comest thou alone, thou who art mighty, Indra,<br> Lord of the Brave? What is thy purpose?<br> Thou greetest us, encountering us the Bright Ones. Lord of<br> Bay Steeds, say what thou hast against us.<br> Indra, great in his power and might. Neer art thou fruitless.<br> Never art thou neglectful.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_271"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 271</font></a></p>  
 <p>28 Those men extolled that deed of thine, O Indra, those who<br> would fain burst through the stall of cattle,<br> Fain to milk her who bare but once, great, lofty, whose sons<br> are many and her streams a thousand.<br> 29 To thee the Mighty One I bring this mighty hymn, for thy<br> desire hath been gratified by my laud.<br> In Indra, yea, in him victorious through his might, the<br> Gods have joyed at feast and when the Soma flowed.<br> 30 May the Bright God drink glorious Soma-mingled mead,<br> giving the sacrifice's lord uninjured life;<br> He who, wind-urged, in person guard our offspring well,<br> hath nourished them with food and shone oer many a<br> land.<br> 31 His bright rays bear him up aloft, the God who knoweth all<br> that lives,<br> Sûrya, that all may look on him.<br> 32 Pure Varuna, with that same eye wherewith thou lookest<br> upon one<br> Actively stirring mid the folk<br> 33 Ye two divine Adhvaryus, come hither upon a sun-bright car:<br> Bedew our sacrifice with stead.<br> Thou in the first old time. See, Vena. The brilliant presence.<br> 34 Loved of all men, may Savitar through praises offered as<br> sacred food come to our synod,<br> That ye too, through our hymns, ye ever youthful, may<br> gladden at your visit all our people.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_272"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 272</font></a></p>  
 <p>35 Whatever, Vritra-slayer! thou Surya hast risen on to-day,<br> That, Indra, all is in thy power.<br> 36 Swift, visible to all art thou, O Sûrya, maker of the light,<br> Illuming all the radiant realm.<br> 37 This is the Godhead, this the might of Sûrya: he hath<br> withdrawn what spread oer work unfinished.<br> When he hath loosed his horses from their station, straight<br> over all Night spreadeth out her garment.<br> 38 In the sky's lap the Sun this form assumeth that Varuna<br> and Mitra may behold it.<br> His Bay Steeds well maintain their power eternal, at one<br> time bright, and darksome at another.<br> 39 Verily, Sûrya, thou art great; truly, Âditya, thou art great.<br> As thou art great indeed thy greatness is admired: yea,<br> verily thou, God, art great.<br> 40 Yea, Sûrya, thou art great in fame: thou, evermore, O God,<br> art great.<br> Thou by thy greatness art the Gods Home-Priest, divine,<br> far-spread, unconquerable light.<br> 41 Turning, as twere, to meet the Sun, enjoy from Indra all<br> good things.<br> When he who will be horn is born with power we look to<br> treasures as our heritage.<br> 42 To-day, ye Gods, when Sûrya hath ascended, deliver us<br> front trouble and dishonour.<br> This boon may Varuna and Mitra grant us, and Aditi and<br> Sindhu, Earth and Heaven.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_273"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 273</font></a></p>  
 <p>43 Throughout the dusky firmament advancing, laying to rest<br> the immortal and the mortal,<br> Borne on his golden chariot he cometh, Savitar, God,<br> beholding living creatures.<br> 44 Soft to the tread the sacred grass is scattered: these go<br> like Kings amid the band around them,<br> At the folk's early call on Night and Morning,Vâyu, and<br> Pûshan with his team to bless us.<br> 45 Indra, Vâyu, Brihaspati, Mitra, Agni, Pûshan, Bhaga,<br> Âdityas, and the Marut host.<br> 46 Be Varuna our chief defence, let Mitra guard us with all aids:<br> Both make us rich exceedingly!<br> 47 Regard us, Indra, Vishnu, here, ye Asvins, and the Marut<br> host, us who are kith and kin to you.<br> Thou in the first old time. See, Vena. O ye eleven Gods.<br> Loved of all men, may Savitar. With the All-Gods. Ye<br> Visvedevas who protect.<br> 48 O Agni, Indra, Varuna, and Mitra, give, O ye Gods, and<br> Marut host, and Vishnu.<br> May both Nâsatyas, Rudra, heavenly Matrons, Pûshan,<br> Sarasvatî, Bhaga accept us.<br> 49 Indra, Agni, Mitra, Varuna, Aditi, the Waters, Mountains,<br> Maruts, Sky, and Earth and Heaven,<br> Vishnu I call, Pûshan and Brahmanaspati, and Bhaga, Samsa,<br> Savitar that they may help.<br> 50 With us are raining Rudras, clouds accordant in call to<br> battle at the death of Vritra,<br> The strong, assigned to him who sings and praises. May<br> Gods with Indra as their chief protect us.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_274"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 274</font></a></p>  
 <p>51 Turn yourselves hitherward this day, ye Holy, that fearing<br> in my heart I may approach you.<br> Protect us, Gods, let not the wolf destroy us. Save us, ye<br> Holy, from the pit and falling.<br> 52 This day come all the Maruts, etc., as in XVIII. 31.<br> 53 Listen, All-Gods, to this mine invocation, ye who inhabit<br> heaven and air's mid-region.<br> All ye; O Holy Ones, whose tongue is Agni, seated upon<br> this sacred grass be joyful.<br> 54 For thou at first producest for the holy Gods the noblest of<br> all portions, immortality.<br> Thereafter as a gift to men, O Savitar, thou openest existence,<br> life succeeding life.<br> 55 I with a lofty song call hither Vâyu all-bounteous, filler of<br> his car, most wealthy.<br> Thou, Sage, with bright path, Lord of harnessed horses,<br> impetuous, promptly honourest the prudent.<br> 56 These, Indra-Vâyu, have been shed, etc., as in VII. 8.<br> 57 Mitra of holy strength I call, and foe-destroying Varuna,<br> Who make the oil-fed rite complete.<br> 58 Nâsatyas, Wonder-workers, yours are these libations with<br> clipt grass.<br> Come ye whose paths are bright with glow.<br> 59 When Saramâ had, found the mountain's fissure, that vast<br> and ancient place she plundered <a href="errata.htm#17">thoroughly</a>.<br> In the floods van she led them forth, light-footed: she who<br> well knew came first unto their lowing.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_275"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 275</font></a></p>  
 <p>60 For nowhere did they find another envoy to lead the way<br> than this Vaisvânara Agni.<br> The Gods immortal strengthened the immortal Vaisvânara<br> to win the land in battle.<br> 61 The strong, dispellers of the foe, Indra and Agni, we invoke:<br> May they be kind to one like me.<br> 62 Sing forth to Indu, O ye men, to him as he is purified,<br> Fain to pay worship to the Gods.<br> 63 Drink Soma, Indra, handed with the Maruts who, Boon<br> Lord! strengthened thee at Ahi's slaughter,<br> Gainst Sambara, Lord of Bays! in winning cattle, and now<br> rejoice in thee, the holy singers.<br> 64 Thou vast born mighty for victorious valour, exulting,<br> strongest, full of pride and courage.<br> There, even there the Maruts strengthened Indra when his<br> most rapid Mother stirred the Hero.<br> 65 O thou who slewest Vritra, come, O Indra, hither to our side,<br> Mighty One with thy mighty aids.<br> 66 Thou in thy battles, Indra, art subduer of all hostile bands.<br> Father art thou, all-conquering, cancelling the curse: vanquish<br> the men who fight with us.<br> 67 Heaven and Earth cling close to thy victorious might<br> As sire and mother to their child.<br> 68 The sacrifice obtains the Gods acceptance, etc., as in VIII. 4.<br> 69 Protect our habitation, Savitar, this day with guardian<br> aids around, propitious, neer beguiled.<br> God of the golden tongue, keep us for newest bliss: let not<br> the evil-wisher have us in his power.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_276"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 276</font></a></p>  
 <p>70 For you have flowed, through noble ministration, pressed<br> by Adhvaryus, bright sweet-flavoured juices.<br> Drive on thy team and come thou hither, Vâyu: drink for<br> thy rapture of the sap of Soma.<br> 71 Ye Cows, protect, etc., as in verse 14.<br> 72 Come ye foe-slayers to the place of meeting, to the<br> birth-places of the two great Sages,<br> With force of intellect unto the dwelling.<br> 73 Ye two divine Adhvaryus, etc., as in verse 33. Thou in<br> the time of old. See, Vena.<br> 74 Transversely was the severing line extended: was it above,<br> or was it, haply, under?<br> There were begetters, there were mighty forces, free action<br> here and energy up yonder.<br> 75 He hath filled heaven and earth and the great realm of light,<br> when at his birth the skilful held him in their hold.<br> He like a steed is led forth to the sacrifice, Sage, graciously<br> inclined, that he may win us wealth.<br> 76 Call hither with the song and lauds the two best slayers of<br> the foe,<br> Delighting even in our hymn.<br> 77 All Sons of Immortality shall listen to the songs we sing,<br> And be exceeding good to us.<br> 78 Mine are devotions, hymns, sweet are libations. Strength<br> stirs, and hurled forth is my rocky weapon.<br> They call for me, for me their lauds are longing. To their<br> libations these my Bay Steeds bear me.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_277"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 277</font></a></p>  
 <p>79 Nothing, O Bounteous Lord, stands firm before thee: among<br> the Gods not one is found thine equal.<br> None born or springing into life comes near thee. Do what<br> thou hast to do, exceeding mighty!<br> 80 In all the worlds That was the Best and Highest whence<br> sprang the mighty God of splendid valour.<br> Quickly when born he overcomes his foemen, he in whom<br> all who lend him aid are joyful.<br> 81 May these my songs of praise exalt thee, Lord, who hast<br> abundant wealth.<br> Men skilled in holy hymns, pure, with the hues of fire, have<br> sung them with their lauds to thee.<br> 82 Good Lord of wealth is he to whom all Âryas, Dâsas here<br> belong.<br> Een over unto thee, the pious Rusama Pavîru, is that<br> wealth brought nigh.<br> 83 He, with his might advanced by Rishis thousandfold, hath<br> like an ocean spread himself.<br> His majesty is praised as true at solemn rites, his power<br> where holy singers rule.<br> 84. Protect our habitation, Savitar, etc., as in verse 69.<br> 85 Come, Vâyu, drawn by fair hymns, to our sacrifice that<br> reaches heaven.<br> Poured on the middle of the straining-cloth and dressed,<br> this bright drink hath been offered thee.<br> 86 Indra and Vâyu, fair to see and swift to hear, we call to us,<br> That in assembly all, yea, all the folk may be benevolent to<br> us and free from malady.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_278"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 278</font></a></p>  
 <p>87 Yea, specially that mortal man hath toiled for service of<br> the Gods,<br> Who quickly hath brought near Mitra and Varuna to share<br> his sacrificial gifts.<br> 38 Approach ye, and be near to us. Drink, O ye Asvins, of<br> the mead.<br> Draw forth the milk, ye mighty, rich in genuine wealth!<br> Injure us not, and come to us.<br> 89 May Brahmanaspati draw nigh, may Sûnritâ the Goddess<br> come,<br> And Gods bring to our rite which gives the fivefold gift the<br> Hero, lover of mankind.<br> 90 Within the Waters runs the Moon, he with the beauteous<br> wings, in heaven.<br> To yellow-hued abundant wealth, object of many a man's<br> desire, loud-neighing goes the tawny Steed.<br> 91 Singing their praise with godlike hymn let us invoke each<br> God for grace,<br> Each God to bring you help, each God to strengthen you.<br> 92 Agni Vaisvânara, set in heaven, with mighty splendour<br> hath shone forth.<br> Increasing in his power on earth, benevolent, he quells the<br> darkness with his light.<br> 93 First, Indra Agni! hath this Maid come footless unto those<br> with feet.<br> Stretching her head and speaking loudly with her tongue,<br> she hath gone downward thirty steps.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_279"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 279</font></a></p>  
 <p>94 For of one spirit are the Gods with mortal man, co-sharers<br> all of gracious gifts.<br> May they increase our strength hereafter and to-day, providing<br> ease and ample room.<br> 95 Indra who quells the curse blew curses far away, and then<br> in splendour came to us.<br> Indra, refulgent with the Marut host! the Gods eagerly<br> strove to win thy love.<br> 96 To Indra, to your mighty Chief, Maruts, sing forth a mighty<br> prayer.<br> Let Satakratu, Vritra-slayer, kill the fiend with hundred-knotted<br> thunderbolt.<br> 97 Indra increased his manly strength at sacrifice, in the wild<br> rapture of this juice;<br> And living men to-day, even as of old, sing forth their<br> praises to his majesty.<br> May these. Good Lord of wealth. He with his might.<br> Stand up erect.</p>
