 <p><a name="page_255"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 255</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">BOOK THE THIRTIETH.</h3>  
 <p>OUR sacrifice, God Savitar! speed onward: speed to his share<br> the sacrifice's patron.<br> May the Celestial Gandharva, cleanser of thought and will,<br> make clean our thoughts and wishes.<br> The Lord of Speech sweeten the words we utter!<br> 2 May we attain that excellent glory of Savitar the God:<br> So may he stimulate our prayers.<br> 3 Savitar, God, send far away all troubles and calamities,<br> And send us only what is good.<br> 4 We call on him distributer of wondrous bounty and of wealth,<br> On Savitar who looks on men.<br> 5 For Brahman (Priesthood) he binds a Brahman to the<br> stake; for Kshatra (Royalty) a Râjanya; for the Maruts<br> a Vaisya; for Penance a Sûdra; for Darkness a robber;<br> for Hell a homicide or a man who has lost his consecrated<br> fire; for Misfortune a eunuch; for Venality an Ayogû;<br> for Kâma a harlot; for Excessive Noise a Mâgadha;</p>  
 <p><a name="page_256"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 256</font></a></p>  
 <p>6 For Dance a stable-master; for Song a public dancer; for<br> Duty one who attends court; for Pastime a timid man;<br> for Sport a chatterer; for Laughter an artist; for Pleasure<br> a woman-lover; for Desire a damsel's son; for Dexterity<br> a car-builder; for Firmness a carpenter;<br> 7 For Trouble a potter's son; for Device an artificer; for<br> Beauty a jeweller; for Welfare a sower; for the Arrow-deity<br> a maker of shafts; for Injury a bowyer; for Action<br> a bowstring-maker; for Fate a rope-maker; for Death a<br> hunter; for the Finisher a dog-leader;<br> 8 For Rivers a fisherman; for Rikshîkâs a Nishâda's son; for<br> the Man-tiger a madman; for the Gandharvas and<br> Apsarases a Vrâtya; for Motives one demented; for Serpents<br> and Genii an untrustworthy man; for Dice a gambler;<br> for Excitement a non-gambler; for Pisâchas a woman who<br> splits cane; for Yâtudhânas a woman who works in thorns;<br> 9 For Rendezvous a lover; for Homestead a paramour; for<br> Trouble an unmarried elder brother; for Nirriti a younger<br> brother who has married before his elder; for Misfortune<br> the husband of a younger sister whose elder sister has<br> not been married; for Representation a woman who<br> embroiders; for Agreement a woman who deals in<br> love-charms; for Garrulity a by-sitter; for Colour an<br> obstinate man; for Strength a yielding man;<br> 10 For Interruptions a hunch-back; for Delight a dwarf,<br> for Doors a blear-eyed man; for Sleep a blind man; for<br> Unrighteousness a deaf man; for Purifying Medium a<br> physician; for Insight an astrologer; for Craving for<br> Knowledge an inquisitive man; for Desire of extra <br> Knowledge an extra-inquisitive man; for Moral Law<br> a question solver;<br> 11 For Eye-diseases an elephant-keeper; for Speed a<br> horse-keeper; for Nourishment a cowherd; for Manliness a<br> shepherd; for Keenness a goatherd; for Refreshment a<br> ploughman; for Sweet Beverage a preparer of Surâ; for<br> Weal a house-guard; for Well-being a possessor of wealth;<br> for Supervision a doorkeeper's attendant;</p>  
 <p><a name="page_257"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 257</font></a></p>  
 <p>12 For Light a wood-bringer; for Brightness a fire-kindler; for<br> the Sun's Station a besprinkler; for Highest Heaven a<br> high steward; for the World of Gods a carver; for the<br> World of Men a distributer; for All-Worlds a pourer-out:<br> for Poverty, Affliction a stirrer-up of strife; for Sacrifice<br> a washerwoman; for Delight a female dyer;<br> 13 For Assault a thievish-hearted man; for Homicide a slanderer;<br> for Discrimination a door-keeper; for Inspection a<br> door-keeper's attendant; for Strength a servant; for<br> Plenty a running footman; for the Beloved a sweet speaker;<br> for Safety the rider of a horse; for the World of<br> Svarga a dealer-out of portions; for Highest Heaven a<br> high steward;<br> 14 For Passion an iron-smelter; for Anger a remover; for<br> Yoking a yoker; for Pain an assailant; for Quiet an unyoker;<br> for Up-hill and Down-hill one who stands on three legs;<br> for Form a conceited man; for Virtue a female<br> ointment-maker; for Nirriti a female scabbard-maker;<br> for Yama a barren woman;<br> 15 For Yama a mother of twins; for the Atharvans a woman<br> who has miscarried; for the First Year a gad-about; for<br> the Parivatsara one who has not borne a child; for the<br> Idâvatsara one who exceeds; for the Idvatsara one who<br> transgresses; for the Vatsara one who is worn out; for<br> the Samvatsara one with grey hair; for the Ribhus a<br> hide-dresser; for the Sâdhyas a currier;<br> 16 For Lakes a fisherman; for Standing Waters a fisher; for<br> Tank-waters a Nishâda; for Reed-beds a fish-vender; for<br> the Opposite Bank one who gropes for fish; for This Bank<br> a fish-catcher; for Fords an Âuda; for shallows a Mainâla;</p>  
 <p><a name="page_258"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 258</font></a></p>  
 <p>for Sounds a Bhilla; for Caverns a Kirâta; for Mountain-heights<br> a destructive savage; for Mountains a wild man;<br> 17 For Abhorrence a Paulkasa; for Colour a goldsmith; for<br> Balance a merchant; for Repentance a sluggard; for All<br> Spirits a leper; for Prosperity a watchful man; for Failure<br> a sleepy man; for Mischief a chatterer; for Misfortune<br> a shameless man; for Undoing one who cuts up into small<br> pieces;<br> 18 For the Dice-king a gambler; for the die Krita one who<br> contemplates his adversary's ill luck, for the Tretâ a<br> gamble-manager; for the Dvâpara a chief manager; for Askanda<br> one who will not leave the gambling-hall; for Mrityu one<br> who approaches cows; for Antaka a Cow-killer; for<br> Hunger one who goes begging to a man who is cutting<br> up a cow; for Misdeed a leader of the Charakas; for<br> Misery a robber;<br> 19 For Echo a reviler; for Noise a snarler; for End a very<br> talkative man; for Endless a mute; for Sound a drummer;<br> for Might a lute-player; for Cry a flute-blower; for<br> Confused Tone a Conch-blower; for the Wood a<br> wood-ranger; for Partly-wooded Land a forest fire guard;<br> 20 For Pastime a harlot; for Laughter a jester; for Lust a<br> woman with spotty skin; for Might these, the head-man<br> of a village, an astrologer, and a watchman; a lute-player;<br> a hand-clapper, a flutist, these for Dance; for Pleasure a<br> musician;<br> 21 For Fire a fat man; for Earth a cripple; for Wind a<br> Chândâla; for Mid-Air a pole-dancer; for Sky a bald-head;<br> for the Sun a green-eyed man; for Stars a spotty man;<br> for the Moon a leper; for Day a white yellow-eyed man;<br> for Night a black man with yellow eyes.</p>  
 <p><a name="page_259"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 259</font></a></p>  
 <p>22 Now he ties up the eight following variform men: one too<br> tall, one too short, one too stout, one too thin, one too<br> white, one too black, one too bald, one too airy. These<br> must be neither Sûdras nor Brâhmans, and must be<br> dedicated to Prajâpati.<br> A minstrel, a harlot, a gambler, and a eunuchneither of<br> Sûdra nor Brâhman casteare to be dedicated to Prajâpati.</p>
