 <p><a name="P8504_2697983"></a>
 <sup>180 </sup>It is important to notice the evidence in this one sentence of haste and want of revision. In the first line we find a genitive (<i>discordiarum</i>-"dissensions"), but not the noun on which it depends; and in the apodosis a verb (<i>disjunctas esse</i>-"have been removed," i.e., "are remote") has no subject, although its gender imperatively requires that <i>has res</i>, or some such words, be supplied. One omission might have been easily ascribed to a slip on the part of the copyist; but two omissions such as these occurring so closely, must, it would seem, be assigned to the impetuous disregard of <i>minutiae</i> with which Arnobius blocked out a conclusion which was never carefully revised. (Cf. Appendix, note 1, and p. 539, n. 8.) The importance of such indications is manifest in forming an opinion on the controversy as to this part of the work. </p>
