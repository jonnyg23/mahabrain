 <sup>438 </sup>Luke xvi. 16.</p>
 
 <p><a name="P3718_1041566"></a>
 <sup>439 </sup>Reading "praeparare et proximos fieri benignae ac diviti menti" for "praeparet proximus fieri benignae hac," etc., as it stands in the Codex Casinensis. Routh suggests "praeparare proximos fieri benignae ac diviti menti et continuo... consequemur" = to take care to draw near to the gracious and liberal mind, and then we shall forthwith receive steadily from it, etc.</p>
 
 <p><a name="P3720_1042670"></a>
 <sup>440 </sup>This epistle is edited not only from the Codex Casinensis, but also by Valesius from the Codex Bobiensis. The most important varieties of reading shall therefore be noted.</p>
 
 <p><a name="P3722_1043809"></a>
 <sup>441 </sup>Summum studium. But the Codex Bobiensis reads <i>suum studium.</i></p>
 
 <p><a name="P3723_1044417"></a>
 <sup>442 </sup>Reading "ex subtegmine atque stamine," etc., with the Codex Bobiensis, instead of "subtemine et, quae stamine," etc., as it is given in the Codex Casinensis. [A beautiful anticipation of Augustine's <i>dictum</i>, "The New is <i>veiled</i> in the Old, the Old <i>unveiled</i> in the New."]</p>
 
 <p><a name="P3724_1044923"></a>
 <sup>443 </sup>We read here "gloriam enim Domini in eodem speculamur." The Codex Bobiensis is vitiated here, giving <i>gloriam um Domini</i>, which was changed by Valesius into <i>gloriam Jesu</i>, etc.</p>
 
 <p><a name="P3725_1045151"></a>
 <sup>444 </sup>Reading, with the Codex Bobiensis, "speculum, cum nobis ipsam imaginem," etc., instead of "speculum nobis per ipsam imaginem," etc.</p>
 
 <p><a name="P3726_1045531"></a>
 <sup>445 </sup>[Here is the literal use of the word "paedagogue," with which Clement took liberties. Vol. ii. p. 209, note 3, this series.] Adopting "qui ad doctores a paedagogo," instead of "qui a doctore iis a paedagogo."</p>
 
 <p><a name="P3727_1045827"></a>
 <sup>446 </sup>"Dehonorare," or, as in the Codex Bobiensis, "dehonestare."</p>
 
 <p><a name="P3728_1045965"></a>
 <sup>447 </sup>Reading "opera ejus non indiget." But the Codex Casinensis gives "<i>ore</i> ejus," etc. </p>
 
 <p><a name="P3729_1047088"></a>
 <sup>448 </sup>The Codex Bobiensis reads here, "accidit vero post tempus ut is qui...requireret," etc. The other codex has, "accedit vero post tempus is qui ...requirere."</p>
 
 <p><a name="P3730_1047553"></a>

 <p><a name="P3731_1047761"></a>

 <p><a name="P3741_1050552"></a>
 <sup>460 </sup>The reading of the Codex Casinensis is, "rogavit dives simul uno tempore ut edisceret majorem doctrinam."  <p><a name="P3790_1065774"></a>
 <sup>507 </sup>The reading in the text is, "non deficiet princeps ex Juda, neque dux de femoribus ejus usquequo veniat," etc. Codex Bobiensis coincides, only giving "de femore ejus." On the whole quotation, which is given in forms so diverse among the old versions and fathers, see Novatian, <i>De Trin.</i>, ch. 9 [vol. v. p. 618], and Cyprian, <i>Adv. Judaeos</i>, i. 21 [vol. v. p. 513].</p>
 
 <p><a name="P3791_1066170"></a>
 <sup>508 </sup>The text gives, "veniat, cujus est." etc. Prudentius Maranus on Justin's <i>Apology</i>, i. ยง 32 [vol. i. p. 173, this series], thinks this was originally an error of transcription for <i>cui jus est</i>, which reading would correspond very much with the <font face="SPIonic">w\| a0po/keitai</font> of some of the most ancient authorities. See Cotelerius on the <i>Constitut. Apostol.</i>, i. 1, and the note in Migne.</p>
