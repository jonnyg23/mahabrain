 <sup>136 </sup><font face="SPIonic">a\ponhstizesqai dei=</font>. Gentianus Hervetus renders this by <i>jejunandus sit dies Paschae</i>; and thus he translates the word by <i>jejunare</i>, "to fast," wherever it occurs, whereas it rather means always, <i>jejunium solvere</i>, it "to have done fasting." In this sense the word is used in the <i>Apostolic Constitutions</i> repeatedly: see book v. chap. 12, 18, etc. It occurs in the same sense in the 89th Canon of the Concilium Trullanum. The usage must evidently be the same here: so that it does not mean, What is the proper hour for fasting on the day of Pentecost? but, What is the hour at which the ante-paschal fast ought to be terminated-whether on the evening preceding the paschal festival itself, or at cockcrowing, or at another time?-Gall. See also the very full article in Suicer, <i>s.v.</i></p>
