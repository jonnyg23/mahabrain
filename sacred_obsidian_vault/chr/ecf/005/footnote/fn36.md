 <p><a name="P3686_1162034"></a>
 <sup>187 </sup>Hades, in the view of the ancients, was the general receptacle of souls after their separation from the body, where the good abode happily in a place of light (<font face="SPIonic"> fwteinw</font>), and the evil all in a place of darkness (<font face="SPIonic"> skotiwterw</font>). See Colomesii <i><font face="SPIonic"> Keimhlia</font> litteraria</i>, 28, and Suicer on <font face="SPIonic">adhj</font>. Hence Abraham's bosom and paradise were placed in Hades. See Olympiodorus on <i>Eccles</i>., iii. p, 264. The Macedonians, on the authority of Hugo Broughton, praying in the Lord's words, "Our Father who art in Hades" (<font face="SPIonic"> IIathr hmwn o en adh</font>) (Fabricius). [Hippolytus is singular in assigning the <i>ultimate</i> receptacle of lost spirits to this Hades. But compare vol. iii. p. 428, and vol. iv. pp 293, 495, 541, etc.]</p>
