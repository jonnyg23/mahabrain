 <p></p>  <h2 align="center">ELEGY XIII</h2>  <h3 align="center">HE BESEECHES ISIS TO COME TO THE AID OF CORINNA IN HER CONFINEMENT.</h3>  <table border="0" width="100%">  <tr>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="44%">   
 <p>MY rash Corinna, seeking to rid herself of the burden she bears in her   womb, hath risked the loss of her own life. For having thus, unknown to me,   courted so great a danger, she merits all my wrath; but wrath gives way to   fear. Howbeit it was by me that she conceived, or such, at least, is my   belief; for oftentimes my facts are only peradventures.</p>   
 <p>Isis, thou who in Parætonium dost dwell, and in Canopus' kindly   meads and Memphis and palm-bearing Pharos and those plains where the Nile,   quitting its mighty bed, flows and bears through seven channels its hurrying   waters to the sea. By thy timbrels I entreat thee, and by the head of dread   Anubis--so may the pious Osiris ever accept thy offerings, so may the drowsy   serpent glide round about thine altars, and the hornèd Apis march in   the procession; look mercifully on Corinna, and spare two lives in one, for   thou to my mistress wilt give life; she will give life to me. Full often, on   days appointed for thy worship, hath she sat within thy temple at the hour   when thy priests enwreathe their brows with laurel.</p>   
 <p>And thou who takest pity on women who are suffering the pangs of   childbirth when they seek to be delivered of</p>   
 <p><a name="page_53"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 53</font></a></p>   
 <p>the burden that stirs within them, come, propitious Ilithyia, and hearken   to my prayers. She merits that thou shouldst count her among thy favoured   ones; and I, apparelled all in white, will offer incense at thine altars. I   at thy feet will lay my votive gifts, and this inscription will I add:   "Ovid for Corinna's safety makes this offering." And all I pray   thee is to justify these same offerings and inscription.</p>   
 <p>And as for thee, Corinna, if, in my panic, I may give thee such advice,   I'll say to thee, once safely out of the wood this time, take heed thou enter   not again therein.</p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="9%">   
 <p> </p>   </td>   <td valign="top" align="top" width="44%">   
 <p>Dum labefactat onus gravidi temeraria ventris,<br>       in dubio vitae lassa Corinna iacet.<br>   illa quidem clam me tantum molita pericli<br>       ira digna mea; sed cadit ira metu.<br>   sed tamen aut ex me conceperat--aut ego credo;<br>        est mihi pro facto saepe, quod esse potest.<br>   Isi, Paraetonium genialiaque arva Canopi<br>       quae colis et Memphin palmiferamque Pharon,<br>   quaque celer Nilus lato delapsus in alveo<br>       per septem portus in maris exit aquas,<br>   per tua sistra precor, per Anubidis ora verendi--<br>       sic tua sacra pius semper Osiris amet,<br>   pigraque labatur circa donaria serpens,<br>       et comes in pompa corniger Apis eat! <br>   huc adhibe vultus, et in una parce duobus!<br>       nam vitam dominae tu dabis, illa mihi.<br>   saepe tibi sedit certis operata diebus,<br>       qua cingit laurus Gallica turma tuas.<br>   Tuque laborantes utero miserata puellas,<br>       quarum tarda latens corpora tendit onus,<br>   lenis ades precibusque meis fave, Ilithyia!<br>       digna est, quam iubeas muneris esse tui.<br>   ipse ego tura dabo fumosis candidus aris,<br>       ipse feram ante tuos munera vota pedes.<br>   adiciam titulum: 'servata Naso Corinna!'<br>       tu modo fac titulo muneribusque locum.<br>   Si tamen in tanto fas est monuisse timore,<br>       hac tibi sit pugna dimicuisse satis!</p>   </td>  </tr> </table>
