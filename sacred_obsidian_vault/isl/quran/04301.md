 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XLIII.: Zu<u>kh</u>ruf, or Gold Adornments.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 1</h3> 
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_001.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>1.  <u>Ha</u>-meem   </p>
 <p>1.  Ha-Mīm</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_002.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>2.  Wa<b>a</b>lkit<u>a</u>bi almubeen<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>2.  By the Book that<br> Makes things clear,—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_003.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>3.  Inn<u>a</u> jaAAaln<u>a</u>hu qur-<u>a</u>nan AAarabiyyan laAAallakum taAAqiloona   </p>
 <p>3.  We have made it<br> A Qur-ān in: Arabic,<br> That ye may be able<br> To understand (and learn wisdom).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_004.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>4.  Wa-innahu fee ommi alkit<u>a</u>bi ladayn<u>a</u> laAAaliyyun <u>h</u>akeem<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>4.  And verily, it is<br> In the Mother of the Book,<br> In Our Presence, high<br> (In dignity), full of wisdom.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_005.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>5.  Afana<u>d</u>ribu AAankumu a<b>l</b><u>thth</u>ikra <u>s</u>af<u>h</u>an an kuntum qawman musrifeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>5.  Shall We then<br> Take away the Message<br> From you and repel (you),<br> For that ye are a people<br> Transgressing beyond bounds?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_006.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>6.  Wakam arsaln<u>a</u> min nabiyyin fee al-awwaleen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>6.  But how many were<br> The prophets We sent<br> Amongst the peoples of old?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_007.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>7.  Wam<u>a</u> ya/teehim min nabiyyin ill<u>a</u> k<u>a</u>noo bihi yastahzi-oon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>7.  And never came there<br> A prophet to them<br> But they mocked him.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_008.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>8.  Faahlakn<u>a</u> ashadda minhum ba<u>t</u>shan wama<u>da</u> mathalu al-awwaleen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>8.  So We destroyed (them)—<br> Stronger in power than these;—<br> And (thus) has passed on<br> The Parable of the peoples<br> Of old.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_009.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>9.  Wala-in saaltahum man khalaqa a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>w<u>a</u>ti wa<b>a</b>l-ar<u>d</u>a layaqoolunna khalaqahunna alAAazeezu alAAaleem<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>9.  If thou wert<br> To question them, "Who created<br> The heavens and the earth?''<br> They would be sure to reply,<br> "They were created by (Him),<br> The Exalted in Power,<br> Full of Knowledge";—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_010.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>10.  Alla<u>th</u>ee jaAAala lakumu al-ar<u>d</u>a mahdan wajaAAala lakum feeh<u>a</u> subulan laAAallakum tahtadoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>10.  (Yea, the same that)<br> Has made for you<br> The earth (like a carpet)<br> Spread out, and has made<br> For you roads (and channels)<br> Therein, in order that ye<br> May find guidance (on the way);</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_011.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>11.  Wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>ee nazzala mina a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>-i m<u>a</u>an biqadarin faansharn<u>a</u> bihi baldatan maytan ka<u>tha</u>lika tukhrajoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>11.  That sends down<br> (From time to time)<br>Rain from the sky<br> In due measure;—<br> And We raise to life<br> Therewith a land that is<br> Dead; even so will ye<br> Be raised (from the dead);—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_012.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>12.  Wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>ee khalaqa al-azw<u>a</u>ja kullah<u>a</u> wajaAAala lakum mina alfulki wa<b>a</b>l-anAA<u>a</u>mi m<u>a</u> tarkaboon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>12.  That has created pairs<br> In all things, and has made<br> For you ships and cattle<br> On which ye ride,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_013.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>13.  Litastawoo AAal<u>a</u> <i><u>th</u></i>uhoorihi thumma ta<u>th</u>kuroo niAAmata rabbikum i<u>tha</u> istawaytum AAalayhi wataqooloo sub<u>ha</u>na alla<u>th</u>ee sakhkhara lan<u>a</u> h<u>atha</u> wam<u>a</u> kunn<u>a</u> lahu muqrineen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>13.  In order that ye may<br> Sit firm and square<br> On their backs, and when<br> So seated, ye may<br> Celebrate the (kind) favour<br> Of your Lord, and say,<br> "Glory to Him Who<br> Has subjected these<br> To our (use), for we<br> Could never have accomplished<br> This (by ourselves),</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_014.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>14.  Wa-inn<u>a</u> il<u>a</u> rabbin<u>a</u> lamunqaliboon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>14.  "And to our Lord, surely,<br> Must we turn back!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/043/043_015.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>15.  WajaAAaloo lahu min AAib<u>a</u>dihi juz-an inna al-ins<u>a</u>na lakafoorun mubeen<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>15.  Yet they attribute<br> To some of His servants<br> A share with Him<br> (In His godhead)!<br> Truly is man a blasphemous<br> Ingrate avowed!</p> <div class="filenav">
