 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XII.: Yūsuf, or Joseph.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 2</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_007.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>7.  Laqad k<u>a</u>na fee yoosufa wa-ikhwatihi <u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tun li<b>l</b>ss<u>a</u>-ileen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>7.  Verily in Joseph and his brethren<br> Are Signs (or Symbols)<br>For Seekers (after Truth).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_008.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>8.  I<u>th</u> q<u>a</u>loo layoosufu waakhoohu a<u>h</u>abbu il<u>a</u> abeen<u>a</u> minn<u>a</u> wana<u>h</u>nu AAu<u>s</u>batun inna ab<u>a</u>n<u>a</u> lafee <u>d</u>al<u>a</u>lin mubeen<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>8.  They said: "Truly Joseph<br> And his brother are loved<br> More by our father than we:<br> But we are a goodly body!<br> Really our father is obviously<br> Wandering (in his mind)!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_009.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>9.  Oqtuloo yoosufa awi i<u>t</u>ra<u>h</u>oohu ar<u>d</u>an yakhlu lakum wajhu abeekum watakoonoo min baAAdihi qawman <u>sa</u>li<u>h</u>een<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>9.  "Slay ye Joseph or cast him out<br> To some (unknown) land,<br> That so the favour<br> Of your father may be<br> Given to you alone:<br> (There will be time enough)<br> For you to be righteous after that!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_010.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>10.  Q<u>a</u>la q<u>a</u>-ilun minhum l<u>a</u> taqtuloo yoosufa waalqoohu fee ghay<u>a</u>bati aljubbi yaltaqi<u>t</u>hu baAA<u>d</u>u a<b>l</b>ssayy<u>a</u>rati in kuntum f<u>a</u>AAileen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>10.  Said one of them: "Slay not<br> Joseph, but if ye must<br> Do something, throw him down<br> To the bottom of the well:<br> He will be picked up<br> By some caravan of travellers."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_011.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>11.  Q<u>a</u>loo y<u>a</u> ab<u>a</u>n<u>a</u> m<u>a</u> laka l<u>a</u> ta/mann<u>a</u> AAal<u>a</u> yoosufa wa-inn<u>a</u> lahu lan<u>as</u>i<u>h</u>oon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>11.  They said: "O our father!<br> Why dost thou not<br> Trust us with Joseph,<br> Seeing we are indeed<br> His sincere well-wishers?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_012.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>12.  Arsilhu maAAan<u>a</u> ghadan yartaAA wayalAAab wa-inn<u>a</u> lahu la<u>ha</u>fi<i><u>th</u></i>oon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>12.  "Send him with us tomorrow<br> To enjoy himself and play,<br> And we shall take<br> Every care of him."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_013.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>13.  Q<u>a</u>la innee laya<u>h</u>zununee an tha<u>th</u>haboo bihi waakh<u>a</u>fu an ya/kulahu a<b>l</b><u>thth</u>i/bu waantum AAanhu gh<u>a</u>filoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>13.  (Jacob) said: "Really<br> It saddens me that ye<br> Should take him away:<br> I fear lest the wolf<br> Should devour him<br> While ye attend not<br> To him."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_014.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>14.  Q<u>a</u>loo la-in akalahu a<b>l</b><u>thth</u>i/bu wana<u>h</u>nu AAu<u>s</u>batun inn<u>a</u> i<u>th</u>an lakh<u>a</u>siroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>14.  They said: "If the wolf<br> Were to devour him<br> While we are (so large) a party,<br> Then should we indeed<br> (First) have perished ourselves!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_015.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>15.  Falamm<u>a</u> <u>th</u>ahaboo bihi waajmaAAoo an yajAAaloohu fee ghay<u>a</u>bati aljubbi waaw<u>h</u>ayn<u>a</u> ilayhi latunabi-annahum bi-amrihim h<u>atha</u> wahum l<u>a</u> yashAAuroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>15.  So they did take him away,<br> And they all agreed<br>To throw him down<br> To the bottom of the well:<br> And We put into his heart<br> (This Message): "Of a surety<br> Thou shalt (one day)<br> Tell them the truth<br> Of this their affair<br> While they know (thee) not"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_016.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>16.  Waj<u>a</u>oo ab<u>a</u>hum AAish<u>a</u>an yabkoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>16.  Then they came<br> To their father<br> In the early part<br> Of the night,<br> Weeping.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_017.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>17.  Q<u>a</u>loo y<u>a</u> ab<u>a</u>n<u>a</u> inn<u>a</u> <u>th</u>ahabn<u>a</u> nastabiqu watarakn<u>a</u> yoosufa AAinda mat<u>a</u>AAin<u>a</u> faakalahu a<b>l</b><u>thth</u>i/bu wam<u>a</u> anta bimu/minin lan<u>a</u> walaw kunn<u>a</u> <u>sa</u>diqeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>17.  They said: "O our father!<br> We went racing with one another,<br> And left Joseph with our things;<br> And the wolf devoured him.…<br> But thou wilt never believe us<br> Even though we tell the truth.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_018.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>18.  Waj<u>a</u>oo AAal<u>a</u> qamee<u>s</u>ihi bidamin ka<u>th</u>ibin q<u>a</u>la bal sawwalat lakum anfusukum amran fa<u>s</u>abrun jameelun wa<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hu almustaAA<u>a</u>nu AAal<u>a</u> m<u>a</u> ta<u>s</u>ifoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>18.  They stained his shirt<br> With false blood. He said:<br>"Nay, but your minds<br> Have made up a tale<br> (That may pass) with you.<br> (For me) patience is most fitting:<br> Against that which ye assert,<br> It is God (alone)<br> Whose help can be sought"…</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_019.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>19.  Waj<u>a</u>at sayy<u>a</u>ratun faarsaloo w<u>a</u>ridahum faadl<u>a</u> dalwahu q<u>a</u>la y<u>a</u> bushr<u>a</u> h<u>atha</u> ghul<u>a</u>mun waasarroohu bi<u>da</u>AAatan wa<b>A</b>ll<u>a</u>hu AAaleemun bim<u>a</u> yaAAmaloon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>19.  Then there came a caravan<br> Of travellers: they sent<br> Their water-carrier (for water),<br> And he let down his bucket<br> (Into the well)… He said:<br> "Ah there! Good news!<br> Here is a (fine) young man!"<br> So they concealed him<br> As a treasure! But God<br> Knoweth well all that they do!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/012/012_020.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>20.  Washarawhu bithamanin bakhsin dar<u>a</u>hima maAAdoodatin wak<u>a</u>noo feehi mina a<b>l</b>zz<u>a</u>hideen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>20.  The (Brethren) sold him<br> For a miserable price,—<br>For a few dirhams counted out:<br> In such low estimation<br> Did they hold him!</p> <div class="filenav">
