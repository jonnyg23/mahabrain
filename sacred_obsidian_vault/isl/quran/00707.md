 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/007/007_054.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>54.  Inna rabbakumu All<u>a</u>hu alla<u>th</u>ee khalaqa a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>w<u>a</u>ti wa<b>a</b>l-ar<u>d</u>a fee sittati ayy<u>a</u>min thumma istaw<u>a</u> AAal<u>a</u> alAAarshi yughshee allayla a<b>l</b>nnah<u>a</u>ra ya<u>t</u>lubuhu <u>h</u>atheethan wa<b>al</b>shshamsa wa<b>a</b>lqamara wa<b>al</b>nnujooma musakhkhar<u>a</u>tin bi-amrihi al<u>a</u> lahu alkhalqu wa<b>a</b>l-amru tab<u>a</u>raka All<u>a</u>hu rabbu alAA<u>a</u>lameen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>54.  Your Guardian-Lord<br> Is God, Who created<br> The heavens and the earth<br> In six Days, and is firmly<br> Established on the Throne<br> (Of authority): He draweth<br> The night as a veil<br> O'er the day, each seeking<br> The other in rapid succession:<br> He created the sun,<br> The moon, and the stars,<br> (All) governed by laws<br> Under His Command.<br> Is it not His to create<br> And to govern? Blessed<br> Be God, the Cherisher<br> And Sustainer of the Worlds!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/007/007_055.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>55.  OdAAoo rabbakum ta<u>d</u>arruAAan wakhufyatan innahu l<u>a</u> yu<u>h</u>ibbu almuAAtadeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>55.  Call on your Lord<br> With humility and in private:<br>For God loveth not<br> Those who trespass beyond bounds.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/007/007_056.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>56.  Wal<u>a</u> tufsidoo fee al-ar<u>d</u>i baAAda i<u>s</u>l<u>ah</u>ih<u>a</u> wa<b>o</b>dAAoohu khawfan wa<u>t</u>amaAAan inna ra<u>h</u>mata All<u>a</u>hi qareebun mina almu<u>h</u>sineen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>56.  Do no mischief on the earth,<br> After it hath been<br> Set in order, but call<br> On Him with fear<br> And longing (in your hearts):<br> For the Mercy of God<br> Is (always) near<br> To those who do good.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/007/007_057.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>57.  Wahuwa alla<u>th</u>ee yursilu a<b>l</b>rriy<u>ah</u>a bushran bayna yaday ra<u>h</u>matihi <u>h</u>att<u>a</u> i<u>tha</u> aqallat sa<u>ha</u>ban thiq<u>a</u>lan suqn<u>a</u>hu libaladin mayyitin faanzaln<u>a</u> bihi alm<u>a</u>a faakhrajn<u>a</u> bihi min kulli a<b>l</b>ththamar<u>a</u>ti ka<u>tha</u>lika nukhriju almawt<u>a</u> laAAallakum ta<u>th</u>akkaroon<b>a</b>    </p>
 <p>57.  It is He Who sendeth<br> The Winds like heralds<br> Of glad tidings, going before<br> His Mercy: when they have<br> Carried the heavy-laden<br> Clouds, We drive them<br> To a land that is dead,<br> Make rain to descend thereon,<br> And produce every kind<br> Of harvest therewith: thus<br> Shall We raise up the dead:<br> Perchance ye may remember.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/007/007_058.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>58.  Wa<b>a</b>lbaladu a<b>l</b><u>tt</u>ayyibu yakhruju nab<u>a</u>tuhu bi-i<u>th</u>ni rabbihi wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>ee khabutha l<u>a</u> yakhruju ill<u>a</u> nakidan ka<u>tha</u>lika nu<u>s</u>arrifu al-<u>a</u>y<u>a</u>ti liqawmin yashkuroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>58.  From the land that is clean<br> And good, by the Will<br> Of its Cherisher, springs up<br> Produce, (rich) after its kind:<br>But from the land that is<br> Bad, springs up nothing<br> But that which is niggardly:<br> Thus do we explain the Signs<br> By various (symbols) to those<br> Who are grateful.</p> <div class="filenav">
