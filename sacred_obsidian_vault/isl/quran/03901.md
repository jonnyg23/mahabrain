 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XXXIX.: Zumar, or the Crowds.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 1</h3> 
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_001.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>1.  Tanzeelu alkit<u>a</u>bi mina All<u>a</u>hi alAAazeezi al<u>h</u>akeem<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>1.  The revelation<br> Of this Book<br> Is from God,<br> The Exalted in Power,<br> Full of Wisdom.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_002.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>2.  Inn<u>a</u> anzaln<u>a</u> ilayka alkit<u>a</u>ba bi<b>a</b>l<u>h</u>aqqi fa<b>o</b>AAbudi All<u>a</u>ha mukhli<u>s</u>an lahu a<b>l</b>ddeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>2.  Verily it is We Who have<br> Revealed the Book to thee<br> In Truth: so serve God,<br> Offering Him sincere devotion.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_003.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>3.  Al<u>a</u> lill<u>a</u>hi a<b>l</b>ddeenu alkh<u>a</u>li<u>s</u>u wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>eena ittakha<u>th</u>oo min doonihi awliy<u>a</u>a m<u>a</u> naAAbuduhum ill<u>a</u> liyuqarriboon<u>a</u> il<u>a</u> All<u>a</u>hi zulf<u>a</u> inna All<u>a</u>ha ya<u>h</u>kumu baynahum fee m<u>a</u> hum feehi yakhtalifoona inna All<u>a</u>ha l<u>a</u> yahdee man huwa k<u>ath</u>ibun kaff<u>a</u>r<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>3.  Is it not to God<br> That sincere devotion<br> Is due? But those who<br> Take for protectors others<br> Than God (say): "We only<br> Serve them in order that<br> They may bring us nearer<br> To God." Truly God<br> Will judge between them<br> In that wherein they differ.<br> But God guides not<br> Such as are false<br> And ungrateful.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_004.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>4.  Law ar<u>a</u>da All<u>a</u>hu an yattakhi<u>th</u>a waladan la<b>i</b><u>st</u>af<u>a</u> mimm<u>a</u> yakhluqu m<u>a</u> yash<u>a</u>o sub<u>ha</u>nahu huwa All<u>a</u>hu alw<u>ah</u>idu alqahh<u>a</u>r<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>4.  Had God wished<br> To take to Himself<br> A son, He could have<br> Chosen whom He pleased<br> Out of those whom He<br> Doth create: but Glory<br> Be to Him! (He is above<br> Such things.) He is God,<br> The One, the Irresistible.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_005.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>5.  Khalaqa a<b>l</b>ssam<u>a</u>w<u>a</u>ti wa<b>a</b>l-ar<u>d</u>a bi<b>a</b>l<u>h</u>aqqi yukawwiru allayla AAal<u>a</u> a<b>l</b>nnah<u>a</u>ri wayukawwiru a<b>l</b>nnah<u>a</u>ra AAal<u>a</u> allayli wasakhkhara a<b>l</b>shshamsa wa<b>a</b>lqamara kullun yajree li-ajalin musamman al<u>a</u> huwa alAAazeezu alghaff<u>a</u>r<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>5.  He created the heavens<br> And the earth<br> In true (proportions):<br> He makes the Night<br> Overlap the Day, and the Day<br> Overlap the Night:<br> He has subjected<br> The sun and the moon<br> (To His law):<br> Each one follows a course<br> For a time appointed.<br> Is not He the Exalted<br> In Power—He Who forgives<br> Again and again?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_006.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>6.  Khalaqakum min nafsin w<u>ah</u>idatin thumma jaAAala minh<u>a</u> zawjah<u>a</u> waanzala lakum mina al-anAA<u>a</u>mi tham<u>a</u>niyata azw<u>a</u>jin yakhluqukum fee bu<u>t</u>ooni ommah<u>a</u>tikum khalqan min baAAdi khalqin fee <i><u>th</u></i>ulum<u>a</u>tin thal<u>a</u>thin <u>tha</u>likumu All<u>a</u>hu rabbukum lahu almulku l<u>a</u> il<u>a</u>ha ill<u>a</u> huwa faann<u>a</u> tu<u>s</u>rafoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>6.  He created you (all)<br> From a single Person:<br> Then created, of like nature,<br> His mate; and He<br> Sent down for you eight head<br> Of cattle in pairs:<br> He makes you,<br> In the wombs<br>Of your mothers,<br> In stages, one after another,<br> In three veils of darkness.<br> Such is God, your Lord<br> And Cherisher: to Him belongs<br> (All) dominion. There is<br> No god but He: then<br> How are ye turned away<br> (From your true Centre)?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_007.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>7.  In takfuroo fa-inna All<u>a</u>ha ghaniyyun AAankum wal<u>a</u> yar<u>da</u> liAAib<u>a</u>dihi alkufra wa-in tashkuroo yar<u>d</u>ahu lakum wal<u>a</u> taziru w<u>a</u>ziratun wizra okhr<u>a</u> thumma il<u>a</u> rabbikum marjiAAukum fayunabbi-okum bim<u>a</u> kuntum taAAmaloona innahu AAaleemun bi<u>tha</u>ti a<b>l</b><u>ss</u>udoor<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>7.  If ye reject (God),<br> Truly God hath no need<br> Of you; but He liketh not<br> Ingratitude from His servants:<br> If ye are grateful, He<br> Is pleased with you.<br> No bearer of burdens<br> Can bear the burden<br> Of another. In the End,<br> To your Lord is your Return,<br> When He will tell you<br> The truth of all<br> That ye did (in this life).<br> For He knoweth well<br> All that is in (men's) hearts.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_008.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>8.  Wa-i<u>tha</u> massa al-ins<u>a</u>na <u>d</u>urrun daAA<u>a</u> rabbahu muneeban ilayhi thumma i<u>tha</u> khawwalahu niAAmatan minhu nasiya m<u>a</u> k<u>a</u>na yadAAoo ilayhi min qablu wajaAAala lill<u>a</u>hi and<u>a</u>dan liyu<u>d</u>illa AAan sabeelihi qul tamattaAA bikufrika qaleelan innaka min a<u>s</u>-<u>ha</u>bi a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>r<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>8.  When some trouble toucheth man,<br> He crieth unto his Lord,<br>Turning to Him in repentance:<br> But when He bestoweth<br> A favour upon him<br> As from Himself, (man)<br> Doth forget what he cried<br> And prayed for before,<br> And he doth set up<br> Rivals unto God,<br> Thus misleading others<br> From God's Path.<br> Say, "Enjoy thy blasphemy<br> For a little while:<br> Verily thou art (one)<br> Of the Companions of the Fire!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/039/039_009.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>9.  Amman huwa q<u>a</u>nitun <u>a</u>n<u>a</u>a allayli s<u>a</u>jidan waq<u>a</u>-iman ya<u>hth</u>aru al-<u>a</u>khirata wayarjoo ra<u>h</u>mata rabbihi qul hal yastawee alla<u>th</u>eena yaAAlamoona wa<b>a</b>lla<u>th</u>eena l<u>a</u> yaAAlamoona innam<u>a</u> yata<u>th</u>akkaru oloo al-alb<u>a</u>b<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>9.  Is one who worships devoutly<br> During the hours of the night<br> Prostrating himself or standing<br> (In adoration), who takes heed<br> Of the Hereafter, and who<br> Places his hope in the Mercy<br> Of his Lord—(like one<br> Who does not)? Say:<br> "Are those equal, those who know<br> And those who do not know?<br> It is those who are<br> Endued with understanding<br> That receive admonition.</p> <div class="filenav">
