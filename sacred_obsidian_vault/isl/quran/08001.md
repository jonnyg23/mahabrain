 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra LXXX.: ’Abasa, or He Frowned.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 1</h3> 
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_001.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>1.  AAabasa watawall<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>1.  (The Prophet) frowned<br> And turned away,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_002.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>2.  An j<u>a</u>ahu al-aAAm<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>2.  Because there came to him<br> The blind man (interrupting).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_003.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>3.  Wam<u>a</u> yudreeka laAAallahu yazzakk<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>3.  But what could tell thee<br> But that perchance he might<br> Grow (in spiritual understanding)?—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_004.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>4.  Aw ya<u>thth</u>akkaru fatanfaAAahu a<b>l</b><u>thth</u>ikr<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>4.  Or that he might receive<br> Admonition, and the teaching<br> Might profit him?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_005.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>5.  Amm<u>a</u> mani istaghn<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>5.  As to one who regards<br> Himself as self-sufficient,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_006.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>6.  Faanta lahu ta<u>s</u>add<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>6.  To him dost thou attend;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_007.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>7.  Wam<u>a</u> AAalayka all<u>a</u> yazzakk<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>7.  Though it is no blame<br> To thee if he grow not<br> (In spiritual understanding).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_008.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>8.  Waamm<u>a</u> man j<u>a</u>aka yasAA<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>8.  But as to him who came<br> To thee striving earnestly,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_009.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>9.  Wahuwa yakhsh<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>9.  And with fear<br> (In his heart),</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_010.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>10.  Faanta AAanhu talahh<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>10.  Of him wast thou unmindful.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_011.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>11.  Kall<u>a</u> innah<u>a</u> ta<u>th</u>kira<b>tun</b>   </p>
 <p>11.  By no means<br> (Should it be so)!<br> For it is indeed<br> A Message of instruction:</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_012.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>12.  Faman sh<u>a</u>a <u>th</u>akarah<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>12.  Therefore let whoso will,<br> Keep it in remembrance.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_013.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>13.  Fee <u>s</u>u<u>h</u>ufin mukarrama<b>tin</b>   </p>
 <p>13.  (It is) in Books<br> Held (greatly) in honour,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_014.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>14.  MarfooAAatin mu<u>t</u>ahhara<b>tin</b>   </p>
 <p>14.  Exalted (in dignity),<br> Kept pure and holy,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_015.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>15.  Bi-aydee safara<b>tin</b>   </p>
 <p>15.  (Written) by the hands<br> Of scribes—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_016.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>16.  Kir<u>a</u>min barara<b>tin</b>   </p>
 <p>16.  Honourable and<br> Pious and Just.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_017.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>17.  Qutila al-ins<u>a</u>nu m<u>a</u> akfarah<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>17.  Woe to man!<br> What hath made him<br> Reject God:</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_018.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>18.  Min ayyi shay-in khalaqah<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>18.  From what stuff<br> Hath He created him?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_019.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>19.  Min nu<u>t</u>fatin khalaqahu faqaddarah<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>19.  From a sperm-drop:<br> He hath created him, and then<br> Mouldeth him in due proportions;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_020.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>20.  Thumma a<b>l</b>ssabeela yassarah<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>20.  Then doth He make<br> His path smooth for him;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_021.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>21.  Thumma am<u>a</u>tahu faaqbarah<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>21.  Then He causeth him to die,<br> And putteth him in his Grave;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_022.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>22.  Thumma i<u>tha</u> sh<u>a</u>a ansharah<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>22.  Then, when it is<br> His Will, He will<br> Raise him up (again).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_023.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>23.  Kall<u>a</u> lamm<u>a</u> yaq<u>d</u>i m<u>a</u> amarah<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>23.  By no means hath he<br> Fulfilled what God<br> Hath commanded him.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_024.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>24.  Falyan<i><u>th</u></i>uri al-ins<u>a</u>nu il<u>a</u> <u>t</u>aAA<u>a</u>mih<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>24.  Then let man look<br> At his Food,<br> (And how We provide it):</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_025.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>25.  Ann<u>a</u> <u>s</u>ababn<u>a</u> alm<u>a</u>a <u>s</u>abb<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>25.  For that We pour forth<br> Water in abundance,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_026.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>26.  Thumma shaqaqn<u>a</u> al-ar<u>d</u>a shaqq<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>26.  And We split the earth<br> In fragments,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_027.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>27.  Faanbatn<u>a</u> feeh<u>a</u> <u>h</u>abb<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>27.  And produce therein Corn,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_028.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>28.  WaAAinaban waqa<u>d</u>b<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>28.  And Grapes and nutritious Plants,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_029.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>29.  Wazaytoonan wanakhl<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>29.  And Olives and Dates,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_030.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>30.  Wa<u>h</u>ad<u>a</u>-iqa ghulb<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>30.  And enclosed Gardens,<br> Dense with lofty trees,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_031.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>31.  Waf<u>a</u>kihatan waabb<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>31.  And Fruits and Fodder,—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_032.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>32.  Mat<u>a</u>AAan lakum wali-anAA<u>a</u>mikum   </p>
 <p>32.  For use and convenience<br> To you and your cattle.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_033.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>33.  Fa-i<u>tha</u> j<u>a</u>ati a<b>l</b><u>ssa</u>khkha<b>tu</b>   </p>
 <p>33.  At length, when there<br> Comes the Deafening Noise,—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_034.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>34.  Yawma yafirru almaro min akheeh<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>34.  That Day shall a man<br> Flee from his own brother,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_035.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>35.  Waommihi waabeeh<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>35.  And from his mother<br> And his father,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_036.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>36.  Wa<u>sah</u>ibatihi wabaneeh<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>36.  And from his wife<br> And his children.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_037.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>37.  Likulli imri-in minhum yawma-i<u>th</u>in sha/nun yughneeh<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>37.  Each one of them,<br> That Day, will have<br> Enough concern (of his own)<br> To make him indifferent<br> To the others,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_038.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>38.  Wujoohun yawma-i<u>th</u>in musfira<b>tun</b>   </p>
 <p>38.  Some Faces that Day<br> Will be beaming,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_039.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>39.  <u>Dah</u>ikatun mustabshira<b>tun</b>   </p>
 <p>39.  Laughing, rejoicing.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_040.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>40.  Wawujoohun yawma-i<u>th</u>in AAalayh<u>a</u> ghabara<b>tun</b>   </p>
 <p>40.  And other faces that Day<br> Will be dust-stained;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_041.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>41.  Tarhaquh<u>a</u> qatara<b>tun</b>   </p>
 <p>41.  Blackness will cover them:</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/080/080_042.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>42.  Ol<u>a</u>-ika humu alkafaratu alfajara<b>tu</b>   </p>
 <p>42.  Such will be<br> The Rejecters of God,<br> The Doers of Iniquity.</p>  <div class="filenav">
