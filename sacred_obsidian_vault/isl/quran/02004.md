 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XX.: Ṭā-Hā. (Mystic Letters, Ṭ. H.)</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 4</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_077.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>77.  Walaqad aw<u>h</u>ayn<u>a</u> il<u>a</u> moos<u>a</u> an asri biAAib<u>a</u>dee fa<b>i</b><u>d</u>rib lahum <u>t</u>areeqan fee alba<u>h</u>ri yabasan l<u>a</u> takh<u>a</u>fu darakan wal<u>a</u> takhsh<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>77.  We sent an inspiration<br> To Moses: "Travel by night<br> With My servants, and strike<br> A dry path for them<br> Through the sea, without fear<br> Of being overtaken (by Pharaoh)<br> And without (any other) fear."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_078.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>78.  FaatbaAAahum firAAawnu bijunoodihi faghashiyahum mina alyammi m<u>a</u> ghashiyahum   </p>
 <p>78.  Then Pharaoh pursued them<br> With his forces, but<br> The waters completely overwhelmed<br> Them and covered them up.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_079.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>79.  Waa<u>d</u>alla firAAawnu qawmahu wam<u>a</u> had<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>79.  Pharaoh led his people astray<br> Instead of leading them aright.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_080.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>80.  Y<u>a</u> banee isr<u>a</u>-eela qad anjayn<u>a</u>kum min AAaduwwikum waw<u>a</u>AAadn<u>a</u>kum j<u>a</u>niba a<b>l</b><u>tt</u>oori al-aymana wanazzaln<u>a</u> AAalaykumu almanna wa<b>al</b>ssalw<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>80.  O ye Children of Israel!<br> We delivered you from<br> Your enemy, and We<br> Made a Covenant with you<br> On the right side of<br> Mount (Sinai), and We sent<br> Down to you Manna<br> And quails:</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_081.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>81.  Kuloo min <u>t</u>ayyib<u>a</u>ti m<u>a</u> razaqn<u>a</u>kum wal<u>a</u> ta<u>t</u>ghaw feehi faya<u>h</u>illa AAalaykum gha<u>d</u>abee waman ya<u>h</u>lil AAalayhi gha<u>d</u>abee faqad haw<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>81.  (Saying): "Eat of the good<br> Things We have provided<br> For your sustenance, but<br> Commit no excess therein,<br> Lest My Wrath should justly<br> Descend on you: and those<br> On whom descends My Wrath<br> Do perish indeed!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_082.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>82.  Wa-innee laghaff<u>a</u>run liman t<u>a</u>ba wa<u>a</u>mana waAAamila <u>sa</u>li<u>h</u>an thumma ihtad<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>82.  "But, without doubt, I am<br> (Also) He that forgives<br> Again and again, to those<br> Who repent, believe,<br> And do right,—who,<br> In fine, are ready to receive<br> True guidance."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_083.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>83.  Wam<u>a</u> aAAjalaka AAan qawmika y<u>a</u> moos<u>a</u>   </p>
 <p>83.  (When Moses was up on the Mount,<br> God said:) "What made thee<br> Hasten in advance of thy people,<br> O Moses?"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_084.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>84.  Q<u>a</u>la hum ol<u>a</u>-i AAal<u>a</u> atharee waAAajiltu ilayka rabbi litar<u>da</u>   </p>
 <p>84.  He replied: "Behold, they are<br> Close on my footsteps:<br>I hastened to Thee.<br> O my Lord,<br> To please Thee."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_085.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>85.  Q<u>a</u>la fa-inn<u>a</u> qad fatann<u>a</u> qawmaka min baAAdika waa<u>d</u>allahumu a<b>l</b>ss<u>a</u>miriy<b>yu</b>   </p>
 <p>85.  (God) said: "We have tested<br> Thy people in thy absence:<br> The Sāmirī has led them<br> Astray."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_086.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>86.  FarajaAAa moos<u>a</u> il<u>a</u> qawmihi gha<u>d</u>b<u>a</u>na asifan q<u>a</u>la y<u>a</u> qawmi alam yaAAidkum rabbukum waAAdan <u>h</u>asanan afa<u>ta</u>la AAalaykumu alAAahdu am aradtum an ya<u>h</u>illa AAalaykum gha<u>d</u>abun min rabbikum faakhlaftum mawAAidee   </p>
 <p>86.  So Moses returned to his people<br> In a state of indignation<br> And sorrow. He said:<br> "O my people! did not<br> Your Lord make a handsome<br> Promise to you? Did then<br> The promise seem to you<br> Long (in coming)? Or did ye<br> Desire that Wrath should<br> Descend from your Lord on you,<br> And so ye broke your promise<br> To me?"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_087.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>87.  Q<u>a</u>loo m<u>a</u> akhlafn<u>a</u> mawAAidaka bimalkin<u>a</u> wal<u>a</u>kinn<u>a</u> <u>h</u>ummiln<u>a</u> awz<u>a</u>ran min zeenati alqawmi faqa<u>th</u>afn<u>a</u>h<u>a</u> faka<u>tha</u>lika alq<u>a</u> a<b>l</b>ss<u>a</u>miriy<b>yu</b>   </p>
 <p>87.  They said: "We broke not<br> The promise to thee, as far<br> As lay in our power:<br> But we were made to carry<br> The weight of the ornaments<br> Of the (whole) people, and we<br>Threw them (into the fire),<br> And that was what<br> The Sāmirī suggested.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_088.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>88.  Faakhraja lahum AAijlan jasadan lahu khuw<u>a</u>run faq<u>a</u>loo h<u>atha</u> il<u>a</u>hukum wa-il<u>a</u>hu moos<u>a</u> fanasiy<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>88.  "Then he brought out<br> (Of the fire) before the (people)<br> The image of a calf:<br> It seemed to low:<br> So they said, "This is<br> Your god, and the god<br> Of Moses, but (Moses)<br> Has forgotten!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/020/020_089.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>89.  Afal<u>a</u> yarawna all<u>a</u> yarjiAAu ilayhim qawlan wal<u>a</u> yamliku lahum <u>d</u>arran wal<u>a</u> nafAA<u>a</u><b>n</b>   </p>
 <p>89.  Could they not see that<br> It could not return them<br> A word (for answer), and that<br> It had no power either<br> To harm them or<br> To do them good?</p> <div class="filenav">
