 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra XXII.: Ḥajj, or The Pilgrimage.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 6</h3> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_039.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>39.  O<u>th</u>ina lilla<u>th</u>eena yuq<u>a</u>taloona bi-annahum <i><u>th</u></i>ulimoo wa-inna All<u>a</u>ha AAal<u>a</u> na<u>s</u>rihim laqadeer<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>39.  To those against whom<br> War is made, permission<br> Is given (to fight), because<br> They are wronged;—and verily,<br> God is most Powerful<br> For their aid;—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_040.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>40.  Alla<u>th</u>eena okhrijoo min diy<u>a</u>rihim bighayri <u>h</u>aqqin ill<u>a</u> an yaqooloo rabbun<u>a</u> All<u>a</u>hu walawl<u>a</u> dafAAu All<u>a</u>hi a<b>l</b>nn<u>a</u>sa baAA<u>d</u>ahum bibaAA<u>d</u>in lahuddimat <u>s</u>aw<u>a</u>miAAu wabiyaAAun wa<u>s</u>alaw<u>a</u>tun wamas<u>a</u>jidu yu<u>th</u>karu feeh<u>a</u> ismu All<u>a</u>hi katheeran walayan<u>s</u>uranna All<u>a</u>hu man yan<u>s</u>uruhu inna All<u>a</u>ha laqawiyyun AAazeez<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>40.  (They are) those who have<br> Been expelled from their homes<br>In defiance of right,<br> (For no cause) except<br> That they say," Lord<br> Is God". Did not God<br> Check one set of people<br> By means of another,<br> There would surely have been<br> Pulled down monasteries, churches,<br> Synagogues, and mosques, in which<br> The name of God is commemorated<br> In abundant measure. God will<br> Certainly aid those who<br> Aid His (cause);—for verily<br> God is Full of Strength,<br> Exalted in Might,<br> (Able to enforce His Will).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_041.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>41.  Alla<u>th</u>eena in makkann<u>a</u>hum fee al-ar<u>d</u>i aq<u>a</u>moo a<b>l</b><u>ss</u>al<u>a</u>ta wa<u>a</u>tawoo a<b>l</b>zzak<u>a</u>ta waamaroo bi<b>a</b>lmaAAroofi wanahaw AAani almunkari walill<u>a</u>hi AA<u>a</u>qibatu al-omoor<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>41.  (They are) those who,<br> If We establish them<br> In the land, establish<br> Regular prayer and give<br> Regular charity, enjoin<br> The right and forbid wrong:<br> With God rests the end<br> (And decision) of (all) affairs.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_042.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>42.  Wa-in yuka<u>thth</u>ibooka faqad ka<u>thth</u>abat qablahum qawmu noo<u>h</u>in waAA<u>a</u>dun wathamood<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>42.  If they treat thy (mission)<br> As false, so did the Peoples<br>Before them (with their Prophets),—<br> The People of Noah,<br> And ’Ad and <u>Th</u>amūd;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_043.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>43.  Waqawmu ibr<u>a</u>heema waqawmu loo<u>t</u><b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>43.  Those of Abraham and Lūt;</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_044.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>44.  Waa<u>s</u>-<u>ha</u>bu madyana waku<u>thth</u>iba moos<u>a</u> faamlaytu lilk<u>a</u>fireena thumma akha<u>th</u>tuhum fakayfa k<u>a</u>na nakeer<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>44.  And the Companions<br> Of the Madyan people;<br> And Moses was rejected<br> (In the same way). But I<br> Granted respite to the Unbelievers,<br> And (only) after that<br> Did I punish them:<br> But how (terrible) was<br> My rejection (of them)!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_045.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>45.  Fakaayyin min qaryatin ahlakn<u>a</u>h<u>a</u> wahiya <i><u>th</u></i><u>a</u>limatun fahiya kh<u>a</u>wiyatun AAal<u>a</u> AAurooshih<u>a</u> wabi/rin muAAa<u>tt</u>alatin waqa<u>s</u>rin masheed<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>45.  How many populations have We<br> Destroyed, which were given<br> To wrong-doing? They tumbled down<br> On their roofs. And how many<br> Wells are lying idle and neglected,<br> And castles lofty and well-built?</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_046.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>46.  Afalam yaseeroo fee al-ar<u>d</u>i fatakoona lahum quloobun yaAAqiloona bih<u>a</u> aw <u>atha</u>nun yasmaAAoona bih<u>a</u> fa-innah<u>a</u> l<u>a</u> taAAm<u>a</u> al-ab<u>sa</u>ru wal<u>a</u>kin taAAm<u>a</u> alquloobu allatee fee a<b>l</b><u>ss</u>udoor<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>46.  Do they not travel<br> Through the land, so that<br> Their hearts (and minds)<br> May thus learn wisdom<br>And their ears may<br> Thus learn to hear?<br> Truly it is not their eyes<br> That are blind, but their<br> Hearts which are<br> In their breasts.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_047.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>47.  WayastaAAjiloonaka bi<b>a</b>lAAa<u>tha</u>bi walan yukhlifa All<u>a</u>hu waAAdahu wa-inna yawman AAinda rabbika kaalfi sanatin mimm<u>a</u> taAAuddoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>47.  Yet they ask thee<br> To hasten on the Punishment!<br> But God will not fail<br> In His promise. Verily<br> A Day in the sight of thy Lord<br> Is like a thousand years<br> Of your reckoning.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/022/022_048.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>48.  Wakaayyin min qaryatin amlaytu lah<u>a</u> wahiya <i><u>th</u></i><u>a</u>limatun thumma akha<u>th</u>tuh<u>a</u> wa-ilayya alma<u>s</u>eer<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>48.  And to how many populations<br> Did I give respite, which<br> Were given to wrong-doing?<br> In the end I punished them.<br> To Me is the destination (of all).</p> <div class="filenav">
