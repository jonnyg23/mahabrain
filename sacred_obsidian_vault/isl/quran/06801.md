 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>The Holy Quran</i>, tr. by Yusuf Ali, [1934], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h1 align="CENTER">Sūra LXVIII.: Qalam, or the Pen, or Nūn.</h1> <h3 align="CENTER">Section 1</h3> 
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_001.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>1.  Noon wa<b>a</b>lqalami wam<u>a</u> yas<u>t</u>uroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>1.  Nūn. By the Pen<br> And by the (Record)<br> Which (men) write,—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_002.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>2.  M<u>a</u> anta biniAAmati rabbika bimajnoon<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>2.  Thou art not,<br> By the grace of thy Lord,<br> Mad or possessed.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_003.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>3.  Wa-inna laka laajran ghayra mamnoon<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>3.  Nay, verily for thee<br> Is a Reward unfailing:</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_004.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>4.  Wa-innaka laAAal<u>a</u> khuluqin AAa<i><u>th</u></i>eem<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>4.  And thou (standest)<br> On an exalted standard<br> Of character.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_005.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>5.  Fasatub<u>s</u>iru wayub<u>s</u>iroon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>5.  Soon wilt thou see,<br> And they will see,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_006.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>6.  Bi-ayyikumu almaftoon<b>u</b>   </p>
 <p>6.  Which of you is<br> Afflicted with madness.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_007.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>7.  Inna rabbaka huwa aAAlamu biman <u>d</u>alla AAan sabeelihi wahuwa aAAlamu bi<b>a</b>lmuhtadeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>7.  Verily it is thy Lord<br> That knoweth best,<br> Which (among men)<br> Hath strayed from His Path:<br> And He knoweth best<br> Those who receive<br> (True) Guidance.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_008.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>8.  Fal<u>a</u> tu<u>t</u>iAAi almuka<u>thth</u>ibeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>8.  So hearken not<br> To those who<br> Deny (the Truth),</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_009.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>9.  Waddoo law tudhinu fayudhinoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>9.  Their desire is that<br> Thou shouldst be pliant:<br> So would they be pliant.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_010.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>10.  Wal<u>a</u> tu<u>t</u>iAA kulla <u>h</u>all<u>a</u>fin maheen<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>10.  Heed not the type<br> Of despicable man,—<br> Ready with oaths,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_011.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>11.  Hamm<u>a</u>zin mashsh<u>a</u>-in binameem<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>11.  A slanderer, going about<br> With calumnies,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_012.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>12.  Mann<u>a</u>AAin lilkhayri muAAtadin atheem<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>12.  (Habitually) hindering (all) good,<br> Transgressing beyond bounds,<br> Deep in sin,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_013.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>13.  AAutullin baAAda <u>tha</u>lika zaneem<b>in</b>   </p>
 <p>13.  Violent (and cruel),—<br> With all that, base-born,—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_014.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>14.  An k<u>a</u>na <u>tha</u> m<u>a</u>lin wabaneen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>14.  Because he possesses<br> Wealth and (numerous) sons.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_015.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>15.  I<u>tha</u> tutl<u>a</u> AAalayhi <u>a</u>y<u>a</u>tun<u>a</u> q<u>a</u>la as<u>at</u>eeru al-awwaleen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>15.  When to him are rehearsed<br> Our Signs,<br> "Tales of the Ancients",<br> He cries!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_016.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>16.  Sanasimuhu AAal<u>a</u> alkhur<u>t</u>oom<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>16.  Soon shall We brand<br> (The beast) on the snout!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_017.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>17.  Inn<u>a</u> balawn<u>a</u>hum kam<u>a</u> balawn<u>a</u> a<u>s</u>-<u>ha</u>ba aljannati i<u>th</u> aqsamoo laya<u>s</u>rimunnah<u>a</u> mu<u>s</u>bi<u>h</u>een<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>17.  Verily We have tried them<br> As We tried the People<br>Of the Garden,<br> When they resolved to gather<br> The fruits of the (garden)<br> In the morning,</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_018.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>18.  Wal<u>a</u> yastathnoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>18.  But made no reservation,<br> ("If it be God's Will").</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_019.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>19.  Fa<u>ta</u>fa AAalayh<u>a</u> <u>ta</u>-ifun min rabbika wahum n<u>a</u>-imoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>19.  Then there came<br> On the (garden)<br> A visitation from thy Lord,<br> (Which swept away) all around,<br> While they were asleep.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_020.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>20.  Faa<u>s</u>ba<u>h</u>at ka<b>al</b><u>ss</u>areem<b>i</b>   </p>
 <p>20.  So the (garden) became,<br> By the morning, like<br> A dark and desolate spot,<br> (Whose fruit had been gathered).</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_021.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>21.  Fatan<u>a</u>daw mu<u>s</u>bi<u>h</u>een<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>21.  As the morning broke,<br> They called out,<br> One to another,—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_022.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>22.  Ani ighdoo AAal<u>a</u> <u>h</u>arthikum in kuntum <u>sa</u>rimeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>22.  "Go ye to your tilth<br> (Betimes) in the morning,<br>If ye would gather<br> The fruits."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_023.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>23.  Fa<b>i</b>n<u>t</u>alaqoo wahum yatakh<u>a</u>fatoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>23.  So they departed, conversing<br> In secret low tones, (saying)—</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_024.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>24.  An l<u>a</u> yadkhulannah<u>a</u> alyawma AAalaykum miskeen<b>un</b>   </p>
 <p>24.  "Let not a single indigent<br> Person break in upon you<br> Into the (garden) this day."</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_025.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>25.  Waghadaw AAal<u>a</u> <u>h</u>ardin q<u>a</u>direen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>25.  And they opened the morning,<br> Strong in an (unjust) resolve.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_026.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>26.  Falamm<u>a</u> raawh<u>a</u> q<u>a</u>loo inn<u>a</u> la<u>da</u>lloon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>26.  But when they saw<br> The (garden), they said:<br> "We have surely lost our way:</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_027.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>27.  Bal na<u>h</u>nu ma<u>h</u>roomoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>27.  "Indeed we are shut out<br> (Of the fruits of our labour)!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_028.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>28.  Q<u>a</u>la awsa<u>t</u>uhum alam aqul lakum lawl<u>a</u> tusabbi<u>h</u>oon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>28.  Said one of them,<br> More just (than the rest):<br> "Did I not say to you,<br> "Why not glorify (God)?"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_029.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>29.  Q<u>a</u>loo sub<u>ha</u>na rabbin<u>a</u> inn<u>a</u> kunn<u>a</u> <i><u>th</u></i><u>a</u>limeen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>29.  They said: "Glory<br> To our Lord! Verily we<br> Have been doing wrong!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_030.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>30.  Faaqbala baAA<u>d</u>uhum AAal<u>a</u> baAA<u>d</u>in yatal<u>a</u>wamoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>30.  Then they turned, one<br> Against another, in reproach.</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_031.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>31.  Q<u>a</u>loo y<u>a</u> waylan<u>a</u> inn<u>a</u> kunn<u>a</u> <u>ta</u>gheen<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>31.  They said: "Alas for us!<br> We have indeed transgressed!</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_032.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>32.  AAas<u>a</u> rabbun<u>a</u> an yubdilan<u>a</u> khayran minh<u>a</u> inn<u>a</u> il<u>a</u> rabbin<u>a</u> r<u>a</u>ghiboon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>32.  "It may be that our Lord<br> Will give us in exchange<br> A better (garden) than this:<br> For we do turn to Him<br> (In repentance)!"</p> <hr>
 <p align="RIGHT"><img src="../gif/068/068_033.gif"></p> 
 <p><a></a>33.  Ka<u>tha</u>lika alAAa<u>tha</u>bu walaAAa<u>tha</u>bu al-<u>a</u>khirati akbaru law k<u>a</u>noo yaAAlamoon<b>a</b>   </p>
 <p>33.  Such is the Punishment<br> (In this life); but greater<br> Is the Punishment<br> In the Hereafter,—<br> If only they knew!</p> <div class="filenav">
