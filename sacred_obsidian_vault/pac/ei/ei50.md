 <p align="CENTER">ATUA MATARIRI.</p> 
 <p>(Plates XXXVIII and XXXIX.)</p> 
 <p>Atua Matariri; Ki ai Kiroto, Kia Taporo, Kapu to Poporo.<br> Ahimahima Marao; Ki ai Kiroto, Takihi Tupufema, Kapu te Kihikehi.<br> Aoevai; Ki ai Kiroto, Kava Kohe Koe Kapu to Koe.<br> Matua anua; Ki ai Kiroto, Kappipiri Haitau, Kapu te Miro.<br> Augingieai; Ki ai Kiroto, Kia Humutoti, Kapu to Maluta.<br> Hiti; Ki ai Kiroto, Kia Heta Kapu te Ti.<br> Atura; Ki ai Kiroto, Katei, Kapa te Monku Uta.<br> Ahan; Ki ai Kiroto, Vava, Kapu te Tureme.<br> Ahekai; Ki ai Kiroto, Hepeue, Kapu te Mataa<br> Viri Koue; Mai Kiroto, Ariugarehe Uruharero, Kapu te Runa.<br> Atua Metua; Ki ai Kiroto, Kariritunarai, Kapa te Niu.<br> Atua Metua; Ki ai Kiroto, Kite Vuhi o Atua, Kapu te Toromiro.<br> Atua Metua; Ki ai Kiroto, Tapuhavaoatua, Kapu te Moana.<br> A Heuru; Ki ai Kiroto, Hetomu, Kapu te Marikuru.<br> A Taveke; Ki ai Kiroto, Pouhutuhututerevaimangaro, Kapu te Veke,<br> A Hahamea; Ki ai Kiroto, Hohio Kapu te Takure.<br> Aukia Ki ai Kiroto; Moremanga, Kapu te Ngarava.<br> Avia Moko; Ki ai Kiroto, Viatea, Kapu te Kena.<br> Tereheue; Ki ai Kiroto, Viaraupa, Kapu te Kaupa.<br> A Heroe; Ki ai Kiroto, Unhipura, Mapu te Ro.<br> Tahatoi; Ki ai Kiroto, Kateapiairiroro, Kapu te To.<br> Irapupue; Ki ai Kiroto, Irakaka, Kapu te Pia.<br> Mangeongeo; Ki ai Kiroto, Herakiraki Kapu te Kape.<br> A Hen; Ki ai Kiroto Pana Kapu te Hue.<br> Heima; Ki ai Kiroto Kairui Kairui-Hakamarui Kapu te Raa.<br> Huruan; Ni ai Kiroto Hiuaoio Kapu te Moa.<br> A Hikua: Ki ai Kiroto Hinaoioi Kapu te Uruara.<br> Tingahae: Ki ai Kiroto Parararahikutea Kapu te Niuki.<br> A Hikue: Ki ai Kiroto Hiuaoioi Kapu te Tabraha.</p> 
 <p><a name="page_521"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 521</font></a></p> 
 <p>Tikitehatu: Ki ai Kiroto Hihohihokiteturu Kapu te Paroko.<br> Tikitehatu: Ki ai Kiroto Hiuapopoia Kapu te Hiuakuhara.<br> Tikitehatu: Ki ai Kiroto Maea Kapu te Heraherakitomea.<br> Tikitehatu: Ki ai Kiroto Ruruatikitehatu Kapu te Teririkatea.<br> Atimoterae: mea a mura i hiki te alu mo tunu o te ita, mo haugai it te ariiki.<br> Takoua: Ki ai Kiroto Tukouo, Kapu te Poopoo.<br> E. Toto te Efi no Kino no naroko no ngaoreno no nga tokutoko ruapapa.<br> Epuoko te nuika no tupa iti no tupa nui.<br> Uku Ki ai Kiroto, Karori Kapu te Ngaatu.<br> Kuhikia Ki ai Kiroto Taurari Kapu to Ngaatu.<br> Kuhikia Ki ai Kiroto Ruperoa Kapu to Turi.<br> Taaria Ki ai Kiroto Taaria, Kapu te Taueehu.<br> Haiuge Ki ai Kiroto hatukuti, Kapu te Evea.<br> Pauaroroko Ki ai Kiroto Hakukuti, Kapu te Taerongoveteve.<br> Hiuitirerire Ki ai Kiroto Kanohotatataporo Kapu te Roporo.<br> Numia a Tangaire Turuhirohero te toto o te o korare.<br> Kamau te Korare taratara te Korare.<br> Turuki te Ua Maanau Manavai roa.<br> Kaunuku raituahea anakihorou eaa e to e tua tanu to tana moko eaha Uaugai e to e ufi e Kumara.</p> <h3 align="CENTER">ENGLISH TRANSLATION OF THE ABOVE TABLET.</h3> <h4 align="CENTER">EASTER ISLAND TRADITION.</h4> 
 <p>The origin of inanimate things is believed to be the result of the marriage of certain gods and goddesses in accordance with the following table.</p> 
 <p>God Atua Matariri and goddess Taporo produced thistle.<br> God Ahimahima Marao and goddess Takihi Tupufema produced rocks.<br> God Aoevai and goddess Kava Kohekoe produced medicine.<br> God Matua anua and goddess Kappipiri Aaitau produced the Miro, tree.<br> God Augingieai and goddess Kia Humutoti produced the paper-mulberry tree.<br> God Hiti and goddess Kia heta produced the tea plant.<br> God Atura and goddess Katei produced bunch grass.<br> God Ahen and goddess Vaua produced fine grass.<br> God Agekai and goddess Hepeue produced obsidian.<br> God Viri Kone and goddess Ariugarehe Uruharero produced the morning-glory plant.</p> 
 <p><a name="page_522"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 522</font></a></p> 
 <p>God Atua Metua and goddess Kariritunaria produced cocoanuts.<br> God Atua Metua and goddess Ki to Vuhi o Atua produced the toromiro tree.<br> God Atua Metua and goddess Tapuhavaoatua produced Hibiscus.<br> God A Heuru and goddess Hetomu produced the blue leaf plant.<br> God A Taveke and goddess Pouhutuhututerevaimangaro produced the white ash.<br> God A Hahamea and goddess Hohio produced flies.<br> God Aukia and goddess Moremanga produced roaches.<br> God A Via Moko and goddess Viatea produced boobies.<br> God Tereheue and goddess Viaraupa produced leaves.<br> God A Heroe and goddess Unhipura produced ants.<br> God Tahatoi and goddess Kateapiairiroro produced sugar-cane.<br> God Irapupue and goddess Irakaka produced arrowroot.<br> God Mangeongeo and goddess Herakiraki produced yams.<br> God Ahen and goddess Pana produced calabash.<br> God Heima and goddess Kairui-hakamarui produced stars.<br> God Huruan and goddess Hiuaoioi produced fowls.<br> God A Hikua and goddess Hiuaoioi produced vermilion.<br> God Tingahae and goddess Pararahikutea produced sharks.<br> God A Hikue and goddess Hiuaoioi produced porpoise.<br> God Tikitehatu and goddess Hihohihokiteturu produced rock-fish.<br> God Tikitehatu and goddess Hiuapopoia produced life.<br> God Tikitehatu and goddess Maea produced luck.<br> God Tikitehatu and goddess Ruruatikitehatu produced man.<br> Atimoterae created brook-fish and established them as the chosen food of the gods.<br> God Takoua and goddess Tukouo produced milk-thistle.<br> E Toto discovered the sweet taste of the yam and made it the principal food of the people.<br> Epuoko created the delicious banana food for the kings.<br> God Uku and goddess Karori produced bullrushes.<br> God Kuhikia and goddess Taurari produced small birds.<br> God Kuhikia and goddess Ruperoa produced sea-gulls.<br> God Taaria and goddess Taaria produced white gulls.<br> God Haiuge and goddess Hakukuti produced wind.<br> God Pauaroroko and goddess Hakukuti produced pain.<br> God Hiuitirerire and goddess Kanohotatataporo produced creeping vines.<br> Numia a Tangaire Turuhirohero was the founder of all things unpleasant and bad smells.<br> Turuki was the first builder of rock fences and barriers.<br> Kuanuku created death by drowning, death in warfare, death by accident, and death by disease.</p>
