 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="img/tease.jpg"></a><br><i>Te Pito Te Henua, or Easter Island</i>, by William J. Thompson, [1891], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="CENTER">NUMERALS.</h3> 
 <p>In counting the natives use the fingers of both hands but never the toes.</p> 
 <p>1 = Ka-tahi.<br> 2 = Ka-rua.<br> 3 = Ka-toru.<br> 4 = Ka-ha.<br> 5 = Ka-rima.<br> 6 = Ka-ono.<br> 7 = Ka-hitu.<br> 8 = Ka-varu.<br> 9 = Ka-iva.<br> 0 = Aanghuru.<br> 10 = Ka tahi te aanghuru.<br> 11 = Ka tahi te aanghuru Ka tahi.<br> 12 = Ka tahi te aanghuru Ka rua.<br> 13 = Ka ta hi te aanghuru Katoru, etc.<br> 20 = Ka rua te aanghuru.<br> 21 = Ka rua te aanghuru Ka tahi.<br> 22 = Ka rua te aanghuru Ka rua.<br> 23 = Ka rua te aanghuru Ka toru, etc.<br> 30 = Ka toru te aanghuru.<br> 31 = Ka toru te aanghuru, Ka tahi.<br> 32 = Ka toru te aanghuru, Ka rua.<br> 33 = Ka toru te aanghuru, Ka toru, etc.<br> 50 = Ka rima te aanghuru.<br> 60 = Ka ono te aanghuru.<br> 70 = Ka hitu te aanghuru.<br> 80 = Ka raru te aanghuru.<br> 90 = Ka ira te aanghuru.<br> 100 = Ka rau.<br> 101 = Ka tahi te rau ma tahi.<br> 102 = Ka tahi te rau ma rua.<br> 200 = Ka rua te rau.<br> 201 = Ka rima te rau ma taki.<br> 300 = Ka toru te rau.<br> 301 = Ka toru te rau ma tahi.<br> 400 = Ka ha te rau.<br> 401 = Ka ha te rau ma tahi.<br> 500 = Ka rima te rau, etc.<br> 1,000 = Piere.<br> 2,000 = Ka rua te piere,<br> 3,000= Ka toru te piere.<br> 4,000 = Ka ha te piere.<br> 10,000 = Ka mano.<br> 100,000 = Ka peka<br> 1.000,000 = Ha ra.<br> Over one million, mingoi-ngoi.</p> 
 <p>From 1 to 10 the syllables are pronounced as one word, in a multiple of ten the words are distinctly separated. A record of numbers was kept by stringing pieces of bulrush together.</p></body>
