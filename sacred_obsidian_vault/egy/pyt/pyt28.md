 <h3 align="center">25. MISCELLANEOUS TEXTS CHIEFLY ABOUT THE DECEASED KING'S RECEPTION AND
 LIFE IN HEAVEN, UTTERANCES 523-533.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 523</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1231a. To say: The sky has strengthened the radiance for N.,</p>
 
 <p>1231b. that N. may lift himself to heaven as the eye of Rē,</p>
 
 <p>1231c. and that N. may stand at this left eye of Horus</p>
 
 <p>1231d. where the word of the gods is heard.</p>
 
 <p>1232a. Thou shalt stand in the presence of the spirits,</p>
 
 <p>1232b. as Horus stood in the presence of the living.</p>
 
 <p>1232c. N. shall stand in the presence of the spirits, the imperishable
 stars,</p>
 
 <p>1232d. as Osiris stands in the presence of the spirits.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 524</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1233a. To say: N. is pure with the purification which Horus did to his eye.</p>
 
 <p>1233b. N. is Thot who avenges thee (the eye); N. is not Set who seizes it.</p>
 
 <p>1233c. Rejoice, O gods; rejoice, O Two Enneads.</p>
 
 <p>1234a. Let Horus approach N.</p>
 
 <p>1234b. N. is crowned with the white crown, the eye of Horus wherewith he is
 powerful.</p>
 
 <p>1234c. The gods rejoice for him who ascends.</p>
 
 <p>1235a. The face of N. is as that of a jackal; the two arms of N. are as
 those of a falcon;</p>
 
 <p>1235b. the extremities of the wings of N. are as those of Thot.</p>
 
 <p>1235c. May Geb let N. fly to heaven,</p>
 
 <p>1235d. that this N. may take the eye of Horus, to himself!</p>
 
 <p>1236a. N. has penetrated your frontier, ye dead;</p>
 
 <p>1236b. N. has overturned your boundary stones, ye who are before and with
 Osiris;</p>
 
 <p>1236c. N. has conjured the paths of Set;</p>
 
 <p>1236d. N. has passed by the messengers of Osiris.</p>
 
 <p>1237a. No god can hold N.;</p>
 
 <p>1237b. no opponent stands in the way of N.</p>
 
 <p>1237c. N. is Thot, the strongest of the gods;</p>
 
 <p>1237d. Atum calls N. to heaven for life.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_205"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 205</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1237e. N. has taken the eye of Horus to himself!</p>
 
 <p>1238a. N. is the son of Khnum; there is nothing evil which N. has done.</p>
 
 <p>1238b. Weighty is this word before thee, O Rē.</p>
 
 <p>1238c. Hear it, bull of the Ennead.</p>
 
 <p>1239a. Open the way of N.; enlarge the place of N. before the gods.</p>
 
 <p>1239b. N. has taken the eye of Horus to himself; N. has attached to himself
 that which went forth from his head.</p>
 
 <p>1240a. N. has caused him to see with both his eyes complete,</p>
 
 <p>1240b. that he may punish his enemies therewith.</p>
 
 <p>1240c. Horus has taken his eye and has given it to N.</p>
 
 <p>1241a. His odour is the odour of a god; the odour of the eye of Horus
 appertains to the flesh of N.</p>
 
 <p>1241b. N. is in front with it; N. sits upon your great throne, O gods;</p>
 
 <p>1241c. N. is side by side with Atum, between the two sceptres.</p>
 
 <p>1242a. N. is the <i>ḫwnnw</i>
 (messenger?) of the gods in search of the eye of Horus;</p>
 
 <p>1242b. N. searched for it at Buto; he found it at Heliopolis;</p>
 
 <p>1242c. N. snatched it from the head of Set, at the place where they fought.</p>
 
 <p>1243a. Horus, give thine arm to N.; Horus take to thyself thine eye;</p>
 
 <p>1243b. it mounts up to thee; it ascends to thee; it comes to thee, N., for
 life;</p>
 
 <p>1243c. the eye of Horus comes to thee with N., before N., for ever.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 525</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1244a. To say: Rē purified himself for thee; Horus adorned himself for
 thee,</p>
 
 <p>1244b. so that blindness (?) might cease and that sleeplessness might be
 repelled,</p>
 
 <p>1244c. before there existed a god, a son of god, a messenger of god.</p>
 
 <p>1245a. N. descends in the lake
  of <i>Kns.t</i>;</p>
 
 <p>1245b. N. purifies himself in the Marsh of Reeds;</p>
 
 <p>1245c. N. is purified by the Followers of Horus,</p>
 
 <p>1245d. who recite for N. "the chapter of those who ascend,"</p>
 
 <p>1245e. who recite for N. "the chapter of those who raise themselves
 up."</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_206"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 206</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1246a. Descend, N., into this thy boat of Rē which the gods row.</p>
 
 <p>1246b. When N. rises they (the gods) rejoice at the approach of N.,</p>
 
 <p>1246c. as they rejoice at the approach of Rē,</p>
 
 <p>1246d. when he comes forth in the East, mounting, mounting.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 526</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1247a. To say: N. has purified himself in the Lake of Reeds,</p>
 
 <p>1247b. wherein Rē was purified.</p>
 
 <p>1247c. Horus dries the back of N., the back of Thot, the legs of N., the
 legs of Shu.</p>
 
 <p>1247d. Shu, take N. to heaven; Nut, give thine arm to N.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 527</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1248a. To say: Atum created by his masturbation in Heliopolis.</p>
 
 <p>1248b. He put his phallus in his fist,</p>
 
 <p>1248c. to excite desire thereby.</p>
 
 <p>1248d. The twins were born, Shu and Tefnut.</p>
 
 <p>1249a. They put N. between them;</p>
 
 <p>1249b. they put N. among the gods in the Marsh of Offerings.</p>
 
 <p>1249c. To say four times: N. mounts to heaven;</p>
 
 <p>1249d. N. descends to the earth; for life everlasting.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 528</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1250a. Further, to say: O <i>Swn<u>t</u></i>,
 who traverses the sky nine times in the night,</p>
 
 <p>1250b. lay hold of the arm of N. for life;</p>
 
 <p>1250c. ferry him on this sea.</p>
 
 <p>1250d. (So) N. descends into this boat of the god,</p>
 
 <p>1250e. in which the corporation of the Ennead rows,</p>
 
 <p>1250f. to row N. in it.</p>
 
 <p>1251a. "The chapter of <i>Bdw</i>"
 is recited for thee;</p>
 
 <p>1251b. "the chapter of natron" is recited for thee.</p>
 
 <p>1251c. Incense stands (as chief) before the Great Ennead,</p>
 
 <p>1251d. while <i>Bdw</i> is seated before
 (or, in) the great <i>itr.t</i>-palace.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 529</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1252a. Further, to say: O this Doorkeeper of heaven,</p>
 
 <p>1252b. pay attention to this messenger of a god, ascending.</p>
 
 <p>1252c. When he goes forth by the western portal of the sky,</p>
 
 <p>1252d. bring him to the southern portal of the sky;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_207"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 207</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1252e. when he ascends by the eastern portal of the sky,</p>
 
 <p>1252f. bring him to the northern portal of the sky.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 530</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1253a. To say: Greetings to thee, Ladder, which the Souls of Buto and the
 Souls, of Nekhen have set up and built:</p>
 
 <p>1253b. Give thou thine arm to N.;</p>
 
 <p>1253c. that N. may sit between the two great gods;</p>
 
 <p>1253d. that the places of N. be in front; and that his arm be held as far as
 the Marsh of Offerings,</p>
 
 <p>1253e. so that he may sit among the stars which are in the sky.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 531</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1254a. To say: O ye two Kites who are on the wing of Thot,</p>
 
 <p>1254b. who are <i>Whnnw.ti</i> and <i>Dndnw.ti</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1254c. bring this (message) to N.; put him on that side.</p>
 
 <p>1254d. N. comes for life as messenger of Horus, the rapid one (or, in (his)
 service).</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 532</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1255a. To say: O Mooring-post of the morning-boat of its lord;</p>
 
 <p>1255b. O Mooring-post of the morning-boat of him who is in it,</p>
 
 <p>1255c. Isis comes, Nephthys comes, one of them on the right, one of them on
 the left,</p>
 
 <p>1255d. one of them as a <i>ḥȝ.t</i>-bird,
 one of them (Nephthys) as a kite.</p>
 
 <p>1256a. They found Osiris,</p>
 
 <p>1256b. after his brother Set had felled him to the earth in <i>Ndi.t</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1256c. when Osiris (N.) said, "come to me," hence comes his name
 as "Seker."</p>
 
 <p>1257a. They prevent thee from rotting, in accordance with this thy name of
 "Anubis";</p>
 
 <p>1257b. they prevent thy putrefaction from flowing to the ground,</p>
 
 <p>1257c. in accordance with this thy name of "jackal of the South";</p>
 
 <p>1257d. they prevent the smell of thy corpse from being bad, in accordance
 with this thy name of "<i>Ḥr-<u>h</u>ȝ.ti</i>."</p>
 
 <p>1258a. They prevent Horus of the East from rotting; they prevent Horus, lord
 of men, from rotting;</p>
 
 <p>125 8b. they prevent Horus of the <i>Dȝ.t</i>
 from rotting; they prevent Horus, lord of the Two Lands from rotting.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_208"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 208</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1258c. And Set will not ever free himself from carrying thee, Osiris N.</p>
 
 <p>1259a. Wake up for Horus; stand up against Set;</p>
 
 <p>1259b. raise thyself up, Osiris N., son of Geb, his first (-born),</p>
 
 <p>1259c. before whom the Two Enneads tremble.</p>
 
 <p>1260a. The keeper (<i>min.w</i>) stands
 up before thee, so that (the feast) of the New Moon may be celebrated for thee;
 thou appearest for (the feast of) the month;</p>
 
 <p>1260b. thou advancest to the sea (of N.); thou traversest to the Great
 Green;</p>
 
 <p>1261a. for thou art "he who stands without being tired" in Abydos;</p>
 
 <p>1261b. thou art spiritualized on the horizon; thou endurest in <i><u>D</u>d.t</i> (Mendes);</p>
 
 <p>1261c. thine arm is taken by the Souls of Heliopolis; thine arm is seized by
 Rē.</p>
 
 <p>1262a. Thy head, N., is raised up by the Two Enneads;</p>
 
 <p>1262b. they have put thee, Osiris N., as chief of the double <i>itr.t</i>-palace of the Souls of
 Heliopolis.</p>
 
 <p>1262c. Thou livest, thou livest, raise thyself up.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 533</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1263a. To say: -----------------------------</p>
 
 <p>1263b. ----------------- which goes forth ----</p>
 
 <p>1263c. N. is the blood, which goes forth from Rē; the sweat which goes
 forth from Isis.</p>
