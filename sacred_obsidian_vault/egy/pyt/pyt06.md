 <h3 align="center">3. A GROUP OF PRAYERS AND CHARMS, UTTERANCES 204-212.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 204</i>.</p>
 
 <p>118a. Rejoice, O hoers; let the heart in the breasts of men be lifted up.</p>
 
 <p>118b. They have swallowed the 'bright eye of Horus which is in Heliopolis.</p>
 
 <p>118c. The little finger of N. draws out that which is in the navel of
 Osiris.</p>
 
 <p>119a. N. thirsts not, he hungers not; the heart of N. faints (?) not,</p>
 
 <p>119b. for it is in the hands of <i>Ḥȝ</i>:
 which hold off his hunger. O fill (him), O fillers of hearts.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 205</i>.</p>
 
 <p>120a. To say: O ye who preside over food, ye who are attached to
 plentifulness (<i>ȝgb</i>)</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_56"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 56</font></a></p>
 
 <p>120b. commend N. to <i>Ftk.tȝ</i>, the
 cup-bearer of Rē, that he may commend him to Rē himself,</p>
 
 <p>120c. that Rē may commend him to the chiefs of the provisions of this
 year,</p>
 
 <p>120d. that they may seize and give him, that they may take and give him
 barley, spelt, bread, beer.</p>
 
 <p>121 a. For as to N., it is his father who gives, to him; it is Rē who
 gives to him barley, spelt, bread, beer.</p>
 
 <p>121b. For he (N.) is indeed the great bull which smote <i>Kns.t</i>.</p>
 
 <p>121c. For to N. indeed belong the five portions of bread, liquid, cake, in
 the mansion,</p>
 
 <p>121d. of which three are in heaven with Rē, and two on earth with the
 Ennead.</p>
 
 <p>122a. For he is one who is unbound, he is indeed set free; for he is one who
 is seen, he is one who is indeed observed.</p>
 
 <p>122b. O Rē, he (N.) is better to-day than yesterday.</p>
 
 <p>123a. N. has copulated with <i>Mw.t</i>;
 N. has kissed <i>Šw-ś.t</i>;</p>
 
 <p>123b. N. has united with <i>Nḫbw.t</i>.</p>
 
 <p>123c. N. has copulated with his beloved, deprived of <i>tbtb</i> (grain?, seed?) and of <i>šśšś</i>.</p>
 
 <p>123d. But as to the beloved of N., she gives bread to N.;</p>
 
 <p>123e. she did well by him in that day.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 206</i>.</p>
 
 <p>123f. To say: O ye who preside over food, ye who are attached to
 plentifulness,</p>
 
 <p>1239. commend N. to <i>Ftk.tȝ</i>, the
 cup-bearer of Rē, that he may commend N. to Rē himself,</p>
 
 <p>123h. that Rē may commend N. to the chiefs of the provisions.</p>
 
 <p>123i. That which he (Rē) bites, he gives to N.; that which he nibbles, he
 gives to N.,</p>
 
 <p>123k. that N. may sleep and be well every day.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 207</i>.</p>
 
 <p>124a. To say: An offering of the butcher; an offering of the butcher; an
 offering of that which is in the eye of Rē;</p>
 
 <p>124b. an offering of the bird-catcher, who is in the eye of the god. the
 cup-bearer who offers water.</p>
 
 <p>124c. Let the fire be hot; let the joint (of meat) be with the pastry',</p>
 
 <p>124d. four hands (full) of water.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_57"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 57</font></a></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 208</i>.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>A
 Variant of Utterance 207</i>.</p>
 
 <p>124e. To say: An offering to Atum; an offering to Atum; an offering of that
 which is in the eye of the boat of the god.</p>
 
 <p>124f. Let the joint (of meat) be with pastry;</p>
 
 <p>124g. four hands (full) of water.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 209</i>.</p>
 
 <p>125a. Shu is well (green); N. has not taken his food (meal).</p>
 
 <p>125b. N. is well (green); Shu has not taken his food (meal).</p>
 
 <p>125c. Let the eastern messengers repeat (double) thy bread.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 210</i>.</p>
 
 <p>126a. To say: The judge is awake; Thot is up;</p>
 
 <p>126b. the sleepers are awake; they that are in <i>Kns.t</i> bestir themselves</p>
 
 <p>126c. before the great bittern, which comes forth from the marsh and
 Wepwawet who comes forth from the tamarisk-bush.</p>
 
 <p>127a. The mouth of N. is pure; the Two Enneads purify N.;</p>
 
 <p>127b. pure is this tongue which is in his mouth.</p>
 
 <p>127c. The abomination of N. is dung; N. rejects urine.</p>
 
 <p>127d. N. loathes his abomination.</p>
 
 <p>128a. The abomination of N., it is dung; he eateth not that abomination,</p>
 
 <p>128b. just as at the same time Set shrinks from these two companions who
 voyage over the sky.</p>
 
 <p>128c. Rē and Thot, take N. with you,</p>
 
 <p>129a. that he may eat of that which ye eat, that he may drink of that which
 ye drink,</p>
 
 <p>129b. that he may live on that which ye live, that he may sit on that which
 ye sit,</p>
 
 <p>129c. that he may be mighty by that whereby ye are mighty, that he may voyage
 in that wherein ye voyage.</p>
 
 <p>130a. The booth of N. is an arbour among the reeds;</p>
 
 <p>130b. the abundance of N. is in the Marsh of Offerings;</p>
 
 <p>130c. his food is among you, ye gods; the water of N. consists of wine like
 that of Rē,</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_58"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 58</font></a></p>
 
 <p>130d. N. compasses the sky like Rē; N. traverses the sky like Thot.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 211</i>.</p>
 
 <p>131a. To say: The abomination of N. is hunger; he does not eat it;</p>
 
 <p>131b. the abomination of N. is thirst; he does not drink it.</p>
 
 <p>131c. It is N. who gives food to those who exist.</p>
 
 <p>131d. His nurse is <i>iȝ.t</i>;</p>
 
 <p>131e. it is she who makes his life (through nourishment?); it is she who
 gave birth to N.</p>
 
 <p>132a. N. was conceived in the night; N. was born in the night.</p>
 
 <p>132b. He belongs, to the Followers of Rē, who are before the morning star.</p>
 
 <p>132c. N. was conceived in Nun; he was born in Nun.</p>
 
 <p>132d. He has come; he has brought to you (some) bread of that which he found
 there.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 212</i>.</p>
 
 <p>133a. To say: The eye of Horus drips on the tuft of the <i><u>d</u>n.w</i>-plant.</p>
 
 <p>133b. <i>Ḫnti-imntiw</i> came to him;</p>
 
 <p>133c. he brought food to him, an offering of Horus who is chief of the
 houses,</p>
 
 <p>133d. (for) he lives on that on which N. lives,</p>
 
 <p>133e. he eats that which N. eats, he drinks, that which N. drinks.</p>
 
 <p>133f. One joint of meat and pastry, that is his meal.</p>
