 <h3 align="center">57. A SERIES OF ADDRESSES TO THE DECEASED KING AS A GOD, UTTERANCE 690.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 690</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2092a. To say: Wake up, Osiris; let the weary god awake.</p>
 
 <p>2092b. The god stands up; the god is powerful over his body.</p>
 
 <p>2093a. Wake up, N.; let the weary god awake.</p>
 
 <p>2093b. The god stands up; the god is powerful over his body.</p>
 
 <p>2094a. Horus stands up; he clothes N. with linen--him who came forth from
 him.</p>
 
 <p>2094b. N. is equipped as a god, standing in the <i>pr.wr</i>-palace, sitting with the Two Enneads.</p>
 
 <p>2095a. "O N., stand up, come in peace," says Rē to thee;
 "messenger of the great god,</p>
 
 <p>2095b. thou goest to heaven; thou goest forth through the doors of the
 horizon;</p>
 
 <p>2096a. Geb sends thee; thou art a soul like a [god, respected like a god];</p>
 
 <p>2096b. [thou art powerful] over thy body, like a god,</p>
 
 <p>2096c. like <i>Ba</i>, chief of the
 living,</p>
 
 <p>2096d. like <i>Śḫm</i>, chief of
 spirits."</p>
 
 <p>2097a. N. comes; he is equipped like a god; his bones are assembled like
 [Osiris];</p>
 
 <p>2097b. [he comes behind his uraeus].</p>
 
 <p>2097c. Thou hast come, O N., out of Heliopolis; thou art avenged; thy heart
 is placed in thy body;</p>
 
 <p>2098a. Thy face is like that of a jackal; thy flesh is like that of Atum;</p>
 
 <p>2098b. thy <i>bȝ</i> is in thy body; thy
 <i>śḫm</i> is behind thee; Isis is before thee; Nephthys is behind thee.</p>
 
 <p>2099a. Thou journeyest through the regions of Horus; thou travelest through
 the regions of Set.</p>
 
 <p>2099b. It is Shu and Tefnut who lead thee, when thou ascendest from Heliopolis.</p>
 
 <p>2100a. O N., Horus has woven his tent over thy head;</p>
 
 <p>2100b. Set has stretched out thy canopy;</p>
 
 <p>2100c. be enclosed, O father, by the divine tent; thou art brought there in
 thy beloved places.</p>
 
 <p>2101a. O N., Horus comes to thee provided with his souls,</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_308"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 308</font></a></p>
 
 <p>2101b. <i>Ḥȝpi</i>, <i>Dwȝ-mw.t.f</i>, <i>Imś.ti</i>, <i>Ḳbḥ-śn.w.f</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2102 a. They bring to thee thy name of "Imperishable";</p>
 
 <p>2102b. thou perishest not; thou diest not.</p>
 
 <p>2103a. O N., thy sister <i>Ḳbḥ.wt</i>
 has purified [thee]</p>
 
 <p>2103b. in <i>Rd-wr</i> chief of the
 lakes.</p>
 
 <p>2103c. Thou appearest to them like a jackal, like Horus chief of the living,</p>
 
 <p>2103d. like Geb chief of the Ennead, like Osiris chief of spirits.</p>
 
 <p>2104. Thou commandest spirits; thou leadest the [imperishable stars].</p>
 
 <p>2105a. The evil of Osiris--the evil of N.--the evil of the bull of the Two
 Enneads--</p>
 
 <p>2105b. the god is loosed (from it), N. has power over his body.</p>
 
 <p>2105c. N. is loosed (from it); N. has power over his body.</p>
 
 <p>2106a. O N., Horus, is standing, he glorifies thee;</p>
 
 <p>2106b. he conducts thee, when thou ascendest to heaven.</p>
 
 <p>2107a. Thy mother Nut receives thee; she lays hold of thine arm,</p>
 
 <p>2107b. that thou mayest not be in need, that thou mayest not moan (like a
 cedar),</p>
 
 <p>2107c. (but) that thou mayest live like the coleoptera (lives) and endure in
 [Mendes].</p>
 
 <p>2108a. O N., thou art adorned like a god; thy face is like (that of) a
 jackal, as Osiris,</p>
 
 <p>2108b. that soul in <i>Ndi.t</i>, that
 mighty one in the great city.</p>
 
 <p>2109. The sky trembles, the earth quakes before the god, before N.</p>
 
 <p>2110a. N. [is not enveloped] by the earth;</p>
 
 <p>2110b. <i>Iḫ.t-wt.t</i>, thou art not
 enveloped by the earth.</p>
 
 <p>2110c. Thy fame is by day; thy fear is by night, as a god, lord of f ear.</p>
 
 <p>2110d. Thou commandest the gods like the mighty one, chief of the mighty.</p>
 
 <p>2111. [O] Osiris, the overflow comes, the inundation hastens, Geb groans.</p>
 
 <p>2112a. I have pitied thee with pity; I have smitten him who acted with evil
 (intent) against thee;</p>
 
 <p>2112b. that thou mayest live, that thou mayest raise thyself up because of
 thy strength.</p>
 
 <p>2113. O N., [the inundation comes 1, [the overflow hastens], Geb [groans].</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_309"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 309</font></a></p>
 
 <p>2114a. Exult in the divine efflux which is in thee; let thy heart live;</p>
 
 <p>214b. thy divine limbs are in good condition; loosen thy bindings.</p>
 
 <p>2115a. Horus comes to thee, N.; he does for thee that which he did for his
 father Osiris,</p>
 
 <p>2115b. that thou mayest live like unto the life of those in heaven, and
 [that thou mayest come into being] more (truly) than those who are on earth.</p>
 
 <p>2116a. Raise thyself up because of thy strength; ascend thou to heaven.</p>
 
 <p>2116b. The sky bears thee like <i>Ś3ḥ</i>;
 thou hast power over thy body;</p>
 
 <p>2116c. thou defendest thyself against thine enemy.</p>
 
 <p>2117. [O N.] [I have wept for thee], I have mourned for thee;</p>
 
 <p>2118a. I shall not forget thee; my heart will not weary to give thee
 offerings every day,</p>
 
 <p>2118b. at the (feast of the) month, at the (feast of the) half month, at the
 (feast of) covering the fire-pan, at the (feast of) Thot, at the <i>wȝg</i>-feast,</p>
 
 <p>2118c. at the (feast of) slaughtering, (at) the (feast of) thy years, (at)
 (the feast of) thy birth, at the beginnings of thy months, during which thou
 livest as a god.</p>
 
 <p>2119. O N., may thy body be clothed, that thou mayest come to me.</p>
