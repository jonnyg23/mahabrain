 <h3 align="center">1. NUT AND THE DECEASED KING, UTTERANCES 1-11</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 1</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1a. To say by Nut, the brilliant, the great: This is (my) son, (my) first
 born, N., opener of (my) womb;</p>
 
 <p>1b. this is (my) beloved, with whom I have been satisfied.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 2</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1c. To say by Geb: This is (my) son, N., of (my) body -----</p>
 
 <p>1d. --------</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 3</i>.</p>
 
 <p>2a. To say by Nut, the great, who is within the lower mansion: This is (my)
 son, N., (my) beloved,</p>
 
 <p>2b. (my) eldest (son), (who is) upon the throne of Geb, with whom he has
 been satisfied,</p>
 
 <p>2c. to whom he gave his inheritance in the presence of the Great Ennead.</p>
 
 <p>3a. All the gods are in exultation; they say: "How beautiful is N.,
 with whom his father Geb is satisfied!</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 4</i>.</p>
 
 <p>A To say by Nut: N., I have given to thee thy sister Isis,</p>
 
 <p>3c. that she may take hold of thee, that she may give thy heart to thee
 which belongs to thy body.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 5</i>.</p>
 
 <p>3d. To say by Nut: N., I have given to thee thy sister Nephthys,</p>
 
 <p>3e. that she may take hold of thee, that she may give thy heart to thee
 which belongs to thy body.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 6</i>.</p>
 
 <p>4a. To say by Nut-Nekhbet, the great: This is (my) beloved, N., (my) son;</p>
 
 <p>4b. I have given the horizons to him, that he may be powerful over them like
 Harachte.</p>
 
 <p>4c. All the gods say: "It is a truth that thy beloved among thy
 children is N.,</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_21"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 21</font></a></p>
 
 <p>4d. to whom one will do service of courtier for ever."</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 </i>7.</p>
 
 <p>5a. To say by Nut, the great, (who is) within the encircled mansion: This is
 (my) son N., of (my) heart.</p>
 
 <p>5b. I have given to him the <i>Dȝ.t</i>,
 that he may be chief therein, like Horus, chief of the <i>Dȝ.t</i>.</p>
 
 <p>5c. All the gods say (to Nut):</p>
 
 <p>5d. "Thy father Shu knows that thou lovest N. more than thy mother
 Tefnut."</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 8</i>.</p>
 
 <p>6. He lives, king of Upper and Lower Egypt,
 beloved of Rē, living for ever.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 9</i>.</p>
 
 <p>7a. Horus [lives], beloved of the two lands, N., king of Upper and Lower
 Egypt, N., of the two goddesses beloved bodily, N., lords of Ombos, N.</p>
 
 <p>7b. Heir of Geb, whom he loves, N., beloved of all the gods, N., given life,
 endurance, joy, health, all happiness, like Rē.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 10</i>.</p>
 
 <p>8a. Horus lives, living apparition of the king of Upper and Lower Egypt, N.,</p>
 
 <p>8b. (of the land of) the two goddesses, living apparition, N.,</p>
 
 <p>8c. (of the land of) the two lords (of Ombos), N.,</p>
 
 <p>8d. Osiris, lord of the <i>Dȝ.t</i>, N.,</p>
 
 <p>8e. the beloved son of Geb, N.,</p>
 
 <p>8f. son of Nut, opener of her body (womb), N.,</p>
 
 <p>8g. endowed with life, endurance, joy, health, like Rē, eternally.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 11</i>.</p>
 
 <p>8h. To say by Nut: I unite thy beauty with this body (and with) this <i>ba</i>, for life, endurance, joy, health</p>
 
 <p>8i. of Horus, divine apparition, king of Upper and Lower
  Egypt, N. (of the land of) the two goddesses, divine apparition,
 N.,</p>
 
 <p>8j. powerful lord (of Ombos), N., living eter[nally].</p>
