 <h3 align="center">12. THE FERRYMAN AND THE DECEASED KING'S ASCENSION, UTTERANCES 300-311.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 300</i>.</p>
 
 <p>445a. To say: O <i><u>H</u>rti</i> of <i>Nsȝ.t</i>, ferryman of the <i>Iḳh.t</i>-boat, made by Khnum,</p>
 
 <p>445b. bring this (boat) to N. N. is Seker of <i>R-Ś<u>t</u>ȝ.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p>445c. N. is on the way to the place of Seker, chief of <i>P<u>d</u>w-š</i>.</p>
 
 <p>445d. It is our brother who is bringing this (boat) for these
 bridge-girderers (?) of the desert.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 301</i>.</p>
 
 <p>446a. To say: Thy established-offering is thine, O <i>Niw</i> (Nun) together with <i>Nn.t</i> (Naunet),</p>
 
 <p>446b. ye two sources of the gods, protecting the gods with their (your)
 shade.</p>
 
 <p>446c. Thy established-offering is thine, O Amūn together with Amūnet,</p>
 
 <p>446d. ye two sources of the gods, protecting the gods with their (your)
 shade.</p>
 
 <p>447a. Thy established-offering is thine, O Atum together with the two lions,
 ye double power of the gods, yourselves, who created yourselves,</p>
 
 <p>447b. that is, Shu together with Tefnut, (who) created the gods, begat the
 gods, established the gods.</p>
 
 <p>448a. Say ye to your father (Ptaḥ)</p>
 
 <p>448b. that N. has given to you your established-offering, that N. has
 satisfied you with your due.</p>
 
 <p>448c. Ye shall (or, should) not hinder N. when he ferries to the horizon to
 him.</p>
 
 <p>449a. N. knows him, knows his name. <i>Nḥi</i>
 is, his name, <i>Nḥi</i> lord of the year
 is his name;</p>
 
 <p>449b. he with the warrior's arm, Horus who is over the <i>ś<u>h</u>d.w</i> of heaven, who causes Rē to live every day.</p>
 
 <p>450a. He will rebuild N.; he will cause N. to live every day.</p>
 
 <p>450b. N. comes to thee, Horus of <i><u>H</u>ȝ.t</i>;
 N. comes to thee, Horus of <i>Šsm.t</i>;</p>
 
 <p>450c. N. comes to thee, Horus of the East.</p>
 
 <p>451a. Behold, N. brings to thee thy great left eye as healer.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_101"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 101</font></a></p>
 
 <p>451b. Take it, the intact (one), to thyself from N.; its water is in it,
 being intact;</p>
 
 <p>451c. its blood is in it, being intact; its breath is in it, being intact.</p>
 
 <p>452a. Enter into it; take possession of it, in this thy name of "Sacred
 <i>Ḥḳ3ś</i>" (a god),</p>
 
 <p>452b. that thou mayest approach to it in this thy name of "Rē"'.</p>
 
 <p>453a. Put it on thy brow, in this, its name of "choice oil",</p>
 
 <p>453b. that thou mayest rejoice in it, in this its name of
 "willow-tree",</p>
 
 <p>454a. that thou mayest sparkle thereby among the gods, in this its name of
 "that which sparkles", (or, "<i><u>t</u>ḥnw.t</i>-oil"),</p>
 
 <p>454b. that thou mayest be pleased with it in this its name of "oil of
 pleasure", (or, "<i>ḥkn.w</i>-oil").</p>
 
 <p>454c. (Then) will the <i>Rnn-wt.t</i>-serpent
 love thee.</p>
 
 <p>455a. Stand there, great reed-float, like <i>Wp-wȝ.wt</i>,</p>
 
 <p>455b. filled with thy splendour, come forth from the horizon,</p>
 
 <p>455c. after thou hast taken possession of the white crown in the
 water-springs, great and mighty, which are in the south of Libya,</p>
 
 <p>456a. (like) Sebek, lord of <i>Bȝ<u>h</u>.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p>456b. Thou journeyest to thy fields, thou passest through thy <i>kśb</i>.t-woods,</p>
 
 <p>456c. thy nose breathes the fragrance of the <i>Šsmt.t</i>-land.</p>
 
 <p>456d. Thou causest the <i>ka</i> of N. to
 approach his side,</p>
 
 <p>456e. like as thy Wig (deified) approaches thee.</p>
 
 <p>457a. Purify N., make N. bright</p>
 
 <p>457b. in this thy jackal-lake, O jackal, where thou purifiest the gods.</p>
 
 <p>457c. Thou art become a soul, thou art become pre-eminent (sharp), (like.)
 Horus lord of the green-stone--(to say) four times--(like) the two green
 falcons.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 302</i>.</p>
 
 <p>458a. To say: Heaven is serene (?); Sothis lives, for it is N. who lives,
 the son of Sothis.</p>
 
 <p>458b. The Two Enneads have purified themselves for him</p>
 
 <p>458c. as (in the form of) the <i>mśḫtiw</i>-hook-star,
 the imperishable.</p>
 
 <p>458d. The house of N. in heaven will not go under, the throne of N. on earth
 will never be destroyed.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_102"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 102</font></a></p>
 
 <p>459a. Men bury themselves (in their grave (?); the gods fly up.</p>
 
 <p>449b. Sothis caused N. to fly to heaven among his brothers the gods.</p>
 
 <p>449c. Nut, the great, uncovered her arms for N.</p>
 
 <p>460a. The two souls who are at the head of the souls of Heliopolis, who genuflected at sun-rise,</p>
 
 <p>460b. passed the night, while they did this, weeping for the god.</p>
 
 <p>460c. The throne of N. is by thee, O Rē; he will not give it to anyone
 else.</p>
 
 <p>461a. N. ascends to heaven, to thee, O Rē;</p>
 
 <p>461b. the face of N. is as (that of) falcons;</p>
 
 <p>461c. the wings of N. are as (those of) geese;</p>
 
 <p>461d. his claws are as the fangs of the god of the <i><u>D</u>w.f</i>-nome.</p>
 
 <p>462a. There is not a word among men on earth against N.;</p>
 
 <p>462b. there is no condemnation of him among the gods in heaven.</p>
 
 <p>462c. N. has removed the word against him; N. has destroyed (it) so as to
 mount up to heaven.</p>
 
 <p>463a. <i>Wp-wȝ.wt</i> has caused N. to
 fly to heaven among his brothers, the gods.</p>
 
 <p>463b. N. moved (flapped) his arms like a <i>śmn</i>-goose;</p>
 
 <p>463c. N. flaps his wings like a kite.</p>
 
 <p>463d. He flies who flies, O men; N. also flies away from you.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 303</i>.</p>
 
 <p>464a. To say: Gods of the West, gods of the East, gods of the South, gods of
 the North--</p>
 
 <p>464b. these four pure reed-floats, which ye placed for Osiris,</p>
 
 <p>464c. for his ascension to heaven,</p>
 
 <p>465a. that he might ferry over to <i>ḳbḥ.w</i>,
 while his son Horus was at hand (at his fingers),</p>
 
 <p>465b. (whom) he reared and whom he caused to dawn as a great god in<i> ḳbḥ.w</i>,</p>
 
 <p>465c. place them for N.</p>
 
 <p>466a. Art thou Horus, son of Osiris? Art thou, O N., the god, the eldest,
 son of Hathor?</p>
 
 <p>466b. Art thou the seed of Geb?</p>
 
 <p>467a. Osiris has ordained that N. dawn as a second Horus.</p>
 
 <p>467b. Those four souls (spirits), who are in Heliopolis, have written it</p>
 
 <p>467c. in the register of the two Great Gods who are in <i>ḳbḥ.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_103"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 103</font></a></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 304</i>.</p>
 
 <p>468a. To say: Greetings to thee, O daughter of Anubis, who is at the
 windows, of heaven,</p>
 
 <p>468b. thou friend of Thot, who is at the double rail (end) of the ladder.</p>
 
 <p>468c. Open the way for N., that N. may pass.</p>
 
 <p>469a. Greetings to thee, O ostrich, who is on the shore of the Winding
 Watercourse.</p>
 
 <p>469b. Open the way for N., that N. may pass.</p>
 
 <p>470a. Greetings to thee, O ox of Rē, with four horns,</p>
 
 <p>470b. thy horn in the West, thy horn in the East, thy horn in the South, thy
 horn in the North.</p>
 
 <p>470c. Incline thy western horn for N., that N. may pass.</p>
 
 <p>47m. Art thou a pure westerner? I come from the falcon city.</p>
 
 <p>471b. Greetings to thee, O Marsh of my Offerings (or, O Marsh of my Peace);</p>
 
 <p>471c. greetings to the honourable ones (dead?) who are in thee. N. will
 honour those who are there.</p>
 
 <p>471d. Comfortable are the pure who are in me.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 305</i>.</p>
 
 <p>472a. To say: The ladder is fastened by Rē in the presence of Osiris;</p>
 
 <p>472b. the ladder is fastened by Horus in the presence of his father Osiris,</p>
 
 <p>472c. as he goes to his <i>ȝḫ</i>
 (spirit).</p>
 
 <p>472d. One of them is on this side, one of them is on this side, while N. is
 between them.</p>
 
 <p>473a. Art thou then a god, pure in dwellings (places)? (I am) come from a pure
 (place).</p>
 
 <p>473b. Stand (here), O N., says Horus; sit (here), O N., says Set;</p>
 
 <p>473c. Take his arm (certificate), says Rē.</p>
 
 <p>474a. The spirit belongs to heaven; the body belongs to the earth.</p>
 
 <p>474b. That which men receive when they are buried</p>
 
 <p>474c. are their thousand loaves of bread, their thousand mugs of beer from
 the offering-table of <i>Ḫnti-imnti.w</i>.</p>
 
 <p>475a. If the heir is poor because he has no testament,</p>
 
 <p>475b. then shall N. (really, "he") write with his great finger;</p>
 
 <p>475c. but he shall not write with his little finger,</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_104"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 104</font></a></p>
 
 <p><i>Utterance 306</i>.</p>
 
 <p>476a. To say: "How beautiful indeed is the sight, how pleasant indeed
 is the view," say they, say the gods,</p>
 
 <p>476b. "'the ascension of this god to heaven, the ascension of N. to
 heaven,</p>
 
 <p>477a. his renown over him, his terror on both sides of him,</p>
 
 <p>477b. his magic preceding him!"</p>
 
 <p>477c. Geb has done for him as was done for himself; (and)</p>
 
 <p>478a. the gods, the Souls of Buto come to him, (and) the gods, the Souls of
 Hierakonpolis, the gods who are in heaven, the gods who are on the earth;</p>
 
 <p>478b. they make for thee (they serve thee as), N., supports of their arms,</p>
 
 <p>479a. and thou ascendest, N., to heaven, and thou climbest on it in this its
 name of "ladder."</p>
 
 <p>479b. "Let heaven be given to N.; let the earth be given to him,"
 said Atum.</p>
 
 <p>480a. He who had spoken (with Atum) about it was Geb.</p>
 
 <p>480b. The regions of the kingdom, the kingdom
 of Horus, the kingdom
  of Set, (and)</p>
 
 <p>480c. the Marshes of Reeds, they adore thee</p>
 
 <p>480d. in this thy name of <i>Dwȝ.m</i>,
 as Sopdu, (who lives) under his <i>kśb.t</i>-trees.</p>
 
 <p>481a. Has he killed thee after his heart had said that thou shouldst die for
 him?</p>
 
 <p>481b. But, behold, thou for thy part become in spite of him as the remaining
 bull of the wild-bulls.</p>
 
 <p>481c. There remains, there remains the remaining bull,</p>
 
 <p>481d. (so) thou art remaining, O N., as their chief, as chief of the
 spirits, eternally.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 307</i>.</p>
 
 <p>482a. To say: A Heliopolitan is in N., O god; a Heliopolitan as thou (art)
 is in N. O god;</p>
 
 <p>482b. a Heliopolitan is in N., O Rē; a Heliopolitan as thou (art) is in N.
 O Rē.</p>
 
 <p>482c. The mother of N. is a Heliopolitan; the father of N. is a
 Heliopolitan.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_105"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 105</font></a></p>
 
 <p>483a. N. himself is a Heliopolitan, who was born in Heliopolis,</p>
 
 <p>483b. when Rē ruled the Two Enneads, (when) Nefertem ruled men,</p>
 
 <p>483c. (as) one without an equal, the heir of his father Geb.</p>
 
 <p>484a. Any god who puts out his arm (menacingly),</p>
 
 <p>484b. when the face of N. turns to thee to adore thee,</p>
 
 <p>484c. (and) when N. calls to thee on behalf of his person, O god, on behalf
 of his nose, O god,</p>
 
 <p>484d. he shall have no bread, he shall have no cake among his brothers, the
 gods;</p>
 
 <p>485a. he shall send no message, he shall not cover in heat among his
 brothers, the gods;</p>
 
 <p>485b. the double doors of the <i>mśkt.t</i>-boat
 shall not be opened for him, the double doors of the <i>mn<u>d</u>.t</i>-boat shall not be opened for him;</p>
 
 <p>485c. his speech shall not be judged as (that of one) in his city; the
 double doors of the destroyer ("Hell") shall not be open (again) for
 him.</p>
 
 <p>486a. N. comes to thee.</p>
 
 <p>486b. N. is the wild-bull of the highlands, the bull with the large head,
 which comes from Heliopolis.</p>
 
 <p>486c. N., wild-bull of the highlands, comes to thee.</p>
 
 <p>486d. Henceforth N. is he who has given birth to thee and who gives birth to
 thee.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 308</i>.</p>
 
 <p>487a. To say: Greetings to thee, O Horus, in the regions of Horus;</p>
 
 <p>487b. greetings to thee, O Set, in the regions of Set;</p>
 
 <p>487c. greetings to thee, O <i>Iȝr.w</i>,
 in the Marshes of Reeds;</p>
 
 <p>488a. greetings to you, ye two harmonius (goddesses), daughters of the four
 gods, who dwell in the great palace (Heliopolis),</p>
 
 <p>488b. ye who are come forth at the voice of N., naked.</p>
 
 <p>489a. N. has looked to you, as Horus looked to Isis;</p>
 
 <p>489b. N. has looked to you, as the <i>Nḥb.w-kȝ.w</i>
 (serpent) looked to <i>Śrḳ.t-ḥtw</i>;</p>
 
 <p>489c. N. has looked to you, as Sebek looked to Neit;</p>
 
 <p>489d. N. has looked to you, as Set looked to the two harmonius (goddesses).</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_106"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 106</font></a></p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 309</i>.</p>
 
 <p>490a. To say: N. is the <i><u>d</u>ḥȝ.i</i>
 of the gods, who is behind the house of Rē,</p>
 
 <p>490b. born of the wish of the gods, which is in the prow of the boat of
 Rē.</p>
 
 <p>490c. N. sits before him;</p>
 
 <p>491a. N. opens his boxes; N. breaks open his edicts;</p>
 
 <p>491b. N. seals his rolls (of papyrus);</p>
 
 <p>491c. N. sends forth his messengers, the indefatigables.</p>
 
 <p>491d. N. does, that which he (Rē) says to N.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 310</i>.</p>
 
 <p>492a. To say: If N. should be bewitched, so will Atum be bewitched.</p>
 
 <p>492b. If N. should be slandered, so will Atum be slandered.</p>
 
 <p>492c. If N. should be beaten, so will Atum be beaten.</p>
 
 <p>492d. If N. should be hindered on this road, so will Atum be hindered.</p>
 
 <p>493a. N, is Horus. N. comes after his father (in time); N. comes after
 Osiris.</p>
 
 <p>493b. O thou, whose face is before him, whose face is behind him,</p>
 
 <p>494a. bring this (boat) to N. Which boat shall I bring to thee, O N.?</p>
 
 <p>494b. Bring to N. that which flies up and alights.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 311</i>.</p>
 
 <p>495a. To say: Look at N., O Rē; recognize N., O Rē.</p>
 
 <p>495b. He belongs to those who know thee. He knows (that)</p>
 
 <p>495c. when his lord (Rē) goes forth, he should not forget the <i>ḥtp-di</i>,</p>
 
 <p>496a. so that she "who excludes whom she will exclude" may open
 the doors of the horizon for the going forth of the boat of the morning-sun.</p>
 
 <p>496b. (N.) knows the hall of the royal throne, which is in the midst of the
 platform of <i>iskn</i>, whence thou goest
 forth,</p>
 
 <p>497a. that thou mayest enter (step down into) the boat of the evening-sun.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_107"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 107</font></a></p>
 
 <p>497b. Commend N.; commend him, commend him--to say four times one after
 another--to those four raging ones (winds),</p>
 
 <p>497c. who are around thee (Rē) who see with two faces, who speak with two
 mouths (?),</p>
 
 <p>498a. who are evil with those who are unfortunate, with those who would
 destroy them (the winds?),</p>
 
 <p>498b. that they put not out their arm, when N. turns to thee, when N. comes
 to thee,</p>
 
 <p>499a. as one who says to thee this thy name of "great flood,"
 which proceeds from the great (one).</p>
 
 <p>499b. N. will not be blind when thou leavest him in darkness;</p>
 
 <p>499c. he will not be deaf when he does not hear thy voice.</p>
 
 <p>500a. Mayest thou take N. with thee, with thee;</p>
 
 <p>500b. he who drives away the storm for thee; be who chases off the clouds
 for thee; he who breaks up the hail for thee.</p>
 
 <p>500c, N. will do homage (upon) homage to thee; he will cause acclamation
 (upon) acclamation to thee.</p>
 
 <p>500d. Mayest thou set N. over <i><u>d</u>t3.t</i>.</p>
