 <h3 align="center">31. TEXTS OF MISCELLANEOUS CONTENTS, UTTERANCES 554-562.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 554</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1370a. To say: N. is verily a son of the great wild-cow; she conceived him
 and gave him birth;</p>
 
 <p>1370b. they place him in the interior of her wing;</p>
 
 <p>1370c. she ferries over the lake with thee (i.e. him); she traverses the
 ~iw-canal with thee (i.e. him).</p>
 
 <p>1371a. Thy fillet as chief of the house is at thy back;</p>
 
 <p>1371b. thy <i>bȝ-mnḥi</i>-sceptre is,
 in thy hand,</p>
 
 <p>1371c. that thou mayest strike, that thou mayest rule, in accordance with
 thy dignity, which appertains to lords of the <i>im3ḫ</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1372a. for indeed thou art of the Followers of Rē, who are behind the
 morning star (<i>Dwȝ</i>).</p>
 
 <p>1372b. Let no evil be to thee; let no evil be to thy name, the first on
 earth.</p>

 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 555</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1373a. To say: N. is come forth from Buto, to the gods of Buto;</p>
 
 <p>1373b. N. is adorned as a falcon, bedecked as the Two Enneads.</p>
 
 <p>1374a. N. dawns as king (of Upper Egypt); he is elevated as Wepwawet,</p>
 
 <p>1374b. (after) he has taken the white crown and the green crown,</p>
 
 <p>1374c. his <i>ḥ<u>d</u></i>-mace on his arm,
 his sceptre in his hand.</p>
 
 <p>1375a. The mother of N. is Isis; his nurse is Nephthys;</p>
 
 <p>1375b. she who suckles N. is <i>Śḫȝ.t-ḥr</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1375c. Neit is behind him; <i>Śrḳt-ḥtw</i>
 is before him.</p>
 
 <p>1376a. The ropes are knotted; the boats of N. are tied together</p>
 
 <p>1376b. for the son of Atum--hungry and thirsty, thirsty and hungry--</p>
 
 <p>1376c. on the southern shore of the Winding Watercourse.</p>
 
 <p>1377a. Thot, who is in the shade of his bush,</p>
 
 <p>1377b. put N. upon the tip of thy wing.,</p>
 
 <p>1377c. on the northern shore of the Winding Watercourse.</p>
 
 <p>1378a. N. is well, his flesh is sound; N. is well, his garments are sound,</p>
 
 <p>1378b. (as) he ascends to heaven like Montu,</p>
 
 <p>1378c. (as) he descends as <i>Bȝ-ib<u>t</u>.f</i>,
 as <i>Bȝ-šm.f</i>.</p>
