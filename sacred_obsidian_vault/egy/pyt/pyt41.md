 <h3 align="center">38. TEXTS OF MISCELLANEOUS CONTENTS, UTTERANCES 578-586.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 578</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1531a. To say: Osiris N., thou shalt not hasten to those lands of the East;</p>
 
 <p>1531b. thou shalt hasten to these lands of the West by the way of the
 Followers of Rē.</p>
 
 <p>1532a. Thy messengers hasten; thy runners go;</p>
 
 <p>1532b. those who are before thee rush on,</p>
 
 <p>1532c. that they may announce thee to Rē, to him who lifts up (his) arm in
 the East.</p>
 
 <p>1533a. Thou dost not know them; thou art astonished at them;</p>
 
 <p>1533b. thou hast laid them in thine arms like herdsmen of thy calves.</p>
 
 <p>1534a. Thou art as he who prevents them from slipping out from thine arms.</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_241"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 241</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1534b. Thou goest forth to them; thou art glorified, by birth; preeminent,
 by birth,</p>
 
 <p>534c. in thy name of "<i>śpd.w</i>";</p>
 
 <p>1535a. A (thy?) whip in thy hand; and thy sceptre on thine arm.</p>
 
 <p>1535b. Enemies fall on their face before thee;</p>
 
 <p>1535c. the imperishable stars kneel before thee.</p>
 
 <p>1536a. Thou art as he who prevents them from falling out of thine arms,</p>
 
 <p>1536b. and (who prevents) that thou be sick because of them, in thy name of
 "<i>mḥi.t</i>."</p>
 
 <p>1537a. They recognize thee, in thy name of "Anubis."</p>
 
 <p>1537b. The gods do not descend to thee, in thy name of <i>iȝ.t</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1538a. Thou standest as chief of the gods, eldest son,</p>
 
 <p>1538b. as heir on the throne of Geb.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 579</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1539a. To say: Thy going from thy house, Osiris N.,</p>
 
 <p>1539b. is the going of Horus in search of thee, Osiris N.</p>
 
 <p>1539c. Thy messengers hasten; thy runners run; thine envoys hurry.</p>
 
 <p>1540a. They announce to Rē,</p>
 
 <p>1540b. that thou, N., art come, as son of Geb, from upon the throne of
 Amūn;</p>
 
 <p>1541a. that thou hast ferried over the Winding Watercourse; that thou hast
 traversed the canal of [<i>Knsi.t</i>].</p>
 
 <p>1541b. Thou settlest down on the eastern side of the sky; thou sittest in
 the double <i>itr.t</i>-palace of the
 horizon;</p>
 
 <p>1541c. thou givest to them thine arm; thou givest thine arm to the gods.</p>
 
 <p>1542a. They praise thee; they come to thee with salutations,</p>
 
 <p>1542b. as they do homage to Rē, as they come to him with salutations.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 580</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1543a. To say: Thou who hast smitten (my) father; he who has killed (one)
 greater than he;</p>
 
 <p>1543b. thou hast smitten (my) father, thou hast killed one greater than
 thou.</p>
 
 <p>1544a. Father Osiris N. I have smitten for thee him who smote thee as an ox;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_242"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 242</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1544b. I have killed for thee him who killed thee as a wild-bull.</p>
 
 <p>1544c. I have overpowered for thee him who overpowered thee as an ox;</p>
 
 <p>1544d. thou art upon his back as he who is upon the back of an ox.</p>
 
 <p>1545a. He who stretched thee out as the stretched out ox; he who slaughtered
 thee as the slaughtered ox;</p>
 
 <p>1545b. he who stunned thee as the stunned ox--</p>
 
 <p>1545c. I have cut off his head; I have cut off his tail;</p>
 
 <p>1545d. I have cut off his two hands; I have cut off his two feet.</p>
 
 <p>1546a. His upper fore-legs including (lit. "being to") his lower
 forelegs belong t[o Atum], father of the gods;</p>
 
 <p>1546b. his two thighs belong to Shu and Tefnut;</p>
 
 <p>1546c. his two sides belong to Geb and Nut;</p>
 
 <p>1547a. his two shoulder blades belong to Isis and Nephthys;</p>
 
 <p>1547b. his two shoulders belong to <i>Mḫnti-irti</i>
 and <i><u>H</u>rti</i>,--</p>
 
 <p>1547c. his spinal column belongs to Neit and Śerḳet; his heart belongs to
 Sekhmet, the great;</p>
 
 <p>1548a. that which is in the back part of his body belongs to those four
 gods, the sons of Horus, his beloved,</p>
 
 <p>1548b. <i>Ḥȝpi</i>, <i>Imś.ti</i>, <i>Dwȝ-mw.t.f</i>, <i>Ḳbḥ-śn.w.f</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1549a. His head, his tail, his two hands, his two feet</p>
 
 <p>1549b. belong to Anubis, who is upon his mountain; to Osiris who is chief of
 his department (or, thigh-offering).</p>
 
 <p>1549c. That which the gods leave belongs to the Souls of Nekhen and the
 Souls of Buto.</p>
 
 <p>1550a. Eat, eat the red ox, for the voyage by sea,</p>
 
 <p>1550b. which Horus did for his father, Osiris N.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 581</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1551a. To say: This thy cavern there is the broad-hall of Osiris N..</p>
 
 <p>1551b. which brings the wind. The north wind refreshes;</p>
 
 <p>1551c. it raises thee as Osiris N.</p>
 
 <p>1552a. <i>Šsm.w</i> comes to thee,
 bearing water and wine;</p>
 
 <p>1552b. <i>Ḫnti-mnwt.f</i> (comes)
 bearing the vases which are before the two <i>itr.t</i>-palaces.</p>
 
 <p>1552c. Thou standest, thou sittest like Anubis, chief of the necropolis.</p>
 
 <p>1553a. Aker stands up for thee; Shu dries (lit. something like. "lies
 down," Wb. V 366) for thee.</p>
 
 <p>1553b. They tremble who see the inundation (when) it tosses;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_243"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 243</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1554a. (but) the marshes laugh; the shores are become green;</p>
 
 <p>1554b. the divine offerings descend; the face of men brightens; the heart of
 the gods rejoices.</p>
 
 <p>1555a. "Deliver N. from his bandages, which restrain (?) the living, O
 gods,"</p>
 
 <p>1555b. (is) in the mouth of those who run to them on the good day of running
 (while running is good).</p>
 
 <p>1556a. "Set is guilty; Osiris is justified,"</p>
 
 <p>1556b. (is) in the mouth of the gods, on the good day of the going upon the
 mountain.</p>
 
 <p>1557a. (When) inundations are upon the land,</p>
 
 <p>1557b. he who hastens with his soul goes to his cave;</p>
 
 <p>1557c. (but) thou marchest behind thy spirit towards <i>Knm-iwnw</i>,</p>
 
 <p>1557d. like the successor of <i><u>H</u>rti</i>,
 chief of [<i>Ns</i>]<i>ȝ.t</i>.</p>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 582</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1558a. To say: N. is come to thee, Horus,</p>
 
 <p>1558b. that thou mayest recite for him this great and good word, which thou
 didst recite for Osiris,</p>
 
 <p>1558c. by which N. may be great; by which he may be powerful.</p>
 
 <p>1559a. His <i>śhm</i> is within him;
 his <i>ba</i> is behind him;</p>
 
 <p>1559b. his <i>śpd</i> is upon him,
 which Horus gave to Osiris,</p>
 
 <p>1559c. that N. may rest in heaven, as a mountain, as a support.</p>
 
 <p>1560a. He shall fly as a cloud to heaven, like a heron;</p>
 
 <p>1560b. he shall pass by the side-locks of the sky;</p>
 
 <p>1560c. the feathers on the two arms of N. shall be like knives.</p>
 
 <p>1561a. <i>Śȝḥ</i> shall give him his
 arm,</p>
 
 <p>1561b. Sothis shall take his hand;</p>
 
 <p>1561c. the ground shall be hoed for N.; an offering shall be made for N.;</p>
 
 <p>1561d. the two nomes of the god shall shout for N.</p>
 
 <p>1562a. He will be more at the head than he who is at the head of the Two
 Enneads;</p>
 
 <p>1562b. he sits upon his firm throne,</p>
 
 <p>1562c. his sceptre glittering in his hand.</p>
 
 <p>1563a. If N. raises his hand towards the children of their fathers,</p>
 
 <p>1563b. they stand up for N.;</p>
 
 <p>1563c. if N. lowers his hand towards them, they sit down.</p>
 
 <p>1564a. The face of N. is like that of a jackal; the middle (of his body) is
 like that of <i>ḳbḥ.w</i>t;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_244"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 244</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1564b. N. judges like Sebek in Crocodilopolis,</p>
 
 <p>1564c. like Anubis in <i>Tȝb.t</i>
 (Hypselis?).</p>
 
 <p>1565a. When N. calls for a thousand,</p>
 
 <p>1565b. there come to him the blessed dead (?) with salutations,</p>
 
 <p>1565c. while they say to him: "Who is it who has done this to
 thee?"</p>
 
 <p>1566a. It is the mother of N., the great wild cow, she with the two long
 feathers,</p>
 
 <p>1566b. with the brilliant head-dress, with the two hanging breasts,</p>
 
 <p>1566c. who has lifted N. up to heaven--she did not leave N. on the earth--</p>
 
 <p>1566d. among the glorious gods,</p>
 
 <p>1567a. that N. may see their spirit and that he may be a spirit likewise.</p>
 
 <p>1567b. N. [is protected] by his, father Osiris (just as) the blessed dead
 (?) protect N.</p>

 <p>1582a (Nt. Jéquier, VIII 14). To say: N. shines like Rē, expelling the
 dawn, establishing Truth behind Rē,</p>
 
 <p>1582b (Nt. VIII 14). shining every day for all of those who are on the
