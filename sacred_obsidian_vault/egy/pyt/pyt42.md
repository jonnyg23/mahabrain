 <h3 align="center">39. AN EARLY HYMN TO THE SUN, UTTERANCE 587.</h3>
 
 <p align="center"><i>Utterance
 587</i>.</p>
 
 <p>1587a. To say: Greetings to thee, Atum.</p>
 
 <p>1587b. Greetings to thee, Khepri, who created himself.</p>
 
 <p>1587c. Thou art high, in this thy name of "<i>Ḳȝ</i>."</p>
 
 <p>1587d. Thou comest into being, in thy name of "Khepri."</p>
 
 <p>1588a. Greetings to thee, eye of Horus, which he adorned with his two hands
 completely.</p>
 
 <p>1588b. He does not make thee hearken to the West;</p>
 
 <p>1588c. he does not make thee hearken to the East;</p>
 
 <p>1588d. he does not make thee hearken to the South;</p>
 
 <p>1588e. he does not make thee hearken to the North;</p>
 
 <p>1588f. he does not make thee hearken to those who are in the middle of the
 land;</p>
 
 <p>1589a. (but) thou harkenest to Horus.</p>
 
 <p>1589b. It is he who adorned thee; it is he who built thee; it is he who
 settled thee;</p>
 
 <p>1590a. thou doest for him everything which he says unto thee, in every place
 whither he goes.</p>
 
 <p>1590b. Thou carriest to him the fowl-bearing waters which are in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1590c. thou carriest to him the fowl-bearing waters which are to be in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1591a. thou carriest to him the gifts which are in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1591b. thou carriest to him every tree which is to be in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1591c. thou carriest to him the food which is in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1591d. thou carriest to him the food (in thee) which is to be in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1592a. thou carriest to him the gifts which are in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1592b. thou carriest to him the gifts which are to be in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1592c. thou carriest to him everything which is in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1592d. thou carriest to him everything which is to be in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1592e. thou carriest (it) to him to every place wherein his heart desires to
 be.</p>
 
 <p>1593a. The doors stand fast upon thee like Inmutef;</p>
 
 <p>1593b. they open not to the West; they open not to the East;</p>
 
 <p>1593c. they open not to the North; they open not to the South;</p>
 
 <p>1593d. they open not to those who are in the middle of the land;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_247"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 247</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1594a. (but) they are open to Horus. It was, he who made them; it was he who
 made them stand fast;</p>
 
 <p>1594b. it was he who rescued them from every evil which Set did to them;</p>
 
 <p>1595a. it was he who settled thee, in this thy name of
 "Settlements" ("Colonies");</p>
 
 <p>1595b. it was he who went, doing obeisance; after thee, in this thy name of
 "City";</p>
 
 <p>1595c. it was he who rescued thee from every evil which Set did to thee.</p>
 
 <p>1596a. Go, go, Nut.</p>
 
 <p>1596b. Geb commanded that thou go, in thy name of "City."</p>
 
 <p>1596c. N. is Horus, who adorned his eye with his two hands completely.</p>
 
 <p>1597a. N. adorned thee with an adornment;</p>
 
 <p>1597b. N. settled for thee these his settlements;</p>
 
 <p>1597c. N. built thee (as) a city of N.,</p>
 
 <p>1597d. that thou mayest do for N. every good thing which the heart of N.
 loves,</p>
 
 <p>1597e. that thou mayest do (it) for N., in every place where he goes.</p>
 
 <p>1598a. Thou shalt not hearken to the West; thou shalt not hearken to the
 East;</p>
 
 <p>1598b. thou shalt not hearken to the North; thou shalt not hearken to the
 South;</p>
 
 <p>1598c. thou shalt not hearken to those who are in the middle of the land;</p>
 
 <p>1599a. (but) thou shalt hearken to N.; it is N. who adorned thee;</p>
 
 <p>1599b. it is N. who built thee; it is he who settled thee.</p>
 
 <p>1600a. Thou doest for him everything which he says unto thee in every place
 whither N. goes.</p>
 
 <p>1600b. Thou carriest to him the fowl-bearing waters which are in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1600c. thou carriest to him the fowl-bearing waters which are to be in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1601a. thou carriest to him every tree which is in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1601b. thou carriest to him every tree which is to be in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1601c. thou carriest to him the food which is in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1601d. thou carriest to him all food which is to be in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1602a. thou carriest to N. the gifts which are in thee;</p>
 
 <p><a name="page_248"><font size="1" color="GREEN">p. 248</font></a></p>
 
 <p>1602b. thou carriest to N. the gifts which are to be in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1602c. thou carriest to him everything which is in thee;</p>
 
 <p>1602d. thou carriest (it) to N. to the place wherein the heart of N. desires
 to be.</p>
 
 <p>1603a. The doors stand fast upon thee like Inmutef;</p>
 
 <p>1603b. they open not to the West; they open not to the East;</p>
 
 <p>1603c. they open not to the North; they open not to the South;</p>
 
 <p>1603d. they open not to those who are in the middle of the land;</p>
 
 <p>1604a. (but) [they are open to] N.</p>
 
 <p>1604b. It was he who made them; it was he who made them fast;</p>
 
 <p>1604c. it was he who rescued them from all the evil which men did to them;</p>
 
 <p>1605a. it was N. who [settled thee], in this thy name of
 "Settlements" ("Colonies");</p>
 
 <p>1605b. it was N. who went, doing obeisance, after thee, in this thy name of
 "City";</p>
 
 <p>1605c. it was N. who rescued thee from all the [evil which men did] to thee.</p>
 
 <p>1606a. Hearken to N. alone; it is N. who made thee.</p>
 
 <p>1606b. Thou shalt not hearken to the malefactor.</p>
