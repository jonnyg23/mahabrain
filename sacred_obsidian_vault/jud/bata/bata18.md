 <p><a name="page_285"><font size="1" color="green">p. 285</font></a></p>  <h3 align="center">GLOSSARY</h3>  
 <p><i>Amora</i> (pl. <i>amoraim</i>), interpreter, teacher, sage of the Talmud after the Mishnaic Age.</p>  
 <p><i>Baraitha</i>, external.</p>  
 <p><i>Denar</i> (pl. <i>denarim</i>), a silver, or gold coin.</p>  
 <p><i>Gemara</i>, lit. completion, also study. The commentary on the <i>Mishna</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Haggada</i> (pl. <i>haggadoth</i>), homiletical portions of the Talmud, legend.</p>  
 <p><i>Halacha</i> (pl. <i>halachoth</i>), traditional law, ruling.</p>  
 <p><i>Halizah</i>, rite performed by a childless widow (<i>Deut</i>. xxv-9).</p>  
 <p><i>Haluzah</i>, childless widow who performed the rite of <i>Halizah</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Hanukah</i>, the festival of the Maccabean.</p>  
 <p><i>Haroseth</i>, a mixture of nut fruit and wine.</p>  
 <p><i>Issar</i>, a copper coin 1/24 of a <i>denar</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Kab</i>, measure of capacity 1/6 of a <i>seah</i>, 4 <i>loogs</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Kilbith</i>, a very small fish.</p>  
 <p><i>Konam</i>, sacred, a term introducing a vow.</p>  
 <p><i>Kor</i>, a measure of capacity = thirty <i>seahs</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Loog</i>, a liquid measure ¼ of a <i>Kab</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Lulab</i>, palm branch.</p>  
 <p><i>Maneh</i>, a weight in gold = 100 <i>zuz</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Midrash</i>, interpretation of Scriptures, exposition.</p>  
 <p><i>Minah</i>, 100 <i>Denars</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Mishna</i>, lit. "teaching," the collection of traditional laws.</p>  
 <p><i>Mezuza</i> (pl. <i>mezuzoth</i>), a rolled up piece of parchment containing <i>Deut</i>. vi, 4–9, xi, 13–17, put on the door post.</p>  
 <p><i>Nathin</i>, f. <i>nathina</i>, descendants of the Gibeonites whom Joshua made into Temple slaves. (<i>Josh</i>. ix–27).</p>  
 <p><a name="page_286"><font size="1" color="green">p. 286</font></a></p>  
 <p><i>Omer</i>, first sheaf of barley offered as a meal offering to the Temple. (<i>Lev</i>. xxiii–10).</p>  
 <p><i>Pandion</i>, coin 2 <i>issar</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Peah</i>, corner of the field.</p>  
 <p><i>Perutha</i>, the smallest copper coin ⅛ of an <i>issar</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Ris</i>, a measure of distance 266–⅔ <i>cubits</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Sanhedrin</i>, supreme court.</p>  
 <p><i>Seah</i>, measure of capacity 1/30 of a <i>Kor</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Selah</i>, a coin 4 <i>denars</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Shekina</i>, the spirit of the Lord as manifested on earth.</p>  
 <p><i>Sinar</i>, a girdle, apron.</p>  
 <p><i>Sotha</i>, wife suspected of adultery.</p>  
 <p><i>Sukah</i>, a hut, a booth used during the Feast of Tabernacles.</p>  
 <p><i>Talmud</i>, lit. study, learning, <i>Mishna</i> and <i>Gemara</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Tanna</i> (pl. <i>Tannaim</i>), a teacher, sages of the <i>mishnaic</i> times.</p>  
 <p><i>Torah</i>, the Law, the Pentateuch.</p>  
 <p><i>Tropaik</i>, ½ <i>denar</i>, ½ <i>zuz</i>.</p>  
 <p><i>Urim athumin</i>, Oracle.</p>  
 <p><i>Zuz</i>, another name for <i>denar</i>.</p>
