 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/uni_t.jpg"></a><br><i>Christian Healing: the science of being</i>, by Charles Fillmore, [1926], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"> <br> </h3>  
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_144"><font size="1" color="green">p. 144</font></a></p> 
 <p>These study helps and questions have been arranged for the convenience of students, whether they are working in class or in the privacy of their own homes. A careful comparison of the students' answers with the text will show how far they have progressed in their study. </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_145"><font size="1" color="green">p. 145</font></a></p> 
 <p>Lesson One--The True Character Of Being </p> 
 <p>1. Is there anything scientific about Spirit? Give reasons for your answer. </p> 
 <p>2. Is it necessary for man's spiritual consciousness to be awake in the beginning of his study of spiritual science? In what attitude should one study? </p> 
 <p>3. What attitude precedes inspiration of spiritual consciousness? May a certain amount of intellectual study help? </p> 
 <p>4. What is the starting point in spiritual attainment? What is the Almighty? How does man understand the Almighty? </p> 
 <p>5. What is one of the most important things that a student of spiritual science can learn? Why? </p> 
 <p>6. What is the way out of confusion? How does it differ from "blind belief"? </p> 
 <p>7. How does one get at the very heart of Being? </p> 
 <p>8. Where is the abode of the Father? </p> 
 <p>9. Explain God as immanent in the universe. How does this understanding differ from the old idea of God? </p> 
 <p>10. Does the Power that creates and sustains the universe include man? What is the key to the whole situation? </p> 
 <p>11. Define Spirit, and explain how it dwells in us. </p> 
 <p>12. What do we mean by studying "Mind back of nature"? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_146"><font size="1" color="green">p. 146</font></a></p> 
 <p>13. What is man's inheritance, and how can he perpetually draw upon it? </p> 
 <p>14. What is life in Being? </p> 
 <p>15. What is the real of the universe? What is practical Christianity? </p> 
 <p>16. Where should the student start his investigations? Why? </p> 
 <p>17. What is the point of contact between man and the perfect Mind? </p> 
 <p>18. What does the parable of the prodigal son typify? </p> 
 <p>19. Why should the student never be discouraged? </p> 
 <p>20. Can Truth be imparted? Why? </p> 
 <p>21. Sum up, in a concise manner, the vital points of this chapter. </p> 
 <p>Lesson Two--Being's Perfect Idea </p> 
 <p>1. What is spiritual Truth? How does the one Mind create? What is the Logos? </p> 
 <p>2. What is the law of divine creation? </p> 
 <p>3. Do the supplications of man change the law of God? Explain. </p> 
 <p>4. What is the key to our understanding of Divine Mind? Can man come to understand himself or the universe in any other way? </p> 
 <p>5. Is it important to understand mind and its laws? Why? </p> 
 <p>6. What is man from the viewpoint of Being? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_147"><font size="1" color="green">p. 147</font></a></p> 
 <p>7. Explain the Trinity and man's place in it. </p> 
 <p>8. What is the demand of the present age regarding spiritual ideals? </p> 
 <p>9. What are the three essentials to perfect manhood? What determines the degree to which any one or all of these phases may be expressd? </p> 
 <p>10. What position does man occupy in relation to God? Is it imperative that man understand this relationship? </p> 
 <p>11. Is the same faculty required to discern the Christ today that was required in the time of Jesus? If so, what is it? </p> 
 <p>12. Is it possible for man today to be divinely guided? How is divine guidance brought about? </p> 
 <p>13. Explain the law of attraction, as applied to mind and ideas. </p> 
 <p>14. What authority does man require for thinking or speaking beyond prescribed standards? </p> 
 <p>15. Through what phase of mind do we commune with God? </p> 
 <p>16. Explain the difference between perceiving and demonstrating one's spiritual sonship. What is the first birth? </p> 
 <p>17. Explain the first birth; the second birth. </p> 
 <p>18. What is it to be "born from above"? </p> 
 <p>19. Explain Jesus' relationship to the Father, and how it was developed. </p> 
 <p>20. Why does man invent mechanical devices? How will he attain the satisfaction that he seeks in these things? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_148"><font size="1" color="green">p. 148</font></a></p> 
 <p>21. How is the Christ Mind brought into manifestation in the individual? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Three--Manifestation </p> 
 <p>1. What characterizes Christianity as a science? </p> 
 <p>2. What will bridge the gulf between spiritual and material science? </p> 
 <p>3. Should the Scriptures be considered allegorical? </p> 
 <p>4. When read in the light of Spirit, what does the 1st chapter of Genesis portray? What is meant by the words, "and God . . . rested from all his work"? </p> 
 <p>5. How is the apparent contradiction between the 1st and 2d chapters of Genesis explained? </p> 
 <p>6. What do the six days of creation mean? How shall we attain the Truth that Jesus said would make us free? </p> 
 <p>7. How does all creation become harmonious and orderly to man? </p> 
 <p>8. Describe the difference between the I AM man and the I will man. How was man driven out of Eden? </p> 
 <p>9. What is the physical body, and how is it formed? </p> 
 <p>10. What will a right idea of the character and origin of the body do for one? </p> 
 <p>11. Where does the resurrection begin, and what takes place? </p> 
 <p>12. How shall we get back to the "Father's house"? </p> 
 <p>13. Where do our dreams, visions, and spiritual experiences come from? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_149"><font size="1" color="green">p. 149</font></a></p> 
 <p>14. How shall we enter into the dominion of the "kingdom" within us? </p> 
 <p>15. Explain man's true identity? </p> 
 <p>16. What have the old and the new religious ideas contributed to the solution of our spiritual problems? </p> 
 <p>17. How shall we get back into the Garden of Eden? </p> 
 <p>18. Explain the grouping of ideas. </p> 
 <p>19. How are centers of consciousness brought to respond to the "I will"? </p> 
 <p>20. What is regeneration? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Four--Formative Power Of Thought </p> 
 <p>1. What is the directive power in man? </p> 
 <p>2. Explain the relation of thought to the functions of the body. </p> 
 <p>3. Why have we failed to demonstrate perfect health? </p> 
 <p>4. Upon what must man base his study of God? </p> 
 <p>5. What is God's work in the creation of man? </p> 
 <p>6. What part has man in the creation of himself and his world? </p> 
 <p>7. How does man build his body and his world? </p> 
 <p>8. Upon what must we base our thinking? </p> 
 <p>9. Why has the physical scientist failed to demonstrate wholeness and perfection? </p> 
 <p>10. Explain the difference between life as expressed by man and life in Divine Mind. </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_150"><font size="1" color="green">p. 150</font></a></p> 
 <p>11. Upon what do the thoughts act to produce form? </p> 
 <p>12. Illustrate in your own words how thought works in the organism. </p> 
 <p>13. Where do ideas pertaining to life have their center of action? </p> 
 <p>14. How may we connect the life center with its spiritual source, and what is the result of making such connection? </p> 
 <p>15. What is the cause of imperfect bodies? </p> 
 <p>16. How may man regain his spiritual dominion? </p> 
 <p>17. What attitude of mind is conducive to a higher state of consciousness? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Five--How To Control Thought </p> 
 <p>1. What is the difference between the original thinker and the secondary thought? </p> 
 <p>2. What is the essential fact in all manifestation? </p> 
 <p>3. How may man exercise his highest dominion? </p> 
 <p>4. What are the two fundamental attitudes of mind? </p> 
 <p>5. What use can man make of these movements of the mind in building his world? </p> 
 <p>6. What is the result of letting one's thoughts dwell on the acquiring of wealth? </p> 
 <p>7. Is excessive affirmation advisable? Explain. </p> 
 <p>8. What is the remedy for the ill effects in the </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_151"><font size="1" color="green">p. 151</font></a></p> 
 <p>organism that have come as a result of too much exercise of the affirmative mental attitude? </p> 
 <p>9. What is the result of excessive denial? What is the remedy for this result? </p> 
 <p>10. What is the object of man's existence? </p> 
 <p>11. What is meant by the "Adam" in man? </p> 
 <p>12. What benefit do we derive from experience? </p> 
 <p>13. What is "Satan" in man's consciousness? </p> 
 <p>14. How may man become free from his dual condition? </p> 
 <p>15. Is experience necessary in man's evolution? </p> 
 <p>16. What is repentance? </p> 
 <p>17. What is the meaning of "Deny himself . . . and follow me"? </p> 
 <p>18. Explain the "Judas" in consciousness. </p> 
 <p>19. Explain the meaning of Paul's words, "I die daily." </p> 
 <p>20. What is the result of this process of dying daily? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Six--The Word </p> 
 <p>1. What is the word? </p> 
 <p>2. How may man comprehend the creative process? </p> 
 <p>3. What is the result when the Word is expressed by Divine Mind? </p> 
 <p>4. Explain how the Word becomes flesh. </p> 
 <p>5. How may man make his creation perfect? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_152"><font size="1" color="green">p. 152</font></a></p> 
 <p>6. Explain the original plan of creation in Divine Mind. </p> 
 <p>7. How may man comprehend the word of God? </p> 
 <p>8. How are divine ideas brought to the outer plane of consciousness? </p> 
 <p>9. Explain the creative law, from the formless to the formed. </p> 
 <p>10. What is the result when man's word conforms to the divine law? </p> 
 <p>11. Explain how we give an account for "every idle word." </p> 
 <p>12. What determines the character of the word? </p> 
 <p>13. What ideas make constructive words? </p> 
 <p>14. Explain the word as the "seed." </p> 
 <p>15. What is the result of putting oneself into the consciousness of the supreme Mind? </p> 
 <p>16. What quality must be included in the word, to accomplish results? </p> 
 <p>17. Wherein does physical science fall short in explaining the plan of creation? </p> 
 <p>18. Explain the effect of the spoken word. </p> 
 <p>19. How must we use the word for sure results? </p> 
 <p>20. Give an illustration of the power of the word. </p> 
 <p>Lesson Seven--Spirituality, Or Prayer And Praise </p> 
 <p>1. What is a symbol? </p> 
 <p>2. Explain how symbols are used to describe man. </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_153"><font size="1" color="green">p. 153</font></a></p> 
 <p>3. What do the twelve sons of Jacob symbolize in man? </p> 
 <p>4. Explain how a higher expression of the twelve faculties is attained. </p> 
 <p>5. What is a parable? What is an allegory? </p> 
 <p>6. How are we able to understand parables and allegories? </p> 
 <p>7. Is it possible to understand the twelve centers through the intellect? </p> 
 <p>8. Where is the center of spirituality? </p> 
 <p>9. What is the work of the Judah faculty? </p> 
 <p>10. Why should praise be a component part of prayer? </p> 
 <p>11. Define prayer in your own words. </p> 
 <p>12. Why do we not receive immediate answer to our prayers? </p> 
 <p>13. Name some of the benefits to be derived from prayer. </p> 
 <p>14. What is the effect of praise in prayer? </p> 
 <p>15. Explain the increasing power of praise. </p> 
 <p>16. What does it mean to ask "in my name"? </p> 
 <p>17. Where do we make connection with God, the Father? </p> 
 <p>18. What is God's will for us? </p> 
 <p>19. What attitude of mind is most conducive to the effectiveness of prayers? </p> 
 <p>20. Name five points that you consider essential in prayer. </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_154"><font size="1" color="green">p. 154</font></a></p> 
 <p>Lesson Eight--Faith </p> 
 <p>1. Define faith in your own words. </p> 
 <p>2. Where does faith find its most perfect expression? </p> 
 <p>3. Explain the difference between faith and belief. </p> 
 <p>4. What disciple represents faith? Why? </p> 
 <p>5. What and where is the center through which faith acts? </p> 
 <p>6. What is the result of placing faith in outer things? </p> 
 <p>7. Where must faith be exercised in order to bring the results spoken of by Jesus? </p> 
 <p>8. What is blind faith? Are the results of blind faith satisfactory? </p> 
 <p>9. How, in consciousness, is faith increased? </p> 
 <p>10. What is the inner power of faith? The outer expression? </p> 
 <p>11. How must faith be used to master the spiritual forces? </p> 
 <p>12. What fixes our relation to outer things? </p> 
 <p>13. Where should faith be centered to insure success? </p> 
 <p>14. Explain, from the experience of Peter on the water, how faith grows. </p> 
 <p>15. What should be added to faith in order to make it more effective? </p> 
 <p>16. What two faculties are developed along with faith? </p> 
 <p>17. Explain how these faculties work with faith. </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_155"><font size="1" color="green">p. 155</font></a></p> 
 <p>18. What does it mean to make friends with the "adversary"? </p> 
 <p>19. Explain how we may have faith in the goodness of all men. </p> 
 <p>20. Why is faith essential in healing? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Nine--Imagination </p> 
 <p>1. How does mind manifest? </p> 
 <p>2. Explain wherein man is the "image" and "likeness" of God. </p> 
 <p>3. What power has man as the "image" and "likeness"? </p> 
 <p>4. Define imagination. </p> 
 <p>5. Explain how the imaging faculty works on substance. </p> 
 <p>6. What effect does the imaging power have upon thought? </p> 
 <p>7. Locate the center of imagination. </p> 
 <p>8. What faculties of mind does the imagination use, and how? </p> 
 <p>9. Is it possible to impress these faculties from without, and if so, what is the result? </p> 
 <p>10. What happens when man allows his imagination to run riot, regardless of law? </p> 
 <p>11. How do the faculties of the mind work? </p> 
 <p>12. How can one avoid receiving impressions from without? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_156"><font size="1" color="green">p. 156</font></a></p> 
 <p>13. What is meant by the "mount," as spoken of in the Scriptures? </p> 
 <p>14. What is the power through which the I AM creates or recreates form? </p> 
 <p>15. How are we to control the things that we create? </p> 
 <p>16. What does Jacob represent in consciousness? </p> 
 <p>17. What does the ladder in Jacob's dream represent? </p> 
 <p>18. What does Joseph represent, and how did he reach the highest place in the king's domain? </p> 
 <p>19. What are dreams, and how may they be made useful? </p> 
 <p>20. What power has the imagination in forming the body? Explain. </p> 
 <p>21. What is the highest work of the imagination? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Ten--Will And Understanding </p> 
 <p>1. To what does Jesus refer in the text at the beginning of this lesson? </p> 
 <p>2. Explain how will and understanding work in the individual mind. </p> 
 <p>3. What does man represent as the will of God? </p> 
 <p>4. Explain man as a free agent. </p> 
 <p>5. How do we build individual consciousness? </p> 
 <p>6. What is the I AM? Explain what the I AM means to you. </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_157"><font size="1" color="green">p. 157</font></a></p> 
 <p>7. How may we unify will and understanding? </p> 
 <p>8. How do we produce the perfect man? </p> 
 <p>9. What results when the will acts independently of the understanding? Explain. </p> 
 <p>10. Explain the effect, on the body, of a strong personal will. </p> 
 <p>11. Explain God and man in relation to will. </p> 
 <p>12. Define understanding, and show how it works in the mind. </p> 
 <p>13. What treatment do you suggest for willfullness? </p> 
 <p>14. Explain why the will should not be broken. </p> 
 <p>15. Should one try to dominate the will of another, in order to develop him spiritually? Explain. </p> 
 <p>16. How should you proceed in order to let Divine Mind express itself through you? </p> 
 <p>17. What is true self-control? How may man exercise it? </p> 
 <p>18. How may we gain spiritual mastery? </p> 
 <p>19. What apostle represents will? Explain the use of will in consciousness. </p> 
 <p>20. Upon what must our understanding be based, that we may be willing to do God's will? </p> 
 <p>Lesson Eleven--Judgment And Justice </p> 
 <p>1. Explain the law as given by Moses. </p> 
 <p>2. Why is judgment necessary in the use of the love faculty? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_158"><font size="1" color="green">p. 158</font></a></p> 
 <p>3. Give the metaphysical meaning of the Urim and Thummim. </p> 
 <p>4. How may love and justice be balanced in one's life? </p> 
 <p>5. What is the cause of injustice? </p> 
 <p>6. Where must judgment be placed, in order to express perfect justice? </p> 
 <p>7. What is the meaning of "Judge not, that ye be not judged"? </p> 
 <p>8. Why is man incapable of judging? </p> 
 <p>9. What is the correct way to judge, as explained by Jesus? </p> 
 <p>10. What is meant by "the day of judgment"? </p> 
 <p>11. Explain how and why we are punished. </p> 
 <p>12. How should we receive punishment? </p> 
 <p>13. What is the result of the active use of judgment without understanding? </p> 
 <p>14. What organ of the body is most affected by the active use of judgment, and why? </p> 
 <p>15. Why should we have definite ideas of justice? </p> 
 <p>16. What is the result of condemnation? </p> 
 <p>17. How may we receive perfect justice? </p> 
 <p>18. Explain the effect of jealousy on the faculty of judgment; give remedy. </p> 
 <p>19. What is the relation of judgment to success? </p> 
 <p>20. State the order of procedure in obtaining justice. </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_159"><font size="1" color="green">p. 159</font></a></p> 
 <p>Lesson Twelve--Love </p> 
 <p>1. Explain love in Divine Mind. </p> 
 <p>2. What is the work of love in man? </p> 
 <p>3. What is the result of centering our love on things? </p> 
 <p>4. How may we realize our true relation to life? </p> 
 <p>5. Explain the process of love in the regeneration. </p> 
 <p>6. What apostle represents the faculty of love? </p> 
 <p>7. Where is the love center? </p> 
 <p>8. How may thoughts of hatred be overcome in consciousness? </p> 
 <p>9. What qualities are brought out in man through the activity of the love principle? </p> 
 <p>10. How shall we make love of real value to ourselves? </p> 
 <p>11. Explain how love is the fulfilling of the law. </p> 
 <p>12. What is our relationship to our fellow man when divine love is established in consciousness? </p> 
 <p>13. When is money really the root of evil? </p> 
 <p>14. Explain the difference between divine love and human love. </p> 
 <p>15. How can we know the love of God in its fullness? </p> 
 <p>16. How shall we proceed to maintain a right relation among all creatures? </p> 
 <p>17. What is the result when divine love enters into the thought process? </p> 
 <p>18. Is it possible to approach God while there is aught in the heart against a brother? </p> 
 <p>19. How may we become established in fearlessness </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_160"><font size="1" color="green">p. 160</font></a></p> 
 <p>and courage? Give an illustration from your own experience. </p> 
 <p>20. What is the unfailing antidote for fear? </p>
