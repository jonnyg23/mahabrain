 <p align="CENTER"><font size="-1" color="GREEN"><a href="index.htm"><img src="../img/uni_t.jpg"></a><br><i>The Twelve Powers of Man</i>, by Charles Fillmore, [1930], at sacred-texts.com</font></p><hr> <h3 align="center"> <br> Question Helps<br> For Students of The Twelve Powers of Man<br> </h3>  
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_176"><font size="1" color="green">p. 176</font></a></p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_177"><font size="1" color="green">p. 177</font></a></p> 
 <p>The Twelve Powers Of Man </p> 
 <p>Chapter One </p> 
 <p>1. What do Jesus' Twelve Apostles represent in man's spiritual ongoing? </p> 
 <p>2. What is symbolized by the first and Second Coming of Christ, as spoken of in the Scriptures? </p> 
 <p>3. How may the Twelve Apostles be likened to the heads of departments in an industrial plant? </p> 
 <p>4. What does Jesus represent in man's consciousness? </p> 
 <p>5. Name each apostle and the corresponding faculty represented. </p> 
 <p>6. Why was Peter (faith) called the first apostle? </p> 
 <p>7. Explain how a center may be baptized by the word of Spirit and given a new power. </p> 
 <p>8. Why does the intellect deny that man can have knowledge of God? </p> 
 <p>9. How should the twelve powers of man be developed and expressed? </p> 
 <p>The Development Of Faith </p> 
 <p>Chapter Two </p> 
 <p>1. What does Abraham represent in man's consciousness? </p> 
 <p>2. Why must this faculty be abiding? </p> 
 <p>3. Why is faith put to a test? </p> 
 <p>4. Give the symbology represented by Abraham's offering of his son Isaac. </p> 
 <p>5. Explain the law of giving and receiving. </p> 
 <p>6. What is symbolized by the "upper room" to which the Holy Spirit comes? </p> 
 <p>7. What is the difference between the prayer of supplication and the affirmative prayer? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_178"><font size="1" color="green">p. 178</font></a></p> 
 <p>8. What are the two attitudes of faith that are brought out? Explain. </p> 
 <p>9. Who was the first and greatest apostle of Jesus? </p> 
 <p>10. What does his name mean? </p> 
 <p>11. Why was his name changed after he became Jesus' apostle? </p> 
 <p>12. What is the difference between trust and real faith? </p> 
 <p>13. Why is the spoken word formative, but not always creative? </p> 
 <p>14. Where is the faith center in the body of man? How is it quickened? </p> 
 <p>15. What is the significance of a change of mind? </p> 
 <p>16. Explain how Jesus accomplished the mighty works credited to Him. </p> 
 <p>Strength--Stability--Steadfastness </p> 
 <p>Chapter Three </p> 
 <p>1. Which one of Jesus' apostles symbolized strength in man's consciousness? </p> 
 <p>2. Wherein is the source of all things? </p> 
 <p>3. Why is man greater than all other expressions of Divine Mind? </p> 
 <p>4. What is the difference between the Jehovah man and the Adam man? </p> 
 <p>5. Explain God's creation of man as "male and female." </p> 
 <p>6. What does the "strong man fully armed," referred to by Jesus, represent in the natural man? </p> 
 <p>7. What does it represent in the regenerate man? </p> 
 <p>8. Explain the symbology brought out by the story of David and Goliath. </p> 
 <p>9. What does Samson symbolize in man's consciousness? </p> 
 <p>10. Explain the symbology of his being robbed of his hair by Delilah. </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_179"><font size="1" color="green">p. 179</font></a></p> 
 <p>11. Why should man conserve the vital essence of his body? </p> 
 <p>12. Explain how one may renew the body through using the various brain centers. </p> 
 <p>13. How does creative law affect man's development? </p> 
 <p>14. Explain man's growth in consciousness through evolution. </p> 
 <p>15. Why is the Word of God likened unto a seed? </p> 
 <p>16. Why is Jesus Christ the Great Teacher? </p> 
 <p>Wisdom--Judgment </p> 
 <p>Chapter Four </p> 
 <p>1. What are the twelve powers of man? </p> 
 <p>2. Where is the wisdom center in man's consciousness? </p> 
 <p>3. Name some attributes of wisdom. </p> 
 <p>4. Is knowledge of the negative side of good necessary to man's unfoldment? </p> 
 <p>5. Explain some of the functions of the solar plexus, and name its presiding ego. </p> 
 <p>6. What is man's first step in the regeneration? </p> 
 <p>7. Where is this step referred to in Scripture symbology? </p> 
 <p>8. How do we "call a disciple"? Give an example. </p> 
 <p>9. What is Christ? </p> 
 <p>10. Explain the lawful method of entering the kingdom of God. </p> 
 <p>11. Can man escape divine judgment? Why? </p> 
 <p>12. What part does spiritual judgment play in the working out of man's salvation? </p> 
 <p>13. Why are Peter, James, and John mentioned more than the other apostles? </p> 
 <p>14. Why was Andrew also among the first-called? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_180"><font size="1" color="green">p. 180</font></a></p> 
 <p>Regenerating Love </p> 
 <p>Chapter Five </p> 
 <p>1. Explain how man may use his creative forces in co-operation with the creative law. </p> 
 <p>2. What are the "sons of God" in Scripture symbology? </p> 
 <p>3. How do we know that Being is masculine and feminine? </p> 
 <p>4. What is the name and nature of God's most beautiful "daughter"? </p> 
 <p>5. Name some virtues and vices of mother love. </p> 
 <p>6. Explain love in its role of "gravity." </p> 
 <p>7. Is love ever a source of misery? Explain. </p> 
 <p>8. Give a remedy for divorce. </p> 
 <p>9. What do Adam, Eve, and the serpent symbolize in the individual? </p> 
 <p>10. Give the metaphysical explanation of Adam and Eve's eating of the fruit of the tree of the "knowledge of good and evil." </p> 
 <p>11. What place has love in the regeneration of man? </p> 
 <p>12. By what means can woman help to lift up man and free him from sense bondage? </p> 
 <p>13. Will incarnation cease when love is lifted up? </p> 
 <p>14. What was Jesus' mission on earth? </p> 
 <p>15. Where is the throne of love in man's spiritual body? </p> 
 <p>16. Who, among Jesus' apostles, personified love? </p> 
 <p>17. Give an original illustration of how unselfish love may be unfolded. </p> 
 <p>18. Should one employ force or suppression in soul unfoldment? Why? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_181"><font size="1" color="green">p. 181</font></a></p> 
 <p>Power--Dominion--Mastery </p> 
 <p>Chapter Six </p> 
 <p>1. What is essential to the realization and demonstration of spiritual power? </p> 
 <p>2. Over what should man exercise his divine birthright of power? </p> 
 <p>3. Locate the throne of power in man's body consciousness, and tell what apostle represents power. </p> 
 <p>4. What effect has development of power on the voice? </p> 
 <p>5. How and to what extent are one's words imbued with power? </p> 
 <p>6. Can we overcome the belief in limited or declining power? How? </p> 
 <p>7. Should man develop power before he overthrows carnal selfishness in himself? Why? </p> 
 <p>8. How can we distinguish between right and wrong in the use of our power? </p> 
 <p>9. What is the key to the exercising of dominion successfully in the material world and the restoring of equilibrium between Spirit and matter? </p> 
 <p>10. What rule did Jesus lay down as the basis of world peace and prosperity? </p> 
 <p>11. When will the "new heaven and . . . new earth" appear? </p> 
 <p>12. What is involved in overcoming the world, the flesh, the Devil, and even death? </p> 
 <p>13. What is the Devil? </p> 
 <p>14. Give the metaphysical interpretation of the Crucifixion. </p> 
 <p>15. What is the acme of man's power and dominion? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_182"><font size="1" color="green">p. 182</font></a></p> 
 <p>The Work Of The Imagination In Regeneration </p> 
 <p>Chapter Seven </p> 
 <p>1. What is the imagination? </p> 
 <p>2. Which apostle represents the imagination? </p> 
 <p>3. Within man what office has the imaging faculty? </p> 
 <p>4. Why were Jesus' ideas so powerful and enduring? </p> 
 <p>5. What is the key to the interpretation of symbols? </p> 
 <p>6. What determines the character of soul and body? </p> 
 <p>7. Do dreams and visions play an important part in man's soul development? Explain why, and give Scripture examples. </p> 
 <p>8. Interpret spiritually Peter's vision, which is described in the 10th chapter of Acts. </p> 
 <p>9. What does man's body represent? Explain why. </p> 
 <p>10. Why do individuals and nations sometimes revert from culture to savagery? </p> 
 <p>11. How should man handle the animal propensities in himself? </p> 
 <p>12. Does the fact that animals are good in their rightful place justify man's using them for food? Why? </p> 
 <p>13. How was the original creation given character and form? </p> 
 <p>14. What, potentially, are animals? </p> 
 <p>15. When will animals cease to be in the objective? </p> 
 <p>Understanding </p> 
 <p>Chapter Eight </p> 
 <p>1. Do wisdom, understanding, knowledge, and intelligence all mean the same thing? </p> 
 <p>2. Explain the two great realms of mind. </p> 
 <p>3. Was Jesus scientific in His teaching? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_183"><font size="1" color="green">p. 183</font></a></p> 
 <p>4. What faculty of mind is given first place by those who have spiritual discernment? </p> 
 <p>5. What phase of mind comes first in soul development? </p> 
 <p>6. What does Solomon stand for? </p> 
 <p>7. What did the Lord also give unto Solomon because He was so well pleased with him? </p> 
 <p>8. What part does the heart play in soul unfoldment? </p> 
 <p>9. Why do we have to be careful in distinguishing between true inspiration and our intellectual reasoning? </p> 
 <p>10. What does John the Baptist stand for in man's consciousness? </p> 
 <p>11. Who did Jesus say John the Baptist had been? </p> 
 <p>12. What takes place in man when he gives himself wholly unto the Lord? </p> 
 <p>13. Explain the relationship between John the Baptist and Jesus. </p> 
 <p>14. How does this relationship apply to man's consciousness? </p> 
 <p>15. What is the greatest of all the powers of man? </p> 
 <p>16. Do all the faculties enter into the regeneration of man? </p> 
 <p>17. Does man have the privilege of attaining eternal life? </p> 
 <p>The Will Is The Man </p> 
 <p>Chapter Nine </p> 
 <p>1. What faculty of mind is the motive power for all the other faculties? </p> 
 <p>2. What other faculty is most closely related to it? </p> 
 <p>3. What part does the imagination play in the development of the soul? </p> 
 <p>4. Why has man been given free will? </p> 
 <p>5. What is the work of the solar plexus? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_184"><font size="1" color="green">p. 184</font></a></p> 
 <p>6. How do the will and the understanding conflict in the expression of the spiritual life? </p> 
 <p>7. What causes hardening of the arteries? </p> 
 <p>8. Where does "motive" come in? </p> 
 <p>9. Explain what is meant by man's partaking of the knowledge of good and evil. </p> 
 <p>10. What does Jesus represent in soul development? </p> 
 <p>11. How does the Christ discipline His apostles (faculties)? </p> 
 <p>12. Explain how the will is often allowed to become destructive. </p> 
 <p>13. What is the will of Divine Mind for man as regards his actions? </p> 
 <p>14. Explain why a person should be positive in submitting his will to the authority of God instead of doing so in a negative way. </p> 
 <p>15. With what power and authority did Jesus endow His disciples to go forth and preach? </p> 
 <p>16. Does God's will for man change? </p> 
 <p>17. Why do sin, sickness, suffering, and death come into man's life? </p> 
 <p>18. Should a person submit his will to the control of another? Explain. </p> 
 <p>19. How should a person make his affirmations? </p> 
 <p>20. Give the words of Jesus that may be used as an affirmation in bringing about relaxation, peace, and harmony. </p> 
 <p>Spiritual Law And Order </p> 
 <p>Chapter Ten </p> 
 <p>1. For what did Jesus denounce the scribes and Pharisees? </p> 
 <p>2. Should teachers or leaders frame rules of thought and action for their members? Why? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_185"><font size="1" color="green">p. 185</font></a></p> 
 <p>3. What did Jesus lay down as the only safe foundation for all religious work? </p> 
 <p>4. Why did Jesus break the Mosaic law? </p> 
 <p>5. What does the Sabbath day stand for in man's consciousness? </p> 
 <p>6. Explain why Jesus said that He came not to break the law, but to fulfill it. </p> 
 <p>7. Why must man erase from his mind all authority and tradition of other men in order to fulfill God's will for him? </p> 
 <p>8. What place have sacred writings in man's life? </p> 
 <p>9. What is the only authority? </p> 
 <p>10. Shall we leave our fellows free to worship God as they see fit, and to what extent do we co-operate with them? </p> 
 <p>11. Where does man find real communion with the Father? </p> 
 <p>12. What part does Jesus, the "only begotten Son" of God, play in man's unfoldment? </p> 
 <p>13. What does Moses' fleeing to the wilderness represent in man's consciousness? Explain. </p> 
 <p>14. What gives our words and thoughts power to accomplish that whereto they are sent? </p> 
 <p>15. What forces do Moses and Pharaoh represent? Explain. </p> 
 <p>16. Explain the place of denial and affirmation in man's growth. </p> 
 <p>Zeal--Enthusiasm </p> 
 <p>Chapter Eleven </p> 
 <p>1. What is the function of the ego in forming mental states of consciousness? Explain. </p> 
 <p>2. What is zeal? </p> 
 <p>3. Can the divine law be broken? Explain. </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_186"><font size="1" color="green">p. 186</font></a></p> 
 <p>4. Should one suppress the impulse of zeal that wells up in consciousness? </p> 
 <p>5. Why is it necessary for zeal to be tempered with wisdom? </p> 
 <p>6. How do one's different combinations of thoughts affect soul and body? </p> 
 <p>7. What is the meaning of the name "Simon Peter"? What is the meaning of the name "Simon, the Cananaean"? </p> 
 <p>8. Explain how the vitamins in the food that we eat are stored up in body consciousness and liberated. </p> 
 <p>9. Has zeal any part to play in one's becoming a genius along any line of endeavor? </p> 
 <p>10. Explain Jesus' words, "The very hairs of your head are numbered." </p> 
 <p>11. Explain Emerson's words, "Be not deceived by dimples and curls; that child is a thousand years old." </p> 
 <p>12. How do you account for Mozart's wonderful ability to play the organ without instruction at the age of four years? </p> 
 <p>13. Where does the zeal center have its throne of dominion? </p> 
 <p>Renunication </p> 
 <p>Chapter Twelve </p> 
 <p>1. What is the first step in experiencing conversion? </p> 
 <p>2. Explain the effect of a change of mind on the body organism. </p> 
 <p>3. In the process of body renewal, where does renunciation come in? </p> 
 <p>4. How does the metaphysician arrive at his conclusions concerning mind action? </p> 
 <p>5. State one big fundamental truth brought out in the study of mind. </p> 
 <p>6. Explain the distinction between the realm of ideas and the realm of thought. </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_187"><font size="1" color="green">p. 187</font></a></p> 
 <p>7. Explain how thoughts are assimilated by the mind. </p> 
 <p>8. How does the mind act on the body? </p> 
 <p>9. Why should wisdom be exercised in the control of the mind? </p> 
 <p>10. Should man be given his freedom? Explain. </p> 
 <p>11. How are traits of mind and body handed down from generation to generation? </p> 
 <p>12. How are these dissolved in memory? </p> 
 <p>13. Explain medical science's method of combating disease germs. </p> 
 <p>14. Why has it failed? </p> 
 <p>15. What is the one healing power? Explain its action. </p> 
 <p>16. Describe the work of Thaddaeus. </p> 
 <p>17. What is the cause of constipation? of tumors? of the slowing down of the life forces? </p> 
 <p>18. What is the cause of adenoids? of inflamed tonsils? of sinus trouble? </p> 
 <p>19. How may these ills be avoided? </p> 
 <p>Generative Life </p> 
 <p>Chapter Thirteen </p> 
 <p>1. What is the life energy? </p> 
 <p>2. Is life intelligence? Explain. </p> 
 <p>3. Why is the life ego called the "adversary"? </p> 
 <p>4. Why are sensation and generation necessary to man's character? Explain. </p> 
 <p>5. What is the Son of man? </p> 
 <p>6. What does the Garden of Eden symbolize? </p> 
 <p>7. On what does the bringing forth of the ideal man depend? </p> 
 <p>8. What three factors enter into every consciousness? </p> 
 <p>9. What is the Lamb of God? Explain. </p> 
 <p>10. What is symbolized by the "river of water of life"? </p> 
 <p>
 </p><p><a name="page_188"><font size="1" color="green">p. 188</font></a></p> 
 <p>11. Wherein comes the danger in experiencing this holy stream of life? </p> 
 <p>12. How are bodily sensations lawfully experienced? Explain. </p> 
 <p>13. Why is it necessary to cleanse this pure stream of life in its flow outward? </p> 
 <p>14. Explain: "As Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of man be lifted up." </p> 
 <p>15. Explain sensation's being a divine creation. </p> 
 <p>16. How is all intemperance overcome? </p> 
 <p>17. Explain your being "dead through your trespasses and sins." </p>
